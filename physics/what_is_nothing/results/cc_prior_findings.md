## 1. Physical Motivation for Each Prior

### Linear (uniform) prior
Motivation: Λ arises as the **SUM** of ~10⁶⁰ independent QFT vacuum-energy contributions
at each mass threshold (electron, pion, W, Planck...). By the central limit theorem a sum
of many independent contributions converges to a Gaussian; for one-sided sampling the
effective prior is approximately uniform on [0, ρ_Planck]. This is the prior **implicit
in the classical statement of the CC problem**.

### Log-uniform (Jeffreys) prior
Motivation: Λ is a **scale parameter** — no preferred scale exists between ε and ρ_Planck.
The Jeffreys prior for a scale parameter is exactly flat in ln Λ. This is natural when:
- Λ is generated as a product of phase-space factors or flux quanta
- The landscape samples Λ via multiplicative/logarithmic selection (Bousso-Polchinski flux
  quanta each shift ln Λ by a roughly fixed amount → landscape prior on ln Λ is approximately uniform)
- One has no a priori information about the scale of Λ

### Gaussian prior (σ = ρ_Planck, centred on 0)
Motivation: **Cancellation** between a large positive Λ_bare and large negative ρ_QFT,
both O(ρ_Planck) but independent Gaussians. Their difference is Gaussian with σ ~ ρ_Planck.
Motivated by SUSY or other symmetry-imposed near-cancellations.

### Key tension
The QFT computation of ρ_QFT is genuinely **additive** → linear/Gaussian prior is
physically motivated for the bare mechanism. But the landscape **selects** from many vacua
→ the landscape prior on the observable Λ may be log-uniform even if each individual vacuum
was generated by an additive process, because flux quanta shift Λ multiplicatively across
many decades. The correct prior depends on the full generative story: mechanism + selection.

## 2. Prior Probabilities P(Λ ≤ Λ_max)

| Prior | P(Λ ≤ Λ_max) | N_expected in window |
|---|---|---|
| Log-uniform (Jeffreys) | **0.5587 (55.9%)** | 10^499.7 |
| Uniform (linear) | 3.84×10⁻¹³⁹ | 10^361.6 |
| Gaussian (σ = ρ_Planck) | 1.53×10⁻¹³⁹ | 10^361.2 |

**Under a log-uniform prior the CC fine-tuning problem DISSOLVES.**
Λ_obs sits in the upper 56% of the distribution — it is not fine-tuned at all.
Under a linear prior the suppression is 10⁻¹³⁹ — the "123 orders of magnitude" problem.

## 3. Posterior Medians — Where is the "Typical" Λ?

| Prior | Median Λ_typical | Distance from Λ_obs |
|---|---|---|
| Log-uniform (Jeffreys) | 10^−43 J/m³ | 17 decades |
| Uniform (linear) | 10^113 J/m³ | 140 decades |
| Gaussian (σ = ρ_Planck) | 10^113 J/m³ | 140 decades |

The log-uniform prior does **not** require Λ_obs to sit at the median — it requires only
that Λ_obs is not anomalously small relative to the prior. With P = 56%, it is not:
Λ_obs is **above-median** in log-space, meaning it is a typical draw from the log-uniform
distribution. The linear prior puts the median at ρ_Planck/2 — 140 decades from Λ_obs,
which is the classical fine-tuning statement.

## 4. Kolmogorov Complexity of Each Prior

| Prior | K estimate | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Log-uniform (Jeffreys) | ~100 bits | log-flatness + 2 endpoints (ε and ρ_Planck) |
| Uniform (linear) | ~50 bits | linear-flat + 1 endpoint (ρ_Planck) |
| Gaussian (σ = ρ_Planck) | ~100 bits | Gaussian shape + σ = ρ_Planck + μ = 0 |

K(which prior, given 3 candidates) ≈ log₂(3) ≈ **1.58 bits**

The three priors are **K-comparable** — no prior has a decisive K-advantage over the others.
The uniform prior is shortest (~50 bits) but produces the worst typicality for Λ_obs.
An extra ~50 bits of prior specification (switching to log-uniform) buys an enormous
improvement: from 10⁻¹³⁹ to 56%.

**The CC fine-tuning problem is sensitive to ~50-bit changes in the prior description.**
This is an unusually tight sensitivity: a 50-bit difference in the prior specification
switches the problem from "catastrophically improbable" to "completely typical".

## 5. K-Information Argument: Prior as Mechanism Knowledge

The prior on Λ is not a free parameter — it encodes the **mechanism** by which Λ
is generated or selected. Each prior implies a distinct physical picture:

| Prior | Mechanism implied |
|---|---|
| Log-uniform | Multiplicative/logarithmic: Λ set by a PRODUCT of factors (flux quanta, instanton factors exp(−8π²/g²), phase-space ratios) |
| Linear | Additive: Λ set by a SUM of QFT zero-point energies at each mass threshold — the explicit QFT calculation |
| Gaussian | Cancellation: Λ = Λ_bare − ρ_QFT with both terms O(ρ_Planck), independent Gaussian |

The prior itself carries **~1.6 bits** of K-information about the mechanism. Knowing which
prior applies tells us whether Λ was set by a multiplicative or additive or cancellation
process. We currently do **not** know which mechanism operates.

**Observational discrimination is in principle possible:**
- If we found evidence that Λ_obs is "typical" (not fine-tuned) → Bayesian evidence for
  log-uniform → multiplicative mechanism (landscape selection)
- If we found a physical mechanism for near-exact SUSY cancellation → Gaussian prior
- The current value of Λ alone cannot discriminate between the priors

**K-information residue:** Even after the fine-tuning problem dissolves under log-uniform,
there remains the ~1.6-bit question of *which mechanism the universe uses*. This is the
true residue of the CC problem once the fine-tuning artifact is removed.

## 6. Final Synthesis — Three Components of the CC Problem

### (a) Technical Problem [prior-INDEPENDENT; real and unresolved]

QFT computes ρ_QFT ≈ ρ_Planck = 4.634×10¹¹³ J/m³.
Observed: ρ_Λ_obs = 5.924×10⁻²⁷ J/m³.
Gap: ρ_QFT / ρ_Λ_obs ≈ 10^140.

This gap is **real regardless of prior**. QFT correctly predicts the magnitude of each
mass-threshold contribution; those contributions are genuinely Planck-scale. The gap
requires a physical mechanism (SUSY, landscape, cancellation, or other). SUSY at
E_SUSY ≈ 1.3×10⁻⁴ meV would cancel it exactly, but this is 1.9×10¹⁸× below the
electroweak scale — not physically motivated. The technical problem is the **hard core**
of the CC problem.

### (b) Fine-Tuning Problem [prior-DEPENDENT; dissolves under log-uniform]

"What is the probability that Λ_obs ≤ Λ_max?"

| Prior | P(Λ ≤ Λ_max) | Assessment |
|---|---|---|
| Linear | 3.84×10⁻¹³⁹ | Catastrophic fine-tuning |
| Log-uniform | 55.9% | **Not fine-tuned** |

**Conclusion:** The fine-tuning problem is an artifact of the implicit linear prior in the
classical statement. Switching to a log-uniform prior (appropriate when Λ is a scale
parameter in a landscape) dissolves the fine-tuning component completely. This does NOT
resolve (a) — the mechanism producing the 10^140 gap is still required — but it removes
the improbability framing.

### (c) Why-This-Λ Problem [prior-INDEPENDENT; resolved by anthropic + landscape]

"Why do we find ourselves in a vacuum with this particular Λ?"

For ALL priors, the landscape provides N >> 10^300 vacua in the anthropic window.
Anthropic selection is viable: observers only exist in the window (Weinberg, 1987).
This component is **resolved** by the combined landscape + anthropic argument.

| Prior | N_expected in anthropic window |
|---|---|
| Log-uniform | 10^499.7 |
| Linear | 10^361.6 |
| Gaussian | 10^361.2 |

### Residue — K-information content of the CC problem

After removing (b) [prior artifact] and (c) [landscape + anthropic], what remains is:

1. **(a) The technical gap**: what mechanism produces the 10^140 cancellation?
2. **~1.6 bits of mechanism knowledge**: is the generating process additive (linear
   prior) or multiplicative (log-uniform prior) or a cancellation (Gaussian prior)?

The CC problem reduces to:

> "What generates the 140-decade gap between the QFT prediction and the observed
> value — and is that mechanism additive or multiplicative in character?"

This is a sharper, more tractable question than "why is Λ so small?"

## Main Finding

**The cosmological constant "fine-tuning problem" is partly or entirely a prior-choice
artifact.**

Under the log-uniform (Jeffreys) prior — appropriate for a scale parameter with no
preferred scale, and naturally arising in the Bousso-Polchinski landscape — the observed
Λ is **not fine-tuned**: P = 56%. The classical "123 orders of magnitude" problem emerges
only under the linear prior implicit in the QFT sum-of-contributions picture.

The prior choice encodes **~1.6 bits** of physical information about the generating
mechanism. This information is not currently available from observation or theory.

The CC problem, properly decomposed, consists of:
- **(b) Fine-tuning**: a prior-artifact — dissolves under log-uniform
- **(c) Why-this-Λ**: resolved by landscape + anthropic selection
- **(a) Technical gap**: the hard residue — what mechanism cancels ρ_QFT to give ρ_Λ_obs?

The prior itself is K-information about the answer to (a).
