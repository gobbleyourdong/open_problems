## Three Prior Distributions on Λ

### Distribution A — Log-uniform (Jeffreys) prior on [ε, ρ_Planck]

A log-uniform (or Jeffreys) prior is flat in ln Λ. It assigns equal probability
to each decade of Λ. Because the anthropic window spans
ln(Λ_max) − ln(ε) ≈ 403 nats out of the full range
ln(ρ_Planck) − ln(ε) ≈ 722 nats, the prior probability is:

  P(Λ ≤ Λ_max | log-uniform) ≈ **56%**

This is a large fraction: under a log-uniform prior the anthropic window
contains more than half the probability mass. Expected vacua in window:

  N_expected ≈ 10^500 × 0.56 ≈ **10^499.7**

### Distribution B — Uniform prior on [0, ρ_Planck]

A uniform prior is the "naive" flat distribution over the linear scale.
The window is a tiny fraction of the Planck range:

  P(Λ ≤ Λ_max | uniform) = Λ_max / ρ_Planck = 3.84×10⁻¹³⁹

Expected vacua in window:

  N_expected = 10^500 × 3.84×10⁻¹³⁹ ≈ **3.84 × 10^361**

### Distribution C — Gaussian centred on 0 (σ = ρ_Planck)

A Gaussian with zero mean and σ = ρ_Planck is the natural "random"
distribution when Λ results from cancellations of Planck-scale terms.
For Λ_max ≪ σ the CDF is linear:

  P(Λ ∈ [0, Λ_max] | Gaussian) ≈ Λ_max / (σ √(2π)) = 1.53×10⁻¹³⁹

Expected vacua in window:

  N_expected ≈ 10^500 × 1.53×10⁻¹³⁹ ≈ **10^361.2**

## Physical Interpretation

### What the landscape resolves

The cosmological constant problem is a 10^140-decade discrepancy between
ρ_Planck and ρ_Λ_obs. The anthropic window (from `anthropic_window.py`)
resolves ~1 decade (the Weinberg factor of ~12–30). The landscape resolves
the remaining ~139 decades by providing 10^500 tries:

  - 10^139 tries needed to hit the window (probabilistically)
  - 10^500 tries available from the landscape
  - 10^361 surplus vacua: selection is vastly overdetermined

### What the landscape does not resolve

The landscape shifts, but does not eliminate, the fine-tuning question.
It replaces "why is our Λ small?" with "why do we find ourselves in one
of the 10^361 anthropically-viable vacua rather than any other?"
This requires an additional measure on the landscape — an unsolved problem
(the landscape measure problem or Boltzmann baby problem).

### Comparison with SUSY at meV

The previous script (`susy_cancellation.py`) showed SUSY at the meV scale
could cancel the CC to the observed value — but requires a SUSY-breaking
scale 10^28× lower than the electroweak scale, which is not physically
motivated. The landscape approach trades one unnatural number (Λ/ρ_Planck)
for a large integer (500 fluxes each taking integer values). Whether this
is "more natural" is a matter of taste, but the landscape is at least a
concrete mechanism grounded in string theory.
