# attempt_001 — Four Senses of Nothing, and Why Only One of Them Is Incoherent

**Date:** 2026-04-09
**Status:** Foundation. Argues that "nothing" has been four distinct concepts in the literature (empty room, physical vacuum, quantum vacuum, and metaphysical nothing), and only the last is the real tier-0 question. The first three are progressively refined physics questions and are not incoherent. The fourth — genuine metaphysical non-being — is conceptually unstable in the way Parmenides diagnosed, and the compression view strengthens the diagnosis by showing that "nothing" as a specifiable state requires structure, which contradicts its own content.

## Four senses of "nothing"

The word "nothing" is doing at least four jobs in the literature. They must be separated before the tier-0 question can be stated clearly.

### Sense 1 — Empty room (absence of objects)

**Claim.** A room is "empty" when it contains no furniture, no people, no objects of interest.

**Status.** This is not nothing in any metaphysical sense. The room still has space, air, walls, and whatever the floor is made of. "Empty" here is absence-relative-to-a-type: no objects of the type the speaker cares about. This sense of nothing is pragmatic and does not bear on the tier-0 question.

### Sense 2 — Physical vacuum (absence of particles and fluid)

**Claim.** A region of space is a "vacuum" when it contains no particles of ordinary matter.

**Status.** Classical physics treats the vacuum as empty. Modern physics does not: a classical vacuum still has electromagnetic fields (including zero-point fields), gravity, and spacetime structure. Pumping out the air gets you to a low-particle-density region, not to nothing.

### Sense 3 — Quantum vacuum (the lowest-energy state of quantum fields)

**Claim.** The quantum vacuum is the ground state of all quantum fields, with no particles present and minimal fluctuation.

**Status.** The quantum vacuum is not empty either. It has vacuum expectation values, zero-point energy, virtual particle creation/annihilation, Casimir force between plates, Lamb shift in atomic spectra. Every observed quantum effect of the vacuum confirms that it has structure. Calling it "nothing" is a category error — it is the ground state of a richly-structured system.

**The cosmological constant problem** is where the quantum vacuum's structure becomes quantitative. The observed vacuum energy density is ~10⁻¹²³ (in Planck units), while naive quantum field theory estimates it at ~10⁰. The discrepancy is ~120 orders of magnitude. This is the largest disagreement between theory and observation in physics. Whatever "the vacuum" is, it is precisely specified, and getting its energy density wrong by 120 orders of magnitude tells us we do not understand the specification. It does not tell us the vacuum is "nothing"; it tells us the vacuum is something we are confused about.

**Krauss 2012** *A Universe from Nothing* — argues that quantum mechanics allows universes to emerge from the quantum vacuum. Philosophers pointed out (correctly) that Krauss's "nothing" is the quantum vacuum, which is a rich structure, so the book does not answer the real tier-0 question. Krauss largely conceded this in later interviews. The book is fine physics; it is not a solution to the metaphysical problem it advertised.

### Sense 4 — Metaphysical nothing (the absence of all being)

**Claim.** A state in which nothing exists at all: no objects, no space, no time, no fields, no laws, no possibilities, no mathematical structure. The pure, complete absence of being.

**Status.** This is the real tier-0 question. It is the sense in which Leibniz's question "why is there something rather than nothing?" has bite. The first three senses are increasingly refined physics; this is metaphysics.

**The Parmenidean move:** this sense may be conceptually incoherent.

## Parmenides, revisited

Parmenides (c. 500 BCE) argued that being is and non-being is not — i.e., that "nothing" cannot genuinely exist because to exist is to be, and to be is not to be nothing.

The modern form of this argument: "nothing" is a concept. To entertain it requires a specification — you have to say what nothing is, and distinguish it from not-nothing. But the act of specification requires structure (a distinction, a predicate, a boundary), and structure is not nothing. Therefore "nothing" as a stably specified state is a self-contradiction.

This is not a proof that something exists. It is an argument that the concept "total absence of being" is unstable under examination: any specification of nothing introduces structure that the specification denies.

**Standard objection.** The Parmenidean argument confuses the concept of nothing with the state of nothing. "Nothing" as a state does not need to specify itself — it just is (or is not). The specification happens from outside, by minds that are contemplating the concept, not from inside a state of non-being.

**Parmenidean reply.** Fine, but then "nothing" is a concept in minds, not a possible state of reality. If nothing is just a thought we think about a state that cannot be instantiated in any way, then the question "why is there something rather than nothing" is asking about a non-alternative. It is like asking "why is there English rather than the particular utterance 'the round square'?" — the round square is not a genuine alternative because no state of affairs corresponds to it. Nothing may not be a genuine alternative in the same way.

This sharpens the disagreement but does not settle it. The compression view adds one piece.

## The compression reframing

From `physics/what_is_information/attempt_001`, K-information is the structure present in a system — the compressibility, the regularity, the pattern-ness. A system with zero K-content has no patterns, no regularities, no distinctions.

Claim: **a state with zero K-content is not a describable state at all.** Any description introduces distinctions; distinctions introduce structure; structure is K-content. You cannot specify "the state with zero K-content" without, in the act of specification, giving it nonzero K-content (namely the content "has zero K-content"). This is not a trivial paradox; it is the compression-view form of the Parmenidean argument.

**Sharper form.** Let a "state" be anything a compressor could observe, specify, or compute about. Then:

- Any state has some K-content (at minimum, the fact that it is THIS state and not another).
- "Nothing" as a state would have zero K-content.
- Zero K-content is not a state (nothing can be said about it, nothing distinguishes it from anything else).
- Therefore "nothing" is not a state.

**Consequence for the tier-0 question.** If nothing is not a state, then "why is there something rather than nothing?" is asking why the actual state of affairs obtains rather than a non-alternative. This is not quite the question it appears to be.

**Honest caveat.** The argument depends on taking "state" to mean "something specifiable by a compressor." An anti-realist could object that there could be a reality without any compressors, and therefore without any specifications, and that such a reality could be nothing. The compression view's response: compression is a necessary condition for any thought about the question, so even the anti-realist version assumes a viewpoint (the anti-realist's), which is a compressor. The argument holds for any view we could reach, which is all views that matter for the question.

## Heidegger's contribution and its limits

Heidegger's *What Is Metaphysics?* famously asks about "the nothing" and gives a phenomenological account: the nothing reveals itself in moods like anxiety, where the totality of beings recedes and is experienced as-a-whole-with-a-boundary. The experience of the nothing is supposed to be a primordial encounter with being as such.

**Contribution.** Heidegger treats the question of nothing as phenomenologically serious, not just a linguistic puzzle. The felt boundary of the totality of beings is a real phenomenon that deserves attention.

**Limit.** The phenomenological nothing is still not metaphysical nothing. Heidegger's "nothing" is the experienced negation-of-all-beings by a being that exists and experiences. It is an existential mood, not a state of non-being. A Heideggerian anxiety in which the totality of beings becomes uncanny is a state of a compressor (the anxious person) compressing the world in a specific way. It is not metaphysical non-being; it is a phenomenal mode of experiencing being.

Under γ, Heidegger's nothing is the self-model's report about an unusual configuration of its world-model. That is interesting but does not bear on sense 4.

## The anti-problem

PROBLEM.md asks: if nothing exists, what is doing the existing of nothing? The self-undermining nature of "nothing" is evidence that something must exist necessarily. But what determines WHICH something?

**The compression view's answer, in two parts.**

**Part 1.** "Nothing" is not a possible state because it is not specifiable. This is the Parmenidean + compression argument from above. Something must exist in the sense that there is no coherent alternative to something.

**Part 2.** What determines which something? The compression view gives a partial answer: the "something" is the converged compression of observations. See `physics/what_is_reality/attempt_001`. But this pushes the question rather than answering it — it says "the something is whatever the compressors compress," which leaves open the question of why the compressors compress what they do, i.e., why the laws of physics are these laws.

**The residue.** The compression view dissolves the first half of the anti-problem (the Parmenidean first half) and inherits the second half (the specific-laws half, which belongs to `what_is_reality`). No theory currently has a good answer to "why these laws" that does not either (a) posit a multiverse of all possible laws (passing the buck), (b) appeal to anthropic selection (useful but not explanatory), or (c) declare the question malformed (the dissolution move).

The compression view is compatible with (c): if "which laws" is really a question about which specific K-specifications the converged compression settles into, and if the compression is a fixed point of observations, then the laws are what they are because they are self-consistent and because they are what anything instantiable must converge on. This is deflationary and does not satisfy everyone; it may be all that is available.

## The five ontologies from PROBLEM.md, revisited

### Metaphysical nothing

Reduced to sense 4. Possibly incoherent per the compression-strengthened Parmenidean argument.

### Physical vacuum

Sense 2. Not nothing; has fields, spacetime, and structure. A fine physics concept but not the tier-0 answer.

### Mathematical nothing (empty set)

A specific K-specification: the set with no elements. Like any mathematical object, it is a compressed description of a structure. The empty set is an interesting mathematical object, not metaphysical nothing. Confusion between them is the standard category error.

### Relational nothing (no distinctions yet)

This is close to a state with zero K-content, which the compression argument says is not a state. If "no distinctions yet" means "distinctions exist potentially but are not yet made," it is a state with K-content (the potential). If it means "no distinctions at all, even potentially," it collapses into the Parmenidean impossibility.

### Impossible

This is the compression/Parmenidean position. Nothingness in sense 4 is conceptually incoherent, therefore not a possible alternative, therefore something must exist.

**The five ontologies collapse into two.** Senses 1-3 are refinements of "physical nothing" and are not nothing in the tier-0 sense. Sense 4 is either coherent and contestable (Leibniz's question has bite) or incoherent and dismissible (the compression/Parmenidean position). The five-way presentation in PROBLEM.md is a pedagogical convenience; the actual dispute is two-way.

## What this attempt closes

- The four senses of nothing are distinguished; only sense 4 is the tier-0 question.
- The Parmenidean argument against sense 4 is developed in compression terms and given a specific formulation: zero K-content is not a specifiable state.
- Heidegger's contribution is acknowledged and correctly located (phenomenological, not metaphysical).
- Krauss-style physics "nothing" is correctly located (senses 2-3, not 4).
- The anti-problem is partly answered (first half dissolved, second half inherited by `what_is_reality`).

## What remains

- The cosmological-constant problem is a real residue: the vacuum energy density is off by 120 orders of magnitude. This is a physics problem about sense 3, not a metaphysical problem about sense 4. It should be treated on its own terms in a further attempt.
- The Parmenidean/compression argument has well-known opponents (some Meinongians, some dialetheists). A rigorous treatment would engage with them directly. This attempt does not.
- "Why these laws" (the second half of the anti-problem) is inherited by `what_is_reality` and was not settled there either.
