# What Is Nothing?

> The absence question. Why is there something rather than nothing?

## Generated Disciplines

- Quantum Field Theory
- Metaphysics
- Theology
- Cosmology

## The Ladder (Tier 0 → Tier 3)

| Tier | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Tier 0** | What is nothing? |
| **Tier 1** | Is genuine non-being possible? Does vacuum count as nothing? |
| **Tier 2** | The quantum vacuum has energy, fields, fluctuations — is it "nothing"? |
| **Tier 3** | What is the vacuum energy density and why is it ~120 orders of magnitude off theory? |

## Competing Ontologies

- **Metaphysical nothing** — No things, no laws, no possibility. Genuine nothing.
- **Physical vacuum** — Zero particles but quantum fields still exist.
- **Mathematical nothing** — Empty set. Pure formal absence.
- **Relational nothing** — No distinctions yet. Prior to differentiation.
- **Impossible** — Nothingness is incoherent. Something must exist.

## Gap Summary

Leibniz's question — why is there something rather than nothing — has no accepted answer. Physics can get close to "nothing" but the quantum vacuum is not empty. Metaphysics argues genuine nothingness is impossible. But we have no proof of its necessity.

## Anti-Problem

If nothing exists, what is doing the existing of nothing? The self-undermining nature of "nothing" is evidence that something must exist necessarily. But what determines WHICH something?
