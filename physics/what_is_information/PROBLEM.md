## Competing Ontologies

- **Shannon (syntactic)** — Information is reduction in uncertainty about a source.
- **Kolmogorov (algorithmic)** — Information is minimum program length to produce the data.
- **Physical (Landauer)** — Information is physical. Erasing bits dissipates heat.
- **Fisher information** — Information is curvature of the likelihood function.
- **Semantic** — Information must be about something. Bits alone aren't information.
- **It from bit (Wheeler)** — Information is the substrate of reality itself.

## Gap Summary

We have multiple incompatible formalizations of "information" and no unifying theory. Physical theories (black hole entropy, holographic principle) suggest information is fundamental to reality, but we don't know what that means ontologically.

## Anti-Problem

What would uninformation look like? Is random noise the absence of information or maximal information? The paradox (noise has high Shannon entropy but zero usefulness) reveals that "information" is doing two different jobs.
