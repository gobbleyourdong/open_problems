## Part 2: Discrimination via Non-GW Experiments

GW events accumulate passive evidence. The experiments below are active discriminants:
they can pay K-debt in both directions (confirming an alternative OR falsifying it),
and some can pay debt faster per experiment than the GW campaign.

### 2a. Proton Decay (String Theory K-Debt Killer)

**Experiment:** Hyper-Kamiokande, operational from 2027. Sensitivity: τ_p > 10^35 years
for p → e⁺π⁰ (the dominant GUT mode).

**Prediction landscape:**

| Scenario | K-impact | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Proton decay observed at τ ~ 10^34–35 yr | Up to ~100 bits paid toward String K-debt | FAVORS String/GUT |
| Not observed to τ > 10^36 yr (decade-scale run) | ~100 bits AGAINST string-motivated GUT | DISFAVORS String |
| SM+GR: no proton decay | Consistent; no K-change | NEUTRAL for SM+GR |

String-motivated GUT (SU(5), SO(10)) predicts τ_p ~ 10^34–35 years. If Hyper-K sees
proton decay at that timescale, it pays ~30–100 bits of String K-debt (exact payment
depends on measurement precision and the prior probability of GUT-scale unification).
The binary confirmation of a new decay mode alone pays 1 bit. Precision matching
the predicted lifetime at 5σ would pay log₂(5σ excess significance) ≈ 4–5 additional
bits. A precision lifetime measurement to 10% accuracy pays log₂(10) ≈ 3.32 bits.
A combined campaign: plausibly 30–100 bits total.

**If proton decay is NOT observed** to τ > 10^36 yr after a full Hyper-K run, this
rules out the simplest SU(5) GUT unification at 95%+ confidence. K-informationalism
assigns ~100 bits AGAINST string-motivated GUT: the landscape vacuum that predicted
proton decay at those rates is excluded, narrowing the viable landscape fraction and
increasing the effective K-debt for string theory's vacuum selection.

**Proton decay does not pay the full 2,161-bit String K-debt.** It addresses only the
~100-bit component attributable to GUT-scale unification. The bulk (1,661 bits of flux-
quanta landscape address) remains.

### 2b. Primordial B-Modes (CCC and Inflation K-Debt)

**Experiment:** CMB-S4, sensitivity to tensor-to-scalar ratio r > 0.003 starting ~2027.

**Prediction landscape:**

| Scenario | K-impact | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| B-modes detected, r ~ 0.01–0.1 | ~20 bits paid to inflationary models | FAVORS inflation |
| B-modes at angular scale predicted by CCC Hawking rings | Up to ~466 bits paid to CCC | FAVORS CCC |
| No B-modes to r < 0.003 | Rules out large-field inflation; ~20 bits AGAINST | DISFAVORS large-field |
| SM+GR (no inflation): tensor modes from standard QG | Minimal tensor prediction; r ≪ 0.003 | NEUTRAL |

CCC predicts observable B-modes from gravitational waves sourced in a previous aeon,
imprinted as a distinctive angular-scale pattern in the CMB. If CMB-S4 detects B-modes
at the predicted CCC angular scales, the payment is potentially the full 466-bit CCC
K-debt. This is the single most efficient non-GW K-debt payment on the timeline.

Inflation (not CCC-specific) predicts r ~ 0.001–0.1 depending on the inflationary
potential. A B-mode detection confirms the tensor channel exists, paying ~20 bits to
any inflationary theory. This does not by itself confirm CCC, which requires the
distinctive ring pattern, not just a detection.

The CMB-S4 sensitivity (r > 0.003) covers the range where most natural inflation
models predict. A null result at this sensitivity meaningfully disfavors large-field
inflation (~20 bits against those specific models).

### 2c. Neutron EDM (CP Violation Beyond SM)

**Experiment:** NIST/SNS neutron EDM measurement, target sensitivity d_n < 10^{-28} e·cm,
expected results 2025–2026.

**Prediction landscape:**

| Scenario | K-impact | Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Signal d_n ~ 10^{-28} e·cm | ~300 bits paid to String/GUT CP-violation models | FAVORS String/BSM |
| Null: d_n < 10^{-28} e·cm | SM prediction verified; ~5 bits toward SM+GR | FAVORS SM+GR |
| Signal d_n ~ 10^{-31} e·cm (SM level) | Indistinguishable from SM; no K-impact | NEUTRAL |

The SM predicts d_n ~ 10^{-31} e·cm — far below current sensitivity. Any signal at
or above 10^{-28} e·cm implies CP violation beyond the SM, which multiple string-
motivated models predict as a consequence of moduli stabilization and soft SUSY
breaking. A confirmed non-SM EDM would pay ~300 bits of String/GUT K-debt: the
detection binary is 1 bit, and the precision of the measurement at 5σ significance
provides log₂(5σ/σ_SM) additional bits, potentially ~300 bits for a 10³-fold
improvement over SM prediction.

**Timeline note:** the SNS experiment was designed for data collection by 2025–2026.
If results have been released by now (2026-04-09), this K-impact is already in.

### Why the first unique prediction matters most

The GW accumulation is a passive default. It runs automatically and excludes all
alternatives that do not make unique predictions. A theory that waits for GW to clear
its debt is losing, not winning.

The first alternative theory to make a correct unique prediction breaks the default.
That prediction pays K-debt at the rate the prediction's precision warrants, and no
further GW accumulation counts against it for that specific K-component. The theory
that predicts and confirms first — even a single 100-bit prediction — becomes a
genuine MDL competitor to SM+GR.

As of 2026-04-09, no alternative TOE has made that move.
