## Part 2: The One True Discriminant

### What it is

The ONLY observation that would cleanly distinguish K-informationalism from
S-informationalism is:

> **Late-time Hawking radiation K-structure recovery (the Page curve K-content)**

This is the only physical observable where the two frameworks make a different
prediction about the K-content of radiation — not merely about its S-content.

### The precise predictions

**Under K-informationalism:**

Hawking radiation has K ≈ 0 throughout the entire evaporation — before and after
the Page time. The ~190 bits of K_matter from the infalling matter are genuinely
destroyed at the thermal horizon. K is not conserved by any law. The late-time
radiation is thermal (K = 0) up to and including the final evaporation. There is
no K-signature of the Page time.

This is consistent with S-unitarity: the S-information may be conserved (Page
curve may hold), but the K-content of that S-information is zero — thermal
correlations do not carry compressible structure even if they carry quantum
entanglement.

**Under S-informationalism:**

After t_Page = 0.6464 × t_evap, the Hawking radiation must carry structured
quantum correlations that encode the K-content of the infalling matter. Unitarity
forces K-recovery: if S-information is conserved (Page curve), then the radiation
at late times must be de-scrambled enough to reconstruct the infalling state — and
that reconstruction necessarily requires recovering K_matter bits.

The K-recovery rate (from black_hole_k_findings.md):

```
dK/dt = K_matter / (t_evap - t_Page) = 190 bits / (0.354 × t_evap)
```

For a solar-mass BH: dK/dt ≈ 10^-72 bits/second — one bit every 10^72 seconds.

### Why it is inaccessible

**For a solar-mass black hole:**

| Quantity | Value |
|---|---|
| t_evap | 10^67.3 years |
| t_Page | 10^67.1 years |
| K_matter | ~190 bits |
| dK/dt (post-Page) | ~10^-72 bits/s |
| Current age of universe | ~1.38 × 10^10 years |
| Ratio t_evap / t_universe | ~10^57 |

No solar-mass (or larger) black hole will complete its evaporation before the heat
death of the universe. Even if one did, detecting K-structure in the late-time
radiation would require performing quantum state tomography on ~10^77 photons
simultaneously — a computation whose K-complexity exceeds the K_laws budget by
many orders of magnitude.

**For a Planck-mass black hole:**

| Quantity | Value |
|---|---|
| M_Planck | ~2.2 × 10^-8 kg |
| t_evap | ~t_Planck ≈ 5.4 × 10^-44 s |
| T_H | ~T_Planck ≈ 1.4 × 10^32 K |

At Planck mass the Hawking calculation breaks down (quantum gravity corrections
dominate). The t_evap is unobservably short and the K-structure would appear on
timescales of ~10^-44 s. No measurement apparatus can access this regime.

**Conclusion on accessibility:**

The discriminant is theoretically clean. No technology in any foreseeable future —
on any physically realizable timescale — can measure Hawking radiation from any
black hole with sufficient precision to detect K-structure recovery at the Page time.

The discriminant is inaccessible by an astronomical margin: the nearest solar-mass
BH will finish evaporating in 10^57 times the current age of the universe. This is
not a technology gap. It is a cosmological-timescale gap.

### The cosmological constant prior question

The Λ fine-tuning question (FTE_lin = 121.4 vs FTE_log = 1.6) is potentially
discriminating but is not a direct empirical test. The two frameworks disagree on
the prior appropriate to Λ, and this prior encodes the underlying ontological
commitment. An observation cannot settle which prior is correct — that choice is
itself part of what is being disputed. This is a second instance of the same
philosophical structure: the empirical data (Λ's measured value) is identical
for both frameworks; the frameworks disagree on what the data means.

## Part 3: What "Consistent with Both" Means Philosophically

### Empirical equivalence

K-informationalism and S-informationalism are **empirically equivalent** on all
currently accessible observations. This is not a failure of the analysis — it is
the content of the analysis. Two ontologically distinct theories make the same
predictions for every observable in the accessible universe.

The formal statement:

> For any experiment E reachable within the observable universe and any finite
> future time T, the probability distribution P(outcome | K-informationalism, E, T)
> is equal to P(outcome | S-informationalism, E, T) up to measurement precision.

This equivalence holds because:

1. Both frameworks accept K_laws = 21,834 bits as the correct description of known physics.
2. Both frameworks accept S_holo = 10^124 bits as the correct holographic bound.
3. Both frameworks accept S-unitarity (the Page curve may hold).
4. Both frameworks accept quantum mechanics, GR, and the SM as the correct dynamics.

They disagree only on the ontological status of these objects: which is fundamental
(K_laws or S_holo), and which is derived.

### Ontological difference

| Question | K-informationalism | S-informationalism |
|---|---|---|
| What is reality? | K_laws — the 21,834-bit regularity stack | The holographic S-content — 10^124 bits |
| What is derived? | S_holo is a bound on what K_laws can produce | K_laws is a short description of the S-state |
| What is the universe "made of"? | Compressed regularities; the laws are more real than the history | Information content; the state is more real than the description of the state |
| What is the black hole paradox? | A confusion of S with K; no paradox | A genuine puzzle about S-unitarity; Page curve is required |
| What is Λ fine-tuning? | Log-uniform prior; FTE_log = 1.6; not severe | Linear prior; FTE_lin = 121.4; demands a mechanism |

### The definition of a metaphysical question

A metaphysical question is one that cannot, even in principle, be resolved by
observation or experiment.

The BH K-recovery discriminant is in principle observational — but is inaccessible
on any cosmological timescale. Whether an inaccessible-in-practice discriminant
makes a question scientific or metaphysical is itself a boundary question. The
most defensible position is:

> K-informationalism vs S-informationalism is a **quasi-metaphysical question**:
> it has a well-defined empirical discriminant (BH K-recovery), but that discriminant
> is inaccessible in practice by a margin of ~10^57 universe-ages. For all practical
> purposes — for the entire future of observational physics — it is a metaphysical
> question. The theoretical discriminant does not save it from this status.

This is a stronger claim than "we don't know the answer yet." It is the claim that
we will never know the answer through experiment, not because the question is
ill-posed, but because the universe does not provide accessible test cases.

The choice between K-informationalism and S-informationalism is therefore a choice
about **which quantity to treat as fundamental** — a definitional-ontological choice,
not an empirical one. This is the precise sense in which it is metaphysical.

## Part 4: The K-Informationalist Pragmatic Argument

Even if K-informationalism and S-informationalism are empirically equivalent, there
are four reasons to prefer the K-informationalist framework for doing physics:

### a. K_laws is computationally tractable; S_holo is not

K_laws = 21,834 bits. This is a finite, specifiable object. It fits in a file smaller
than a JPEG image. Physical calculations based on K_laws (SM Lagrangian, GR action,
ΛCDM parameters) are tractable on existing hardware.

S_holo = 10^124 bits. No computation can explicitly represent this object. No algorithm
can enumerate the S_holo degrees of freedom. Working directly with S_holo requires
approximations (holographic bounds, entropy inequalities) that are always derived from
K-simple rules — not from S_holo itself.

In practice, every calculation in physics uses K_laws, not S_holo. S-informationalism
cannot provide a computational basis that K-informationalism does not already subsume.

### b. K_laws is approximately physically invariant; S_holo is region-dependent

K_laws varies by ~12–16% across unit systems (SI, natural, Planck) — and this variation
is gzip-noise on text length, not genuine K variation. The dimensionless content
(α, α_s, sin²θ_W, etc.) is exactly unit-invariant.

S_holo depends on the spatial region chosen: the holographic bound for the observable
universe differs from the bound for the Solar System by many orders of magnitude.
There is no unique, physically distinguished S_holo — the bound requires specifying
a surface.

A quantity that is approximately invariant across all physically meaningful
descriptions is a better candidate for fundamental status than one that varies by
orders of magnitude depending on the region of space chosen.

### c. K_laws gives specific testable predictions; S_holo does not

K-informationalism generates three active testable predictions:

| Prediction | Test | Timeline |
|---|---|---|
| SM+GR remains MDL winner | GW events accumulate against alternatives; LQG K-debt exhausted ~2027, String ~2031 | Ongoing, O4/O5 |
| Running vacuum w = -0.827 → Euclid f·σ₈ +1.76% | Euclid Year 3 | ~2028 |
| No new BSM signals | Neutron EDM (SNS), proton decay (Hyper-K), CMB-S4 B-modes | 2025–2030 |

S-informationalism generates no predictions that K-informationalism does not already
subsume. A framework that makes testable predictions is more useful than one that
is empirically empty by construction.

### d. K_laws connects to computation theory; S_holo does not

K (Kolmogorov complexity) is the quantity that algorithmic information theory, circuit
complexity theory, and computational complexity theory address. The question "what is
the tight lower bound on K_laws?" has the formal structure of a complexity-theoretic
problem. The connection to P vs NP, circuit lower bounds, and natural proofs programs
provides a mathematical path to tightening the K_laws estimate from below.

S_holo is a Shannon-entropic quantity. Shannon entropy is a well-developed theory —
but it does not connect to computation theory in the same way. The S-bound tells you
how many bits can be stored; it does not tell you which bits are laws and which are
random. The distinction between K-information and S-information is precisely the
distinction between structured compressibility and raw storage capacity.

K-informationalism is therefore not merely a philosophical preference. It is aligned
with the only existing mathematical framework — algorithmic information theory — that
can address the tight lower bound on physical laws.

### Synthesis of the pragmatic argument

K-informationalism is not empirically superior to S-informationalism. But it is:

- Computationally tractable (K_laws fits in 21,834 bits)
- Approximately physically invariant (K_laws stable across unit systems)
- Predictively active (three testable claims this decade)
- Mathematically connected (to AIT, circuit complexity, MDL theory)

S-informationalism is none of these four things. It is a coherent ontology but an
inert framework. For doing physics — for making predictions, computing outcomes, and
updating on data — K-informationalism is the only practical choice.

This is the pragmatic argument for K-informationalism: not that it is true, but that
it is useful, and that its competitor is not.
