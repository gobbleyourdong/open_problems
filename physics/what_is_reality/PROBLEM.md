## Competing Ontologies

- **Physicalism** — Everything is physical. Mind supervenes on matter.
- **Mathematical Universe (Tegmark)** — Reality is a mathematical structure. Every consistent structure exists.
- **Information (Wheeler, "it from bit")** — Reality is information. Bits precede atoms.
- **Process (Whitehead)** — Reality is events, not substances. Verbs not nouns.
- **Idealism** — Reality is mental. Consciousness is primary.
- **Neutral monism** — Reality is neither mental nor physical. Both are aspects of something else.
- **Relational (Rovelli)** — Reality has no absolute state. Everything is relations.

## Gap Summary

Every physical theory presupposes a reality to measure. The question "what IS reality" is prior to physics and cannot be answered from within it. Multiple self-consistent theories predict the same observations but describe radically different realities — observations underdetermine ontology.

## Anti-Problem

What would it mean for reality to NOT exist? If nothing exists, what is doing the "nothing"? The impossibility of genuine non-being is evidence that reality is a necessary substrate, not a contingent fact.
