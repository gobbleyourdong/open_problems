## Why this is the gap

See attempt_001 for the full treatment. Key moves:

1. **The "what is reality really made of" question is malformed.** It presupposes a substrate beneath the structure. The compression view denies that presupposition: there is no "beneath the structure," because the structure is what "is" means.
2. **The seven ontologies unify.** Each is pointing at the same converged regularity stack with different emphasis. The apparent dispute is terminological.
3. **The simulation hypothesis reduces to a question about how many layers of compression are stacked.** The difference between "real" and "simulated" reality dissolves if no layer can see its outer layer.
4. **The Parmenidean response to "why is there something rather than nothing":** genuine nothingness may be conceptually incoherent because it requires a distinction (nothing vs not-nothing), and distinctions are structure, and structure is not nothing. Mild favor for this over the anthropic-ish "just because" response.

## Three residual questions

- **R1.** Is the converged compression finitely K-specifiable? Related to physical Church-Turing and to whether reality's regularities exceed any finite description.
- **R2.** S-informationalism vs K-informationalism: is there an experimental signature that picks out one over the other? Most current physics works with S; the compression view makes K primary. A decisive experiment would be a major contribution.
- **R3.** Does the Parmenidean argument against genuine nothingness hold up, or is it a linguistic trick?
