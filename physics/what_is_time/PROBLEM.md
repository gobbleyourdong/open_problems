## Competing Ontologies

- **Eternalism (block universe)** — All times exist equally. The flow of time is an illusion.
- **Presentism** — Only the present exists. The past is gone, the future is not yet.
- **Growing block** — Past and present exist. The future is unreal.
- **Relationalism** — Time is just change. No time without things changing.
- **Emergent time** — Time emerges from entanglement/information structure. Fundamental physics is timeless.
- **Phenomenological time (Husserl)** — Time is constituted by consciousness — retention, now, protention.

## Gap Summary

Relativity says there is no privileged "now" — all times are equally real in the block universe. But we EXPERIENCE time as flowing. This contradiction has no accepted resolution. Neither physics nor phenomenology can explain why there is a "now" at all.

## Anti-Problem

What would a timeless reality feel like? If you believe time doesn't flow, why does it feel like it does? The phenomenology is data that physics must eventually account for.
