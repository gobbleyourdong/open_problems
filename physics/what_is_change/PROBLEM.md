# What Is Change?

> The difference question. How does anything become something else?

## Generated Disciplines

- Differential Geometry
- Process Philosophy
- Metaphysics of Time
- Dynamical Systems

## The Ladder (Tier 0 → Tier 3)

| Tier | Question |
|------|----------|
| **Tier 0** | What is change? |
| **Tier 1** | Is change fundamental or is it a sequence of unchanging states? |
| **Tier 2** | Zeno's paradoxes — can motion be reduced to instants? |
| **Tier 3** | Is a stopped clock in some sense "still changing"? |

## Competing Ontologies

- **Parmenidean** — Change is illusion. Reality is unchanging Being.
- **Heraclitean** — Everything flows. Change is fundamental.
- **Block universe** — Change is an illusion of moving consciousness through static 4D.
- **Process philosophy (Whitehead)** — Reality is events (occasions of experience), not substances.
- **Dispositions** — Change happens because things have powers to affect each other.
- **Humean mosaic** — Change is just succession. No causal glue underneath.

## Gap Summary

Zeno's paradoxes are technically resolved by calculus but philosophically unresolved. If time is continuous, how can anything change at a single instant? If discrete, what happens between quanta? The question of what MAKES change happen (vs just being a sequence) is unanswered.

## Anti-Problem

What would a truly changeless universe look like? If every state just IS what it is, and the next state ALSO just IS what it is, what connects them into "change"?
