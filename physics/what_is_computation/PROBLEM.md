## Competing Ontologies

- **Turing model** — Computation is symbol manipulation on a tape.
- **Lambda calculus** — Computation is function application.
- **Pancomputationalism** — Everything computes. The universe is a computation.
- **Physical Church-Turing** — Physically realizable computation is exactly Turing-computable.
- **Hypercomputation** — Some physical systems might exceed Turing power.
- **Quantum computation** — BQP may be strictly more powerful than P.

## Gap Summary

P vs NP has resisted proof for 50+ years. The three barriers (relativization, natural proofs, algebrization) have killed every obvious approach. More fundamentally: we don't know what computation IS — just how to recognize it when we see it. The Turing model is one formalization, but does it capture "computation" in general?

## Anti-Problem

What would a non-computational physical process look like? If every physical process is Turing-computable, is computation just "physics"? If not, which physical processes are NOT computations?
