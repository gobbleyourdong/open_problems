**Last updated**: 2026-04-15
**Phase**: 3 (two parallel tracks populated)
**Scope**: the evolutionary biology of persistent human viruses and the
parallel evolutionary history of the human immune system.

## The gap, in one paragraph

> **Two parallel stories span >3 Ga of immune evolution and ~100 Ma of
> host-pathogen coevolution. On the human side: the immune system is a
> layered architecture built over 3.5 billion years, with each layer
> solving problems the previous could not handle at new scales of
> organization. On the pathogen side: persistent viruses (EBV, HCMV,
> CVB, HPV, HHV-6) have been the dominant selection pressure on
> primate adaptive immunity over the last ~100 Ma, driving HLA
> polymorphism and KIR diversification. The two stories meet at the
> "persistent-virus coevolution" frontier: persistent pathogens have
> reshaped our genome in specific ways (HLA polymorphism, KIR locus
> diversification, HERV integrations) that are still operational
> determinants of modern disease susceptibility. Within this frame,
> the specific open questions are where research pays off.**

## Well-established knowledge

The following are settled at the level of scientific consensus and
should not be relitigated:

1. **The MHC was invented in the common ancestor of jawed vertebrates
   ~450 Ma**, via at least two whole-genome duplications plus
   domestication of a Transib-family transposon producing RAG1/RAG2.
   Kapitonov & Jurka 2005 PLoS Biol 3:e181; Liu et al. 2019 Nature
   569:79.

2. **Jawless vertebrates have an independently-evolved adaptive
   immune system** (VLRs, lampreys + hagfish, ~500 Ma). Three
   lymphocyte lineages (VLRA/VLRB/VLRC) parallel the jawed-vertebrate
   αβ-T/B/γδ-T cells. Pancer & Cooper 2004 Nature 430:174; Hirano
   2013 Nature 501:435.

3. **HLA is the most polymorphic region of the human genome**, with
   >25,000 alleles identified. Polymorphism concentrated in the
   peptide-binding groove. Maintained by pathogen-mediated balancing
   selection. Hedrick 2002 Evolution 56:1902.

4. **Persistent viruses drive HLA and KIR evolution disproportionately**
   versus acute pathogens. HCMV is the dominant driver of KIR
   diversification in humans. Parham & Moffett 2013 Nat Rev Immunol
   13:133.

5. **HERVs are ~8% of the human genome** and represent fossilized
   retroviral integrations from the last 100+ million years.
   Syncytin-1 for placenta is the canonical co-option.

6. **Prokaryotic immunity is richer than textbooks suggest**: 60+
   defense systems known by 2023 (BREX, DISARM, Gabija, Thoeris,
   Zorya, CBASS, Retrons, Pycsar, etc.). Doron 2018 Science
   359:eaar4120; Bernheim & Sorek 2020 Nat Rev Microbiol 18:113.

7. **cGAS-STING signaling has bacterial ancestry (CBASS)** — a ~3 Ga
   deep homology connecting prokaryotic to mammalian innate
   signaling. Cohen et al. 2019 Nature 574:691.

8. **Toll/NF-κB pathway is pan-metazoan, ~600-700 Ma old.** Lemaitre
   et al. 1996 Cell 86:973 for *Drosophila*; Wolenski et al. 2011 for
   *Nematostella*; Poltorak 1998 for mammalian TLR4.

9. **Class switching and affinity maturation evolved in mammalian
   (or pre-mammalian) B cells via AID**. Muramatsu et al. 2000 Cell
   102:553 (+ Revy 2000 Cell 102:565 for human HIGM2 phenotype).

10. **Placental tolerance is a mammalian-specific elaboration** with
    multiple mechanisms: HLA-G, uterine NK tolerance, Tregs, IDO,
    PD-L1. Erlebacher 2013 Annu Rev Immunol 31:387.

## Open frontier questions

### Substantial, addressable with current tools

1. **What was the "pre-adaptive" state of the vertebrate ancestor?**
   The jawless VLR system and jawed V(D)J system use completely
   different molecular machinery but evolved in parallel ~500 Ma.
   What starting material did both have? Amphioxus (cephalochordate,
   sister to vertebrates) still has an active RAG-like transposon
   (Huang 2016), providing a window into this pre-split state.

2. **How exactly do persistent viruses exploit layer boundaries?**
   The 001-series attempts each describe a specific niche. What is
   the deeper structural principle? Does every persistent virus
   exploit a layer-boundary vulnerability, and if so, can we
   catalog them all?

3. **Why is the innate-adaptive boundary so asymmetrically
   traversed?** Adaptive immune responses require innate priming;
   innate responses can proceed without adaptive contribution.
   Evolutionarily, the adaptive layer was added on top, which
   shapes the causation. Can this asymmetry be exploited
   therapeutically?

4. **Do any invertebrates have "trained immunity" that functionally
   approximates memory?** Some evidence in insects, echinoderms,
   lophotrochozoans. If so, adaptive-like memory may be more
   widespread than a jawed-vertebrate-specific innovation.

5. **How will vaccine-driven selection reshape HLA evolution over
   the next 100-500 years?** HPV vaccination is the first ever
   human-directed persistent-virus selection. Observable effects
   on HLA allele frequencies may emerge over centuries.

### Harder or data-limited

6. **Mitochondrial immunity**: the organelle retains bacterial
   character. How much of its antiviral function (MAVS signaling,
   OMM dynamics, mitochondrial DNA release) derives from its
   bacterial ancestry versus host-lineage elaboration?

7. **Monotreme immunology**: the most basal living mammals are
   under-characterized. Data here would clarify which mammalian
   innovations are ancestral vs eutherian-specific.

8. **Microbial and parasite coevolution with HLA**: much less
   studied than viral coevolution. The HLA-B27/ankylosing
   spondylitis association may involve gut microbial mimicry;
   HLA-DR alleles vary with P. gingivalis exposure; etc. Needs
   systematic study.

9. **Why do some prokaryotic defense systems keep being discovered
   post-2018?** Is there an upper limit to defense-system diversity
   per bacterial genome? Sorek lab estimates ~12 systems per genome
   as the cap.

10. **How can we formalize "trained immunity" and epigenetic
    immune memory**? Evidence exists but lacks a unified framework.
    May bridge innate and adaptive in ways not yet formalized.

## Methodological cautions

1. **Deep-time dates (>500 Ma) have large uncertainty**. Molecular-
   clock estimates routinely vary by factor 1.5-2. Use qualitative
   "~X Ga" framing; cite primary sources when specific values are
   needed.

2. **"Convergent" vs "homologous" are distinct claims**. VLR and
   Ig-based adaptive immunity are CONVERGENT (no common machinery).
   Bacterial CBASS and mammalian cGAS-STING are HOMOLOGOUS (shared
   molecular ancestry).

3. **HLA and MHC are species-specific names for the same locus
   class**. Humans: HLA. Mice: H-2. Use whichever is contextually
   appropriate.

4. **Cross-referencing between 001- and 100-series attempts should
   go both directions**. Each reader should find the other track
   via explicit links in cross-cutting attempts (001 and 107).

## Synthesis: what the 100-series established

1. **Immune systems are plural, not singular.** Plants (attempt_111),
   jawless vertebrates (104), jawed vertebrates (105) each evolved
   distinct immune architectures. The layered-architecture framing
   of attempt_100 is animal-centric.

2. **Convergent evolution of adaptive/memory features is common.**
   VLR vs V(D)J (attempts 104-105), IgT vs IgA (attempt 112),
   plant SAR vs animal trained immunity (108 + 111). "Memory" and
   "specificity" are functional solutions with multiple molecular
   implementations.

3. **Deep homologies connect prokaryotic and animal immunity.**
   CBASS → cGAS-STING (~3 Ga), TIR domain signaling pre-dates
   animals (protist TirA, attempt_110). The innate layer is older
   than the adaptive layer by at least 2.5 Ga.

4. **RAG is a domesticated transposon.** The adaptive immune system's
   core machinery came from a Transib transposon ~450 Ma.
   Retroviral capture, hepadnavirus integration, bornavirus RT-mediated
   integration — the immune system has many pieces of "domesticated"
   viral material (HERVs, syncytin, EVEs).

5. **Persistent viruses reshape the host.** HLA polymorphism, KIR
   diversity, host restriction-factor positive selection, and even
   placental morphology (syncytin) have been driven by persistent-
   virus coevolution over the last ~100 Ma.

6. **Inflammaging is an unselected byproduct.** attempt_113's
   integration of disposable-soma + antagonistic pleiotropy applied
   to immunity: the aging immune system's pro-inflammatory drift
   is predictable from evolutionary theory.

## 2026-04-18 v9.1 discipline addendum

Per `~/SIGMA_METHOD.md` v9.1 C5 / D2:

**Would falsify (persistent-virus-drives-immune-architecture framework):** The framework claims that host restriction factors + MHC polymorphism + placental-morphology evolution were shaped by persistent-virus coevolution over ~100 Ma. This predicts **temporal co-incidence**: selection-signature dates for host restriction factors (APOBEC3, TRIM5α, SAMHD1) and MHC class I / II allele birth dates should cluster near the emergence dates of their corresponding persistent-virus lineages (ERV classes, HHV/HPV/HBV lineages) within reasonable evolutionary error (~20 Myr). **If a systematic molecular-clock analysis finds host restriction factors' positive-selection signatures consistently predate or postdate their persistent-virus targets by > 50 Myr across ≥ 5 independent factor–virus pairs, the coevolution framing is falsified** (in favor of e.g., generic anti-non-self defense that pre-existed the specific viruses). Weaker falsifier: if a specific prediction in attempts 100-113 (convergent jawless/jawed adaptive immunity, syncytin-from-ERV placentation, etc.) is contradicted by a new finding, that attempt is flagged but the broader framework may survive.

**Prior art:** Virus-host coevolution ≈ classical co-evolution literature (Van Valen 1973 Red Queen; Anderson & May 1979 on parasite-host dynamics). ERV-as-functional-element ≈ Lander 2001 *Nature* + Kapitonov 2007 on transposon-to-gene domestication. Syncytin-from-ERV ≈ Mi 2000 *Nature* (syncytin-1) + Blaise 2003 (syncytin-2). MHC-polymorphism-from-pathogen-pressure ≈ Hughes & Nei 1988 *Nature* + Doherty-Zinkernagel heterozygote advantage. Sigma addition: integrating the ~8-persistent-organism framework (CVB, Demodex, P. gingivalis, EBV, H. pylori, HPV, Malassezia, gut dysbiosis; see `medical/persistent_organisms/`) with the **immune-timeline series (100-113)** to produce cross-organism predictions about which downstream diseases should emerge first, and using the immune-architecture clock to date specific host-factor emergence events against known viral-lineage dates.
