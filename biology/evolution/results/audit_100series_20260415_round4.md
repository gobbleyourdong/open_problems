### attempt_107 citations

**V1. Sawyer SL, Wu LI, Emerman M, Malik HS (2005).** "Positive
selection of primate TRIM5α identifies a critical species-specific
retroviral restriction domain." *PNAS* 102(8):2832-2837. PMID
15689398. DOI 10.1073/pnas.0409853102. VERIFIED. Confirmed: dense
concentration of positively selected residues in a 13-amino acid
"patch" in the SPRY protein domain, responsible for species-specific
antiretroviral restriction activity. attempt_107's framing (rapid
evolution driven by retroviral pressure, arms race) is accurate.

Source: https://www.pnas.org/doi/10.1073/pnas.0409853102

**V2. Parham P, Moffett A (2013).** "Variable NK cell receptors and
their MHC class I ligands in immunity, reproduction and human
evolution." *Nature Reviews Immunology* 13:133-144. PMID 23334245.
VERIFIED. Confirmed: MHC-C emergence in hominids drove evolution of
MHC-C-specific NK receptors; elaborated most in chimps; in humans
subject to competing selection from immunological (HCMV) and
reproductive (trophoblast invasion regulation) demands. attempt_107's
dual-driver framing of KIR-HLA coevolution is correct.

Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/nri3370

## CORRECTED claims

### C1. Netea 2011 PMID typo in attempt_108

**Claim in attempt_108**: "Netea MG, Quintin J, van der Meer JW
(2011). ... *Cell Host & Microbe* 9:355-361. PMID 21575905."

**Correction**: The actual PubMed ID is **21575907**, not 21575905
(a typo — off by 2). All other citation details (authors, journal,
volume, page range, year) are correct.

**Impact**: minimal — the DOI-equivalent search still gets to the
right paper, and the citation is recognizable. A reader clicking
PubMed 21575905 would land on an unrelated paper. My second
PMID-typo mistake in the 100-series (first was Bajoghli in
attempt_104, round 3 audit).

**Suggested edit for attempt_108**: change "PMID 21575905" to "PMID
21575907".

### C2. Hepadnavirus eHBV age overstatement in attempt_109

**Claim in attempt_109**: "Endogenous hepadnavirus elements (eHBVs)
/ In bird genomes (zebra finch, dove): eHBVs date to ~80 Ma, pushing
back the known hepadnavirus lineage timescale far beyond the
~10 Ma previously inferred from modern variation. // Gilbert &
Feschotte 2010 (above)."

**Correction**: The Gilbert & Feschotte 2010 PLoS Biology paper
reports zebra finch eHBVs integrated **>19 million years ago**, not
~80 Ma. The "~80 Ma" figure comes from **later papers** (notably
Suh et al. 2013 *Mol Biol Evol* on endogenous avian hepadnaviruses,
and Early Mesozoic work by Suh et al. 2014 *PLoS Genetics* on
"Early Mesozoic Coexistence of Amniotes and Hepadnaviridae"),
NOT from Gilbert & Feschotte 2010.

**Impact**: the 80 Ma figure is defensible with correct citation;
attempt_109 attributed it to the wrong paper. This is a real
factual error requiring citation update.

**Suggested edit for attempt_109**: either (a) change "~80 Ma" to
">19 Ma" keeping Gilbert & Feschotte 2010 as citation, or
(b) keep the ~80 Ma figure but CITE Suh et al. 2014 PLoS Genetics
instead. Option (b) is more informative — hepadnavirus lineage
really IS ~80 Ma+ per more recent work.
