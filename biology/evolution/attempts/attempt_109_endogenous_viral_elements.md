# attempt_109 — Non-Retroviral Endogenous Viral Elements: The Paleovirology Window

> Second extension to the 100-series after the main sweep (100-107)
> and trained-immunity (108). attempt_100 mentioned HERVs as ~8% of
> the human genome — retroviral fossils. But retroviruses are not
> the only viral family whose members have integrated into vertebrate
> genomes. Since ~2010, a new field ("paleovirology") has revealed
> endogenous elements derived from bornaviruses, filoviruses,
> parvoviruses, hepadnaviruses, and others. These non-retroviral
> endogenous viral elements (EVEs) are deep fossils of viral families
> that normally do not integrate. Their existence provides unique
> windows into ancient host-virus coevolution.

---

## Why non-retroviral EVEs were a surprise

Retroviruses are expected to endogenize. They are defined by their
reverse-transcriptase-plus-integrase machinery that inserts proviral
DNA into host chromosomes. If such an integration happens in a
germline cell, the provirus is transmitted vertically to offspring.

Non-retroviral RNA viruses (bornaviruses, filoviruses, etc.) do NOT
have integrase activity. Their genomes never normally enter the host
DNA. Textbook teaching through the 2000s: "non-retroviruses cannot
endogenize."

This was wrong. Starting around 2010, multiple groups independently
discovered host genomic sequences matching non-retroviral viruses:

**Horie M, Honda T, Suzuki Y, Kobayashi Y, Daito T, Oshida T, Ikuta K,
Jern P, Gojobori T, Coffin JM, Tomonaga K (2010).** "Endogenous
non-retroviral RNA virus elements in mammalian genomes." *Nature*
463:84-87. PMID 20054395.

The Horie paper showed that 19 mammalian species carry endogenous
bornavirus-like N elements (EBLNs) — germline-integrated sequences
derived from the nucleoprotein gene of an ancient bornavirus
lineage. The EBLN integrations are estimated to have occurred
~40 Ma.

## How non-retroviruses end up integrated

Three mechanisms are now recognized:

### 1. LINE-1 mediated reverse transcription (most common)

Mammalian LINE-1 retrotransposons encode reverse transcriptase.
Viral mRNA that happens to be in a germline cell can be reverse-
transcribed and integrated by hijacked LINE-1 machinery.

This is how most EBLNs are thought to have arisen: bornavirus mRNA
(single-stranded negative-sense RNA transcribed by the viral
polymerase into mRNA in the nucleus — bornaviruses are unusual in
replicating in the nucleus) happened to be in a germline cell with
active LINE-1, which retrotransposed the bornavirus nucleoprotein
mRNA into a new genomic location.

### 2. Parvovirus ssDNA recombination

Parvoviruses have single-stranded DNA genomes. Parvoviral ssDNA can
integrate into host chromosomes via non-homologous end-joining after
DNA damage. Endogenous parvovirus elements (EPVs) have been found
across vertebrate genomes.

Reference: **Belyi VA, Levine AJ, Skalka AM (2010).** "Sequences from
ancestral single-stranded DNA viruses in vertebrate genomes: the
Parvoviridae and Circoviridae are more than 40 to 50 million years
old." *Journal of Virology* 84:12458-12462. PMID 20861255.

### 3. Hepadnavirus integration (defective products)

Hepadnaviruses (HBV and relatives) have a partially double-stranded
DNA genome that replicates through an RNA intermediate. Aberrant
integration products can enter the germline. Endogenous hepadnavirus
sequences in bird genomes date back >80 Ma.

Reference: **Gilbert C, Feschotte C (2010).** "Genomic fossils
calibrate the long-term evolution of hepadnaviruses." *PLoS Biology*
8:e1000495. PMID 20927357.

## Key EVE discoveries post-2010

### Endogenous bornavirus-like elements (EBLNs)

Across mammals: primates, rodents, bats, elephants. ~40 Ma old.
One specific EBLN (EBLN-1 in humans, chromosome 11) is transcribed
in modern humans. Its function is debated — possibly antiviral
(interferes with exogenous bornavirus replication), possibly
neutral.

### Endogenous filovirus-like elements (EFVs)

Horie group and others have found endogenous sequences matching
Ebolavirus / Marburgvirus nucleoprotein and VP35 genes in several
mammalian genomes, including humans. Suggests filovirus lineages
have been infecting mammals for >20 Ma.

Reference: **Taylor DJ, Ballinger MJ, Bowman SM, Bruenn JA (2013).**
"Evaluating the links between retroviruses and pathogenic
non-retroviruses: evidence and implications of the non-retroviral
RNA virus reservoir hypothesis." *Ecology and Evolution* 3:3683-3701.

### Endogenous parvovirus elements (EPVs)

Widespread in vertebrates. Some humans carry EPVs ~65 Ma old.
A 2010 Belyi et al. paper (above) showed these are real and useful
for dating parvovirus lineages.

### Endogenous circovirus elements

Present across vertebrates including humans. Circoviruses are small
ssDNA viruses; their endogenous relatives date back tens of millions
of years.

### Endogenous hepadnavirus elements (eHBVs)

In bird genomes (zebra finch, dove): eHBVs were first reported by
Gilbert & Feschotte 2010 *PLoS Biology* 8:e1000495 at >19 million
years old. Later work (Suh et al. 2013 *Mol Biol Evol* and
subsequent papers) pushed the inferred hepadnavirus lineage
timescale to ~80 Ma, far beyond the ~10 Ma previously inferred
from modern variation.

## Evolutionary uses of EVE data

### Dating viral lineages

Molecular-clock estimates based on modern viral evolution give
recent dates for viral lineages (tens of thousands of years for most
RNA viruses; millions of years for some DNA viruses). These estimates
are often wrong by orders of magnitude.

EVEs provide MUCH deeper calibration:
- EBLNs date bornavirus lineage to ≥40 Ma (previous estimate: a few
  thousand years based on modern variation).
- EPVs date parvovirus lineage to 40-98 Ma.
- eHBVs date hepadnavirus lineage to ≥80 Ma.

Each EVE is a "fossil" whose integration date provides a LOWER bound
for that viral lineage's age.

Reference: **Katzourakis A, Gifford RJ (2010).** "Endogenous viral
elements in animal genomes." *PLoS Genetics* 6:e1001191. PMID
21124940. Foundational review.

### Revealing ancient pathogen exposure

Each EVE implies that an ancestor of the host species was infected by
that viral lineage, and that infection reached the germline. The set
of EVEs in a genome is an archaeology of ancestral pathogen pressure.

### Understanding modern antiviral genes under positive selection

Host restriction factors (TRIM5α, APOBEC3G, etc., see attempt_107)
show positive selection signatures that partially correlate with
EVE integration times — hosts adapted to block viruses whose relatives
left fossils.

## EVE-derived host functions (co-option)

As with HERVs providing syncytin for placenta (attempt_106), some
non-retroviral EVEs have acquired host functions:

### Bornavirus N-derived piRNA precursors

Some endogenous bornavirus N sequences are transcribed and processed
into piRNAs that silence endogenous LINE-1 retrotransposons.
Evolutionary recycling: an ancient viral sequence is used as an
antiviral/anti-transposon tool.

### Parvovirus Rep-like sequences

Rare cases where parvoviral Rep-like ORFs have been domesticated
into host proteins with non-viral functions. Rare and lineage-specific.

### Bornavirus NP as antiviral in tree shrews

Tree shrew EBLNs encode a functional protein that inhibits
exogenous bornavirus replication. "Self-made antiviral drug" from
a co-opted viral fragment.

Reference: **Horie M, Kobayashi Y, Honda T, Fujino K, Akasaka T,
Kohl C, Wibbelt G, Mühldorfer K, Kurth A, Müller MA, Corman VM,
Gillich N, Suzuki Y, Schwemmle M, Tomonaga K (2016).** "An RNA-
dependent RNA polymerase gene in bat genomes derived from an
ancient negative-strand RNA virus." *Scientific Reports* 6:25873.
PMID 27174689.

## Implications for the 100-series framework

### Extending the "8% genome is retroviral" story

attempt_100 and attempt_107 discussed HERVs as 8% of the human
genome. Non-retroviral EVEs add a SMALLER but informative
contribution — EVEs are a tiny fraction of the genome (probably
<0.1%) but they are disproportionately informative about ancient
viral ecology because each represents a distinct deep-time
integration event.

### Extending the paleovirology approach

The 100-series so far emphasized COEVOLUTION from the host side
(adaptive immunity evolution, HLA polymorphism). EVEs complement
this by revealing the HISTORICAL virome — the actual viruses that
selected those host adaptations. They are "fossils of the
selectors," not just of the selected.

### Methodological implications

A systematic EVE inventory of any vertebrate genome now provides:
- Lineage-specific exposure history
- Dating anchors for viral family phylogenies
- Candidate antiviral-gene co-options
- Clues to modern virus-host-adaptation signatures

## Open questions

1. **What fraction of vertebrate genome contents is EVE-derived
   overall?** Current estimates are dominated by retroviruses (HERV
   8%). Non-retroviral EVE content is probably <1% but has been
   systematically cataloged only in a few species.

2. **Do any non-retroviral EVEs have MAJOR modern physiological
   roles comparable to syncytin?** Current evidence suggests minor
   or lineage-specific roles only. A syncytin-equivalent for a
   non-retroviral virus family has not been found.

3. **Are there endogenous coronaviruses, paramyxoviruses, or
   flaviviruses?** Active search. A few candidate sequences exist
   but robust endogenization is rarer than for bornaviruses,
   filoviruses, and parvoviruses.

4. **How does EVE burden correlate with modern host immune
   repertoire?** Species with more EVEs might be expected to have
   more specialized antiviral genes; this would be a testable
   hypothesis.

5. **Can EVE data be used to predict emerging viral threats?**
   An ancient viral lineage with many EVE fossils may have a
   circulating modern descendant. Recent finding: Andes virus
   (hantavirus) has multiple hantavirus-like EVEs in mammalian
   genomes, suggesting ancient mammalian-hantavirus relationships.

## Gap opened

- **Systematic EVE catalog** for humans specifically is incomplete —
  different tools (BLAST, HMMER, custom pipelines) give different
  counts. A unified benchmark would sharpen the inventory.
- **EVE-to-function map** for the identified elements: most are
  presumed non-functional, but this is less systematically checked
  than for HERVs.
- **EVE-integration-dating-vs-host-immune-evolution correlation**:
  formal statistical tests of whether EVE integration rates
  correlate with host antiviral gene evolution rates have been
  partial only.
