## The landscape of "what has been tried"

As of 2026, the adult thymic regeneration interventions that have
been tested in humans with thymic endpoints are:

| Intervention | Trial(s) | N | Thymic effect measured? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **GH + DHEA + metformin** (TRIIM) | Fahy 2019 | 9 | YES (MRI + sjTREC + naive T cells) |
| **GH + personalized combo** (TRIIM-X) | NCT04375657, ~2020-2024 | ~80-100 | In progress / primary completion Nov 2024 |
| **LHRH agonists** (prostate cancer population) | Sutherland 2005 et seq | 30+ | YES (sjTREC + naive T cells) |
| **LHRH agonists** (HSCT recipients) | multiple Phase 2 | 30-100 | YES (accelerated T cell recovery) |
| **CYT107 (recombinant IL-7)** | IRIS-7 (sepsis), HIV, HSCT | 300+ cumulative | Partial (peripheral T cells > thymic specifically) |
| **Palifermin (KGF)** | Multiple HSCT-focused | 100+ | Mixed / inconclusive |
| **F-652 (IL-22 fusion)** | Early-phase HSCT / GVHD | <50 | Preliminary |
| **Cultured thymic tissue** (RETHYMIC) | Pediatric athymia | 105+ | YES (life-saving) |

There is NO approved pharmacological adult thymic regenerator.
TRIIM and its successors are the closest candidate.

## TRIIM — the landmark pilot trial

### The study

**Fahy GM, Brooke RT, Watson JP, Good Z, Vasanawala SS, Maecker H,
Leipold MD, Lin DTS, Kobor MS, Horvath S (2019).** "Reversal of
epigenetic aging and immunosenescent trends in humans." *Aging Cell*
18(6):e13028. PMID 31496122. DOI 10.1111/acel.13028.

Principal investigator: **Greg Fahy** (Intervene Immune). Biomarker
analysis: Steve Horvath (UCLA — of Horvath clock fame).

### Design

- Single-arm (no placebo)
- 9 healthy men, ages 51-65
- 12-month intervention
- Primary endpoint: thymic regeneration (MRI)
- Secondary endpoints: epigenetic age (multiple clocks), immune
  biomarkers, metabolic markers

### Intervention

Three-component combination:

1. **Recombinant human growth hormone (rhGH / somatropin)**, daily
   subcutaneous injection at individualized doses. Rationale: GH
   is known to support thymic function; GH levels decline with age;
   GH-deficient patients have reduced thymic output.
2. **Dehydroepiandrosterone (DHEA)** — adrenal androgen precursor
   that declines dramatically with age. Rationale: counter-androgen
   to testosterone in thymic context (attempt_005), general
   metabolic/endocrine support, reduces GH's diabetogenic effects.
3. **Metformin** — the insulin-sensitizing biguanide. Rationale:
   mitigate GH's diabetogenic effects, caloric-restriction-mimetic
   via AMPK activation (attempt_008 mechanism).

The GH was the primary thymic-regenerator; DHEA and metformin were
"protective co-agents" that allow sustained GH use without diabetes.

### Results

**Thymic regeneration (primary endpoint)**:
- 7 of 9 men showed INCREASED thymic functional mass on MRI
- Adipose-to-parenchymal ratio shifted toward parenchyma (reversing
  the fatty infiltration of involution, attempt_004)
- Mean thymic density increased

**Epigenetic age reversal**:
- Mean epigenetic age decreased by ~1.5 years during 12-month
  intervention
- Accounting for expected +1 year from normal aging: **net -2.5
  year reversal** in biological age
- This was measured across multiple Horvath-family clocks (2013,
  PhenoAge, GrimAge) with consistent direction
- The MOST ROBUST reversal signal was in the GrimAge clock (most
  mortality-predictive clock)

**Immune markers**:
- Increased naive CD4+ and CD8+ T cells
- Improved monocyte:lymphocyte ratio
- Reduced exhaustion markers

**Metabolic / risk**:
- PSA and free PSA improved (prostate cancer risk index)
- Glucose control maintained (metformin + DHEA worked as protection)
- No serious adverse events

### Caveats

- **N=9, no placebo control** — pilot/proof-of-concept, not
  definitive
- **All male** — unclear generalizability to women
- **Selection bias**: volunteers motivated toward longevity
  interventions
- **Combination intervention** — individual contribution of GH vs
  DHEA vs metformin not resolvable
- **Short duration** (12 months) — durability unknown

Despite caveats: this was the FIRST HUMAN TRIAL to show MEASURABLE
REVERSAL OF EPIGENETIC AGE, which turned a lot of heads across
gerontology. The thymic regeneration was the most mechanistically
grounded result.

### Why GH works (mechanism)

GH has several thymic-supportive actions:
- Direct IGF-1-mediated TEC support (TECs have GH and IGF-1
  receptors)
- Modulates FOXN1 expression (cross-reference to attempt_007)
- Stimulates thymopoiesis via multiple pathways
- Its decline with age parallels thymic decline ("somatopause")

GH as a thymic regenerator had been investigated in HSCT patients
prior to TRIIM; Fahy et al. extended it to healthy aging adults
and added the DHEA + metformin protective cocktail.

## TRIIM-X — the follow-up Phase 2

### Design

**Clinicaltrials.gov ID**: NCT04375657. Sponsor: Intervene Immune.
Principal investigator: Greg Fahy.

- Expanded pilot / early Phase 2
- ~80-100 participants, both sexes, ages 40-80
- Similar combination intervention (GH + metformin + DHEA) with
  **personalized dosing**
- 12-month treatment duration
- Expanded endpoints: epigenetic age, immunosenescence markers,
  frailty indices, cardiovascular/diabetes/dementia risk factors,
  infectious disease markers (COVID-19 was added during the trial
  period)
- Primary completion: November 2024

### Status

Results as of early 2026: primary data analysis underway; no full
peer-reviewed publication yet; interim signals consistent with
TRIIM-1 direction (thymic and epigenetic effects observed). Fahy
has presented preliminary data at longevity conferences.

Full peer-reviewed publication expected 2026-2027. This will be
the first well-powered human trial of adult thymic regeneration
with replicated primary endpoints.

## LHRH agonist trials in non-cancer populations

### HSCT recipients

Several Phase 2 trials have tested LHRH agonists (leuprolide,
goserelin) peri-hematopoietic stem cell transplantation:

- Premise: androgen blockade regenerates thymus (attempt_005),
  faster thymic recovery = faster T cell reconstitution = fewer
  infections
- Results: generally positive — faster naive T cell recovery,
  improved TREC production, some reduction in infections
- Side effects of short-term LHRH use in HSCT patients are
  tolerable (they're already on many medications)

**Sutherland JS, Spyroglou L, Muirhead JL, et al. (2008).**
"Enhanced immune system regeneration in humans following
allogeneic or autologous stem cell transplantation by temporary
sex steroid blockade." *Clinical Cancer Research* 14:1138-1149.

### HIV / persistent lymphopenia

Small trials have tested LHRH in HIV patients with CD4+ T cell
deficits despite antiretroviral therapy. Results: modest thymic
effects, reduced impact than in HSCT.

### Healthy elderly

Not tested in large trials. The risk-benefit profile (hot flashes,
libido loss, bone loss from long-term androgen blockade) does not
justify the intervention in healthy individuals. A TEC-selective
AR antagonist would transform this — doesn't exist yet.

## CYT107 (recombinant IL-7) trials — attempt_006 expansion

The most clinically advanced "cytokine" thymic-adjacent therapy:

- **IRIS-7 (Francois 2018)**: sepsis — lymphocytopenic reversal
- **HIV studies (Sereti 2009)**: CD4 expansion
- **HSCT studies (Perales 2012)**: T cell recovery acceleration
- **Post-COVID lymphopenia**: Phase 2 trial in severe COVID
  (Laterre 2020); signal observed; not definitive on mortality

CYT107's main thymic-proximate benefit: supports thymocytes
through selection, may modestly increase TREC output, but does
NOT directly regenerate TECs. Best used in combination (with LHRH
for TEC regrowth, or with KGF for TEC support).

Revimmune (formerly Cytheris) continues to develop CYT107.

## Palifermin (KGF) clinical experience

- **FDA-approved for chemo-induced oral mucositis** (2004)
- **Off-label thymic effects**: some HSCT trials showed benefit;
  others were null
- Combination KGF + other thymic regenerators not systematically
  tested

Not a standalone adult thymic regenerator in clinical practice,
but a well-characterized TEC-trophic factor.

## iPSC-derived thymic organoids — the future

A major research front: generate functional thymic tissue from
induced pluripotent stem cells.

### The rationale

- Donor thymic tissue (RETHYMIC) requires pediatric cardiac-surgery
  leftover tissue — limited supply, HLA-mismatched
- Autologous iPSC-derived thymic tissue would be:
  - Patient-specific (perfect HLA match)
  - Unlimited supply
  - Usable for adults (including involution reversal)

### Progress

**Ramos SA, Armitage LH, Morton JJ, et al. (2023).** "Generation
of functional thymic organoids from human pluripotent stem cells."
*Stem Cell Reports* 18:829-840. PMID 36963390.

- Directed differentiation of hPSCs → thymic epithelial progenitors
- Self-organized into 3D organoids with cortical and medullary
  architecture
- Supported T cell differentiation from CD34+ progenitors in
  co-culture
- Generated CD4+ and CD8+ T cells with diverse TCR repertoires
- Generated AIRE+ mTEC-like cells

**Campinoti S, Gjinovci A, Ragazzini R, et al. (2020).**
"Reconstitution of a functional human thymus by postnatal stromal
progenitor cells and natural whole-organ scaffolds." *Nat Commun*
11:6372.

- Decellularized thymic scaffolds repopulated with stromal
  progenitors → functional thymic-like organ on kidney capsule

**Provin N, Giraud M (2023).** "Differentiation of pluripotent
stem cells into thymic epithelial cells and generation of thymic
organoids: Applications for therapeutic strategies against APECED."
*Front Immunol* 14:1149559.

Reviews of this space: field moving rapidly. First human trials
of autologous iPSC-derived thymic implants likely 2027-2030.

### 2024-2026 advances

- **Combinatory differentiation protocols** (Nature Communications
  2026): hPSCs → complete thymic epithelium with functional DC
  and CD4/CD8 T cell induction in co-culture
- **iPSC-derived iNKT cells via artificial thymic organoid
  (ATO)**: 3D organoid redifferentiation produces functional
  CD4+ iNKT cells from iPSCs (Communications Biology 2025)
- **Tissue-engineered artificial human thymus** from hiPSCs
  (Chhatta et al. 2022): functional T cell immunity studied in vitro

### Challenges

- Organoid-derived T cell output still much lower than native thymus
- In vivo implantation scale-up unresolved (quadriceps site like
  RETHYMIC? Orthotopic?)
- Long-term safety of implanted hPSC-derivatives (teratoma risk)
- Regulatory path is novel

## iTEC / FREF cell therapy — attempt_007 extension

- Bredenkamp 2014 (Nat Cell Biol) showed fibroblast → iTEC works
  in mice
- Tajima 2020 extended to aged-mouse therapeutic context
- No human trials yet
- Blackburn lab and others developing human iTEC protocols

Potential: take patient's own dermal fibroblasts, FOXN1-reprogram
to iTEC, implant. Advantages over iPSC-thymic-organoid route:
simpler (direct reprogramming, one transgene), faster.

Challenges: requires forced FOXN1 expression → vector integration
risk, regulation.

## The honest summary

For an adult seeking thymic regeneration TODAY (2026):

- **Validated clinical interventions**: none approved specifically
  for thymic regeneration in healthy adults.
- **Evidence-supported options**:
  - Lifestyle (CALERIE-style CR + zinc repletion + healthy BMI +
    adequate vitamin D + exercise) — attempt_008
  - Short-term LHRH agonist — effective in trials but side-effect
    profile unfavorable for healthy individuals
  - GH + DHEA + metformin (TRIIM protocol) — off-label, Fahy's
    Intervene Immune clinic provides this; not an FDA-approved
    indication
  - IL-7 (CYT107) — investigational; limited access
- **Near-future options**:
  - TRIIM-X Phase 2 results should establish GH+DHEA+metformin
    combination more definitively
  - FOXN1-inducing small molecules — preclinical
  - iPSC-derived thymic organoid implants — 2027+ first trials
- **Distant options**:
  - TEC-selective AR antagonist (does not exist)
  - Gene therapy for FOXN1 (not yet developed for thymus)
  - Full autologous thymic tissue banking from youth (conceptual)

## Combining the evidence: the missing trial

What the field needs: a **factorial Phase 2 trial** testing
combinations of:
1. TRIIM protocol (GH + DHEA + metformin)
2. LHRH agonist
3. IL-22 or IL-7

In 100+ healthy adults 50-75, with MRI thymic volume, TREC output,
naive T cell fraction, and epigenetic age as primary endpoints.

Mouse data predict the combination should be roughly additive.
Clinical predicted effect: 5-10 year reversal of biological age
with multi-year durability.

This trial has not been done. It would be expensive (~$20-50M)
and requires a sponsor willing to work off-label. The regulatory
path is unclear (no disease indication for "healthy aging").

The TRIIM-X result, when published, may catalyze this next step.

## Gap opened

- **Factorial combination trials** (GH+LHRH+IL-22 etc.): the
  rational next step; not yet initiated.
- **Autologous iPSC-thymic organoid human trials**: likely
  2027-2030.

  FDA pathway currently exists for treating immunosenescence in
  healthy adults. This is a field-wide barrier.
- **Who should be treated?**: biomarker stratification (GrimAge,
  TREC levels, naive T cell fractions) to select responders is
  not yet established.
