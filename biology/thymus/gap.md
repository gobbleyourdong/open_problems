# Thymus Thread — Gap List

> Running list of open questions identified across attempts 001-011.
> This is the "what's next" document for the thymus thread.

## Biology-side gaps

### Involution mechanism (attempt_004)
- What is the upstream signal that begins FOXN1 silencing at age ~1?
- Why are AIRE+ mTECs disproportionately vulnerable to age?
- Is involution programmed (clock-driven) or damage-driven?
  Separating these experimentally would inform whether reversal is
  temporary or durable.
- Cell-by-cell epigenetic characterization of FOXN1 silencing
  (methylation, histone marks) is partial, not complete.

### FOXN1 regulation (attempt_007)
- What upstream factors drive FOXN1 transcription? (Identified:
  BMP4, retinoic acid, Wnt — but the aging-specific regulator is
  unclear.)
- Are there tissue-specific FOXN1 cofactors in TECs vs hair
  follicles that could be therapeutically targeted without
  disturbing the skin program?
- Long-term safety of forced FOXN1 expression: TEC dysplasia /
  thymic neoplasm risk in long-follow-up animal studies has not
  been systematically quantified.

### Selection and tolerance (attempt_002)
- Does adult-regenerated thymus restore proper central tolerance?
  Preclinical FOXN1 rescue data suggest yes, but human data are
  absent.
- Does regenerated adult thymus unmask latent autoimmunity in
  individuals with subclinical self-antigen drift?

### Chemistry inventory (attempt_003)
- Are there novel thymic-specific bioactive peptides still
  uncharacterized? Thymosin α1, thymulin, thymopoietin are well-
  known; additional TEC-secreted factors may exist.
- Quantitative proteomics of the aged vs young human thymus has
  not been published at high resolution.

## Clinical/translation-side gaps

### TRIIM line (attempt_010)
- **TRIIM-X Phase 2 full publication**: primary completion
  November 2024; peer-reviewed publication expected 2026-2027.
  The field's single most consequential pending result.
- Sex differences: TRIIM-1 was male-only. TRIIM-X includes women;
  whether the intervention works equally in both sexes is open.
- Durability: TRIIM-1 was 12 months. Whether thymic regrowth
  holds post-cessation is unknown (participants are being
  followed).

### Factorial combination trials
- GH + LHRH + IL-22 has never been tested in humans. Mouse data
  predict super-additive effects. No sponsor has committed to the
  trial. Estimated cost ~$20-50M.
- CYT107 + palifermin + LHRH similarly untested as a full combination.

### iPSC-derived thymic organoids (attempt_010)
- First-in-human trials expected 2027-2030.
- Orthotopic vs ectopic (quadriceps) implantation site for adults
  not resolved.
- Long-term safety: teratoma risk from residual undifferentiated
  hPSC derivatives.
- Scale-up: current organoid T cell output is substantially below
  native thymus.

### Small-molecule FOXN1 activators
- Several preclinical programs active; no clinical candidates yet
  announced as of early 2026.
- Transcription-factor drugging is historically hard; may require
  targeting upstream regulators or FOXN1-specific cofactors.

### TEC-selective androgen receptor antagonist (attempt_005)
- Would allow thymic regeneration without systemic androgen
  blockade side effects.
- Does not exist; SARM technology has not produced a TEC-specific
  compound.
- No drug-discovery program publicly pursuing this.

## Regulatory and systemic gaps

### Aging as a treatable indication
- FDA does not currently recognize aging per se as a disease
  indication.
- Without this, no standard regulatory path for adult thymic
  regeneration in healthy individuals.
- TAME trial (metformin for aging) is pushing this boundary but
  hasn't yet established it.
- Field-wide bottleneck: solved biology, no regulatory home.

### Tissue banking and ethics
- Infant cardiac-surgery thymus is now a valuable therapeutic
  resource (RETHYMIC source). Regulations around consent, donor
  identification, and allocation are evolving.
- Autologous thymic tissue banking from youth for later
  transplantation has been proposed but no infrastructure exists.

### Biomarker stratification
- No validated biomarker predicts who will respond to thymic
  regeneration intervention.
- Candidate composite: GrimAge + TREC levels + naive T cell
  fraction — not yet validated prospectively.
- Without stratification, trial design is less efficient and
  clinical use is harder to target.

## Connected-thread gaps

### Thymus ↔ inflammaging ↔ dysbiosis
- Mechanistic connections are characterized (IL-6 suppresses
  FOXN1; reduced Treg output worsens gut inflammation) but
  intervention studies treating the loop as a system are rare.
- Relevant for medical/dysbiosis thread.

### Thymus ↔ persistent organisms
- Reduced elderly thymic output plausibly contributes to CVB, EBV,
  H. pylori persistence.
- Not studied: does thymic regeneration improve clearance of
  persistent organisms?
- Relevant for medical/persistent_organisms thread.

### Cross-species thymic biology
- Why do long-lived sharks retain thymic function into old age?
  The mechanism of their non-involuting thymus is not characterized.
- Understanding this could inform the evolutionary-contingency
  question (attempt_004): mammalian involution is derived, and
  there are species where it doesn't happen.

## Questions I DON'T have a good answer for

- How would you prioritize limited resources: TRIIM-style combo
  protocols vs iPSC organoids vs small-molecule FOXN1?
- Is the 3-fold mortality increase from adult thymectomy (Kooshesh
  2023) causally mediated by thymic loss, or is there residual
  confounding I'm missing?
- Why has nobody run the obvious factorial combination trial yet?
  Is it funding, or is there a technical or regulatory barrier I'm
  not seeing?
- What's the ceiling on adult regeneration? Mice get near-complete
  rescue with FOXN1. Humans with TRIIM showed ~30-50% functional
  recovery. Is the difference biological (human TECs less
  responsive) or pharmacological (weaker intervention)?
