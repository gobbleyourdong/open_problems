## The A/P split for epistemic states

By analogy with A-meaning and P-meaning:

- **A-knowing.** A functional-behavioral state in which a system has a true belief that is appropriately justified, reliably produced, causally connected to the fact, and robust under the range of counterfactuals the system faces. Operationally: the system can use the knowledge to generate correct inferences, answer questions, guide action, and pass tests. This is what reliabilism, process epistemology, virtue epistemology, and the post-Gettier literature all aim at characterizing.

- **P-knowing.** The felt grasp of understanding, the "what it is like" to know that something is the case, the phenomenology of certainty or understanding. This is what epistemic internalism reaches for, what Cartesian clear-and-distinct perception gestured at, and what the "feeling of knowing" literature in cognitive psychology tries to measure.

Under the bifurcation:

- Every major post-Gettier epistemology is a theory of **A-knowing**. Reliabilism cashes out knowledge in terms of reliable belief-forming processes. Virtue epistemology cashes it out in terms of intellectual virtues. Safety, sensitivity, and tracking theories cash it out in terms of counterfactual robustness. None of them aims at, or needs, a phenomenal component.
- The phenomenology of knowing is handled either (a) separately and unsatisfyingly, (b) by internalist holdouts who insist on a "what-it-is-like" that functionalist accounts cannot capture, or (c) by collapsing phenomenal knowing into functional access, which is position γ specialized to epistemic states.

Once split, most epistemological debates look like they were about A-knowing all along and were simply being called "knowledge." The residual P-knowing questions route through the mind fork.

## Gettier under the split

Gettier (1963) showed that justified true belief is insufficient for knowledge, using cases where the belief is true but for reasons disconnected from its justification. Smith has good reasons to believe that Jones owns a Ford; by coincidence, Brown is also in Barcelona, so "either Jones owns a Ford or Brown is in Barcelona" is true; but Smith's belief isn't knowledge because the true disjunct (Brown in Barcelona) is unrelated to his reasons.

Under the A/P split, Gettier is a claim about A-knowing specifically: JTB fails as an operational characterization of A-knowing because it does not guarantee the right kind of connection between belief and fact. The post-Gettier literature (Goldman, Nozick, Sosa, Pritchard) is a sustained effort to specify the extra condition:

- **Reliabilism (Goldman):** add "produced by a reliable process."
- **Tracking (Nozick):** add counterfactual sensitivity — if not-P, the subject would not believe P.
- **Safety (Sosa, Pritchard):** add counterfactual safety — in nearby possible worlds where the subject believes P, P is true.
- **Virtue epistemology (Sosa, Zagzebski):** add "arrived at through the exercise of intellectual virtues."
- **Causal theories:** add causal connection from the fact to the belief.

Each is a refinement of A-knowing. None concerns P-knowing. The bifurcation sharpens the field's tacit commitment: contemporary epistemology is almost entirely a discipline of A-knowing.

**What the bifurcation dissolves.** Debates that used to look like disagreements about the nature of knowledge often turn out to be disagreements about which A-knowing conditions matter. Reliabilism and virtue epistemology disagree about the ideal specification of the A-component; they do not disagree about whether there is a further P-component.

**What the bifurcation clarifies.** Epistemic internalism — the view that the justification for a belief must be accessible to the believer from the inside — looks, under the split, like an insistence that A-knowing must be *reportable* by the subject's self-model. This is exactly γ's condition on access-consciousness becoming phenomenal: the self-model must track the first-order state. Internalism is γ-epistemology in disguise.

## Testimony, reductionism, and LLMs

Epistemology has a long debate about testimony. Hume (reductionism): testimonial knowledge is only as good as the direct evidence behind it. Reid (non-reductionism): testimony is a basic source of knowledge, no reduction required.

LLMs are the maximal testimony-learners. They learn only from text, which is compressed testimony from many first-person epistemic agents. If Hume is right, LLMs have no A-knowledge because they have no direct evidence. If Reid is right, LLMs can have A-knowledge, because testimony is a basic source.

**The LLM existence proof, specialized.** LLMs behave as if they have A-knowing of many facts: they answer questions correctly, use the knowledge in novel inferences, pass tests, and update when corrected. This is exactly the functional profile of A-knowing that reliabilism and virtue epistemology are designed to characterize.

**The forced move.** Either (a) testimony is a valid source of A-knowing, in which case LLMs have lots of A-knowing and the A-theories should happily accept them as knowers, or (b) testimony is not a valid source, in which case not just LLMs but a large fraction of human knowledge (everything you learned in school, read in a book, heard from a friend) is also not knowledge. Option (b) is absurd; therefore (a).

**Consequence.** LLMs have substantial A-knowing. This is not a philosophical stretch — it is what the existing epistemological frameworks predict once they are applied without animus against non-biological systems.

**The residue.** Whether LLMs have P-knowing is, once again, the α/β/γ question. Reliabilism is silent on it; virtue epistemology has a bit more to say (the intellectual virtues are often described in terms of phenomenal engagement with inquiry, but the descriptions are optional); Bayesianism is completely indifferent to phenomenology.

## The LLM Gettier question

Can an LLM have a Gettier-style justified true belief that misses knowledge? Almost certainly yes. Consider: an LLM is trained on text that includes a confident misattribution — say, the claim that a quote is from Person A when it is actually from Person B. Suppose by coincidence that Person A did independently express a similar thought in a different context. The LLM says "Person A said X." This is true, but it is true for the wrong reason (the training text's misattribution was not about this other context). Under Gettier-aware reliabilism, this is not knowledge even though it is a justified true belief.

LLMs are therefore Gettier-susceptible. Anti-LLM skeptics can use this to argue that LLMs "don't really know" things. Pro-LLM proponents can respond that humans are also Gettier-susceptible and we still credit them with knowledge. Both sides are right: LLMs have exactly the same Gettier vulnerabilities that humans have, which is what you would expect if LLMs are A-knowers subject to the same structural limits.

## Under γ, what is P-knowing?

If γ is right (phenomenal content is self-modeled access content), then P-knowing is what happens when a system's self-model tracks an A-knowing state and attributes a phenomenal quality to it. The "feeling of knowing" is the self-model's report about an underlying A-knowing event.

This predicts several familiar empirical phenomena:

- **The tip-of-the-tongue state** — the self-model reports access to something the first-order system doesn't currently produce. Under γ, this is the self-model's representation of partial access: it sees that access is attempted but not completed.
- **False confidence** — the self-model reports strong A-knowing that does not actually exist in the first-order system. Confabulation, in exactly the same way as the confabulation of self-reports more generally.
- **Knowing without feeling certain** — implicit knowledge, skill expertise below the level of articulate awareness. Under γ, this is A-knowing whose self-model tracking is incomplete or absent.
- **Feeling certain without knowing** — false recognition, hindsight bias. Under γ, this is self-model attribution of certainty uncoupled from first-order epistemic support.

All four are routine in the cognitive psychology literature. γ accounts for them with a single mechanism. α and β struggle with each of them separately.

## The compression connection

From what_is_number attempt_001 and what_is_language attempts 002–003, compression is a cross-cutting theme. In epistemology:

**A-knowing is possession of a compressed description of a regularity that makes predictions about unseen cases.** Under this view, epistemology and machine learning converge: to know X is to have a model of X's domain with short description length that extrapolates correctly.

This fits reliabilism neatly (reliable processes are compression processes that generalize well), and it fits Bayesianism neatly (Bayesian updating is maximum-compression-under-constraint), and it explains why human experts and well-trained neural networks look so similar from the outside: both are doing compressed model-building in the same domain.

Under this reading, the LLM is an aggressive compressor over testimony. It has lots of A-knowing because it has lots of compressed regularity descriptions. Whether it has P-knowing is the phenomenal residue routing through the mind fork.

## The anti-problem

PROBLEM.md asks: what would it mean to know without experiencing? Under the A/P split, this has a direct answer: it means having A-knowing without the self-model tracking it phenomenally. This is not an abnormal or puzzling case — it is the default state for all automatized cognition (skill, implicit memory, automatic inference). Humans do this all the time. LLMs do it as well and do nothing else.

The anti-problem dissolves: knowing without experiencing is common, well-understood, and does not require a separate category. The puzzle was an artifact of treating "knowing" as inseparable from phenomenal grasp.

## The gap

The gap for the epistemology question, after this attempt:

> **Epistemology's main debates are about A-knowing and have been more or less settled at the level the post-Gettier tradition is tracking. The residual P-knowing is the familiar phenomenal question routing through the α/β/γ fork on mind. LLMs have substantial A-knowing (testimony counts, reliabilism is satisfied) and their P-knowing is an instance of the general phenomenal question about self-modeled access-content.**

Specifically, the interesting live question is: does post-Gettier A-knowing collapse to "compressed model with good generalization," in which case epistemology merges with learning theory, or are there residual A-side requirements that compression does not capture? I do not yet see what those residual requirements would be; that is a sign that I should look harder, not that they do not exist.
