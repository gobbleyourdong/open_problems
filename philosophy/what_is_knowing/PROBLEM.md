## Competing Ontologies

- **Justified true belief (Plato)** — Knowledge = JTB. Gettier broke this in 1963.
- **Reliabilism** — Knowledge = belief produced by reliable processes.
- **Virtue epistemology** — Knowledge = belief arrived at through intellectual virtue.
- **Bayesianism** — Knowledge is degree of belief, updated by evidence.
- **Embodied cognition** — Knowing is doing. Knowledge is in the body, not just the brain.
- **Extended mind (Clark, Chalmers)** — Knowledge can live outside the head — in notebooks, tools, phones.

## Gap Summary

Gettier showed JTB is insufficient. No replacement has been universally accepted. Meanwhile, large language models demonstrate "knowing" without any standard definition fitting them — they can answer correctly without understanding, justification, or belief in any classical sense.

## Anti-Problem

What would it mean to know without experiencing? Can a system that has never felt confusion have real knowledge? What would false knowledge look like that isn't just false belief?
