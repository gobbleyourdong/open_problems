## Competing Ontologies

- **Metabolism-first (Dyson, Kauffman)** — Life began as autocatalytic metabolic networks.
- **Replicator-first (Dawkins)** — Life began as self-replicating molecules (RNA world).
- **Autopoiesis (Maturana, Varela)** — Life is a self-creating, self-maintaining process.
- **Information (Davies, Walker)** — Life is information processing that reproduces itself.
- **Dissipative structures (Prigogine)** — Life is a far-from-equilibrium self-organizing system.
- **Computational (Langton)** — Life is a class of computations. Silicon life is possible.

## Gap Summary

No universally accepted definition of life exists despite 70+ years of attempts. The boundary cases (viruses, prions, self-replicating molecules, artificial life) keep breaking proposed definitions. We don't know how life started on Earth, and we have no rigorous criterion for identifying it elsewhere.

## Anti-Problem

What would be the simplest thing that we'd still call alive? Below that threshold, what fails first — metabolism, replication, boundary, response?
