## Competing Ontologies

- **Reference theory** — Meaning is what symbols refer to.
- **Use theory (Wittgenstein)** — Meaning is use in a language game.
- **Causal theory (Kripke, Putnam)** — Meaning is fixed by causal chains to original baptism.
- **Conceptual role** — Meaning is determined by inferential role in the cognitive system.
- **Embodied semantics** — Meaning is grounded in sensorimotor experience.
- **Distributional (vectors)** — Meaning is statistical context. "You shall know a word by the company it keeps."

## Gap Summary

LLMs can use words meaningfully without any grounding in the world — they only see statistical patterns. This either proves meaning IS statistical (distributional semantics wins) or proves LLMs don't really mean anything (grounding problem). No consensus. The Chinese Room argument still isn't resolved.

## Anti-Problem

What would meaningless language look like? If a system outputs coherent, contextual, helpful text, in what sense could it fail to mean what it says? Conversely, what would ADDING meaning to pure text look like?
