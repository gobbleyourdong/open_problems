### 3. Predictive test

- **α/β/γ fork**: what_is_mind has a **falsified β-prediction** —
  β's crossing-cell prediction (RNN+min > FF+rich) was REJECTED at
  p<0.0001 across 10-20 seeds at n=4,6. γ's prediction (FF+rich >
  RNN+min) was CONFIRMED. This is **documented framework
  discrimination — one of three positions was refuted by
  numerics**. Strong evidence against over-selection (E2).
- **A/P bifurcation**: no specific prediction test; "absorbs all
  positions" is more taxonomy than prediction.
- **Compression backbone**: inherits K-framework predictions (Q10 =
  1.68, etc.).

## Evidence for E1 (structurally correct)

- **The what_is_mind β-rejection is the strongest E1 evidence in
  the corpus.** β's prediction was made, tested empirically via
  Phi computation on binary networks (20 seeds at n=4), and
  REJECTED at p<0.0001. This is precisely what a non-selection-
  biased framework should do: discriminate among its own positions
  empirically.
- **The A/P bifurcation is imported from external literature**,
  not invented to fit. If the corpus applied an externally-
  validated distinction and it worked across domains, that's
  genuine structural import.
- **The 4-of-9 non-invocation pattern** (language, meaning, number,
  life don't invoke α/β/γ) shows the fork isn't mechanically applied
  everywhere — it's specific to subdirs where phenomenal
  consciousness is load-bearing. This discrimination is inconsistent
  with mechanical over-selection.

## Evidence for E2 (over-selected)

- **Zero documented rejections of any of three patterns**. Even
  with β's falsification, α and γ both survived; the fork didn't
  collapse to one position.
- **Three overlapping patterns from one operator** amplify the
  cross-subdir coupling concern.
- **The "what_is_life is done for this track"** closure claim
  suggests the corpus DOES consider some questions finished under
  the compression-backbone framework — which in sigma v7 is a
  warning, not a feature, if the framework was selected to finish
  them.

## The strongest unfired test

γ's crossing-cell prediction (FF+rich > RNN+min) was **CONFIRMED
on Phi (p<0.0001) but TRENDING on G×L (p=0.062)**. Scaling to
n > 20 is Phi#P-hard, computationally unreachable within current
methods.

If a non-Φ-computation test (e.g., comparing self-model richness
in GPT-class models to human introspection outputs, or using Φ*
approximations on larger nets) produced a result that distinguished
γ from α, this would be the second falsification point for the
fork. That would strongly support E1.
