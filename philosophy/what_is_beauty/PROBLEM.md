## Competing Ontologies

- **Objective beauty (Platonic)** — Beauty is a property of things themselves.
- **Subjective beauty** — Beauty is in the eye of the beholder. No objective fact.
- **Evolutionary (Buss, Barkow)** — Beauty signals fitness, health, fertility.
- **Information-theoretic (Schmidhuber)** — Beauty is optimal compression. Elegance = short program.
- **Formal (symmetry, proportion)** — Beauty is formal properties — symmetry, Fibonacci, golden ratio.
- **Kant's disinterested pleasure** — Beauty is purposiveness without purpose.

## Gap Summary

Cross-cultural convergence on many beauty judgments (facial symmetry, natural landscapes, musical consonance) hints at objective foundations. But disagreement on higher-level art shows cultural factors dominate at the top. No theory unifies both levels.

## Anti-Problem

What would ugliness look like to an alien that never evolved on Earth? If our beauty is evolved, would an AI with no body find anything beautiful? If yes, in what sense?
