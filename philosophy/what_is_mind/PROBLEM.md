## Competing Ontologies

- **Functionalism** — Mind is what the brain does. Any substrate implementing the same function is conscious.
- **Biological naturalism (Searle)** — Consciousness is a biological phenomenon. Silicon won't do it.
- **IIT (Tononi)** — Consciousness is integrated information (Phi). Any system with Phi > 0 has experience.
- **Higher-order theories** — Consciousness requires representing your own mental states.
- **Global Workspace (Baars, Dehaene)** — Consciousness is broadcast across a shared workspace.
- **Panpsychism** — Consciousness is fundamental. Every physical thing has a tiny degree.
- **Illusionism (Dennett, Frankish)** — Consciousness doesn't exist as it appears. The "hard problem" is confused.

## Gap Summary

The hard problem of consciousness (Chalmers 1995): why is there subjective experience at all? No physical or functional description accounts for the "what it's like" quality. This gap has not been closed despite 30+ years of sustained attack from multiple disciplines.

## Anti-Problem

What would a philosophical zombie look like? If we can coherently imagine a being physically identical to a conscious one but without experience, then experience is not physically necessary — which means physics is incomplete.
