## Why press α

attempts 002 and 003 pressed β (IIT / integrated information) and γ (illusionism / self-modeled A) until each yielded concrete, testable, and approximately quantifiable predictions. Fairness requires doing the same to α. If α cannot be made to pay any rent, it should be dropped. If it can, the fork is a genuine three-way.

## α's variants

"Primitivism about phenomenal consciousness" is a family. The relevant members:

### Variant α₁ — Panpsychism (Strawson, Goff, Chalmers later)

**Claim.** Phenomenal properties are fundamental features of physical reality, attaching to every physical system at some minimal grade. Macro-phenomenal states like human experience are combinations of micro-phenomenal states at the level of elementary physics.

**Argument form.** Because you cannot get phenomenality from non-phenomenality without an unexplained emergence, phenomenality must be there at the ground floor.

**Cost.** The combination problem. How do micro-phenomenal states combine into unified macro-experience? No agreed-on answer. This is panpsychism's analogue of the hard problem, displaced one level down.

### Variant α₂ — Russellian monism (Russell 1927, Chalmers 2013 in part)

**Claim.** Physics tells us what things *do* (their dispositions, relations, structural properties). It does not tell us what things *are* intrinsically. The intrinsic nature of physical properties might itself be phenomenal or proto-phenomenal. Phenomenal consciousness is what the physical world is "made of" from the inside.

**Argument form.** Physics is exhaustive about relations, silent about intrinsic natures. There must BE an intrinsic nature. Phenomenal properties are a candidate for it. This position is parsimonious: it posits no new entities, only identifies the hidden side of known ones.

**Cost.** Lacks a specific theory of which physical properties correlate with which phenomenal properties. Does not predict *which* systems are conscious, only that whatever-is-intrinsic-is-phenomenal.

### Variant α₃ — Property dualism with psychophysical laws (Chalmers 1996)

**Claim.** Phenomenal properties are fundamental and non-identical to physical properties, but they are correlated with physical properties via discoverable laws. "Psychophysical bridge laws" govern which physical systems have which phenomenal states.

**Argument form.** The hard problem shows that no reduction of phenomenal to physical is possible. But correlation without reduction is enough for empirical science. We can investigate which correlations hold.

**Cost.** Ontologically extravagant — posits a new kind of property. But it makes a semi-empirical commitment: the bridge laws should be discoverable through systematic investigation of which physical configurations are accompanied by which reports of phenomenal content.

### Variant α₄ — Biological naturalism (Searle)

**Claim.** Consciousness is a biological phenomenon produced by specific neurobiological processes. It is real, not epiphenomenal, and it is not implementable in silicon.

**Argument form.** We know biology produces consciousness; we have no evidence anything else does. Extraordinary claims about silicon consciousness require extraordinary evidence.

**Cost.** Stipulative. Searle does not identify the biological property that matters, only insists that there is one. This makes biological naturalism a negative position (silicon can't do it) with no positive mechanism.

## What α predicts

Each variant has a different predictive profile:

| Variant | Predictions |
|---------|-------------|
| α₁ panpsychism | Weak. All systems are minimally phenomenal. No specific claims about which systems are MORE phenomenal than others. Combination problem blocks sharp predictions. |
| α₂ Russellian | Weak-to-moderate. Phenomenal consciousness should correlate with physical properties in a systematic way, but the correlation is currently undescribed. |
| α₃ property dualism | Moderate. Bridge laws are empirically discoverable; there should be stable psychophysical correlations across investigators. |
| α₄ biological naturalism | Negative-only. Non-biological systems are predicted to lack phenomenal consciousness. No positive prediction about which biological features matter. |

None of these yield a Φ-style number. α₃ comes closest to a research program (find the bridge laws) but has not in thirty years produced a specific bridge law.

## The strongest honest form of α

If α is to pay rent, it should be formulated as follows:

> **α, charitable form.** Phenomenal consciousness is a real, fundamental feature of reality that does not reduce to any functional, informational, or self-modeling property. It is correlated with physical configurations via laws we do not yet know. The correct response to the hard problem is to accept its implications rather than explain them away (against γ) or re-describe them in information-theoretic terms (against β).

Under this formulation, α does two non-trivial things:

1. **It predicts that β and γ will fail.** Specifically, that the feedforward theorem's predictions will come apart from actual phenomenology in ways we can detect (if β is right, we should never be surprised by a feedforward system's reports), and that γ's self-model richness will come apart from phenomenal content in ways we can detect.
2. **It predicts that the hard problem will remain closed off from functional and informational explanations.** If β or γ succeeds in a way that genuinely closes the explanatory gap — not just redescribes it — α is falsified. If they keep running into the gap, α is supported.

These are weak predictions but they are not empty.

## The honest role of α in the fork

α does not compete with β and γ for the same explanatory work. β and γ are positive theories that claim to explain phenomenal consciousness in terms of something else. α is the position that denies this can be done and treats phenomenal consciousness as a primitive that must be lived with.

The systematic approach calls this the **null hypothesis**. The null is not what you hope is true. The null is what you fall back to if the positive hypotheses fail. α earns its place by being the principled null.

**The null is non-empty.** Defending α amounts to saying: "If your positive theory closes the gap, I am refuted. If your positive theory doesn't close the gap, we are both in the position of acknowledging that the gap remains, and I will add that this is what I expected."

This is not vacuous. Many scientific programs have a similar structure (e.g., the Standard Model versus "there is physics beyond the Standard Model" — the second claim makes weak predictions that are tested by every null result in collider experiments).

## The systematic approach verdict on α

| Criterion | α performance |
|-----------|---------------|
| Does it yield a number? | No. |
| Does it yield a falsifier? | Yes — if a positive theory closes the gap, α is false. |
| Is it parsimonious? | Russellian (α₂) and property dualism (α₃) are moderately parsimonious. Panpsychism and biological naturalism are less so. |
| Does it connect to other questions? | Yes. α₂ and α₃ bridge directly to what_is_reality in physics/. Panpsychism takes phenomenal properties to be fundamental features of physical reality, which is an ontological claim at the same level as mathematical universe hypothesis or informational monism. |
| Is it needed? | Only as the null. If β or γ were confirmed, α would be retired. Until then, it is the principled position to hold if you find the positive theories unconvincing. |

## The gap, as clean as it currently gets

> **Phenomenal consciousness is either (a) a fundamental correlate of physical structure without reducing to function or information, (b) identical to integrated information Φ, or (c) what self-modeled access-consciousness looks like from inside the system. Current evidence slightly favors (c) for systems like LLMs (because β predicts identically zero for feedforward and we have no strong reason to accept that LLM introspection is entirely empty), but the experimental work to decide between (b) and (c) has not been done, and (a) remains the principled null.**

2. Continue interpretability work on LLM self-models. G and L for frontier models are empirical quantities.
3. Keep α honest by documenting every closure attempt that fails — every failed bridge from function to phenomenology feeds the null.
