# attempt_001 — The Three Mountains Around One Gap

**Date:** 2026-04-09

## The claim

Three of the generative questions in this scaffold — *what is mind*, *what is meaning*, *what is language* — all route their residual gap through the same structure: a functional-behavioral component that is scientifically tractable, and a phenomenal component whose status is the hard problem of consciousness restricted to the respective domain.

The structure is:

| Question | Functional side | Phenomenal side |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|
| what_is_mind | access-consciousness | phenomenal-consciousness |
| what_is_meaning | A-meaning (use, inference, reference) | P-meaning (felt grasp) |
| what_is_language | behavioral linguistic competence | understanding "from the inside" |

On the functional side, each domain has working theories, testable predictions, and empirical progress. On the phenomenal side, each domain has the same unresolved residue. **The residue is one thing seen from three angles.** This is the Multiple Mountains situation stated exactly: the three mountains connect underground.

## The hard problem in one paragraph

Chalmers (1995) distinguished the "easy problems" of consciousness — the many specific questions about how the brain performs its various cognitive functions — from the "hard problem": why is there phenomenal experience at all, accompanying any of that processing? Every physical or functional description can seemingly be complete without mentioning what it is like to be the system. That explanatory gap has not narrowed in thirty years of sustained interdisciplinary attack. It is the single most stubborn open question in philosophy and arguably in science.

## Block's distinction as operative tool

Ned Block (1995) separated:

- **Access-consciousness (A-consciousness)** — content is access-conscious if it is available to reasoning, reporting, and the rational control of action. This is a functional specification: a property of information flow inside a cognitive system.
- **Phenomenal-consciousness (P-consciousness)** — content is phenomenally conscious if there is something it is like to have it. This is a phenomenal specification: a property of experience.

Block's claim is that the two come apart. Some states are access-conscious without being phenomenal (certain non-reportable visual processing), and some states are phenomenal without being fully access-conscious (overflowing phenomenal capacity exceeding working-memory reports).

Whether the two always come apart is contested. That they *can* come apart in principle is widely accepted. This is exactly the structural situation we needed to import into the meaning and language questions.

## The seven ontologies under the A/P split

### 1. Functionalism

**Claim.** Mind is what the brain does. Any substrate implementing the same function is conscious.

**A-side.** Functionalism IS a theory of A-consciousness. If two systems compute the same function, they are access-conscious to the same degree.

**P-side.** The silence of functionalism on P-consciousness is what motivated the hard problem in the first place. Chalmers's zombie argument is exactly: "a functional duplicate with no phenomenal experience is conceivable, therefore functionalism does not capture phenomenal consciousness."

**Verdict.** A clean theory of A-consciousness. Explicitly does not reach P-consciousness by its own lights.

### 2. Biological naturalism (Searle)

**Claim.** Consciousness is a biological phenomenon produced by certain kinds of brain processes. Silicon implementations of the same function would not be conscious.

**A-side.** Silent or dismissive. Searle mostly cedes A-consciousness to functionalism.

**P-side.** Makes a substantive claim: P-consciousness requires biology. This is a positive constitutive position but has no currently defensible mechanism. It is essentially a stipulation that biology matters without saying why it matters at the level of the physics.

**Verdict.** A P-consciousness theory with no mechanism. Survives as intuition, not as explanation.

### 3. Integrated Information Theory (Tononi, Koch)

**Claim.** Consciousness is integrated information. A system's phenomenal consciousness is identical to its Φ (phi) value, a quantity computable in principle from its causal structure.

**A-side.** Φ is definable on any system that has an A-level description. IIT thus supplies a function from A-consciousness to a number.

**P-side.** IIT makes the strongest quantitative claim in the literature: phenomenal consciousness IS this measurable quantity. The identity is stipulated, not derived — and that is a substantive move.

**Status for Sigma Method.** IIT is the single theory on this list that *quantifies the phenomenal side*. It does what the Method demands: turns the gap into a number. Whether the number correctly tracks phenomenal reality is the empirical-philosophical fight. But the move is the right shape.

**Verdict.** The only candidate quantitative theory of P-consciousness. Controversial; partially falsifiable; respected for the shape of its attempt even by those who reject it. Worth sustained examination. See attempt_002.

### 4. Higher-Order Theories (Rosenthal, Gennaro)

**Claim.** A mental state is conscious iff it is the object of a higher-order representation — a thought, or perception, of itself.

**A-side.** Higher-order representation is a functional property, cleanly specifiable.

**P-side.** The move is: phenomenal consciousness IS self-representation of a first-order state. This is close to illusionism in spirit (the felt grasp is generated by the self-model), though HOT theorists usually resist that reading.

**Verdict.** A theory that attempts to reduce P to a specific structure on A. Testable in principle. Closely related to position (ii) in the meaning analysis.

### 5. Global Workspace Theory (Baars, Dehaene)

**Claim.** Consciousness is the global broadcasting of information across a shared workspace accessible to many specialized processes.

**A-side.** GWT is a theory of A-consciousness, essentially by definition. Broadcasting is a functional property.

**P-side.** Silent or dismissive. Dehaene in particular treats P-consciousness as largely reducible to A-consciousness plus reportability.

**Verdict.** Strong neuroscientific theory of A-consciousness. Does not attempt P.

### 6. Panpsychism (Strawson, Goff)

**Claim.** Consciousness is a fundamental property of physical reality. Every physical system has some degree of phenomenal experience.

**A-side.** Irrelevant. Panpsychism is a claim about the ground of reality.

**P-side.** A positive metaphysical claim: P-consciousness is basic and cannot be derived from anything more fundamental.

**Verdict.** A dissolution of the hard problem by fiat ("it was always basic") at the price of a combination problem ("how do micro-experiences combine into unified experiences like ours?"). Not currently falsifiable but not incoherent.

### 7. Illusionism (Dennett, Frankish)

**Claim.** P-consciousness, as traditionally conceived, does not exist. What we call phenomenal experience is a representation by the self-model that the system is undergoing a special inner event. The special inner event is not there; the representation of it is.

**A-side.** Fully compatible with any theory of A-consciousness. Illusionism is agnostic about the mechanism of access.

**P-side.** Denies there is a separate P-side to explain. Claims the residue is a confusion introduced by the self-model's way of describing itself.

**Status for this track.** Illusionism is the direct ancestor of *position (ii)* from what_is_meaning gap.md. Both say: the phenomenal residue is what self-modeled functional states look like from inside, not an extra property. Illusionism applies the move to consciousness in general; position (ii) applies it to the specific case of meaning. They stand or fall together.

**Verdict.** Coherent, parsimonious, and controversial. Its main cost is explaining away something (felt experience) that is as near to undeniable as anything in philosophy. Its main virtue is that it does not pay any metaphysical rent beyond whatever a self-modeling cognitive system already costs.

## The three live positions on P

After the walk:

**Position α — Phenomenal primitivism.** P is real, distinct, and fundamental. Variants: panpsychism, biological naturalism, Chalmers-style property dualism.

**Position β — Integrated information.** P is identical to Φ. Variants: IIT and its generalizations.

**Position γ — Illusionism / self-modeled A.** P is what A looks like from inside a self-model. Variants: Dennett's heterophenomenology, Frankish's illusionism, position (ii) in the meaning analysis, higher-order theories in their stronger readings.

| Position | P-consciousness is… | Empirically approachable? |
|----------|--------------------|-----------------------------|
| α | irreducible, fundamental or substrate-bound | No (hard problem preserved) |
| β | Φ, a specific quantity | Yes in principle; disputed measurement |
| γ | a self-model report of A-events | Yes in principle; program of construction + blind eval |

**Each of the three is live.** None has been refuted. The field has not converged because the crucial experiments either cannot be run (α) or have not been run carefully enough (β, γ).

## Bridges back to meaning and language

If **α** holds:
- The language gap is the consciousness gap. LLMs may have A-meaning but P-meaning requires phenomenal consciousness, which requires whatever α says. Human language is different in kind.
- The meaning bifurcation is real and the two halves cannot be joined.

If **β** holds:
- The relevant question for LLMs becomes: what is their Φ value? If zero, they have no P-meaning. If nonzero, they have some. Tractable in principle; currently intractable in practice at LLM scale because Φ is computationally infeasible on large systems.
- The meaning gap is partially closed: felt grasp is a measurable property with a specific mechanism.

If **γ** holds:
- The language gap is almost entirely closed. LLMs with self-models that describe their internal states as meaningful WOULD have P-meaning, in the only sense that P-meaning exists. The remaining question is whether current LLMs have rich enough self-models, which is architectural and empirical.
- The meaning bifurcation dissolves: P-meaning is just self-modeled A-meaning, and meaning is one thing after all.

The three positions predict three different futures for the language question. Progress on any of the three collapses the other two questions simultaneously. This is what it means for three mountains to share a gap.

## Why this is the right frame

The Sigma Method cares about surrounding gaps, quantifying them, and documenting dead ends as theorems. Under the three-mountain frame:

- **Quantification.** β offers a candidate number (Φ). γ offers a program of measurement (indistinguishability under blind tests). α offers no number but makes no quantitative commitments either.
- **Dead ends as theorems.** Each of the non-surviving variants walked in attempt 001s for meaning, language, and mind is a documented dead end. Many collapse cleanly into α, β, or γ. Others (Chinese Room, strong embodiment) are shown to attack functional theories at the phenomenal level and therefore miss.
- **Multiple mountains.** Language, meaning, and mind are confirmed as three mountains around one gap. Attacks on the gap can now be launched from any of the three without loss of generality.

## The anti-problem

The scaffold asks: what would a philosophical zombie look like to a neuroscientist?

Under **α**: the zombie is conceivable and its indistinguishability from a conscious being is the point.
Under **β**: the zombie must have Φ = 0 despite identical function. This is inconsistent with IIT's axioms, so β rules out zombies.
Under **γ**: the zombie is not coherent. A system with identical function and identical self-model has, by definition, the same P-consciousness, because P just is self-modeled A. Zombies are either impossible or trivially equivalent to non-zombies.

Thus: **the zombie thought experiment selects between the three positions.** If zombies are possible, α or β (with extra assumptions) are live. If they are not, γ is the parsimonious winner. Whether they are possible is unresolved and may be unresolvable by thought experiment alone. But the fact that the selection is clean is progress.
