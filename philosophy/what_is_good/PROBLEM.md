## Competing Ontologies

- **Moral realism** — Moral facts exist independently of belief.
- **Error theory (Mackie)** — Moral claims are systematically false — there are no moral facts.
- **Expressivism** — Moral claims express attitudes, not facts.
- **Constructivism** — Moral facts are constructed by rational agreement.
- **Naturalism** — Moral facts reduce to natural facts about flourishing, etc.
- **Divine command** — Good is what God commands.

## Gap Summary

Hume's is-ought gap: no amount of descriptive facts implies a prescriptive conclusion. After 3000 years, we have no universal ethical theory. Meta-ethics keeps splintering. But there is striking cross-cultural convergence on many practical morals — which hints at SOMETHING underlying, but what?

## Anti-Problem

What would a moral monster optimal by their own values look like? If someone's terminal value is destruction, can we argue they are "wrong"? The inability to refute that from pure logic is the hole at the bottom of ethics.
