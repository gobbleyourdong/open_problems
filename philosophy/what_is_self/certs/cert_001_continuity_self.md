**Teleportation** (score=0.90, consensus=0.50) and **Fission** (score=0.86,
consensus=0.30) are the hardest cases from Parfit 1984. Both have HIGH
continuity scores but UNCERTAIN or NEGATIVE identity consensus.

This is not a failure — it is the confirmation of Parfit's insight:
the score measures **what matters** (psychological continuity); the consensus
measures **strict numerical identity**. These come apart in exactly the cases
Parfit predicted.

**"Failures" are confirmations:** If the continuity score perfectly predicted
identity consensus, Parfit would be refuted (continuity would = identity).
The divergence at teleportation/fission shows the two are genuinely distinct.
