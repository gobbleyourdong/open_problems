## Competing Ontologies

- **Cartesian ego** — The self is a continuous non-physical substance.
- **Bundle theory (Hume)** — The self is just a bundle of perceptions. No continuous entity.
- **Narrative self** — The self is the story we tell about ourselves.
- **No-self (anatta, Buddhism)** — The self is an illusion constructed by aggregates.
- **Biological continuity** — The self is the continuous organism.
- **Psychological continuity (Locke, Parfit)** — The self is continuous memory/personality.

## Gap Summary

Every theory breaks on transporter-style thought experiments. If you're disassembled and perfectly reconstructed, are you still you? Everyone answers differently and no theory of self survives the edge cases. Neuroscience finds no "self-module" in the brain — the self is distributed, reconstructed every moment.

## Anti-Problem

What would it mean to not be yourself? If you can imagine being replaced while feeling continuous, the felt continuity isn't proof of sameness. What is?
