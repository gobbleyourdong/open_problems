## Competing Ontologies

- **Platonism** — Numbers exist independently of minds, in an abstract realm.
- **Formalism (Hilbert)** — Math is symbol manipulation. Numbers don't "exist" — rules do.
- **Logicism (Frege, Russell)** — Math is reducible to logic. Numbers are logical objects.
- **Intuitionism (Brouwer)** — Math is mental construction. Only constructive proofs count.
- **Structuralism** — Numbers are positions in structures. Math studies the structures.
- **Fictionalism** — Numbers are useful fictions. Math is a game that happens to apply to reality.

## Gap Summary

Gödel showed any sufficiently powerful formal system is incomplete — there are true statements it cannot prove. This means math cannot be fully axiomatized. Meanwhile, Wigner's "unreasonable effectiveness" asks WHY math applies to physical reality at all. No answer has stuck.

## Anti-Problem

What would a universe without number look like? Could there be physical reality without mathematical structure? If not, is reality FUNDAMENTALLY mathematical?
