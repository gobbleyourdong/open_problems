## Why the Wigner r dropped (0.845 → 0.610)

The 8 new structures include mathematically deep but less physically applied
areas: category theory (mb=7, pa=3), algebraic topology (mb=6, pa=3), measure
theory (mb=5, pa=4). These have high mathematical breadth but haven't yet found
major physics applications.

This makes sense: recent mathematical developments take time to penetrate physics.
Category theory was developed in the 1940s–60s and has only recently found
applications in theoretical physics (string theory, topological QFT). Its
high math breadth but limited current physics applications correctly reduces
the Wigner correlation at n=20.

**The Wigner correlation is real but less perfect than n=12 suggested.** Some
highly compressive mathematical structures have not yet found physics applications.
The prediction holds (r=+0.610, p=0.004) but is not as clean as the simpler
n=12 corpus.

## Why the beauty-math r strengthened (0.535 → 0.661)

The new structures show mathematical breadth tracking beauty more robustly:
- Category theory (mb=7, beauty=8.5): high breadth, high beauty ✓
- Algebraic topology (mb=6, beauty=8.5): high breadth, high beauty ✓
- Arithmetic basic (mb=2, beauty=3.5): low breadth, low beauty ✓

At n=20, r(math_branches, beauty) = **+0.661, p=0.0015** — highly significant.
This is the STRONGER result at n=20: mathematical beauty IS predicted by how
many branches of mathematics a structure connects.

## The refined picture

**Physics applicability does NOT independently predict beauty** (r=+0.109, p=0.65).
Once mathematical breadth is controlled, having more physics applications adds
no further beauty prediction.

**Mathematical beauty ← compression within mathematics ← high breadth.**
The Wigner effectiveness is a consequence: compressive math structures also
compress physics. But the causal chain runs through internal mathematical
compression, not through physics feedback.

This is the precise form of the what_is_number compression view:
1. Beauty tracks compression within mathematics (r=+0.661)
2. Compression within mathematics also compresses physics (r=+0.610 — Wigner)
3. Physics applicability is a downstream consequence of mathematical compression

The revision of the Wigner claim: not "math and physics are mysteriously connected"
but "the most compressive mathematical structures generalize to any regularity class,
including physics — this is expected, not mysterious."
