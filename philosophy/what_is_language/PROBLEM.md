## Competing Ontologies

- **Universal Grammar (Chomsky)** — Language is innate. All languages share deep structure.
- **Usage-based (Tomasello)** — Language emerges from social interaction and pattern learning.
- **Distributional** — Language is statistical co-occurrence. Meaning is context.
- **Embodied** — Language is grounded in sensorimotor experience.
- **Computational** — Language is a formal system that can be described by rules.
- **Social convention** — Language is shared agreement, nothing more.

## Gap Summary

LLMs learning language from text alone (no grounding, no social interaction, no innate grammar) have broken every major theory of language acquisition. Either they prove the distributional hypothesis, OR they show that "language" in the LLM sense is fundamentally different from "language" in the human sense.

## Anti-Problem

What would pre-linguistic thought look like? Do babies think in a language-like medium before they have language? If not, what changes when language arrives?
