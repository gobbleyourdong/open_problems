# P vs NP

## Statement
Does every problem whose solution can be verified in polynomial time also have a solution that can be found in polynomial time? (Is P = NP?)

## Status: OPEN (Clay Millennium Prize, $1M)

## Key Objects
- P: problems solvable in polynomial time by a deterministic Turing machine
- NP: problems whose solutions are verifiable in polynomial time
- NP-complete: hardest problems in NP (Cook-Levin: SAT is NP-complete)
- co-NP: complements of NP problems
- P ⊆ NP ∩ co-NP (known)

## Known Results
- P ≠ NP widely believed but unproven
- Oracle separations exist (Baker-Gill-Solovay, 1975)
- Natural proofs barrier (Razborov-Rudich, 1997)
- Relativization barrier (Baker-Gill-Solovay, 1975)
- Algebrization barrier (Aaronson-Wigderson, 2009)
- Circuit complexity lower bounds: parity not in AC⁰ (Furst-Saxe-Sipser)
- Geometric Complexity Theory (Mulmuley-Sohoni): algebraic geometry approach

## Approaches
- Circuit complexity (prove super-polynomial lower bounds)
- Proof complexity (propositional proof systems)
- Algebraic geometry (GCT program)
- Communication complexity
- Kolmogorov complexity
