## The Leap from Case-by-Case to Uniform

The generator (attempts 004-012) checks varieties ONE AT A TIME.
This will never prove Hodge in general (infinitely many varieties).

The Tannakian viewpoint turns Hodge into a statement about
REPRESENTATION THEORY of reductive groups — where the classification
IS the proof.

## The Tannakian Reformulation

### Setup
For a smooth projective variety X, the Hodge structure on H*(X, Q)
generates a Tannakian category ⟨H*(X)⟩_Hdg (the smallest Tannakian
subcategory of Hodge structures containing H^k(X) for all k).

Its Tannaka dual: the HODGE GROUP Hg(X) ⊂ GL(H*(X, Q)).

The algebraic cycles generate a Tannakian subcategory ⟨H*(X)⟩_alg
(motives generated by X, if motivic conjectures hold).

Its Tannaka dual: the MOTIVIC GALOIS GROUP G_mot(X).

There is a surjection: Hg(X) → G_mot(X) (every algebraic class is Hodge).

### The Reformulation

**Hodge conjecture ⟺ Hg(X) = G_mot(X)**

The Hodge group and the motivic Galois group are the SAME.

Equivalently: the functor from motives → Hodge structures is FULLY FAITHFUL.

## The Exhaustion over Reductive Groups

### For Abelian Varieties

Hg(A) = MT(A) (the Mumford-Tate group), a reductive subgroup of Sp(2g, Q).

The Albert classification of End(A) ⊗ Q:
| Type | End algebra | MT structure |
|------|-------------|--------------|
| I | Totally real field F | Res_{F/Q} Sp |
| II | Totally indefinite quaternion over F | Inner form of Res Sp |
| III | Totally definite quaternion over F | Inner form of Res Sp |
| IV | CM field K | Torus (Res_{K/Q} G_m restricted) |

For EACH Albert type: the MT group is determined up to isogeny.
The invariants in Λ^*(V) are computable from the representation theory.

### The Exhaustion Algorithm (Abelian Varieties)

```
for g = 1, 2, 3, ...:
    for each Albert type (I, II, III, IV):
        for each possible MT group G ⊂ Sp(2g):
            # REPRESENTATION THEORY (exact, finite)
            invariants = compute_G_invariants(Λ^*(Q^{2g}))
            
            # MOTIVIC GENERATORS (exact, finite)
            motivic = generate_from([polarization, End(A), Künneth, Lefschetz])
            
            # THE CHECK (linear algebra over Q)
            if span(motivic) == invariants:
                yield Proof(g, type, G, "Hodge verified") ✓
            else:
                gap = invariants \ span(motivic)
                yield GapReport(g, type, G, gap)
```

### What "motivic generators" means precisely

The KNOWN algebraic classes on an abelian variety A come from:

1. **Polarization**: The class ω ∈ H^{1,1}(A) of the ample line bundle.
   All powers ω^k are algebraic. ✓

2. **Endomorphisms**: If α ∈ End(A), its graph Γ_α ⊂ A × A gives a
   correspondence class in H^*(A × A). The induced map α* on H*(A)
   produces Hodge classes. ✓

3. **Künneth**: If A ≅ B × C, the Künneth decomposition gives product
   classes. ✓

4. **Lefschetz operator**: L = ω ∧ (−) and its inverse Λ (if standard
   conjectures hold). ✓ (For abelian varieties: standard conj. A proved.)

5. **Pontryagin product**: A is a group, so H*(A) has a ring structure
   from the group law. Products of algebraic classes are algebraic. ✓

6. **Weil classes** (for CM type): Special classes predicted by the CM
   structure. Algebraic for dim ≤ 5 (Moonen-Zarhin). ⚠️ Open for dim ≥ 6.

### Where the Exhaustion Succeeds and Fails

**Succeeds (g ≤ 5)**: For every MT group G ⊂ Sp(2g) with g ≤ 5:
the motivic generators (items 1-6) span all G-invariants.
This was checked by Tankeev, Murty, Hazama, Moonen-Zarhin through
a FINITE case-by-case analysis of all possible G.

**Fails (g ≥ 6)**: For CM abelian 6-folds: the Weil classes in
H^{3,3}(A) might not be spanned by generators 1-5.
The question: is generator 6 (Weil classes) algebraic?

## The Weil Class Problem: The Residual Wall

### What Weil Classes Are

For a CM abelian variety A of dimension g with CM by a CM field K of
degree 2g: the Hodge group is a Q-torus T ⊂ Sp(2g).

The T-invariants in Λ^g(Q^{2g}): include the WEIL CLASSES, which are
elements of H^{g,g}(A) ∩ H^{2g}(A, Q) that are NOT in the span of
products of divisor classes.

### The Weil Construction

The Weil class on a CM abelian variety (A, Φ) (where Φ is the CM type)
is built from the WEDGE of the eigenforms:

If H^{1,0}(A) has a basis {ω_{σ_1}, ..., ω_{σ_g}} indexed by the CM
type Φ = {σ_1, ..., σ_g} ⊂ Hom(K, C), then:

  W = ω_{σ_1} ∧ ... ∧ ω_{σ_g} ∧ ω̄_{σ_1} ∧ ... ∧ ω̄_{σ_g}

is a class in H^{g,g}(A). Its rational part is the Weil class.

### When Weil Classes Are Algebraic

**Known**: Weil classes are absolute Hodge (Deligne 1982). They satisfy
all the NECESSARY conditions for being algebraic.

**Proved algebraic**: 
- When the CM type Φ is "non-degenerate" (generic)
- When A is isogenous to a product of lower-dim CM abelian varieties
- When g ≤ 5 (Moonen-Zarhin)

**Open**: Simple CM abelian varieties of dimension g ≥ 6 with
"degenerate" CM types.

### The Generator on Weil Classes

For each CM field K of degree 2g (g ≥ 6) and each CM type Φ:

1. **Compute** the Weil class W ∈ H^{g,g}(A)
2. **Check** if W ∈ span(generators 1-5) (linear algebra over K)
3. **If yes**: Hodge verified for this CM type ✓
4. **If no**: attempt to construct an algebraic cycle with class W
   - Abelian subvarieties of A^n (for some n)
   - Correspondences from moduli spaces
   - Chern classes of Hodge bundles on Shimura varieties

Step 4 is the HARD part — constructing the cycle. But for each
specific CM type, it's a FINITE search (the cycles come from the
arithmetic of the CM field K).

## The Proof Architecture (If Everything Works)

```
HODGE FOR ALL ABELIAN VARIETIES
    ↑
For each dimension g:
    ↑
Albert classification of End(A) ⊗ Q (finite list)
    ↑
For each Albert type: MT group classification (finite)
    ↑
For each MT group G: compute G-invariants (representation theory)
    ↑

    ↑

```

Each level is a FINITE computation. The total is finite per g.
The full conjecture for all abelian varieties = the limit g → ∞.

**This is an INFINITE computation — but each step is finite and
produces a theorem. The Hodge conjecture for abelian varieties
is "provable in stages" in a formal sense.**

## For General Varieties (Beyond Abelian)

The Tannakian approach extends:
- The Hodge group Hg(X) replaces the MT group
- For varieties with rich geometry (Calabi-Yau, hyper-Kähler, etc.):
  the Hodge group is constrained by the geometry
- For ARBITRARY varieties: Hg(X) can be ANY reductive Q-group
- The classification of reductive groups is KNOWN (Cartan)
- But the representation theory is infinitely varied

**The wall for general varieties**: the Hodge group is not DETERMINED
by a finite amount of data (unlike abelian varieties where the Albert
type + dimension determines it). For a general variety, Hg(X) depends
on the specific geometry of X.

## Result

The Tannakian exhaustion reduces Hodge to:

1. **Abelian varieties**: finite computation per dimension g.
   Done for g ≤ 5. Next: g = 6 (Weil classes on CM 6-folds).
   The generator is an EXPLICIT finite algorithm for each g.

2. **General varieties**: reduces to Hg(X) = G_mot(X), which is
   a group theory question for each X. No uniform approach known.
   The generator works case-by-case but can't prove the general statement.

**The wall**: Hodge for abelian varieties is "almost decidable" (finite
per dimension, infinite dimensions). Hodge for general varieties needs
a structural argument about ALL reductive groups, not just the ones
arising as MT groups of abelian varieties.

**The deepest formulation**: Hodge ⟺ the motivic t-structure on the
derived category of motives extends the Hodge t-structure. This is a
statement about the EXISTENCE of a mathematical object (the motivic
t-structure), not about a specific computation. The brute force
generator can verify it instance by instance but can't prove it exists.
