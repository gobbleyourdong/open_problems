# Attempt 002 — Route Survey for Hodge

**Date**: 2026-04-07
**Phase**: 0 → 1
**Instance**: Even (Theory)

## The Landscape

Hodge is the MOST ALGEBRAIC of the Clay problems. Unlike NS (PDE),
YM (physics), RH (analysis), or BSD (number theory), Hodge lives
entirely within algebraic geometry. The tools are sheaves, cohomology,
intersection theory, and motives.

## Rankings

### 1. Standard Conjectures (Grothendieck) ★★★★

The standard conjectures predict algebraicity of certain cohomology
operations (Künneth projectors, Lefschetz involution). They IMPLY Hodge.

**What's proved**: Standard conjectures for abelian varieties in
characteristic 0 (Lieberman 1968, using Hodge theory ironically).
In positive characteristic: wide open (Grothendieck's original motivation).

**The gap**: Proving Künneth projectors are algebraic for general varieties.
This is a weaker-seeming statement than Hodge but nobody can prove it either.

**Sigma Method fit**: MEDIUM. The conjectures are precise but the techniques
are abstract. Limited computational component.

### 2. Abelian Varieties ★★★★★

**Why #1**: The most tractable special case. Abelian varieties have rich
structure (group law, dual variety, endomorphisms) that constrains the
Hodge classes. Hodge is PROVED for dim ≤ 3.

**The frontier**: dim 4 and 5. For "generic" abelian varieties, Hodge
is known (the Hodge ring is generated by divisor classes). For SPECIAL
abelian varieties (with extra endomorphisms, CM type), exotic Hodge
classes can appear.

**Sigma Method fit**: HIGH. Can compute period matrices numerically.
Can enumerate Hodge classes for specific abelian varieties. Can test
whether they're algebraic.

### 3. K-Theory / Chern Character ★★★

The Chern character ch: K(X) → H^*(X, Q) maps algebraic K-theory to
rational cohomology. Its image lands in Hodge classes. Hodge asks
whether the image is ALL Hodge classes.

**The gap**: K(X) is hard to compute, and ch isn't surjective in general.
The Atiyah-Hirzebruch spectral sequence from K-theory to cohomology
has differentials that can kill classes.

### 4. Motivic Cohomology ★★

Voevodsky's motivic cohomology provides a "universal" cohomology theory.
The Hodge conjecture is related to the Hodge realization functor being
conservative on motives.

**The gap**: Motivic cohomology is itself poorly understood for general
varieties. The conjecture is rephrased but not simplified.

### 5. Period Computations (Computational) ★★★

For specific varieties: compute the period matrix (integrals of
holomorphic forms over cycles), identify Hodge classes, check
algebraicity.

**Sigma Method fit**: HIGH for Odd instance. CAN compute periods
numerically for explicit varieties (hyperelliptic curves, K3 surfaces,
abelian varieties).

## Decision

**Primary**: Route 2 (abelian varieties) — extend to dim 4-5
**Computational**: Route 5 (period computations) — build iron fortress
**Framework**: Route 1 (standard conjectures) — study what they'd give
**Long shot**: Route 3 (K-theory) — structural approach

## The Honest Assessment

Hodge is HARD in a different way from the other problems:
- NS, YM: construction/estimation problems (PDE techniques)
- RH, BSD: number theory (L-functions, arithmetic)
- Hodge: pure algebraic geometry (sheaves, cycles, motives)

The Sigma Method is WEAKEST here because:
1. Limited computational component (period matrices are computable
   but connecting them to algebraic cycles is the conjecture itself)
2. No "iron fortress" analog (can't numerically verify Hodge for
   specific classes without already knowing the algebraic cycle)
3. The problem is EXISTENTIAL (does a cycle exist?) not QUANTITATIVE
   (is a number positive?)

**Hodge may be the hardest Millennium Problem for the Sigma Method.**
