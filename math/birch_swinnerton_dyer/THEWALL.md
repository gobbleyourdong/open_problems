# THE WALL — Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer

> Phase 1 complete. The wall identified in 2 attempts + 1 research agent.

## The Problem
For E/Q: rank(E(Q)) = ord_{s=1} L(E,s).

## What's Proved
- Rank 0: ✓ (Kolyvagin 1990)
- Rank 1: ✓ (Gross-Zagier 1986 + Kolyvagin 1990)
- Full BSD formula at rank 0/1: ✓ at all but finitely many primes (Jetchev-Skinner-Wan 2017)
- 66.48% of all E/Q satisfy BSD: ✓ (Bhargava-Skinner-Zhang 2014)

## The Wall

**In one sentence:**

> No construction produces two independent rational points on an elliptic
> curve from L-function data. Gross-Zagier gives one. Nobody can give two.

The Gross-Zagier formula connects L'(E/K,1) to the height of ONE Heegner
point. Kolyvagin's Euler system leverages this ONE point to bound the
Selmer group. For rank ≥ 2: need TWO independent points (or TWO
independent Euler system classes). Neither exists.

## Why It's Structural

| Rank | Points needed | Available | Status |
|------|--------------|-----------|--------|
| 0 | 0 | — | PROVED |
| 1 | 1 | Heegner point y_K | PROVED |
| 2 | 2 | ??? | OPEN |
| 3 | 3 | ??? | OPEN |

The Heegner construction is INTRINSICALLY rank 1:
- CM theory gives ONE special point per quadratic field K
- Gross-Zagier relates ONE derivative to ONE height
- Kolyvagin's system is generated by ONE element

For rank 2: would need a "pair of Heegner points" or a "rank-2 Euler
system." The Loeffler-Zerbes program (2020s) constructs Euler systems
from higher-rank automorphic forms, but they're still rank-1 systems
applied to different L-functions — they don't produce two independent
classes for the SAME elliptic curve.

## The Anti-Problem

"What would a rank-2 construction look like?"

It would be a map: (E, K₁, K₂) → (P₁, P₂) ∈ E(Q)² where P₁, P₂ are
independent, and a formula:

  L''(E, 1) = c · det(⟨P_i, P_j⟩_NT)  (height regulator)

This is the "second-order Gross-Zagier formula." Nobody has one.

Candidates for where the two points might come from:
1. Two different quadratic fields K₁, K₂ (but Heegner points from
   different K's are NOT independent in general)
2. Diagonal cycles on triple products of modular curves (Darmon-Rotger)
3. K-theoretic elements on higher-dimensional Shimura varieties
4. Something completely new

## Comparison with Other Millennium Problems

| Problem | Wall type | Structural? |
|---------|----------|-------------|
| NS | One inequality at max ω | Quantitative |
| YM | GC > 0 (gradient correlation) | Quantitative (closable?) |
| RH | No framework exists | Conceptual |
| **BSD** | **One point → two points** | **Structural (new math needed)** |
| Poincaré | Entropy formula | Conceptual (found by Perelman) |

BSD's wall is similar to RH: it likely requires fundamentally new
mathematics, not just better estimates. The construction of rank-2
Euler systems or second-order Gross-Zagier formulas would be a
revolutionary advance in arithmetic geometry.
