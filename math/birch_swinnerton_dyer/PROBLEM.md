# Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture

## Statement
For an elliptic curve E over Q, the rank of E(Q) equals the order of vanishing of L(E,s) at s = 1.

## Status: OPEN (Clay Millennium Prize, $1M)

## Key Objects
- E: elliptic curve y² = x³ + ax + b over Q
- E(Q): group of rational points (finitely generated by Mordell)
- rank(E): rank of the free part of E(Q)
- L(E,s): Hasse-Weil L-function = Π_p L_p(s)
- L_p(s) = (1 - a_p p^{-s} + p^{1-2s})^{-1} for good primes
- Ш(E): Tate-Shafarevich group (conjectured finite)

## Full BSD
- ord_{s=1} L(E,s) = rank(E)
- Leading coefficient: lim_{s→1} L(E,s)/(s-1)^r = (Ω · Reg · |Ш| · Π c_p) / |E(Q)_tors|²

## Known Results
- True for rank 0 and 1 (Gross-Zagier, Kolyvagin, 1986-1990)
- Modularity theorem (Wiles et al.): every E/Q is modular
- Parity conjecture: partial results via root number
- Goldfeld conjecture: average rank ≤ 1/2 (partial: Bhargava-Shankar)
- p-adic BSD: Kato, Skinner-Urban

## Approaches
- Iwasawa theory (main conjecture → BSD)
- Euler systems (Kolyvagin, Kato)
- Automorphic forms (Langlands program)
- Arithmetic geometry (heights, Néron models)
- Computational: extensive databases (Cremona, LMFDB)
