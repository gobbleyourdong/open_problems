# arxiv 2510.22788 — U(N) lattice Yang-Mills in the 't Hooft regime

> Author: Nissim (2025)
> Status: Strong-coupling result. NOT relevant to millennium prize.

## Result
Mass gap + unique infinite volume limit + large N factorization for U(N) lattice YM in d ≥ 2, at strong coupling (β < β̄(d)).

## Technique
U(1) × SU(N) decomposition. U(1) sector: cluster expansion (Osterwalder-Seiler style). SU(N) sector: Langevin dynamics + Bakry-Emery curvature criterion (Shen-Zhu-Zhu 2023).

## Why not relevant to us
- Strong coupling only (small β) — the "easy" regime where mass gap has been known since OS 1978
- Does NOT address weak coupling / continuum limit (β → ∞)
- Techniques (Bakry-Emery, Langevin mixing) are orthogonal to our approach (Tomboulis, FKG, MK decimation)
- The millennium prize requires the continuum limit, not just lattice strong coupling
