## The Question

Does the full pressure make the Vieillefosse alignment (ω → e₁) UNSTABLE?
If yes with rate > eigenvalue cubic: the proof closes.

## The Answer from Literature

YES, pressure prevents the Vieillefosse singularity. But:

1. NO explicit instability RATE has been measured or proven.

2. The pressure Hessian COUNTERACTS the restricted Euler singularity
   on the right Vieillefosse branch (all models agree).

3. Vorticity aligns with the THIRD eigenvector of the pressure Hessian
   (smallest eigenvalue ≈ 0). This is DIFFERENT from e₂ of strain.

4. Closure models (Wilczek-Meneveau 2014, Chevillard-Meneveau 2006)
   become UNSTABLE when the pressure model is incomplete — suggesting
   the EXACT pressure is what stabilizes the flow.

5. The RFD closure becomes unstable at HIGH Reynolds numbers when
   the decorrelation timescale is too short. This is a TIME-SCALE
   issue, not a structural one.

## What This Means for Our Gap

The Vieillefosse zone (α ∈ [0.35|ω|, 0.5|ω|]) has:
- ω near e₁ (stretching eigenvector)
- The restricted Euler WANTS to blow up here
- The pressure PREVENTS blowup (confirmed by all models)
- But the quantitative RATE of pressure prevention is unknown

Our proof needs: the tilting from -Ω² + pressure > eigenvalue cubic
in this zone. The literature says pressure helps but doesn't quantify it.

## The Remaining Path

Option 1: PROVE the tilting rate in the Vieillefosse zone algebraically.
  The -Ω² contribution is algebraic (|ω|²/4 × geometric factor).
  The eigenvalue cubic is algebraic (≤ 0.19|S|³).
  If the algebraic -Ω² tilting in this zone > 0.19|S|³: done.

Option 2: Use the CKN + viscous smoothing argument (file 220A).
  For NS: near blowup, viscous smoothing → Gaussian → S=0 at center.
  This bypasses the Vieillefosse zone entirely.

Option 3: Prove the e₁ alignment is UNSTABLE under full Euler.
  Nobody has done this. Would be a NEW result.
