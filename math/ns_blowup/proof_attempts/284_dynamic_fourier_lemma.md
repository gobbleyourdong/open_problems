## Why Df/Dt Has Positive z-Cosine When α > 0

Df/Dt = D(|ω|²/2 - |S|²)/Dt.

Leading term: D(|ω|²/2)/Dt = |ω|²α.

At the max of |ω| (z=0):
  |ω|² has a z-maximum at z=0 (by definition — it's the max).
  α > 0 at z=0 (the stretching case).

CLAIM: |ω|²α has a z-maximum at z=0.

If α also peaks near z=0: the product peaks at z=0.
The k-th z-cosine of a function peaking at z=0 is POSITIVE
for the fundamental mode (same argument as file 267):

  (|ω|²α)_k^c = (1/π)∫ |ω(z)|²α(z)cos(kz)dz > 0

This is positive because the integrand is concentrated near z=0
where cos(kz) = 1, and small elsewhere where cos oscillates.

## The One Gap: α Peaks at z=0

Why does α peak near the z-maximum of |ω|²?

PHYSICAL REASON: The stretching rate α = ê·S·ê depends on the
local strain S, which is generated by the local vorticity through
Biot-Savart. Where |ω| is largest: |S| is largest (from the attractor
|S| = |ω|/2). And where |S| is largest: α can be largest.

FORMAL GAP: α = ê·S·ê depends on the ALIGNMENT (cᵢ), not just |S|.
The alignment could be different at z=0 vs nearby. If α peaks at
z ≠ 0 (a different z-location): the product |ω|²α might not have
a clean z-cosine structure.

FROM DATA: α at the max-|ω| point ≈ 2.5, while α at other high-|ω|
points nearby ≈ 1-2. So α DOES peak at the max. But not proven.
