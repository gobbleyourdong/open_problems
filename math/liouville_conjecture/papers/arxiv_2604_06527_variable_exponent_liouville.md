# arxiv 2604.06527 — Liouville Above the Critical 9/2 Threshold (April 7, 2026)

> Author: Vergara-Hermosilla
> Status: BRAND NEW (days old). Steady solutions only.

## Main result
Smooth stationary NS solutions in variable-exponent L^{9/2+ε(x)} are trivial,
where ε(x) > 0 can decay like 1/|x|. Breaks Galdi's L^{9/2} threshold.
Key insight: no integrability needed inside any compact ball — only tail behavior matters.

## Technique
Variable-exponent Lebesgue spaces L^{p(x)}. Exponent transitions p=6 near
origin → p → 9/2+ at infinity. Energy testing with cutoffs + Hölder in
variable-exponent spaces.

## Steady only
Purely stationary. No ancient solutions, no time variable. Intrinsically elliptic.

## Relevance to our campaign
Zero technical overlap. Philosophically parallel: both isolate the "dangerous
regime" (theirs at spatial ∞, ours at t → -∞) and show rigidity propagates.
But elliptic tools don't transfer to parabolic ancient solutions.
