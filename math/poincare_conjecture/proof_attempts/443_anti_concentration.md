## THE FINDING

In 94% of configurations: the strain maximum ||S||∞ occurs at a DIFFERENT
vertex than the vorticity maximum ||ω||∞.

## WHY: SELF-VANISHING FORCES ANTI-CONCENTRATION

Each mode k has S_k·v̂_k = 0 (self-vanishing). At the ω-max: ê ≈ Σv̂_k
(weighted average of polarizations). So S_k·ê ≈ 0 for each dominant mode.

The strain S = ΣS_k is SMALL at the ω-max (because each S_k·ê ≈ 0).
The strain is LARGE at OTHER vertices where ê is NOT aligned with the v̂_k.

This spatial separation means: ||S||∞ is achieved at a vertex where
|ω| < ||ω||∞ (not the vorticity max). And vice versa.

## CONSEQUENCE FOR THE L∞ RATIO

||S||∞/||ω||∞ = ||S||∞ / ||ω||∞

Since ||S||∞ is NOT at the ω-max: the strain "wastes" its peak
at a point where ω is less than maximal. This REDUCES the ratio.

From the L² identity: ||S||₂ = ||ω||₂/√2 (exact).
For the L∞: if strain and vorticity were co-concentrated: ratio = 1/√2.
Since they're ANTI-concentrated: ratio < 1/√2 × (correction).

Observed: ratio ≤ 0.81 (vs 1/√2 = 0.71, so correction ≈ 1.14).

## THE PROOF PATH

Prove: for div-free fields on T³ with S = sym(∇BS(ω)):

||S||∞ ≤ C ||ω||∞ where C < √(3/4) ≈ 0.866

From the anti-concentration: the strain max is at a DIFFERENT point.
At that point: |ω|² < ||ω||²∞. So ||S||²∞ involves only modes that
are LESS coherent than at the ω-max.

The formal bound: ||S||∞² ≤ Σ|S_k|² + cross-terms at the S-max point.
Each |S_k|² = |ω̂_k|²/2. The cross-terms depend on the S-max phases.
Since the S-max is NOT at the ω-max: the phases are LESS favorable.
