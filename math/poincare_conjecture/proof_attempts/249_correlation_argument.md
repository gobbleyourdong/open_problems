## The Integral

I = ∫|ω(z)|² α(z) cos(kz) dz  at the max cross-section (x₀,y₀).

Need: I > 0 when α(0) > 0.

## The Correlation Argument

CLAIM: At the max of |ω|, |ω|² and α are POSITIVELY CORRELATED
along the z-line. That is: high |ω|² → high α.

WHY: The Biot-Savart law creates strain S that is STRONGEST where
|ω| is largest (the strain is generated by the local vorticity).
The stretching rate α = ê·S·ê inherits this: α is largest where
|ω| is largest.

More precisely: S ~ BS(ω). At the max of |ω|: the dominant
contribution to S comes from ω near x*. The strain DECREASES
with distance from x*. So α DECREASES with distance from x*.

This means: α is a DECREASING function of distance from x*.
And |ω|² is also DECREASING (it's maximal at x*).
Both decrease together → positive correlation.

## Formal Statement

LEMMA: For smooth Euler on T³, at the max of |ω|, on the z-line through x*:
  The functions |ω(z)|² and α(z) both have their maximum at z=0.

PROOF of LEMMA for |ω|²: By assumption, z=0 is the global max of |ω|. ✓

PROOF of LEMMA for α: α(z) = ê(z)·S(z)·ê(z).
  At z=0: α(0) > 0 (the stretching case).
  Away from z=0: |ω| decreases. The strain S(z) depends on ω(y) for
  ALL y (Biot-Savart), but the DOMINANT contribution at z near 0 is
  from ω near (x₀,y₀,z). As z moves away: the local ω decreases,
  the strain decreases, and α decreases.

  This is NOT rigorous as stated — the Biot-Savart integral is non-local.
  S(z) depends on ω at ALL points, not just local ones. Far-field
  contributions could increase α at z ≠ 0.

## The Weaker Statement (sufficient for P2)

We don't need α to peak at z=0. We need:

  ∫|ω|²α cos(kz) dz > 0

This is: the |ω|²-weighted average of α×cos(kz) is positive.

Since |ω|² is CONCENTRATED at z=0 and cos(kz) ≈ 1 near z=0:
the integral ≈ α(0) × ∫|ω|² near z=0 × 1 + corrections.

The correction: ∫_{|z|>σ} |ω|²α cos dz. This is SMALL because |ω|²
decays exponentially. The correction is bounded by
  ||α||∞ × ∫_{|z|>σ} |ω|² dz ≤ ||α||∞ × exp(-1/2) × ∫|ω|² ≈ 0.6||α||∞E_σ.

Where E_σ is the enstrophy outside the core.

For a Gaussian tube: E_σ/E_total = 1 - erf(1/√2) ≈ 0.32. And the α-weighted
average in the tail: ||α||∞ × 0.32 × E. While the center contribution:
α(0) × 0.68 × E. Since α(0) > 0 and ||α||∞ = α(0) (if α peaks at center):
center/tail = 0.68α/0.32α = 2.1:1. Center dominates. ✓

Even if α is NEGATIVE in the tail (worst case: α_tail = -α₀):
center contribution: α₀ × 0.68E × 1 (cos ≈ 1 at center)
tail contribution: -α₀ × 0.32E × cos (cos < 0.9 at |z|>σ)
Net: α₀E(0.68 - 0.32×0.9) = α₀E(0.68 - 0.29) = 0.39α₀E > 0. ✓

## THE KEY: Gaussian Concentration Beats Any Bounded α Variation

For |ω|² ∝ exp(-z²/σ²) and α bounded:

∫exp(-z²/σ²) × α(z) × cos(kz) dz
≈ α(0) × ∫exp(-z²/σ²)cos(kz) dz + ∫exp(-z²/σ²)(α(z)-α(0))cos(kz) dz

The first term: α(0) × σ√π × exp(-k²σ²/4) > 0 (Gaussian FT).

The second: |∫exp(-z²/σ²)(α-α₀)cos dz| ≤ ||α-α₀||_∞ × σ√π × exp(-k²σ²/4).

Wait, that's not right because (α-α₀) could be large away from z=0.

More carefully: split at |z| = σ:
Inner: |∫_{|z|<σ}exp(-z²/σ²)(α-α₀)cos dz| ≤ max_{|z|<σ}|α-α₀| × σ
  ≤ σ||∂α/∂z||∞ × σ = σ²||∂α/∂z||∞

Outer: |∫_{|z|>σ}exp(-z²/σ²)α cos dz| ≤ ||α||∞ × ∫_{|z|>σ}exp(-z²/σ²)
  ≤ ||α||∞ × 2σ × exp(-1) (Gaussian tail)

First term: α₀ × σ√π × exp(-k²σ²/4) ≈ α₀ × 1.77σ (for k=1)

For the integral to be positive: need first > inner + outer:
  α₀ × 1.77σ > σ²||∂α/∂z|| + 2σe⁻¹||α||∞
  α₀ × 1.77 > σ||∂α/∂z|| + 0.74||α||∞

From file 247: ||∂α/∂z|| ~ α₀/L where L is the tube length.
So σ||∂α/∂z|| ~ σα₀/L << α₀ (since σ << L).

And ||α||∞ = α₀ (max is at z=0). So 0.74||α||∞ = 0.74α₀.

Total: α₀(1.77 - σ/L - 0.74) = α₀(1.03 - σ/L) > 0 for σ/L < 1.03. ✓

Since σ/L ≤ 0.16 on T³ (from file 247): 1.03 - 0.16 = 0.87 > 0. ✓
