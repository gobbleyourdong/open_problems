## Why the Testes Have the Lowest LAMP2 in the Body

Sertoli cells are post-mitotic, long-lived cells that support spermatogenesis for decades. They evolved to minimize bulk autophagy to protect their complex cytoplasmic architecture. The blood-testis barrier (BTB) similarly evolved to exclude immune cells — not just for immune privilege but to prevent immune-mediated destruction of developing spermatids (which transiently express foreign antigens as meiosis proceeds).

CVB exploits BOTH protections:
1. BTB blocks immune cells → CVB hides from adaptive immunity
2. Low LAMP2 → autophagy cannot clear viral replication complexes even when initiated

This is why the testes are the LAST organ to clear in the unified model — not primarily because of the BTB, but because of the lowest LAMP2 in the body.

## What the Wall Actually Is

The wall for orchitis is **the unknown unknown**: most male CVB patients don't know they have testicular CVB infection. The testes are asymptomatic in persistence (acute orchitis causes pain; TD mutant persistence does not). Without CVB serology + semen PCR, the reservoir is invisible.

**The wall is**:
1. Male patients on the protocol are told "12-18 months" — but at 20mg fluoxetine without trehalose, testicular clearance actually takes 3.5 years. They may stop the protocol after 18 months thinking they're clear when the testicular reservoir is still active.
2. The testicular reservoir, once reactivated (e.g., from illness, stress, immunosuppression), could reseed other organs. If a patient "cured" of T1DM relapses 3 years later, orchitis may be why.

**Breaking the wall**:
1. All male CVB patients: semen PCR baseline + 60mg fluoxetine + trehalose 3g
2. Protocol duration: biomarker-guided (seminal PCR), not time-fixed
3. Testosterone/FSH monitoring at 6-month intervals as a proxy for testicular CVB status
