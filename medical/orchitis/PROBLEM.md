# Viral Orchitis (Coxsackievirus B)

## The Problem
Testicular inflammation caused by CVB infection. CVB is the second most common viral cause of orchitis after mumps. Primarily CVB5, also B2 and B4. Can cause testicular pain, swelling, and in severe cases subfertility or infertility.

## Status: USUALLY SELF-LIMITING (but fertility implications)

## Key Biology

| Component | What | Role |
|-----------|------|------|
| Sertoli cells | Testicular support cells | Express CAR receptor, CVB entry point |
| Leydig cells | Testosterone-producing cells | Can be damaged → hormonal effects |
| Blood-testis barrier | Immunologically privileged site | CVB can persist in testes (immune sanctuary) |
| CVB5 | Primary causative serotype | Testicular tropism |

## Connection to T1DM
- Testes are immunologically privileged (like pancreatic islets) — CVB can persist
- Persistent testicular CVB = potential reservoir for reinfection of other organs
- T1DM male patients should consider testosterone/fertility screening if symptomatic
- Same antiviral protocol applies — clearing CVB from ALL reservoirs matters
