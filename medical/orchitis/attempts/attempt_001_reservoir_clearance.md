# Attempt 001: Testicular Reservoir Clearance — Systemic Protocol Adequacy

## Approach
Determine whether the T1DM protocol achieves sufficient testicular drug penetration to clear CVB from the immune-privileged testicular compartment. If not, the testes reseed other organs after clearance — defeating the entire protocol.

## Pharmacokinetic Analysis

### Fluoxetine
- Volume of distribution: 20-45 L/kg (HUGE — distributes to all tissues)
- Highly lipophilic → crosses blood-testis barrier (BTB)
- CSF/plasma ratio: 0.5-0.8 → CNS penetration confirmed
- **Testicular penetration: EXPECTED** based on Vd and lipophilicity
- Literature: fluoxetine detected in seminal fluid at therapeutic concentrations
- **VERDICT: adequate for testicular CVB clearance**

### Itraconazole
- Volume of distribution: 11 L/kg (moderate)
- Highly protein-bound (99.8%) → limits free drug in privileged sites
- Poor CNS penetration (does not cross BBB well)
- **Testicular penetration: POOR** — high protein binding limits free drug behind BTB
- **VERDICT: insufficient for testicular CVB clearance as monotherapy**

### Autophagy (FMD-induced)
- Autophagy is a cellular process — acts in ALL cells regardless of compartment barriers
- If Sertoli cells or spermatogonia undergo autophagy, they clear intracellular TD mutants
- FMD triggers systemic autophagy including in testes
- **VERDICT: effective regardless of drug penetration**

## The Protocol Assessment

| Component | Testicular penetration | CVB clearance in testes |
|-----------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Fluoxetine | YES (high Vd, lipophilic) | YES — 2C ATPase block |
| Itraconazole | NO (protein-bound) | NO — can't reach virus |
| FMD/autophagy | N/A (cellular process) | YES — clears TD mutants |
| WHM cold exposure | N/A (systemic cytokines) | MAYBE — IL-10 is anti-inflammatory |
| BHB/ketones | YES (crosses all barriers) | YES — NLRP3 suppression |

**3 of 5 protocol components reach the testes. This should be sufficient.**

## The Diagnostic Question
Before worrying about testicular clearance, confirm testicular CVB persistence exists:

### In male T1DM patients
- Testosterone (total + free) — low T suggests Leydig cell damage
- FSH — elevated suggests Sertoli cell damage  
- Inhibin B — direct Sertoli cell function marker
- Testicular ultrasound — inflammation, atrophy
- If abnormal: semen analysis with enteroviral RT-PCR in seminal fluid

### Expected findings (if hypothesis correct)
- Male T1DM patients have lower testosterone and higher FSH than age-matched controls
- Enteroviral RNA detectable in seminal fluid of a subset
- This would confirm testes as CVB reservoir

## The Fertility Implication
If CVB persists in testes:
- Subfertility in male T1DM patients may be underrecognized
- CVB in semen → potential sexual transmission (documented for Zika, HIV — unknown for CVB)
- Clearance protocol may restore fertility as a secondary benefit
