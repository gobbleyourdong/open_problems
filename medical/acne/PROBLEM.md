# Acne Vulgaris

## The Problem

Chronic inflammatory pilosebaceous disorder with characteristic comedones, papules, pustules, and — in severe cases — nodules, cysts, and scarring. Affects ~85% of adolescents; 20-40% carry it into adulthood with variable severity. Female-predominant in adult persistence. Often comorbid with PCOS, insulin resistance, and the broader sebum-spectrum inflammatory dermatosis cluster (seborrheic dermatitis, rosacea, perioral dermatitis).

## Status: TREATABLE, MECHANISM KNOWN, UNDERAPPRECIATED AS DYSBIOSIS

Acne is well-understood mechanically — hormonal sebum surge + follicular hyperkeratinization + *C. acnes* overgrowth + innate-immune inflammation. The gap is not therapeutic (isotretinoin works; topical retinoids work; antibiotics work short-term) — the gap is that acne is **not routinely framed as a site-specific instance of the broader sebum-spectrum dysbiosis** (M5 substrate × M2 community × M4 threshold), even though its mechanism maps cleanly onto the dysbiosis framework.

## The Disease Cascade (Dysbiosis-Aligned)

1. **Genetic susceptibility**: heritability ~80% (twin studies). Overlap with rosacea (shared HLA + IL-1 family variants) and PCOS (shared IGF-1 axis)
2. **Substrate surge** (M5): pubertal androgens + Western diet insulin/IGF-1 elevation → sebum output up
3. **Substrate composition shift** (M5): altered sebum fatty acid profile favors C. acnes biofilm and keratinocyte differentiation abnormalities
4. **Community selection** (M2): C. acnes phylotype IA1 outcompetes other phylotypes in hyperseborrheic follicles; Demodex and Malassezia can secondarily colonize
5. **Follicular blockage**: keratin plug + sebum retention → anaerobic closed environment favoring C. acnes
6. **Innate immune activation** (M4): C. acnes biofilm → TLR2 → NLRP3 → IL-1β → neutrophil recruitment → papule/pustule
7. **Scarring**: sustained inflammation + MMP-9 activity → dermal remodeling

## Differential Diagnosis

| Condition | Distinguishing feature |
|-----------|------------------------|
| Perioral dermatitis | Spares vermillion border; papules perioral/nasolabial; no comedones |
| Rosacea | Adult-onset centrofacial erythema; telangiectasias; flushing; no comedones |
| Pityrosporum folliculitis | Uniform monomorphic follicular papules; itchy; responds only to antifungal |
| Demodex folliculitis ("Demodex acne") | Adult, often female; refractory to standard acne therapy; responds to ivermectin topical |
| Hidradenitis suppurativa | Apocrine gland; axillary/inguinal; abscesses and sinus tracts |
| Keratosis pilaris | Extensor surfaces (arms/thighs); non-inflammatory in classic form |

## Known Treatment Tiers

### Tier 1 — Topical (mild-moderate)
- **Benzoyl peroxide 2.5-10%** — bactericidal vs C. acnes, no resistance development
- **Topical retinoids** (adapalene, tretinoin) — normalize keratinization, anti-inflammatory
- **Azelaic acid 15-20%** — anti-inflammatory + mild keratolytic + anti-C.-acnes (same agent as rosacea)
- **Topical clindamycin** (usually + BPO to prevent resistance)

### Tier 2 — Systemic (moderate-severe)
- **Oral doxycycline / minocycline** — sub-antimicrobial dose for anti-inflammatory effect
- **Oral spironolactone** (females) — androgen receptor blockade
- **Combined oral contraceptive** (females) — SHBG up → free androgen down
- **Metformin** — if PCOS or insulin resistance present; reduces sebum via IGF-1 axis

### Tier 3 — Nuclear option
- **Isotretinoin (Accutane)** — permanent sebaceous gland atrophy (~70% size reduction); effectively removes M5 substrate. Teratogenic (pregnancy registry required). **Validates the dysbiosis framework's substrate-starve prediction**: remove substrate → community starves → disease resolves.

### Experimental / mechanistic
- **Anti-IL-17** (in trials) — direct M4 threshold modulation
- **Topical clascoterone** (anti-androgen without systemic effect) — M5 hormonal axis, localized
- **Probiotic / postbiotic topicals** — early data, community modulation

## Connection to the Dysbiosis Framework

Acne fits cleanly as a site-specific instance of the broader dysbiosis mechanism. What acne uniquely demonstrates:

- **M5 substrate-starve via Accutane** is the cleanest empirical validation of the framework — ~70% permanent sebum reduction produces sustained disease remission. This is the strongest "starve the substrate → resolve the community disease" evidence in dermatology.
- **Genetic overlap with rosacea + PCOS + metabolic syndrome** supports the shared-M5-genetics hypothesis (IGF-1/insulin axis as a cross-disease risk amplifier).
- **Dietary intervention evidence is strongest here** (Kitavan study + low-GI RCTs) — provides M5 intervention validation that extends to other sebum-spectrum diseases.
- **Strain-level community selection (C. acnes phylotypes)** gives acne as the clearest case of "same commensal, different phylotype, different disease outcome" — relevant to the M2 host-threshold specificity question.

## Open Questions

1. Why is acne comorbid with PCOS at ~70% but adult rosacea at only ~3-5%? Shared IGF-1 axis but different site tropism — what determines?
2. Does topical clascoterone (localized anti-androgen) cure without systemic effects?
3. Is the "Demodex acne" variant (adult female, ivermectin-responsive) actually a distinct disease, or just late-onset rosacea with comedonal features?
4. Does early isotretinoin (in severe teen acne) reduce lifetime risk of rosacea / ocular rosacea / chalazion? Permanent sebum-gland reduction should prevent downstream sebum-spectrum diseases — untested prospectively.
5. Does gut dysbiosis (M1) contribute independently? Probiotic RCTs for acne show mixed results; the mechanism might route through IGF-1 rather than direct gut-skin axis.
