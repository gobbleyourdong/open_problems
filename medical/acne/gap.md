# Acne — Mechanistic Gaps

## The Core Gap: Why Some People Scar and Others Don't

Acne severity and scarring propensity vary widely within the same genetic/dietary context. Why?

Candidate drivers (not yet resolved):
1. **C. acnes phylotype inheritance** — phylotype IA1 is the "acne-associated" lineage; vertical transmission from mother may set lifelong susceptibility
2. **NLRP3 / IL-1β polymorphisms** — gain-of-function variants → more inflammation per unit biofilm
3. **MMP-9 / MMP-1 expression variants** — determines scarring vs healing
4. **Skin Treg density** — M4 threshold variation manifesting in a specific tissue
5. **Healing-phase cytokine timing** (TGF-β vs IL-17) — inflammation-to-resolution transition

## Gap: Dietary Intervention Resistance

Despite Kitavan and low-GI RCT evidence, dietary intervention for acne has poor real-world compliance and modest clinical-trial effect sizes vs. pharmaceutical interventions. The gap is behavioral (compliance) OR biological (the effect is modest even with perfect compliance) — not clearly resolved.

## Gap: The "Demodex Acne" Variant

Adult female Demodex-folliculitis presenting clinically as acne and responding to ivermectin is increasingly recognized but not standardized diagnostically. Gaps:
- How prevalent is it in the "adult acne" population?
- Is it a distinct disease or overlapping with rosacea papulopustular subtype?
- Does standard acne therapy (BPO, retinoids) ever work, or does it require anti-Demodex specifically?
- Is there a family-history pattern (e.g., does Demodex transmission from mother explain clustering)?

## Gap: Accutane's Long-Term Sequelae

Isotretinoin permanently shrinks sebaceous glands — cures acne, but what else does it do long-term?

Known post-Accutane patterns:
- **Dry eye / MGD** (meibomian glands are sebaceous — they also shrink) → ocular rosacea / chalazion risk up
- **Dry skin** lifelong
- **Possible mood effects** (controversial; recent meta-analyses say no average effect but individual variance exists)
- **Reduced lifetime risk of rosacea?** — untested but predicted by the framework

Open: does post-Accutane cohort have LOWER rosacea / chalazion / seb derm incidence because their sebaceous substrate is permanently suppressed? If yes, that's a natural experiment validating the M5 substrate-starve hypothesis across the sebum-spectrum.

## Gap: Hormonal Axis vs Gut Axis in Adult Female Acne

Adult female acne responds to BOTH:
- Spironolactone / anti-androgen (M5 hormonal)
- Probiotic / gut intervention (M1 → M5 via IGF-1)

Unclear which is dominant for which patient. Biomarker panel (SHBG + free testosterone + IGF-1 + gut metagenomics) might stratify — but not clinically done.

## Gap: Pediatric Early-Onset Acne as a Predictor

Acne onset before age 9 ("preadolescent acne") is associated with severe adult acne AND higher rates of PCOS / metabolic syndrome. Is this:
- An early M5 substrate surge (premature pubarche)?
- An M6 early-life Treg floor deficit visible as early inflammation?
- A hormonal axis disorder masquerading as dermatology?

Pediatric endocrinology referral is indicated but dermatologists often don't make it.

## Testable Predictions

If the framework mapping is correct:
- **Prediction 1**: Accutane-treated cohorts should show lower lifetime rosacea + ocular rosacea + chalazion incidence vs acne-severity-matched controls not treated with Accutane. Natural experiment available in insurance claim data.
- **Prediction 2**: GLP-1R agonists (semaglutide, tirzepatide) for weight loss should incidentally improve acne in subjects with BMI elevation — via IGF-1 reduction. Anecdotally reported; should be RCT-testable.
- **Prediction 3**: Maternal C. acnes phylotype should predict offspring acne severity. Untested directly; requires longitudinal mother-child microbiome cohort.
- **Prediction 4**: Berberine (GSK3β inhibitor → FOXP3 stabilizer) should improve acne severity in high-insulin-resistance patients. Small trials exist; framework predicts effect size correlates with baseline HOMA-IR.

## Status

Acne is well-understood mechanically, treatable across a ladder, and a clean fit to the dysbiosis framework. Sigma-method scaffolding is for framework-completeness and cross-pollination target, not for cracking a hard open problem. Priority: MEDIUM.
