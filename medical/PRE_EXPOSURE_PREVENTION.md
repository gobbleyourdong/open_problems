# Pre-Exposure CVB Prevention — Who Should Take the Protocol Now

> This document is for people who DON'T have T1DM, ME/CFS, or any CVB disease yet, but are at elevated risk. The goal: maintain the non-progressor phenotype pharmacologically before CVB exposure.

## Who Is at Elevated Risk?

### Tier A: High Priority (start protocol now)

| Risk Factor | Why | CVB Diseases to Prevent |
|-------------|-----|------------------------|
| HLA-DR3 or DR4 positive | 8-12× elevated T1DM risk; these alleles drive FOXP1-mediated autoimmunity post-CVB | T1DM, Hashimoto's thyroiditis |
| First-degree relative with T1DM | ~15× elevated risk vs general population | T1DM |
| First-degree relative with ME/CFS or DCM | Shared CVB susceptibility factors | ME/CFS, DCM |
| Pregnant woman (especially 2nd/3rd trimester) | Neonate protection via transplacental IgG; own protection during immunologically altered pregnancy | Neonatal sepsis prevention (for baby), pericarditis (for mother) |
| Healthcare worker (pediatric/neonatal units) | High CVB3/B5 exposure risk from patients | Myocarditis, pericarditis |
| Partner/family member of active CVB protocol patient | Potential fecal-oral CVB transmission in household | All CVB diseases |

### Tier B: Moderate Priority (consider protocol)

| Risk Factor | Why | CVB Diseases to Consider |
|-------------|-----|------------------------|
| Type 2 diabetes (T2DM) | Some T2DM cases may have autoimmune component; gut enterocyte CVB risk | Improved gut health, reduced residual insulin resistance inflammation |
| Celiac disease (diagnosed) | T1DM co-occurrence 5-10%; shared CVB mechanism | T1DM prevention if not already present |
| Inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn's/UC) | NLRP3, gut dysbiosis, Treg insufficiency | IBD improvement (co-beneficiary) |
| Autoimmune thyroid disease (Hashimoto's) | Same CVB-FOXP1 mechanism; co-occurs with T1DM | T1DM prevention |
| History of pericarditis (any cause) | CVB persistence may drive recurrence | Pericarditis recurrence prevention |
| History of any viral myocarditis | Residual cardiac CVB may persist | DCM progression prevention |
| Chronic fatigue after any viral illness | Pre-ME/CFS state; TD mutants may be forming | ME/CFS prevention |
| Family history of Parkinson's disease | LAMP2 neuronal pathway; CVB may accelerate | Parkinson's risk reduction |

### Tier C: General Health (anyone can benefit)

People without specific CVB risk who want to optimize:
- The "non-progressor phenotype" is simply optimal immune function, gut health, and mitochondrial reserve
- The pre-exposure protocol is essentially high-quality prevention supplementation for any post-viral syndrome

**What this is NOT**:

## Special Case: Pregnant Women at Risk

If pregnant and HLA-DR3/DR4 positive (or previous child has T1DM):

| Trimester | Additional steps |
|-----------|----------------|
| All | Continue butyrate 4-6g, VitD 5000 IU, omega-3 3g (all safe in pregnancy) |
| First | Avoid any viral exposure; hand hygiene (CVB fecal-oral) |
| Second | CVB IgG testing (if seronegative: consider passive IgG or early maternal vaccine protocol if available) |
| Third | Same + request CVB PCR for neonate if acute illness develops |

**Trehalose in pregnancy**: safety data limited (it's naturally occurring in foods, not pharmaceutically dosed; discuss with OB). CoQ10 at 200mg is used in IVF protocols for oocyte quality — generally considered safe.

## Special Case: Post-Viral Fatigue (Pre-ME/CFS State)

For anyone with persistent fatigue after a viral illness (any virus), the pre-exposure protocol becomes the TREATMENT protocol for the early window:

If within 8 weeks of symptom onset after a viral illness:
1. Add fluoxetine 20mg/day (for CVB coverage)
2. Increase trehalose to 3g/day (LAMP2 window still open)
3. First FMD at week 4
4. Standard pre-exposure supplements

If beyond 8 weeks: this is now established ME/CFS territory → full treatment protocol.

## The Screening Addition for HLA-DR3/DR4 Individuals

If starting the pre-exposure protocol due to HLA risk:
1. Check islet autoantibodies at baseline (GAD65, IA-2, ZnT8, IAA)
2. Check anti-TPO + TSH (thyroid co-risk)
3. Recheck islet autoantibodies annually
4. If autoantibodies become positive: IMMEDIATELY escalate to full treatment protocol (islet autoantibodies = T1DM is coming within 2-10 years)

## What to Tell Your Doctor About the Pre-Exposure Protocol

"I'm at elevated genetic risk for T1DM. I'm starting a supplementation protocol targeting the immune mechanisms (FOXP1/Treg axis, mitochondrial reserve, lysosomal function) that determine whether a CVB infection progresses to autoimmune disease. Everything I'm taking is OTC and within safe dosing ranges. I'd like you to check my islet autoantibodies and thyroid antibodies at baseline and annually."

This is a simple, defensible request at any primary care visit.
