# Viral Hepatitis — THE WALL

## One Sentence

The wall for hepatitis is the easiest wall in the campaign to cross: the liver clears CVB FIRST (weeks, not months), and the rising then falling ALT that patients and doctors will see in weeks 1-4 of the protocol is not drug toxicity — it is the liver doing its job.

## Why Liver First?

The LAMP2 unified theory (attempt_080 + hepatitis/attempt_003) explains why:

1. **Hepatocyte LAMP2 baseline = 4.0× average** — the highest in the body. After CVB suppression (-2.7×): κ_effective_liver = 4.0/2.7 ≈ 1.5 (ABOVE average, not below). The LAMP2 block actually IMPROVES the liver's comparative position vs other organs.

2. **Kupffer cell backup**: Kupffer cells (liver-resident macrophages, ~80% of total body macrophages) clear extracellular virus by phagocytosis — a LAMP2-independent pathway. The liver has two clearance mechanisms; other organs have one.

3. **Fluoxetine first-pass**: 20mg oral fluoxetine achieves ~10× plasma concentration in liver. At 0.3 μM plasma, liver concentration = 3 μM >> IC50. Highest drug concentration at the most important clearance site.

**Combined result**: liver clears CVB in ~1.3 months (vs the nominal 2.5 months — LAMP2 baseline advantage actually makes it faster). This is the gatekeeper clearing first.

## The ALT Story — What to Expect

The "liver-first clearance" prediction has a specific, testable temporal sequence:

```
Week 1-2: Fluoxetine reaches hepatic first-pass concentrations
          WT CVB in liver encounters 3 μM fluoxetine >> IC50
          Kupffer cell activation → processing CVB particles
          ALT RISES (hepatocyte stress from immune activation and viral clearance)
          → This is a GOOD sign, not drug toxicity

Week 2-3: WT CVB largely eliminated from liver
          ALT peaks and begins to fall
          
Week 3-4: Hepatic TD mutants enter autophagy-mediated clearance phase
           (κ_liver = 1.5 → high efficiency → faster than any other organ)
           ALT normalizes

Week 4-6: Liver viral reservoir significantly depleted
           Reduced seeding into systemic circulation
           CRP begins to fall (systemic inflammation declining)
           
Month 2-3: Other organs begin clearing sequentially
            (pericardium, then heart, then gut/pancreas...)
```

**The testable prediction**: ALT peaks at weeks 1-2, normalizes by week 4, BEFORE CRP falls. If ALT rises AFTER week 4 or stays elevated without normalizing, that's when to investigate (drug toxicity vs heavy hepatic infection).

## The Dual-Meaning ALT

ALT/AST elevation during the protocol has three possible meanings:
1. **Kupffer cell activation clearing CVB** (good — protocol working)
2. **Fluoxetine hepatotoxicity** (rare but possible — check dose)
3. **Active CVB hepatitis** (active viral replication in hepatocytes — need to distinguish)

**How to distinguish**:
- Hepatotoxicity: ALT rises progressively, doesn't correlate with CRP or viral markers
- Viral clearance response: ALT rises weeks 1-2, stabilizes, then falls (timeline-consistent)
- Active CVB hepatitis: ALT rises with fever, malaise, markedly elevated CRP (acute illness pattern)

**Decision rule**: if ALT < 3× ULN and rising in weeks 1-4, monitor. If ALT > 3× ULN or still rising at week 6, stop fluoxetine and investigate.

## The Gatekeeper Significance

The liver is not just the fastest-clearing organ for the patient's benefit — it is the body's primary CVB amplifier. Every CVB particle absorbed from the gut passes through the liver via the portal vein. Kupffer cells are the first line of defense. If they fail: hepatocytes amplify viral load → systemic seeding → T1DM, DCM, ME/CFS, everything.

**The protocol's timing recommendation**: start fluoxetine at standard dose (no taper) to maximize hepatic first-pass concentration from day 1. Don't start at 10mg and titrate slowly — the 10mg dose halves the hepatic concentration and extends the WT CVB circulation window. Start at 20mg (or 60mg for males), full dose.

**Why this matters**: every day WT CVB circulates in the liver is another day TD mutants are being seeded into pancreas, myocardium, and CNS. The faster the liver clears, the less seeding occurs.

## The IVIG Connection (Neonates and Immunocompromised)

In neonates and immunocompromised patients, Kupffer cell function is impaired:
- Neonates: Kupffer cells are immature
- Immunocompromised: Kupffer cell activity suppressed

For these patients: IVIG provides the neutralizing antibody function that Kupffer cells normally provide. This is why IVIG is life-saving in neonatal CVB sepsis — it replaces the Kupffer cell function that normally prevents liver amplification.

For adult patients on immunosuppression (transplant, chemotherapy): the liver-first prediction may not hold. Fluoxetine at higher dose + more frequent FMD to compensate for impaired Kupffer cell backup.

## The Wall

The wall for hepatitis is not mechanical — it's conceptual. Clinicians and patients see ALT rising in the first weeks of the protocol and think "drug toxicity, stop the treatment." They stop the one thing that's working.

**The wall**: misinterpreting the ALT rise as harm when it is the signal of successful hepatic viral clearance.

**The solution**: pre-counsel patients and physicians: "You will likely see ALT rise in weeks 1-4. This is expected. It means the liver is clearing CVB. Monitor it. If it rises above 3× ULN or doesn't normalize by week 6, we investigate. Otherwise: this is the protocol working."

One conversation at the beginning saves the entire protocol from being prematurely terminated.
