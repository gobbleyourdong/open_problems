# Attempt 001: Subclinical CVB Hepatitis Detection in T1DM Patients

## Approach
CVB hepatitis is underdiagnosed because CVB is never tested in hepatitis workups. This attempt designs a diagnostic protocol to detect subclinical CVB hepatitis in T1DM patients, which would (a) confirm multi-organ CVB persistence and (b) guide safe drug dosing for the T1DM protocol.

## The Hypothesis
```
T1DM patient has persistent CVB in pancreas (confirmed by DiViD) →
Portal venous blood drains pancreas → liver →
CVB seeds hepatocytes during transit →
Subclinical hepatitis: mildly elevated ALT/AST attributed to "metabolic" causes →
Actually CVB hepatitis
```

## The Protocol

### Cohort
- T1DM patients (any duration) with "unexplained" mild ALT/AST elevation (1-3x ULN)
- Exclude: NAFLD by imaging, alcohol, hepatitis B/C/autoimmune, medications

### Testing
1. Standard hepatitis panel (HBsAg, anti-HCV, ANA, anti-SMA, anti-LKM) — to exclude other causes
2. **Serum enteroviral RNA by RT-PCR** — direct detection of CVB viremia
3. **CVB serotype-specific neutralizing antibodies** (B1-B5) — immune response pattern
4. **Liver fibroscan/elastography** — assess fibrosis without biopsy
5. If available: **liver biopsy with enteroviral RNA in situ hybridization** — gold standard

### Primary outcome
- Prevalence of enteroviral RNA positivity in T1DM patients with unexplained transaminase elevation vs T1DM patients with normal LFTs vs non-diabetic controls

### Secondary outcomes
- Correlation between liver enteroviral RNA and islet autoantibody titers
- Correlation between ALT/AST levels and CVB serotype
- Liver fibrosis stage in CVB-positive vs CVB-negative T1DM patients

## Clinical Relevance
If subclinical CVB hepatitis is confirmed in T1DM patients:
1. **Drug dosing**: itraconazole (hepatotoxic, CYP3A4) may need dose reduction or avoidance
2. **Monitoring**: LFTs should be part of standard T1DM protocol monitoring
3. **Treatment response**: ALT/AST normalization becomes a surrogate marker for CVB clearance
4. **Multi-organ model**: confirms that CVB persists in multiple organs simultaneously

## Status: DIAGNOSTIC STUDY DESIGN — no treatment, observational only
