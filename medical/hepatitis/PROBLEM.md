# Viral Hepatitis (Coxsackievirus B)

## The Problem
CVB-induced liver inflammation. Usually mild and self-limiting in adults but can be severe in neonates (fulminant hepatic failure). Often co-occurs with pancreatitis and/or pericarditis. Underdiagnosed because CVB is rarely tested in hepatitis workups.

## Status: USUALLY SELF-LIMITING (but neonatal form can be fatal)

## Key Biology

| Component | What | Role |
|-----------|------|------|
| Hepatocytes | Liver parenchymal cells | Target of CVB infection |
| CVB1-5 | Causative serotypes | Direct hepatocyte cytolysis |
| ALT/AST | Liver transaminases | Elevated during active hepatitis |
| Neonatal form | Vertical/perinatal transmission | Fulminant hepatic necrosis, high mortality |

## Connection to T1DM
- Multi-organ CVB infection: hepatitis + pancreatitis + myocarditis can co-present
- Liver inflammation may impair drug metabolism (relevant for itraconazole, fluoxetine dosing)
- Subclinical hepatitis may be present in persistent CVB — check liver enzymes
