# Attempt 001: CNS Persistence Study — Meningitis → ME/CFS Pathway

## Approach
Test the hypothesis that CVB aseptic meningitis establishes CNS persistence (TD mutants in microglia/neurons), which later manifests as ME/CFS neuroinflammation.

## The Study

### Cohort
- Patients hospitalized with confirmed CVB aseptic meningitis (enteroviral RT-PCR positive CSF)
- Age-matched controls hospitalized with non-CVB aseptic meningitis (HSV, other enteroviruses)

### Prospective follow-up (5 years)
| Timepoint | Assessment |
|-----------|-----------|
| Acute | CSF viral load, cytokines, cell count. Serum CVB antibodies. MRI brain. |
| 3 months | Neurocognitive battery (MoCA + detailed). ME/CFS symptom screen. Fatigue severity scale. |
| 6 months | Repeat neuro battery. Autonomic function (tilt table if symptomatic). NK cell assay. |
| 12 months | Full assessment. PET-MRI if available (microglial activation tracer: [11C]PBR28). |
| 24 months | Full assessment. |
| 60 months | Full assessment. |

### Primary outcome
- Incidence of ME/CFS (Fukuda + Canadian Consensus Criteria) at 5 years in CVB meningitis vs non-CVB meningitis

### Secondary outcomes
- Microglial activation on PET (CVB vs non-CVB)
- Neurocognitive trajectory
- NK cell function trajectory
- Correlation between acute CSF viral load and chronic symptoms

## The Intervention Question
If the observational arm confirms CVB meningitis → ME/CFS pathway, the next trial:
- Fluoxetine + FMD during acute CVB meningitis → does early antiviral prevent ME/CFS?
- This would be the CNS equivalent of the pancreatitis → T1DM prevention trial
