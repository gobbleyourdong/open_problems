# Aseptic Meningitis — Anti-Problem

## The Question
"What determines whether CVB CNS invasion stops at the meninges (meningitis) or invades the parenchyma (encephalitis)?"

## The Answer: The Meningeal Immune Firewall

The meninges are not passive membranes. They contain a dense immune surveillance network:
- Meningeal macrophages (border-associated macrophages, BAMs)
- Dendritic cells
- Mast cells
- ILC2 innate lymphoid cells
- Recently discovered meningeal lymphatic vessels (Louveau 2015, Nature)

This immune layer is the LAST checkpoint between systemic circulation and brain parenchyma.

```
CVB in blood → crosses BBB endothelium → enters CSF/meningeal space →

MENINGEAL FIREWALL:
  BAMs detect CVB → phagocytosis + antigen presentation
  Type I IFN produced locally → antiviral state in surrounding cells
  Neutrophils recruited → early containment
  NK cells → kill infected meningeal cells
  
IF FIREWALL HOLDS (95-99% of cases):
  → Inflammation confined to meninges (headache, stiffness, photophobia)
  → CSF: lymphocytic pleocytosis, normal glucose, elevated protein
  → Self-limiting (5-10 days)
  → Full recovery, minimal sequelae
  
IF FIREWALL FAILS (1-5% of cases):
  → CVB penetrates pia mater into brain parenchyma
  → Neurons infected → encephalitis
  → Microglia activate → bystander damage
  → Potential permanent neurological damage
```

## What Makes the Firewall Fail

1. **Viral load overwhelms capacity** — too many CVB particles, not enough BAMs
2. **Neonatal immaturity** — meningeal immune network not fully developed
3. **Prior meningeal inflammation** — disrupted barrier from previous infection
4. **Immunodeficiency** — congenital or acquired (HIV, chemotherapy)
5. **Specific CVB strain virulence** — more neurotropic variants (potentially selenium-dependent per Beck mechanism)

## The Anti-Problem for the Campaign

Most CVB meningitis resolves completely. The patients who recover well had:
- Mature meningeal immune system (age >2 years)
- Adequate IFN response (genetic + nutritional support)
- Moderate viral load (liver gatekeeper functioned → low systemic viremia)
- No progression to encephalitis (firewall held)

**But did they truly clear CVB from the CNS?** Or did TD mutants establish in autonomic ganglia (meningitis attempt 002 hypothesis) while the meninges cleared?

The critical unknown: **meningitis may resolve clinically while leaving silent CNS reservoirs.** The headache goes away. The virus stays. Years later: autonomic dysfunction, chronic fatigue, cognitive symptoms.

This reframes meningitis from "benign, self-limiting" to "acute symptom resolves, chronic seeding may persist." The protocol's value isn't treating meningitis (it doesn't need treatment). It's preventing the SILENT PERSISTENCE that follows.
