# Aseptic Meningitis (Coxsackievirus B)

## The Problem
Viral inflammation of the meninges (brain/spinal cord membranes). CVB is one of the most common causes of aseptic (non-bacterial) meningitis, especially in children and young adults. Usually self-limiting but can cause severe headache, photophobia, neck stiffness. Rarely progresses to encephalitis.

## Status: USUALLY SELF-LIMITING (supportive care)

## Key Biology

| Component | What | Role |
|-----------|------|------|
| Meninges | Brain/spinal cord membranes | Target of CVB-induced inflammation |
| CSF | Cerebrospinal fluid | Lymphocytic pleocytosis (elevated WBCs), normal glucose |
| CVB1-5 | Causative serotypes | CNS invasion during viremia |
| Blood-brain barrier | Endothelial tight junctions | CVB can cross during high-titer viremia |

## Connection to T1DM
- CNS invasion demonstrates CVB's ability to reach immunologically privileged sites
- Neurotropism may contribute to autonomic dysfunction in T1DM (gastroparesis, orthostatic hypotension)
- Meningitis episode in history = confirmed CVB exposure with CNS-penetrating viremia
