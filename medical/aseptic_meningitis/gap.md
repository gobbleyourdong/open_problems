# Aseptic Meningitis — Gap Analysis (Initial)

## The Gap

Aseptic meningitis itself is self-limiting (supportive care). The gap is the same as pleurodynia — **recognition and downstream surveillance**.

CVB meningitis proves CNS invasion occurred during viremia. This means:
1. CVB crossed the blood-brain barrier
2. CNS tissue was exposed to viral seeding
3. TD mutants MAY have established in CNS (microglia, neurons)
4. This is the potential mechanism for later ME/CFS neuroinflammation, autonomic dysfunction

## Clinical Relevance

A history of aseptic meningitis in a patient with:
- T1DM → confirms CVB exposure with multi-organ viremia
- ME/CFS → suggests CNS persistence as mechanism for brain fog/cognitive dysfunction
- Autonomic dysfunction → CVB may have persisted in autonomic ganglia

## Anti-Problem
"Do patients with documented CVB meningitis have higher rates of later ME/CFS?"

If yes: meningitis is the CNS seeding event, and antiviral treatment during meningitis could prevent ME/CFS.

## Protocol
- Acute: supportive (standard) + fluoxetine (antiviral, prevent CNS persistence)
- Post-acute: neurocognitive baseline, ME/CFS symptom monitoring, autonomic function testing
- Long-term: if cognitive symptoms develop → full ME/CFS protocol

## Convergence
Aseptic meningitis is CNS pleurodynia — a sentinel of CVB neurotropism. The disease itself is mild. What it predicts is not.
