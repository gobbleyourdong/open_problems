# Pleurodynia (Bornholm Disease) — THE WALL

## One Sentence

## What Pleurodynia Actually Signals

Bornholm disease is a 2-3 week self-limiting illness. Chest or abdominal pain from intercostal or epigastric muscle infection. Summer/fall seasonality. CVB5 (primarily), sometimes CVB4.

The clinical picture: patient presents with sudden severe pain, doctor diagnoses "epidemic pleurodynia," NSAIDs prescribed, reassured the pain will resolve, discharged in 5-7 days. Correct.

The hidden picture: while those intercostal muscles are hurting, CVB is simultaneously (silently) infecting:
- Beta cells (CVB4 diabetogenic, CVB5 muscle/orchid tropic) → 2-5 year T1DM risk
- Cardiomyocytes → 5-10 year DCM risk
- CNS → months to years ME/CFS risk (if CVB2 co-infection)
- Testes (in males) → subclinical orchitis

The patient goes home having been told "it's just a viral illness, you'll be fine." This is medically true for pleurodynia. It is epidemiologically incorrect for everything else that's happening simultaneously.

## The Bornholm Island Natural Experiment

Bornholm Island, Denmark, had major pleurodynia epidemics in the 1930s. The name "Bornholm disease" came from these outbreaks (Sylvest 1930).

The campaign hypothesis (pleurodynia/attempts/attempt_002 + attempt_003): if CVB → T1DM with a 3-5 year lag, then Bornholm Island should show elevated T1DM incidence in 1935-1945 vs mainland Denmark.

**This natural experiment has never been formally analyzed.** The Danish registers (excellent, continuous since the 1920s) have the data. The analysis would require:
1. Pleurodynia epidemic data by municipality, Bornholm, 1928-1940
2. Danish T1DM incidence by municipality, 1932-1950
3. Cross-correlation with lag τ = 2-8 years

If the correlation is positive (especially in the pediatric cohort, which has the shortest lag): this becomes Grade B evidence for CVB → T1DM causation. No experiments needed. Historical data already exists.
