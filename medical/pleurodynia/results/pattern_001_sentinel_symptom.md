# Pattern 001: Pleurodynia as the Sentinel Symptom of CVB Infection

## The Pattern

Epidemic pleurodynia (Bornholm disease) is the most clinically recognizable acute CVB manifestation and serves as a sentinel/early warning marker for CVB circulation in a population. Historical epidemic pleurodynia outbreaks correlate with subsequent T1DM incidence spikes in the same geographic regions, with a lag of 2-10 years -- the time needed for islet autoimmunity to progress to clinical diabetes.

## Evidence

### Pleurodynia as sentinel -- epidemic surveillance

- **Sylvest, 1934 (original Bornholm description):** Epidemic on Bornholm island, Denmark. Acute-onset severe intercostal/abdominal pain, fever, clearly clustered in time and space. Affected ~10% of the island's population.
- **Warin et al., 1953:** Epidemic pleurodynia outbreaks in UK tracked by public health surveillance -- summer/autumn seasonality, 3-5 year epidemic cycles matching CVB circulation patterns.
- **CDC enterovirus surveillance data:** Pleurodynia clusters reported consistently during CVB epidemic years (B3 and B5 most common). These same years show increased isolation of CVB from other clinical specimens.
- **Pleurodynia is the "tip of the iceberg":** For every symptomatic pleurodynia case, ~10-50 subclinical CVB infections occur in the same population (based on seroconversion rates during outbreaks).

### Temporal correlation: pleurodynia outbreaks -> T1DM incidence

- **Gamble, 1980 (UK):** CVB4 epidemic years (identified by pleurodynia clusters and virological surveillance) preceded T1DM incidence peaks by 2-4 years.
- **Dahlquist et al., 1995 (Sweden):** Enterovirus epidemics (tracked by clinical syndromes including pleurodynia) correlated with T1DM incidence in children born during those years. Lag: 5-10 years from birth-year exposure to T1DM onset.
- **Hyoty et al., 1995 (Finland):** CVB infection waves preceded islet autoantibody appearance in the DIPP cohort. Temporal clustering consistent with epidemic pleurodynia surveillance data.
- **Lonnrot et al., 2000:** Enterovirus seroconversion in Finnish children associated with islet autoantibody appearance with 6-12 month lag.

### The sentinel timeline

```
Epidemic pleurodynia outbreak (summer, year 0)
  |
  v  [same CVB wave infects children subclinically]
  |
  v  [3-12 months] Islet autoantibody seroconversion in susceptible children (TEDDY/DIPP data)
  |
  v  [2-5 years] Progressive beta cell loss (subclinical)
  |
  v  [3-10 years] Clinical T1DM diagnosis (>80% beta cell loss)
```

### Why pleurodynia is the best sentinel

- **Clinically unmistakable:** Sudden-onset severe chest/abdominal pain worsened by breathing. Patients present to ER. Gets documented.
- **Short incubation (2-5 days):** Marks the acute viremia phase precisely.
- **Epidemic clustering:** Multiple cases in short time = community CVB circulation confirmed.
- **Low mortality:** Patients survive and can be followed longitudinally.
- **Serotype data:** CVB B3 and B5 dominate pleurodynia; B4 dominates T1DM. But epidemic years circulate MULTIPLE serotypes, so B5 pleurodynia = B4 co-circulation.

### The Iceberg Ratio

```
During a CVB epidemic:
  Clinical pleurodynia cases:        1x  (visible, reported)
  Clinical other CVB syndromes:      2-3x (myocarditis, meningitis, HFM)
  Subclinical CVB infections:        50-100x (seroconversion only)
  Pancreatic seeding events:         ? (unknown, but subset of subclinical)
  Future T1DM cases from this wave:  0.01-0.1x of subclinical infections

One epidemic pleurodynia case = signal that 50-100 subclinical pancreatic
seedings may have occurred in the same community.
```

## Connection to T1DM Protocol

1. **Retrospective diagnostic tool:** If a T1DM patient reports unexplained severe chest/rib pain in the years before diagnosis, it was likely pleurodynia -- confirming CVB as the etiologic agent for THEIR T1DM.
2. **Population-level prediction:** Pleurodynia outbreaks could predict T1DM incidence 5 years later. This supports the CVB->T1DM causal model and could guide preventive intervention timing.
3. **Vaccine timing:** A CVB vaccine deployed during or just before a pleurodynia epidemic year could prevent the downstream T1DM wave. The sentinel gives early warning.
4. **the patient question:** Did the patient have unexplained chest pain or intercostal muscle pain in childhood? If yes, that WAS the seeding event.
