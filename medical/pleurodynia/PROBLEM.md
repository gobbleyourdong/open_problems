# Epidemic Pleurodynia (Bornholm Disease)

## The Problem
Acute CVB infection of intercostal muscles causing severe paroxysmal chest/abdominal pain ("devil's grip"). Worsens with breathing. Named after outbreak on Bornholm island (Denmark, 1930s). Self-limiting (2-14 days) but can recur. Often occurs in epidemics.

## Status: SELF-LIMITING (no specific treatment, NSAIDs for pain)

## Key Biology

| Component | What | Role |
|-----------|------|------|
| Intercostal muscles | Muscles between ribs | Target of CVB infection → pain with breathing |
| Diaphragm | Primary breathing muscle | Can also be involved → severe pleuritic pain |
| CVB1-5 | Causative serotypes (B3, B5 most common) | Direct muscle cell infection |
| Epidemic pattern | Fecal-oral spread | Summer/fall outbreaks, clustered cases |

## Connection to T1DM
- Pleurodynia may be the presenting symptom of the SAME CVB infection that seeds the pancreas
- Epidemic outbreaks of pleurodynia correlate with T1DM incidence spikes
- A sentinel: if you had unexplained chest/rib pain before T1DM diagnosis, it may have been pleurodynia
