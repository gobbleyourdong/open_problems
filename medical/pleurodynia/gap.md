# Pleurodynia (Bornholm Disease) — Gap Analysis (Initial)

## The Sentinel Disease

Pleurodynia is not a major clinical problem on its own (self-limiting, 2-14 days). Its importance is **epidemiological**: it's the most recognizable acute CVB presentation, and outbreaks of pleurodynia correlate temporally with spikes in T1DM incidence.

## The Gap

There is no treatment gap — the disease resolves with NSAIDs.

The gap is **recognition and follow-up**:
1. Diagnose pleurodynia as CVB (most cases are called "viral chest pain" or "costochondritis")
2. Test for CVB serotype
3. Monitor for downstream sequelae: T1DM (islet autoantibodies), myocarditis (troponin, echo), ME/CFS

## Anti-Problem
"Did the patient have an episode of unexplained chest/rib pain before T1DM diagnosis?"

If yes: that was likely pleurodynia from the SAME CVB infection that seeded the pancreas. The timeline reconstruction would confirm the viral etiology.

## Protocol
- Acute: NSAIDs (standard) + fluoxetine (antiviral, novel — prevent persistent infection)
- Post-acute: islet autoantibody screening, cardiac screening, ME/CFS symptom monitoring
- **Treat pleurodynia as the canary in the coal mine.** The chest pain is nothing. What CVB is doing to the pancreas, heart, and CNS during the same viremia is everything.

## Convergence
Pleurodynia → pancreatitis → T1DM is a plausible temporal sequence in many patients. Intervening at the pleurodynia stage with antivirals is the earliest possible prevention of all downstream diseases.
