# Attempt 001: Pleurodynia as Sentinel Surveillance for CVB-Related Disease

## Approach
Use pleurodynia outbreaks as an early warning system for downstream CVB diseases. Pleurodynia is the most clinically recognizable acute CVB presentation. Tracking it enables prospective monitoring for T1DM, myocarditis, and ME/CFS.

## The Protocol

### Surveillance design
1. **Case identification**: ED visits for acute chest/abdominal pain in summer/fall with:
   - Pleuritic quality (worse with breathing)
   - Fever
   - No pneumonia on CXR
   - No cardiac cause on ECG/troponin
   - → Currently diagnosed as "viral pleurisy" or "costochondritis" → **add CVB testing**

2. **CVB confirmation**: Stool enteroviral RT-PCR + serum CVB IgM + throat swab PCR

3. **Prospective follow-up** (CVB-confirmed pleurodynia cases):
   - Islet autoantibodies at 3, 6, 12, 24 months (T1DM sentinel)
   - Troponin + ECG at 2 weeks (myocarditis sentinel)
   - Cardiac MRI if troponin elevated
   - ME/CFS symptom questionnaire at 3, 6, 12 months
   - Neurocognitive screening at 6, 12 months

4. **Intervention arm** (if ethics permit):
   - Fluoxetine 20mg × 3 months for CVB-confirmed cases
   - Hypothesis: early antiviral prevents downstream sequelae

### Primary outcome
- Incidence of islet autoantibody seroconversion, subclinical myocarditis, or ME/CFS symptoms in pleurodynia patients at 24 months

### Value
- Cheap (adding CVB testing to existing ED workup: ~$100/patient)
- Prospective (captures the temporal sequence that retrospective studies miss)
- If positive: establishes pleurodynia → T1DM causal pathway
- Enables the earliest possible antiviral intervention in the CVB disease cascade

## Status: STUDY DESIGN — epidemiological surveillance with optional intervention arm
