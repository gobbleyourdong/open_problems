# Attempt 004: Keshan Disease — The Natural Experiment That Proves CVB Causes Cardiomyopathy

## What Is Keshan Disease?

An endemic cardiomyopathy discovered in Keshan County, Heilongjiang Province, China in 1935. Affected millions in a belt stretching across northeastern to southwestern China. Characterized by acute or chronic myocarditis progressing to dilated cardiomyopathy. Predominantly affected children and women of childbearing age.

**Cause: Coxsackievirus B infection in selenium-deficient populations.**

## Why This Is the Strongest Evidence in the Entire Campaign

Keshan disease is a **controlled natural experiment** that no ethics board would approve:

```
VARIABLE 1: CVB exposure (ubiquitous — everyone exposed)
VARIABLE 2: Selenium status (geographically determined by soil content)

SELENIUM-ADEQUATE regions: CVB infection → clearance → no disease
SELENIUM-DEFICIENT regions: CVB infection → persistence → cardiomyopathy

Same virus. Same population genetics. Same diet (except selenium).
One variable changed. Disease appears.
```

### The proof:
1. **Selenium supplementation eliminated Keshan disease.** Chinese government sodium selenite supplementation program (1974-present) reduced incidence from ~40/100,000 to <1/100,000. The disease essentially vanished.
2. **CVB isolated from Keshan disease patients.** CVB3 and CVB4 most common.
3. **Selenium-deficient mice develop severe CVB myocarditis.** Beck 1994, 1995 (Nature Medicine): selenium-deficient mice infected with normally benign CVB3 → severe myocarditis + viral mutations → the virus BECOMES more virulent in selenium-deficient hosts.
4. **The viral mutation mechanism.** Selenium deficiency → reduced glutathione peroxidase → increased ROS → increased viral RNA mutation rate → normally benign CVB3 acquires virulence mutations. The host's nutritional status changes the VIRUS, not just the immune response.

## The Beck Mutation Discovery

Beck et al. (Nature Medicine, 1994-1995) showed something profound:

```
EXPERIMENT:
  Take CVB3/0 (an avirulent, non-pathogenic strain of CVB3)
  Infect selenium-adequate mice → no disease, virus clears normally
  Infect selenium-DEFICIENT mice → SEVERE myocarditis

THE TWIST:
  Sequence the virus recovered from selenium-deficient mice
  It has ACQUIRED 6 point mutations that make it virulent
  
  Take that mutant virus → infect selenium-ADEQUATE mice
  → STILL causes myocarditis!

  The selenium-deficient host didn't just fail to fight the virus.
  The selenium-deficient host CHANGED THE VIRUS INTO A MORE DANGEROUS VERSION.
  And that dangerous version remains dangerous even in a healthy host.
```

**Mechanism:**
- Selenium → glutathione peroxidase → neutralizes ROS
- Without selenium → ROS accumulate → oxidative damage to viral RNA
- Damaged viral RNA → increased mutation rate during replication
- Most mutations are lethal (virus dies) but some are gain-of-function
- CVB3 acquires mutations in the 5' UTR and capsid → enhanced virulence
- The selenium-deficient host is a **viral mutation accelerator**
