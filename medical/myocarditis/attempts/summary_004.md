## What We Know Now

1. **The T1DM protocol transfers almost completely to myocarditis.** The antiviral arm (fluoxetine + autophagy) and anti-inflammatory arm (BHB, colchicine, omega-3, vitamin D) are organ-agnostic. The gap is regeneration: beta cells regenerate, cardiomyocytes barely do.

2. **The critical difference from T1DM is irreversibility.** In T1DM, you tip R > D. In myocarditis, you PREVENT damage because you can't repair it. Early intervention is everything.

3. **Kühl 2003 proves the concept.** IFN-β cleared enterovirus from human hearts and improved LVEF by +8.5%. Our protocol substitutes oral fluoxetine (cheaper, better tolerated) + GDMT (additive remodeling benefit). There is clinical precedent.

4. **A direct 2A protease inhibitor would be transformative.** It would stop dystrophin cleavage immediately while the antiviral clears the virus. This is a drug development opportunity.

5. **Selenium is not optional — it's Grade A evidence.** Keshan disease proves CVB causes cardiomyopathy. Beck's work proves selenium deficiency creates more virulent CVB strains.

## The Gap (Updated)

The original gap was "cardiomyocytes don't regenerate." After 4 attempts, the gap has refined:

**The gap is TIMING.** If myocarditis is caught before significant fibrosis, the protocol halts progression and allows the heart to compensate for minor damage. If caught late (established DCM), the protocol stops worsening but can't restore lost muscle.

**the patient's gap is diagnostic:** does subclinical myocarditis exist? Cardiac screening (attempt 003) answers this.
