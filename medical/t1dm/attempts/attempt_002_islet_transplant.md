# Attempt 002: Islet Transplantation (Edmonton Protocol)

## Approach
Isolate islets from deceased donor pancreases, infuse into the portal vein of the liver. Beta cells engraft in hepatic sinusoids and produce insulin.

## Key Trial
- **Edmonton Protocol** (Shapiro et al., NEJM 2000): 7/7 patients insulin-independent at 1 year
- Revised protocol: ~50% insulin independence at 5 years
- FDA approved Lantidra (donislecel) June 2023

## Results
- Restores endogenous insulin production (C-peptide positive)
- Eliminates severe hypoglycemia in most patients
- HbA1c normalization in responders
- ~50% maintain some graft function at 5 years

## Why It Fails as a Cure
1. **Requires lifelong immunosuppression.** Tacrolimus + sirolimus or mycophenolate. The immunosuppression side effects (infection risk, nephrotoxicity, cancer risk) may be WORSE than the diabetes itself for many patients.
2. **Donor shortage.** Need 2-3 donor pancreases per recipient. ~8,000 deceased donor pancreases/year in the US, ~1.5M T1DM patients. Mathematically impossible to scale.
3. **Graft loss over time.** The autoimmune attack resumes AND allorejection occurs. Even with immunosuppression, beta cells die.
4. **Site-specific issues.** Islets in the liver lose function over time (IBMIR — instant blood-mediated inflammatory reaction, hypoxia, lack of native microenvironment).
5. **Does not address the cause.** The autoimmune process that destroyed the original beta cells is still active and will attack the transplanted ones.

## What It Teaches Us
- Beta cell replacement WORKS mechanistically — the cells produce insulin, sense glucose, close the loop
- The problem is not the beta cells — it's the immune environment they live in
- Even with immunosuppression, the autoimmune memory persists (graft loss)
- The gap is the immune system, not the beta cells

## The Gap It Reveals
We can replace the cells. We can't protect them. The gap: **immune protection without systemic immunosuppression.** Either encapsulation (physical barrier) or tolerance (immunological barrier removal).

## Status: WORKS BUT DOESN'T SCALE — proof of concept, not a cure
