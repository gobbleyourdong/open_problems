## RED findings

### R17 — attempt_042 L58–76 (rituximab for ADE antibody clearance)

**The claim**: "Option A: Low-dose rituximab (anti-CD20, depletes B
cells) → B cells stop producing VP4 antibodies → existing VP4 IgG
decays with ~21 day half-life → after 3 months: VP4 antibody levels
drop 90%+ → ADE pathway disabled" with the Critical Sequence at
L104–125 making this Stage 1b of the updated protocol.

**Why load-bearing**: the updated protocol structurally requires
rituximab (or plasmapheresis, or ~1yr of time) to eliminate ADE
antibodies before teplizumab can be given. If the underlying "ADE
driven by VP4 antibodies drives T1DM persistence" premise is
uncertain, the rituximab recommendation is a significant intervention
(B-cell depletion for 6+ months, rare but serious AEs: PML, HBV
reactivation, hypogammaglobulinemia) justified by a hypothesis, not a
finding.

**Concern**:
1. ADE for CVB in human T1DM is not established. ADE is well-
   characterized for dengue and a few other viruses. Soppela et al.
   proposed a ΔVP4 VLP vaccine design **because** VP4-directed
   antibodies are non-neutralizing and MAY promote ADE in principle —
   vaccine-design caution, not mechanistic proof that ADE is the
   driver of T1DM persistence in humans.
2. Rituximab HAS been tested in new-onset T1DM (Pescovitz et al. 2009
   *NEJM*, PMID 19864418) with modest C-peptide preservation at 1
   year, but via a different rationale (general B-cell contribution
   to autoimmunity, not ADE). The attempt extends the Soppela vaccine-
   design hypothesis to recommend rituximab in a novel way without
   engaging the Pescovitz trial.
3. "After 3 months: VP4 antibody levels drop 90%+" assumes monotonic
   IgG decay with no memory B cell re-engagement. In practice, B-cell
   recovery after rituximab varies (6–24 months), and long-lived
   plasma cells in bone marrow are NOT depleted by rituximab — so VP4
   antibody titer may plateau at nonzero rather than decay to zero.

**Required fix**:
1. Mark Option A (rituximab) as hypothesis-driven, not evidence-based.
2. Cite Pescovitz 2009 *NEJM* as the actual human T1DM rituximab
   evidence, note its rationale was different, note the modest effect
   size and duration.
3. Note the Soppela VLPΔVP4 reference supports vaccine caution against
   ADE-promoting antibodies, not a treatment mandate for rituximab.
4. Add the known rituximab AE profile so readers weigh it against the
   uncertain mechanistic justification.
5. Option B (wait + antiviral alone) should be the default; Option A
   should be labeled exploratory.

  "required protocol step."
