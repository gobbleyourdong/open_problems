# Attempt 091: IBD (Inflammatory Bowel Disease) as Co-Beneficiary Disease

## The Population

Inflammatory bowel disease:
- Crohn's disease: ~850,000 Americans
- Ulcerative colitis: ~900,000 Americans
- Total: ~1.7 million Americans, ~5 million globally
- No cure, chronic relapsing-remitting course

## Why IBD Is in the Campaign

IBD is NOT CVB-caused (CVB is not prominently in IBD literature as a trigger). But IBD shares enough mechanism with the protocol's components that it belongs as a **Category 2 co-beneficiary** — similar to eczema and psoriasis.

## The Autophagy Connection in Crohn's

**This is the strongest mechanistic connection between the campaign and IBD:**

NOD2 mutations are the strongest genetic risk factor for Crohn's disease. NOD2 → defective autophagy in macrophages and intestinal epithelial cells → impaired clearance of intracellular bacteria → inappropriate inflammation.

**Trehalose specifically addresses this**: TFEB activation → lysosomal biogenesis → improved autophagy completion → better intracellular pathogen clearance. The same mechanism that addresses the CVB LAMP2 block in islets also addresses the NOD2-related autophagy defect in Crohn's.

**Published evidence**: trehalose has shown benefit in multiple IBD animal models and in preliminary human data (reduces intestinal permeability). This is independent of the CVB campaign — it's the Crohn's autophagy literature.

## The FOXP1-IBD Connection

FOXP1 is expressed in intestinal epithelial cells and intestinal Tregs. Published (PMID:29343755): FOXP1 suppression in intestinal Tregs → loss of mucosal immune tolerance → IBD-like colitis in mouse models.

**For the campaign**: butyrate 4-6g/day (HDAC → FOXP1) + vitamin D 5000 IU (VDR → FOXP1) restores intestinal FOXP1. This is the same FOXP1 restoration mechanism as in the T1DM islet context — just operating in a different tissue.

## Protocol for IBD Patients

The CVB campaign core protocol provides IBD benefit through multiple mechanisms:

**Missing from core protocol for IBD**:
- 5-ASA (mesalazine) for ulcerative colitis — mucosal anti-inflammatory, specific to UC
- Probiotics — Lactobacillus/Bifidobacterium strains are additive to butyrate
- Exclusive enteral nutrition (EEN) — for Crohn's remission induction (more aggressive than FMD)

## What IBD Patients Can Do Today

The core campaign protocol (butyrate 4-6g + FMD monthly + VitD + omega-3 + trehalose) addresses IBD through mechanisms that are largely evidence-based AND are distinct from current IBD biologics.

**For patients on biologics (infliximab, vedolizumab, ustekinumab)**: the protocol is additive, not competitive. Adding butyrate + FMD + VitD to existing biologic therapy addresses the upstream gut dysbiosis and FOXP1 mechanisms that biologics don't target.

**For patients not on biologics**: the protocol may be sufficient for mild-moderate IBD and could delay or prevent the need for biologics.

## The Protocol Can Answer a Specific Question for IBD Patients

**Can regular FMD (5-day monthly) induce and maintain IBD remission?**

Published data suggests fasting-type dietary interventions can induce remission in Crohn's. The mechanism: gut microbiome reset, NLRP3 suppression, autophagy induction, FOXP1 restoration.

Monthly FMD + butyrate + trehalose could be tested as a maintenance protocol to reduce IBD flares. This is a tractable clinical trial with clear endpoints (CRP, fecal calprotectin, Harvey-Bradshaw Index) that could be started at any gastroenterology center.
