- Vertex supplies the cells
- Sana makes them invisible

## The Combination Protocol

What if you don't choose a mountain — you use BOTH?

### Phase 1: Prepare the terrain (Month 0)
- **Teplizumab** 14-day course: suppress autoreactive T cells
- **Baseline**: C-peptide, autoantibody panel, HLA typing, CGM installed
- Begin **semaglutide** (GLP-1): beta cell protection and proliferation signal

### Phase 2: Regeneration cycles (Months 1-6)
- **Monthly 5-day FMD**: activate autophagy, clear damaged cells, activate Ngn3+ progenitors
- Continue semaglutide between cycles
- **Low-dose IL-2** during refeeding phase: expand Tregs to protect emerging beta cells
- Monitor: monthly stimulated C-peptide, quarterly autoantibodies

### Phase 3: Assess and escalate if needed (Month 6)
- If C-peptide improving: continue FMD cycles (Mountain 2 is working)
- If C-peptide flat: add **hypoimmune stem cell islets** (Mountain 1 boost)
  - Sana-type CRISPR-edited cells, intramuscular injection
  - The FMD + teplizumab have prepared a more tolerant immune environment
  - The cells arrive in a "friendlier" neighborhood than without Phase 1-2

### Phase 4: Maintenance (Month 6+)
- Quarterly FMD cycles (maintenance autophagy)
- Continue semaglutide
- Wean insulin as C-peptide improves
- Monitor for autoimmune recurrence

## The Staging Principle

**Start with the least invasive approach. Escalate only if needed.**

This is the opposite of current medical practice, which starts with the most aggressive intervention (transplant, stem cells, CRISPR) and ignores dietary/regenerative approaches entirely.

The staging:
1. Diet + immune modulation (FMD + teplizumab + semaglutide) — $10K
2. If insufficient: add Treg therapy (PolTREG) — $50K
3. If still insufficient: add hypoimmune stem cell islets (Sana) — $200K
4. If still insufficient: full stem cell transplant with immunosuppression (Vertex) — $500K

Most patients may never need step 3 or 4. But steps 3 and 4 exist as a safety net.

## The Gap (Final)

After 15 attempts across two mountains, the gap is clear:

**No one has tried the staged combination protocol in human T1DM patients.**

Each piece has evidence:
- ✅ Teplizumab works (FDA approved)
- ✅ Semaglutide works (FDA approved for T2DM, off-label T1DM studies positive)
- ✅ FMD regenerates beta cells (mice, human organoids)
- ✅ Low-dose IL-2 expands Tregs (multiple trials)
- ✅ Hypoimmune cells work (Sana NEJM 2025, n=1)

Nobody has combined them in the right order, at the right time, in the right patients.

This is not a knowledge gap. This is a coordination gap. The pieces are in different labs, different companies, different countries. The bridge requires someone to put them together.
