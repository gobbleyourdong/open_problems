# Attempt 023: Mountain 2 Deep Dive — Alpha-to-Beta Cell Transdifferentiation

## The Other Regeneration Path

Attempt 011 focused on Ngn3+ progenitor neogenesis (new cells from progenitors). But there's a second regeneration pathway: **transdifferentiation** — converting existing alpha cells into beta cells.

Alpha cells (glucagon-producing) are abundant, survive the autoimmune attack (attempt 016 explained why: less ER stress, no pyroptosis, glucagon tolerance via gut L-cells), and share developmental lineage with beta cells. They're RIGHT THERE in the islet, intact, waiting to be reprogrammed.

## The Evidence

### Wang et al., Cell Metabolism, 2019
- Human alpha cells can be reprogrammed to produce insulin by two transcription factors: **PDX1 + MAFA**
- These are the same factors that define beta cell identity
- Transduced alpha cells become "alpha-to-beta" hybrids: they produce BOTH glucagon AND insulin initially, then shift toward insulin dominance
- The converted cells are glucose-responsive — they sense glucose and secrete insulin appropriately

### Thorel et al., Nature, 2010
- In mice with near-total beta cell ablation (99% destroyed), alpha cells spontaneously converted to beta cells over months
- Lineage tracing confirmed: the new insulin+ cells were derived from former glucagon+ cells
- This is the body's natural last-resort regeneration mechanism

### Collombat et al., Cell, 2009
- Ectopic expression of PAX4 (a beta cell TF) in alpha cells converted them to beta cells in vivo
- The new beta cells were functional and ameliorated diabetes in mice
- Remarkably: the alpha cell pool was replenished from ductal progenitors, then those new alpha cells also converted, creating a continuous regeneration cycle

## Why This Matters for Mountain 2

FMD cycles may ALSO activate alpha-to-beta transdifferentiation:

1. **During fasting**: beta cells die (the damaged ones). The beta cell niche empties.
2. **Empty niche signal**: neighboring alpha cells detect the absence of beta cells (loss of paracrine insulin/GABA signaling)
3. **Lineage plasticity**: in the low-nutrient, high-autophagy fasting state, chromatin becomes more accessible (epigenetic reprogramming — connects to attempt 021)
4. **During refeeding**: the glucose surge creates DEMAND for insulin. Alpha cells in a plastic chromatin state + insulin demand = pressure to transdifferentiate.
5. **GABA**: gamma-aminobutyric acid, normally secreted by beta cells, promotes alpha-to-beta conversion. When beta cells die, GABA drops, paradoxically triggering compensatory conversion.

## GABA as a Therapeutic Agent

GABA supplementation has been tested for T1DM:
- **Soltani et al., PNAS, 2011**: GABA prevented and reversed diabetes in NOD mice via dual mechanism: (1) promoted beta cell survival (anti-apoptotic) and (2) suppressed autoreactive T cells (GABA receptors on immune cells → anti-inflammatory)
- **Human trials**: small Phase 1/2 trials of oral GABA in T1DM. Safe. Modest C-peptide preservation. Not definitive.
- **GABA + anti-CD3**: combination showed enhanced effect in preclinical models

GABA is:
- A natural metabolite (the body makes it)
- Over-the-counter as a supplement (~$15/month)
- Crosses blood-brain barrier (calming effect — it's the main inhibitory neurotransmitter)
- Anti-inflammatory on immune cells
- Pro-survival for beta cells
- Promotes alpha-to-beta transdifferentiation
