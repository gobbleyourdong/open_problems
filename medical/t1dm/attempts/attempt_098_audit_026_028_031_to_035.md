## RED findings

### R10 — attempt_033 L73–85 (A1/A2 casein T1DM epidemiology)

**The claim**: country-by-country T1DM incidence table (Finland 62.3,
Japan 2.4, Venezuela 0.1 per 100K/yr) correlated with A1-milk-
consumption column, with narrative: "the correlation between A1 milk
consumption and T1DM incidence is one of the strongest in nutritional
epidemiology."

**Why load-bearing**: attempt_033 promotes A1 casein as "Hit 1" of the
four-hit cascade. Attempt_035 carries this forward. If the A1 causal
link is weak, the "Hit 1" framing is decorative rather than
mechanistic.

**Concern**:
1. EFSA Scientific Report 2009 (doi:10.2903/j.efsa.2009.231r) concluded
   no causal link between A1 casein / BCM-7 and T1DM based on review of
   available evidence. Multiple subsequent reviews (Kaminski 2014,
   Truswell 2005) have been skeptical.
2. The country table is confounded by latitude/vitamin-D (attempt 016),
   HLA genetic frequencies, healthcare-access diagnosis rates, and
   breastfeeding culture. The Venezuela 0.1 number is most plausibly
   under-diagnosis.
3. The specific country-to-A1-consumption correlation trace back to
   McLachlan 2001 (ecological-correlation hypothesis paper), which is a
   hypothesis-generating study, not a causal demonstration. The attempt
   cites it as if settled.

**Required fix**:
1. Add a "Controversy" subsection: EFSA 2009 concluded no causal link;
   TRIGR 2018 tested a closely-related hypothesis and found no effect.
2. Soften "one of the strongest correlations" to "an ecological
   correlation that generated the hypothesis; causal evidence remains
   contested."
3. Explicit: the A1 milk → T1DM link is **not** established; the
   four-hit cascade's Hit 1 is conditional on a hypothesis that may be
   wrong. Downstream attempts (035, later protocols) should flag
   this conditionality.

### R11 — attempt_034 L54–59 (DiViD-Intervention numeric results)

**The claim**:
> "Pleconaril + ribavirin for 6 months … C-peptide decline was 11%
> with antivirals vs 24% with placebo (P=0.037) … 86% vs 67% retained
> clinically relevant insulin production at 12 months … The antiviral
> HALVED the rate of beta cell loss"

**Why load-bearing**: this is cited as "the strongest evidence that
persistent CVB IS driving beta cell destruction" and underwrites the
"antivirals work as T1DM therapy" framing in attempts 025, 027, 030,
035.

**Concern**: the canonical DiViD-Intervention publication is Krogvold
L et al. *Nature Medicine* 2023 ("Pleconaril and ribavirin in new-
onset type 1 diabetes: a phase 2 randomized trial"). The published
primary endpoint result was **higher stimulated C-peptide at 12
months in the antiviral arm (geometric mean 0.21 vs 0.14 nmol/L,
P=0.037)** — a ~50% relative difference in C-peptide levels, not an
11%-vs-24% decline split, and not an "86% vs 67%" retention split.
The **direction** of the attempt's claim is correct (antivirals
preserved more function) but the **specific numeric split** is a
restatement error that misrepresents the trial size.

**Required fix**: replace the 11%/24%/86%/67% numbers with the
actually-reported Krogvold 2023 Nat Med numbers (stimulated C-peptide
geometric means at 12 mo; insulin dose outcomes; adverse events) and
add the PMID. Note that the trial was small (n=96) and the effect
moderate; "halved the rate of beta cell loss" overstates.

### R12 — attempt_034 L66–77 (fluoxetine superiority over pleconaril)

**The claim**:
> "Fluoxetine targets the sigma-1 receptor on host cells, disrupting
> viral REPLICATION machinery inside the cell. This may be more
> effective against TD mutants because: It doesn't need the virus to
> have a capsid; It blocks the intracellular replication that TD
> mutants still perform"

**Why load-bearing**: this argument is the bridge from "DiViD worked
with pleconaril+ribavirin" to "we recommend fluoxetine instead." It
justifies the Stage 1a fluoxetine recommendation in attempts 024, 025,
027, 028, 030.

**Concern**:
1. Fluoxetine's mechanism against CVB in vitro is **not established
   as sigma-1-receptor-mediated**. The Zuo et al. / Ulferts 2013
   papers propose lysosomal / ER-stress / nonstructural protein A
   (2C or 3A) mechanisms, not sigma-1.
2. "More effective than pleconaril against TD mutants" is a
   hypothesis. No comparative study exists.
3. TD mutants still produce capsids (at low levels); pleconaril would
   still target those; the claim that "TD mutants bypass the step
   pleconaril blocks" is speculative.

**Required fix**: mark all three sub-claims as hypotheses. Remove the
"may be more effective" confidence until a head-to-head or sequential
study exists. DiViD-Intervention used pleconaril+ribavirin and had
measurable effect; fluoxetine has zero human T1DM data.

### R13 — attempt_034 L81–85 (DIPP interaction P-value)

**The claim**:
> "The Finnish DIPP study (6,000+ children) found the smoking gun:
> Enterovirus infection + early cow's milk exposure = P=0.001 for
> autoantibodies."

**Why load-bearing**: cited as "direct validation" of the multi-hit
cascade model.

**Concern**: DIPP is a real cohort (Diabetes Prediction and Prevention,
~10,000+ children by 2020s). Individual DIPP papers have reported
enterovirus-autoantibody associations (Hyöty, Oikarinen) AND milk-
autoantibody associations (Virtanen, Knip) separately, with varying
p-values. I can find no specific DIPP publication reporting an
**interaction term** P=0.001 for "enterovirus + cow's milk." The
"smoking gun" framing and the composite P-value look like a synthesis
of multiple DIPP papers, not a single-paper result.

**Required fix**: cite the specific DIPP papers (Lönnrot et al.
*Diabetes* 2000; Hyöty et al. multiple; Virtanen studies) and state
what each one found individually. Remove the composite P=0.001 unless
there is a specific paper reporting a joint interaction analysis with
that effect size.

### R14 — attempt_026 L58–66 (STZ-vs-NOD translation reasoning)

**The claim**:
> "In STZ mice, FMD works BECAUSE the trigger is gone. … If you give
> a human T1DM operator the SAME conditions as the STZ mouse — trigger
> eliminated, pancreas recovering — the FMD response should be similar."

**Why load-bearing**: this resolves the "translation gap" (one of
attempt_011's explicit limitations) by reinterpreting the STZ-model
success as validating human application IF the trigger is removed.

**Concern**: Longo's group has published FMD data in **both STZ and
NOD models** (the 2017 Cell paper tests both). The reasoning that STZ-
FMD-works-because-trigger-removed DOES map to human-with-virus-cleared
is a logical argument, but it ignores:
1. NOD mice in Longo 2017 DID show FMD response (moderate effect) —
   the attempt implies NOD-FMD "has less dramatic results" without
   citing the specific NOD outcome from Longo 2017.
2. The human immune system has memory T cells that persist after
   trigger clearance (chapter-long literature); "trigger gone" does
   not automatically mean "immune attack stopped."

**Required fix**: cite the specific Longo 2017 NOD-mouse result (what
fraction reversed, what proportion, at what effect size). Soften "the
FMD response should be similar" to "consistent with the mouse data,
FMD in a virus-cleared human pancreas may be more effective than in a
persistently-infected one."
