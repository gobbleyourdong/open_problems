# Attempt 021: Mountain 3 Deep Dive — Epigenetics as the Bridge Between Environment and Autoimmunity

## The Missing Link

Mountain 3 catalogued environmental triggers (viruses, toxins, microbiome, air quality). But HOW do these triggers cause autoimmunity? The answer is epigenetics — environmental factors modify gene expression WITHOUT changing DNA sequence.

## The Epigenetic Mechanism

```
ENVIRONMENT → EPIGENETIC MODIFICATION → GENE EXPRESSION CHANGE → IMMUNE DYSREGULATION → T1DM
     ↓                  ↓                        ↓                      ↓
  Toxins/virus    DNA methylation           Treg genes silenced     Attack on beta cells
  Diet/stress     Histone modification      Pro-inflammatory ON     Insulitis
  Microbiome      miRNA changes             Tolerance genes OFF     Beta cell death
```

### Key Epigenetic Changes in T1DM

1. **FOXP3 promoter hypermethylation**: FOXP3 is the master regulator of Tregs. In T1DM patients, the FOXP3 promoter is MORE methylated → less FOXP3 expression → fewer functional Tregs → loss of immune tolerance. This is REVERSIBLE.

2. **INS gene hypermethylation**: The insulin gene promoter becomes methylated in the thymus during development. This reduces thymic insulin expression → T cells that recognize insulin are NOT deleted during negative selection → autoreactive T cells escape into the periphery.

3. **HLA region epigenetic changes**: Environmental factors can modify chromatin accessibility at HLA loci, altering which peptides are presented to T cells. Same HLA gene, different epigenetic state, different immune response.

4. **miRNA dysregulation**: miR-21, miR-146a, miR-155 are altered in T1DM. These regulate NF-κB signaling, TLR pathways, and Treg/Teff balance. Circulating miRNAs may serve as early biomarkers AND therapeutic targets.

## What Reverses Epigenetic Changes?

This is where Mountains 2 and 3 converge:

### Fasting/Autophagy (Mountain 2)
- Fasting activates **sirtuins** (SIRT1, SIRT3) — NAD+-dependent deacetylases that REMOVE acetyl marks from histones, reprogramming gene expression
- **AMPK activation** during fasting promotes SIRT1 activity
- Autophagy clears cells with damaged epigenomes, allowing regeneration of cells with clean epigenetic states
- The refeeding phase allows the new cells to establish FRESH epigenetic marks in a non-inflamed environment

### Nutrition (Mountain 3)
- **Methyl donors**: folate, B12, betaine, choline — provide the methyl groups for DNA methylation. Deficiency → hypomethylation → aberrant gene expression
- **Polyphenols**: EGCG (green tea), resveratrol (grapes), curcumin — inhibit DNMTs (DNA methyltransferases) and HDACs (histone deacetylases). Can REVERSE aberrant methylation.
- **Short-chain fatty acids** (butyrate): HDAC inhibitor. Produced by gut bacteria from dietary fiber. Promotes FOXP3 expression and Treg differentiation. THIS IS THE GUT-IMMUNE-EPIGENETIC AXIS.
- **Vitamin D**: Epigenetic modifier — vitamin D receptor (VDR) recruits histone-modifying enzymes to target gene promoters.

### Removing Toxins (Mountain 3)
- BPA causes aberrant DNA methylation (shown in animal models)
- Heavy metals interfere with SAM (S-adenosylmethionine) cycle → disrupted methylation
- PFAS alter miRNA profiles
- Removing exposures allows the epigenome to normalize over time
