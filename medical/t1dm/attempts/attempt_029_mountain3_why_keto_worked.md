# Attempt 029: Mountain 3 — Why Keto Worked for 5 Years (And Why It Stopped)

## The Question Nobody Asked

The operator went 5 years without insulin on a keto diet. The medical establishment would say "honeymoon period" and move on. But a 5-year honeymoon is extraordinary — most last 9 months. Something about keto was ACTIVELY preserving beta cell function, not just passively riding residual capacity.

What was keto doing across all three mountains?

## Keto Through the Three-Mountain Lens

### Mountain 1 (Cell Supply) — Beta Cell REST
- Near-zero carb intake → near-zero glucose spikes → near-zero insulin demand
- Beta cells go from "maximum output all day" to "barely needed"
- Resting beta cells: reduced ER stress, reduced proinsulin misfolding, reduced neoantigen generation
- A resting beta cell presents FEWER autoantigens on MHC-I → less visible to autoreactive CD8+ T cells
- **Keto made the beta cells invisible by making them quiet.** Same principle as HLA engineering (attempt 019), achieved through metabolism.

### Mountain 2 (Regeneration) — Constitutive Autophagy
- Keto + caloric moderation = chronic mild ketosis = chronic mTOR suppression
- mTOR suppression → constitutive low-level autophagy (not the acute burst of FMD, but a steady hum)
- Damaged cellular components continuously recycled
- Beta cell quality maintained even as quantity is limited
- Ketone bodies (β-hydroxybutyrate, BHB) are themselves an HDAC inhibitor — same mechanism as butyrate!

### Mountain 3 (Environment) — Gut and Immune Shift
- Keto eliminates: processed food, sugar, most grains, most emulsifiers
- Keto increases: fat, protein, vegetables (depending on implementation)
- Gut microbiome shifts dramatically on keto — some studies show REDUCED inflammatory species
- BHB (a ketone body) directly suppresses the NLRP3 inflammasome — the innate immune sensor that drives IL-1β production
- IL-1β is one of the key cytokines in insulitis
- **Keto was anti-inflammatory at the molecular level, not just the dietary level**

## Why It Stopped Working

The operator reintroduced carbs because keto made them too thin. Then insulin became necessary again. What changed?

### The Demand Shock
- 0g carbs/meal → 15-20g carbs/meal
- Each meal now requires an insulin response
- Beta cells go from resting to working 3x/day
- Working beta cells: increased ER stress, increased proinsulin production, increased MHC-I presentation of insulin-derived peptides
- Beta cells become VISIBLE again to the autoimmune system

### The Metabolic Shift
- Ketosis → glucose metabolism
- BHB drops → loss of HDAC inhibition → loss of NLRP3 suppression
- mTOR reactivates (glucose + amino acids) → autophagy decreases
- The constitutive cleanup stops → cellular damage accumulates
- Inflammatory baseline rises

### The Gut Shift
- Carb reintroduction changes the microbiome again
- If processed carbs: emulsifiers, preservatives re-enter the gut
- Gut permeability may increase
- Inflammatory signaling from gut to pancreas resumes via portal vein

**Keto wasn't just a diet. It was accidentally implementing all three mountains simultaneously.** When it stopped, all three mountains' benefits stopped.

## The Lesson: Keto Was the Prototype Protocol

| Keto effect | Mountain | Protocol equivalent |
|------------|----------|-------------------|
| Beta cell rest | M1 | Reduced insulin demand (but unsustainable — too thin) |
| BHB as HDAC inhibitor | M2/M3 | Butyrate supplementation (same HDAC mechanism, from gut) |
| mTOR suppression | M2 | FMD cycles (more potent, intermittent instead of chronic) |
| NLRP3 suppression | M3 | BHB from keto OR from FMD-induced ketosis during fasting days |
| Eliminated processed food | M3 | Diet cleanup (Stage 1b) |
| Autophagy maintenance | M2 | 18:6 IF (already doing) + monthly FMD (to intensify) |

**The protocol IS keto's benefits without keto's costs.** The operator got too thin because chronic keto is catabolic — constant fat-burning, muscle wasting, inadequate calories. The FMD protocol gives the SAME metabolic benefits (ketosis, autophagy, mTOR suppression, BHB production) in 5-day bursts, with 25 days of normal eating in between. You get the benefits without the wasting.

## The BHB Connection

β-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) is produced during:
- Keto diet (chronic, low levels)
- Fasting (acute, high levels)
- FMD (acute, moderate levels)
- Exercise (modest levels)

BHB is:
- An HDAC inhibitor (like butyrate → FOXP3 derepression → Tregs)
- An NLRP3 inflammasome suppressor (anti-inflammatory at the innate level)
- A fuel for the brain and heart (alternative to glucose)
- An epigenetic modifier (histone β-hydroxybutyrylation — a newly discovered modification)

**BHB may be the single molecule that links all three mountains.** It's produced by fasting (M2), it suppresses inflammation (M3), and it rests beta cells by providing alternative fuel (M1).

The operator was producing BHB for 5 years on keto. When they stopped keto, BHB production dropped. The benefits evaporated. The protocol needs to restore BHB production WITHOUT chronic keto:
- Monthly FMD → high BHB during fasting days
- 18:6 IF → moderate BHB during the 18-hour fast
- Exogenous BHB supplementation → top-up between fasts ($30-50/mo)
