# Attempt 013: Nutritional Protocols — What Actually Works in Humans

## The Evidence Landscape

### Fasting-Mimicking Diet (Longo/ProLon)
- **Human metabolic data** (Sci Transl Med, 2017): 100 participants, 3 monthly cycles. Reduced fasting glucose, IGF-1, CRP, body weight. Effects strongest in metabolically unhealthy subjects.
- **No human T1DM trial completed.** Safety concerns are the blocker.
- **ProLon protocol**: 5 days/month. Day 1: ~1100 kcal. Days 2-5: ~750 kcal. High fat, low protein, low carb. Designed to keep the body in fasting-like metabolic state while providing minimal nutrition.

### Very Low Calorie Diet (Roy Taylor, Newcastle)
- **DiRECT trial** (Lancet, 2018): 800 kcal/day for 8-20 weeks → 46% T2DM remission at 1yr, ~30% at 5yr.
- **Mechanism**: Liver and pancreatic fat clearance → beta cell FUNCTIONAL recovery (not regeneration). The beta cells were damaged by ectopic fat, not destroyed.
- **T1DM relevance**: Limited. T1DM is autoimmune destruction, not fat toxicity. But the principle that beta cells can recover function when the toxic environment is removed is relevant.

### Ketogenic Diet
- **Beta cell rest**: Very low carb → very low glucose → very low insulin demand → beta cells rest.
- **Short-term**: May preserve remaining beta cell function in honeymoon T1DM.
- **Long-term concern**: Chronic keto may cause beta cell atrophy from disuse (Guo et al., Nature Metabolism, 2020).
- **T1DM practical issue**: Insulin dosing is complex on keto. DKA risk if insulin reduced too much.

### Time-Restricted Eating
- **8-hour eating window**: Improves insulin sensitivity, HOMA-B (beta cell function index) in prediabetes.
- **Not strong enough for T1DM**: Improves metabolic markers but no evidence of beta cell regeneration.

## The Missing Trial

**What needs to happen:**

A Phase 1/2 trial in newly diagnosed T1DM (honeymoon period, C-peptide positive):
- 5-day FMD cycles, monthly, for 6-12 months
- Close medical supervision: CGM continuous monitoring, insulin dose adjustment protocol
- Co-intervention: single course of teplizumab at study start (to suppress autoimmunity during the regeneration window)
- Primary endpoint: stimulated C-peptide at 12 months (measures beta cell mass/function)
- Secondary: HbA1c, insulin dose, time-in-range, autoantibody titers, Treg frequency

**Why this trial doesn't exist:**
1. No pharma company profits from fasting (you can't patent not eating)
2. Safety/liability concerns for fasting in T1DM patients
3. Academic funding is limited for dietary interventions
4. The T1DM community is focused on high-tech solutions (stem cells, CRISPR)

**Who could run it:**
- Longo's group at USC (they have the FMD expertise)
- JDRF-funded academic center (they fund novel approaches)
- Combination with a teplizumab study (piggyback on existing infrastructure)

## The Practical Protocol (What a Operator Could Try Today)

**IMPORTANT: This is NOT medical advice. T1DM patients should NEVER fast without medical supervision.**

That said, the theoretical protocol based on available evidence:

1. **Baseline**: Newly diagnosed or honeymoon T1DM, C-peptide > 0.2 nmol/L, on insulin, with CGM
2. **Preparation**: Teplizumab course (if accessible) to dampen autoimmunity
3. **Monthly FMD cycle** (5 days):
   - Day 1: 1100 kcal (high fat, low protein, low carb)
   - Days 2-5: 750 kcal (same macros)
   - Insulin reduced ~50-70% (MUST be done with endocrinologist)
   - CGM alarming at 70 mg/dL and 250 mg/dL
   - Ketone monitoring twice daily (DKA prevention)
4. **Refeeding** (days 6-7): Gradual calorie increase, protein reintroduction (mTOR reactivation signal)
5. **Between cycles** (25 days): Normal diet, normal insulin, add semaglutide (GLP-1, beta cell protection)
6. **Monitor**: Monthly C-peptide, quarterly HbA1c, autoantibody panel every 3 months
7. **Duration**: 6-12 cycles (6-12 months). Assess for C-peptide improvement.

## The Gap

The gap on this mountain is not mechanism (understood) or feasibility (achievable) — it's **clinical courage.** Someone has to run the trial. The biology says it should work. The data in mice says it works. The human organoid data says the pathway exists in human tissue. But no one has put a T1DM operator through cyclical FMD with proper monitoring and measured beta cell regeneration.

This is an execution gap, driven by the medical establishment's bias toward high-tech pharmaceutical solutions over dietary interventions that can't be patented.
