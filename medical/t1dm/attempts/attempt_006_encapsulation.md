# Attempt 006: Encapsulation Devices (Vertex VX-264, ViaCyte, others)

## Approach
Enclose beta cells (stem cell-derived or donor) in a semipermeable membrane/device. The membrane allows glucose and insulin to pass but blocks immune cells and antibodies. Physical barrier instead of immunological tolerance.

## Key Results
- **VX-264 (Vertex)**: Subcutaneous device with stem cell islets. DISCONTINUED March 2025. C-peptide increases were not at levels necessary for clinical benefit. The device did not deliver sufficient islet engraftment.
- **ViaCyte PEC-Encap**: Earlier generation. Similar issues with fibrosis and cell survival.
- **Multiple academic devices**: Alginate hydrogels, PDMS membranes, 3D-printed scaffolds. None have achieved >2 year function in humans.

## Why It Fails
1. **Fibrotic overgrowth.** The body treats the device as a foreign body. Fibroblasts deposit collagen around it within weeks-months. This fibrotic capsule blocks oxygen and nutrient transport. Beta cells inside starve and die.
2. **Oxygen limitation.** Beta cells are among the most oxygen-demanding cells in the body (they're heavily vascularized in native islets). Encapsulated cells are avascular — oxygen must diffuse through the membrane AND the fibrotic capsule. Hypoxia kills them.
3. **Size constraints.** To encapsulate enough islets for insulin independence (~500K-1M islets), the device must be large. Large devices have worse surface-area-to-volume ratios → worse diffusion → more cell death in the center.
4. **No vascularization.** Native islets are exquisitely vascularized. Encapsulated islets have no blood supply. This limits both nutrient delivery and glucose sensing speed.

## What It Teaches Us
- **The immune system can't be tricked by a physical barrier long-term.** The body's foreign body response (fibrosis) is too strong. Even "biocompatible" materials eventually get encapsulated.
- **Encapsulation is an engineering proxy for the biological problem.** If we had immunological tolerance, we wouldn't need a device. The device exists because we can't solve the biology.
- **VX-264's failure is definitive for this generation of devices.** Vertex had the best cells (proven functional in VX-880) and significant engineering resources. If they couldn't make it work, the device technology needs a fundamental breakthrough, not incremental improvement.

## The Gap It Reveals
Physical barriers can't substitute for biological tolerance. The immune system finds a way (fibrosis). The gap remains immunological.

## Status: FAILED — fibrosis defeats physical barriers
