# Attempt 007: CRISPR Hypoimmune Beta Cells (Sana Biotechnology)

## Approach
Use CRISPR-Cas12b to gene-edit donor islet cells:
1. Knock out HLA class I and II (prevents T cell recognition — cells become "invisible")
2. Overexpress CD47 ("don't eat me" signal — prevents innate immune killing by macrophages/NK cells)
3. Transplant via intramuscular injection (17 injections, not portal vein)
No immunosuppression required.

## Key Results (NEJM, August 2025)
- **First patient**: 42-year-old man, T1DM for 30+ years
- 17 intramuscular injections of CRISPR-edited islets (UP421)
- **No immunosuppression given**
- Cells survived 6+ months
- Glucose-dependent insulin production (measurable C-peptide)
- Evaded BOTH autoimmune attack AND allogeneic rejection
- First human proof that hypoimmune engineering works for T1DM

## Why It's Not Yet a Cure
1. **Single patient, short follow-up.** 6 months is promising but T1DM is lifelong. Need years of data.
2. **C-peptide but not insulin independence.** The patient still needs exogenous insulin. The cell dose or engraftment was not enough for full independence.
3. **Intramuscular site.** Not the native pancreatic niche. Long-term function at this site is unknown.
4. **Off-target edits.** CRISPR editing carries risk of unintended mutations. Long-term oncogenicity surveillance needed.
5. **HLA knockout = infection/cancer risk?** Cells invisible to immune system could theoretically harbor infections or become malignant without immune surveillance. Mitigated by CD47 (which is a "don't eat me" not a "don't kill me if I'm infected" signal) but the concern exists.
6. **Scalability.** Still uses donor islets (not stem cells). Sana has suspended other programs to focus here. Manufacturing scale unclear.

## What It Teaches Us
- **THE IMMUNE PROBLEM IS SOLVABLE.** This is the most important result in T1DM in years. The autoimmune attack AND allorejection can both be defeated by gene editing. No drugs needed.
- **The approach bypasses the tolerance gap entirely.** Instead of teaching the immune system to tolerate beta cells (the gap), you make the beta cells invisible to the immune system. Different philosophy: instead of fixing the attacker, cloak the target.
- **Intramuscular transplant works.** You don't need the pancreas or the liver. Beta cells can function from muscle. This simplifies surgery dramatically.
- **Convergence with stem cells.** Apply the same CRISPR edits to stem cell-derived beta cells (Vertex VX-880 type) → unlimited supply + immune invisibility. That's the endgame.

## The Gap It Reveals
This attempt doesn't point to a gap — it points to a SOLUTION. The gap (immune protection) may be closable via gene editing rather than immune tolerance. The remaining gaps are:
1. Dose optimization (enough cells for insulin independence)
2. Long-term safety (off-target, oncogenicity)
3. Combining with unlimited stem cell supply (Sana + Vertex-type cells)

## Status: BREAKTHROUGH — first proof that gene editing solves the immune problem
