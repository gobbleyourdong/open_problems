# T1DM Gap Analysis — After 8 Attempts

## The Pattern (Updated)

| # | Attempt | What it solves | What it doesn't | Status |
|---|---------|---------------|-----------------|--------|
| 001 | Teplizumab | Delays onset 2yr | Non-specific, temporary | PARTIAL |

## The Gap Has SHIFTED

After 5 attempts (001-005), the gap was: **antigen-specific immune tolerance.**

After 8 attempts (adding 006-008), the gap has shifted. Two approaches may BYPASS tolerance entirely:

1. **CRISPR hypoimmune (007)**: Don't teach the immune system tolerance — make the cells invisible. Works in first human. Needs dose optimization + stem cell combination.

2. **Autologous iPSC (008)**: Use the patient's own cells — no rejection, and somehow no autoimmune recurrence. Works in first patient. Mechanism unknown.

## The New Gap

The gap is no longer a single wall. It's a **fork**:

### Fork A: Universal donor cells + immune invisibility
Combine Vertex-type stem cell differentiation with Sana-type CRISPR editing.
- Unlimited supply (stem cells) + immune invisible (CRISPR HLA-KO + CD47)
- Remaining gaps: dose optimization, long-term safety, oncogenicity surveillance, manufacturing scale
- Timeline: 3-5 years to clinical proof, 5-8 years to approved therapy

### Fork B: Autologous cells
Patient-derived iPSC/CiPSC → islet clusters → transplant.
- No immune issues at all (self cells)
- Remaining gaps: why doesn't autoimmunity recur? (understand mechanism), manufacturing per-patient, cost
- Timeline: 5-10 years (manufacturing bottleneck)

### Fork C: True immune tolerance (original gap)
Antigen-specific tolerogenic immunomodulation.
- Would work with ANY cell source and fix the ROOT CAUSE
- Remaining gaps: multi-antigen delivery, tolerogenic context, durability, epitope spreading
- Most promising lead: mRNA tolerogenic vaccines (preclinical, T1DM incidence 67%→13% in mice)
- Timeline: 7-15 years (most uncertain)

## The Anti-Problem (Updated)

### Original: "What would a patient whose immune system spontaneously tolerated beta cells look like?"
Still valid. Study non-progressors, discordant twins, honeymoon patients.

### New: "Why did Deng's autologous cells survive?"
This is the most urgent question. Three hypotheses:
1. **Site privilege** — rectus sheath is immunologically quiet → test by transplanting autologous cells elsewhere
2. **Autoimmune burnout** — 25yr of T1DM exhausted autoreactive T cells → test in newly diagnosed patients
3. **CiPSC difference** — chemically reprogrammed cells present different antigens → compare CiPSC vs native beta cell surface markers

If hypothesis 2 is true: autologous cells only work in long-duration patients (disappointing but still useful).
If hypothesis 1 or 3 is true: potentially universal.

### New: "What would happen if you gave Sana's hypoimmune cells to 1000 patients?"
The n=1 is spectacular. But:
- Do the cells maintain function for years?
- Do they accumulate mutations without immune surveillance?
- Can the dose be increased enough for insulin independence?
- Can this be manufactured from stem cells (not donor islets)?

## Convergence

The three forks are not independent. They converge:

**Vertex stem cells + Sana CRISPR edits + PolTREG Treg support = the combination cure.**

- Unlimited beta cells (Vertex solves supply)
- Immune invisible (Sana solves rejection + autoimmunity)
- Treg backup (PolTREG provides durable immune regulation as safety net)

This combination addresses every failure mode from attempts 001-008. No single approach is sufficient. The combination might be.

## The Bridge

Unlike NS (where the gap is a single mathematical statement), the T1DM gap is an engineering integration problem. The pieces exist:
- Functional beta cells: YES (Vertex, Deng)
- Immune protection: YES (Sana CRISPR, OR autologous, OR Tregs)
- Durability: MAYBE (PolTREG 12yr data, Deng 1yr data)

The bridge is **combining them in one therapy and proving it at scale.** This is not a conceptual gap — it's an execution gap. The science is largely done. The engineering and clinical validation remain.
