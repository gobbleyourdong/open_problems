# Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus (T1DM)

## The Problem
Autoimmune destruction of pancreatic beta cells leads to absolute insulin deficiency. No cure exists. Current treatment is lifelong exogenous insulin. The goal: restore endogenous insulin production or prevent/reverse the autoimmune attack.

## Status: UNSOLVED

## Key Biology

| Component | What | Role |
|-----------|------|------|
| Beta cells | Islets of Langerhans, pancreas | Produce insulin in response to glucose |
| Autoreactive T cells | CD4+ and CD8+ | Destroy beta cells (the attack) |
| Autoantibodies | GAD65, IA-2, ZnT8, insulin | Biomarkers of autoimmunity (not the cause) |
| HLA genes | HLA-DR3, HLA-DR4, DQ8, DQ2 | Genetic susceptibility (~50% of risk) |
| Regulatory T cells (Tregs) | CD4+CD25+FoxP3+ | Suppress autoreactive T cells (the defense) |
| Insulin | Peptide hormone (51 AA) | Glucose uptake, anabolism |
| C-peptide | Byproduct of proinsulin cleavage | Marker of residual beta cell function |

## The Autoimmune Cascade
1. Genetic susceptibility (HLA + ~60 non-HLA loci)
2. Environmental trigger (viral? microbiome? diet? unknown)
3. Breaking of immune tolerance to beta cell antigens
4. Insulitis: immune infiltration of islets
5. Progressive beta cell destruction (years, often silent)
6. Clinical diagnosis when ~80-90% of beta cells destroyed
7. Honeymoon period: residual C-peptide, easier control
8. Total beta cell loss: brittle diabetes, no endogenous insulin

## Current Approaches

### Prevention (pre-clinical T1DM)
- **Teplizumab** (anti-CD3): FDA approved 2022 for Stage 2 T1DM. Delays onset by ~2 years. Does NOT prevent.
- **Oral insulin / intranasal insulin**: Tolerance induction attempts. Mixed results.
- **GAD-alum vaccine**: Phase 3 failed (but hints in HLA-DR3-DQ2 subgroup)
- **Abatacept** (CTLA-4-Ig): Preserved C-peptide at 2 years, effect waned

### Cure attempts (established T1DM)
- **Islet transplantation** (Edmonton protocol): Works but requires lifelong immunosuppression + donor shortage
- **Stem cell-derived beta cells** (Vertex/ViaCyte): VX-880 Phase 1/2 showing C-peptide restoration, still needs immunosuppression
- **Encapsulation** (Semma/ViaCyte): Protect cells from immune system without drugs. Device biocompatibility unsolved.
- **Gene therapy**: Reprogram alpha cells → beta cells, or make cells invisible to immune system (HLA engineering)
- **Treg therapy**: Expand operator's own regulatory T cells, reinfuse to suppress autoimmunity
- **Combination**: Teplizumab + stem cell beta cells + encapsulation?

### The real gap
Every approach either:
1. Suppresses the immune system globally (immunosuppression — unacceptable long-term risk)
2. Delays but doesn't stop the autoimmune attack (teplizumab)
3. Replaces beta cells without addressing the autoimmune cause (transplant, stem cells)

**The gap: antigen-specific immune tolerance.** Teach the immune system to STOP attacking beta cells WITHOUT suppressing its ability to fight infections and cancer. This is the Holy Grail of autoimmune disease, not just T1DM.

## systematic approach Adaptation

The systematic approach was designed for mathematical proofs but the structure applies:
- **Papers → Manifest**: immunology literature, clinical trial data, single-cell RNA-seq datasets
- **theory track (theory)**: mechanistic models of immune tolerance, pathway analysis, in silico drug screening
- **numerical track (numerics)**: genomic/transcriptomic data analysis, clinical trial meta-analysis, molecular dynamics
- **Proof attempts → Treatment attempts**: each therapy tried is an "attempt" with a documented failure mode
- **The gap**: same concept — what single mechanism, if solved, would unlock the cure?
- **Anti-problem**: "What would a operator whose immune system spontaneously tolerized beta cells look like?" (These patients exist — spontaneous remission cases. Study them.)
