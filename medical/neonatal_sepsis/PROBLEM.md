# Neonatal Enteroviral Sepsis (Coxsackievirus B)

## The Problem
Systemic CVB infection in neonates (first 28 days of life). Multi-organ involvement: heart, liver, brain, adrenals. High mortality rate (up to 30-50% for severe cases). No antiviral treatment. CVB1-4 most commonly implicated. Vertical transmission (mother → infant) or postnatal exposure.

## Status: UNSOLVED (supportive care, IVIG sometimes used)

## Key Biology

| Component | What | Role |
|-----------|------|------|
| Neonatal immune system | Immature T/B cells, low IgG | Cannot mount effective antiviral response |
| Maternal antibodies | Transplacental IgG | Protective IF mother has CVB-specific antibodies |
| CVB1-4 | Primary causative serotypes | Multi-organ tropism in neonates |
| Myocarditis | Heart involvement | Leading cause of neonatal death from CVB |
| Hepatic necrosis | Liver involvement | Fulminant hepatic failure, coagulopathy |
| Encephalitis | Brain involvement | Seizures, developmental damage |
| IVIG | Pooled immunoglobulins | Contains anti-CVB antibodies, efficacy uncertain |

## The Cascade
1. Maternal viremia OR postnatal fecal-oral exposure
2. Neonate lacks specific antibodies (no prior exposure)
3. Immature innate immunity can't contain initial replication
4. Unchecked viremia → simultaneous infection of heart, liver, brain, adrenals
5. Fulminant multi-organ failure
6. If survived: potential for persistent infection → later T1DM, DCM, etc.

## Connection to T1DM
- Neonatal CVB infection may be the EARLIEST seeding event for later T1DM
- TEDDY/DAISY studies: enteroviral seroconversion in infancy correlates with islet autoantibody appearance
- Prevention: maternal CVB vaccination (if it existed) would prevent both neonatal sepsis AND later T1DM
- The Soppela VLP vaccine (VLP∆VP4) addresses this — remove VP4 to avoid ADE
