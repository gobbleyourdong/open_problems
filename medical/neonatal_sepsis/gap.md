# Neonatal Sepsis — Gap Analysis (Initial)

## Why This Is the Most Urgent of the 12

Mortality rate 30-50% in severe cases. These are newborns. Every other disease on the list has time — neonatal sepsis does not.

## The Gap: No Antiviral Exists for Neonates

```
Neonate exposed to CVB → immature immune system can't contain it →
unchecked viremia → simultaneous heart + liver + brain infection →
multi-organ failure → death (days to weeks)
```

Current treatment: **supportive care + IVIG (uncertain efficacy)**. That's it.

## The Fork

### Fork A: Treatment (acute neonatal CVB sepsis)
- Pleconaril (enteroviral capsid inhibitor) — developed but FDA rejected in 2002 (safety concerns for rhinovirus indication)
- Pocapavir — capsid inhibitor, used compassionately for neonatal enteroviral sepsis
- Fluoxetine — CVB 2C ATPase inhibitor, but dosing in neonates is uncharted
- **IVIG with high anti-CVB titer** — passive immunity while neonate mounts own response
- **The gap**: no approved antiviral, and off-label options have no neonatal PK data

### Fork B: Prevention (maternal)
- **CVB vaccine for pregnant women** — transfer of anti-CVB IgG across placenta
- Soppela VLP vaccine (VLP-deltaVP4) — would prevent neonatal infection entirely
- Maternal screening + IVIG for seronegative mothers
- **This is the highest-impact intervention**: one vaccine prevents the seed event for all 12 diseases

### Fork C: Prevention (early life)
- Neonatal CVB vaccination (if safe in first month of life)
- Breastfeeding promotion (IgA in breast milk neutralizes enteral CVB)
- NICU hygiene protocols (fecal-oral transmission prevention)

## The Anti-Problem
"Why do most neonates survive CVB exposure?" Maternal anti-CVB antibodies. Neonates with anti-CVB IgG from seropositive mothers clear the virus. The ones who die are born to seronegative mothers.

**This is entirely a problem of passive immunity.** The solution is guaranteed maternal seropositivity — either by vaccination or by IVIG for seronegative mothers.

## Connection to T1DM
Neonatal CVB infection may be the patient's origin event. If CVB seeded the pancreas in infancy, persisted as TD mutants for years, and finally triggered autoimmunity at T1DM diagnosis — then preventing neonatal infection prevents the entire cascade of all 12 diseases.

## Priority
This problem has the **clearest single intervention**: a CVB vaccine. Every dollar and hour spent on CVB vaccine development has the highest ROI of any intervention across all 12 diseases.
