# Attempt 002: IVIG Bridge Protocol — Actionable Now, No Vaccine Needed

## The Problem With Attempt 001
The maternal CVB vaccine (VLP-ΔVP4) is the endgame but doesn't exist for human use yet. Timeline: 5-10 years minimum. Neonates are dying NOW.

## The Bridge: Targeted IVIG for High-Risk Neonates

### Who Dies from Neonatal CVB Sepsis?
Neonates born to CVB-seronegative mothers. No transplacental IgG → no passive immunity → unchecked viremia → multi-organ failure.

### Who Survives?
Neonates born to CVB-seropositive mothers. Transplacental anti-CVB IgG neutralizes virus during viremia → infection contained → survival.

**The entire difference is maternal antibody status.**

## The Protocol

### Step 1: Identify high-risk pregnancies
Screen maternal serum for CVB neutralizing antibodies (CVB1-5) during routine prenatal labs:
- Seropositive (titer ≥1:32 for all 5 serotypes): low risk → standard care
- Seronegative for ANY serotype: elevated risk → proceed to Step 2

### Step 2: Passive immunization for seronegative mothers
**Option A: Maternal IVIG (antepartum)**
- Standard IVIG contains pooled anti-CVB antibodies (most adults are seropositive)
- Administer at 28-32 weeks gestation
- Transplacental IgG transfer occurs primarily in third trimester
- Target: cord blood anti-CVB titer ≥1:32
- Cost: $5,000-10,000 per dose
- Precedent: IVIG is used in pregnancy for alloimmune thrombocytopenia, anti-Rh

**Option B: Neonatal IVIG (postpartum)**
- For neonates born to seronegative mothers OR neonates presenting with symptoms
- IVIG 500mg/kg within 24 hours of birth (prophylactic) or at symptom onset (therapeutic)
- Provides immediate anti-CVB antibodies
- Duration: IgG half-life ~21 days → covers the highest-risk neonatal period
- Cost: $2,000-5,000 per dose

**Option C: Hyperimmune CVB-IVIG (ideal but doesn't exist)**
- IVIG manufactured from donors with HIGH anti-CVB titers
- Like hepatitis B immune globulin (HBIG) or varicella VZIG
- Would provide concentrated anti-CVB activity at lower volume
- **Development path**: screen blood donors for CVB neutralizing titers → manufacture dedicated product
- This is a PRODUCT DEVELOPMENT opportunity

### Step 3: Surveillance
- Stool enteroviral PCR at birth, day 3, day 7
- If positive: therapeutic IVIG + supportive care + consider fluoxetine (off-label, no neonatal data)
- Cardiac monitoring (echo, troponin) — neonatal myocarditis is the leading cause of CVB death
- Liver function monitoring — fulminant hepatitis is second cause

## Cost-Benefit Analysis

```
WITHOUT screening + IVIG:
  ~300-500 neonatal CVB sepsis cases/year (US)
  ~100-200 deaths (30-50% mortality)
  ~200-300 survivors with potential chronic sequelae (T1DM, DCM, ME/CFS)
  
WITH screening + IVIG:
  ~4M births/year × $50 CVB serology = $200M screening cost
  ~20-30% seronegative for ≥1 serotype = 800K-1.2M identified
  ~5% of seronegative mothers have infants exposed to CVB = 40K-60K at risk
  IVIG for exposed neonates only (Option B, reactive): $2K × ~500 = $1M
  
  Deaths prevented: ~100-200/year
  Chronic disease prevented: ~200-300 cases (some of which become T1DM, DCM, ME/CFS)
  Cost per death prevented: ~$1M / 150 = ~$7,000
  Cost per lifetime of insulin prevented: incalculable
```

**The screening is expensive ($200M) but the targeted IVIG is cheap ($1M).** The question is whether universal screening is justified. Alternative: screen only during CVB outbreaks (detected via enteroviral surveillance).

## The Faster Path: Targeted Screening During Outbreaks

CVB outbreaks are seasonal (summer/fall) and regional. During documented outbreaks:
1. Alert OB units in the region
2. Screen pregnant women near term for CVB serology
3. Seronegative mothers in outbreak zone → IVIG at delivery
4. Cost: orders of magnitude less than universal screening

**This is implementable with existing infrastructure.** No new drugs, no new technology. Just awareness + a serological test + IVIG that's already manufactured.

## Connection to All 12 Diseases

If neonatal CVB infection seeds every organ during initial viremia:
- Preventing neonatal infection prevents T1DM seeding (→ 30+ years of insulin)

**One IVIG dose at birth may prevent a lifetime of chronic disease across multiple organs.**
