# Attempt 001: Maternal CVB Vaccination — Prevention of All 12 Diseases

## Approach
Vaccinate pregnant women against CVB serotypes B1-B5 → transplacental IgG transfer → neonate born with anti-CVB antibodies → prevents neonatal sepsis AND the initial seeding event for all downstream CVB diseases.

## Why This Is the Highest-ROI Intervention

One vaccine, given once (during pregnancy), prevents:
1. Neonatal sepsis (30-50% mortality → ~0%)
2. T1DM (if neonatal infection is the seeding event)
3. Myocarditis, DCM, ME/CFS, pancreatitis, pericarditis, hepatitis, pleurodynia, meningitis, encephalitis, orchitis

**No other single intervention addresses all 12 diseases simultaneously.**

## The Vaccine Candidate: Soppela VLP (VLP-ΔVP4)

From the T1DM literature (attempt 044 and related):
- Virus-Like Particles (VLPs) with VP4 deleted
- VP4 deletion prevents ADE (antibody-dependent enhancement) — critical safety feature
- Multivalent: covers CVB1-5 serotypes
- Preclinical: strong neutralizing antibody response in animal models
- No live virus → safe in pregnancy

## The Protocol

### Maternal vaccination
- VLP-ΔVP4 multivalent (CVB1-5) during second trimester (weeks 20-28)
- Booster at 4 weeks if needed for adequate titer
- Target: maternal anti-CVB IgG ≥1:128 at delivery

### Monitoring
- Cord blood anti-CVB IgG titers at delivery
- Neonatal enteroviral surveillance (stool PCR at 1, 2, 4 weeks)
- Infant islet autoantibody screening at 6, 12, 24 months

### Primary endpoint (neonatal sepsis prevention)
- Neonatal enteroviral sepsis incidence in vaccinated vs unvaccinated mothers

### Secondary endpoint (T1DM prevention)
- Islet autoantibody seroconversion in offspring at 5, 10, 15 years
- This is the TEDDY/DAISY framework extended with an intervention

## The Gap
**The vaccine doesn't exist yet (for human use).** Soppela VLP is preclinical.

### What's needed
1. GMP manufacturing of multivalent VLP-ΔVP4
2. Phase 1 safety (non-pregnant adults first, then pregnant women)
3. Phase 2 immunogenicity (neutralizing antibody titers)
4. Phase 3 efficacy (neonatal sepsis prevention — achievable endpoint)
5. Phase 4 long-term follow-up (T1DM prevention — decades)

### Timeline
- Vaccine to Phase 1: 2-3 years (if funded)
- Neonatal sepsis efficacy data: 5-7 years
- T1DM prevention data: 15-20 years

## Interim Alternative: IVIG for Seronegative Mothers

While the vaccine is developed:
- Screen pregnant women for anti-CVB IgG
- Seronegative mothers (especially in CVB-endemic areas) → CVB-specific IVIG at 28 weeks
- Provides passive immunity to neonate
- **Immediately actionable, no vaccine development needed**

### Cost
- CVB IgG screening: ~$50
- IVIG (if needed): ~$5,000-10,000 per dose
- Expensive but justified for high-risk pregnancies (prior neonatal CVB death, CVB outbreak)

## Anti-Problem Applied
"Why do most neonates survive CVB?"
→ Because their mothers are seropositive and transferred IgG.
→ Neonatal death occurs in seronegative mothers.
→ Make all mothers seropositive. Problem solved.
