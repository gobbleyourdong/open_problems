## The Neonatal Immune Gap

| Parameter | Neonate | Adult |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Multi-organ persistence probability | 72.5% | 38-55% (per organ) |
| Critical infection threshold | 14 cells | 74-378 cells |
| IFN response speed | Immature (delayed 48-96h) | Mature (delayed 4-12h) |
| Maternal antibody protection | Wanes by 4-6 weeks postpartum | N/A |
| NK cell function | Immature | Mature |
| CVB-specific T cell priming | None | Possible if prior exposure |

The neonatal critical threshold of only **14 cells** is a consequence of all these deficits simultaneously. A single infected cell can lead to multi-organ spread in some neonates.

## Vaccine Prevention Analysis

Maternal vaccination before pregnancy (preconception or first trimester):
- Maternal IgG crosses placenta → neonatal passive immunity
- Vaccine preventable fraction: **95%** (highest of all 12 diseases)
- DALYs averted: 4,853,750/year
- Deaths prevented: 51,300/year

This is the **highest vaccine ROI** of any CVB disease. A pentavalent CVB vaccine given to women of childbearing age would have the greatest impact on neonatal mortality of any vaccine since Hib.
