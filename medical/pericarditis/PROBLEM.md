# Viral Pericarditis (Coxsackievirus B)

## The Problem
Inflammation of the pericardium (sac surrounding the heart). CVB is a leading viral cause. Usually self-limiting but can become recurrent (30% recurrence rate) or progress to constrictive pericarditis or effusive-constrictive pericarditis. Colchicine is standard treatment — notably, colchicine also blocks NLRP3.

## Status: TREATABLE (colchicine + NSAIDs), but recurrence is common

## Key Biology

| Component | What | Role |
|-----------|------|------|
| Pericardium | Fibrous sac surrounding heart | Protects heart, reduces friction |
| CVB1-5 | Causative serotypes | Direct pericardial cell infection |
| NLRP3 inflammasome | IL-1β production platform | Drives pericardial inflammation |
| Colchicine | Anti-inflammatory drug | Disrupts NLRP3 microtubule transport, $5/month |
| IL-1β | Pro-inflammatory cytokine | Key mediator of pericardial inflammation |

## Connection to T1DM
- Colchicine (standard pericarditis treatment) hits NLRP3 — same target as BHB in T1DM protocol
- CVB pericarditis and myocarditis can co-occur (myopericarditis)
- Recurrent pericarditis may indicate persistent CVB infection — same TD mutant mechanism
- Colchicine 0.5mg daily already recommended in T1DM protocol as cheap NLRP3 inhibitor
