# Attempt 001: Colchicine + Fluoxetine for Recurrent Pericarditis

## Approach
Add CVB clearance (fluoxetine) to standard colchicine therapy. Test the hypothesis that recurrent pericarditis is caused by persistent CVB, and that clearing the virus eliminates recurrence.

## Why This Is the Best First Clinical Proof

| Advantage | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Standard treatment exists | Colchicine + NSAID is guideline-directed → ethical to ADD fluoxetine |
| Measurable endpoint | Recurrence yes/no at 12 months (binary, hard) |
| High recurrence rate | 30% baseline → easy to detect reduction |
| Both drugs are cheap generics | Colchicine $5/mo, fluoxetine $4/mo |
| Both drugs are well-characterized | Decades of safety data, known interactions |
| Patient population accessible | Recurrent pericarditis is common in cardiology clinics |
| Small trial sufficient | 60 patients per arm detects 30%→10% reduction at 80% power |

## The Protocol

### Control arm (standard of care)
- Colchicine 0.5mg daily × 6 months
- Ibuprofen 600mg TID × 2 weeks, then taper

### Intervention arm
- Colchicine 0.5mg daily × 6 months
- Ibuprofen 600mg TID × 2 weeks, then taper
- **Fluoxetine 20mg daily × 6 months** (the addition)
- **FMD: one 5-day cycle at month 2** (autophagy boost, optional arm)

### Primary endpoint
- Recurrence of pericarditis at 12 months (≥1 episode meeting ESC diagnostic criteria)

### Secondary endpoints
- Time to first recurrence
- Number of recurrence episodes
- CRP trajectory
- Enteroviral RNA in serum (if detectable)
- Patient-reported chest pain frequency

## Drug Interaction Check
- Colchicine + fluoxetine: no significant interaction
- Colchicine is CYP3A4 substrate; fluoxetine is CYP2D6 inhibitor → minimal overlap
- Both are well-tolerated at these doses
- NSAID + SSRI: increased bleeding risk (monitor, use PPI if needed)

## Prediction
- Control arm: ~30% recurrence at 12 months (historical rate)
- Intervention arm: <10% recurrence at 12 months
- Mechanism: fluoxetine clears CVB TD mutants → no viral trigger for NLRP3 reactivation → colchicine can actually be stopped without relapse

## The Stronger Prediction
If the viral hypothesis is correct, the intervention arm should show:
1. Lower recurrence during treatment (months 0-6)
2. **Lower recurrence AFTER stopping treatment** (months 6-12) — because the virus is gone
3. The control arm recurs when colchicine is stopped — because the virus is still there

This differential pattern (divergence after drug cessation) would be strong evidence for viral persistence as the recurrence mechanism.

## Cost of Trial
- Drugs: ~$10/patient/month → negligible
- Monitoring: standard cardiology follow-up
- Sample size: 120 patients (60 per arm)
- Duration: 18 months (6 months enrollment + 12 months follow-up)
- **This is a cheap, fast, low-risk trial with a clear binary endpoint.**
