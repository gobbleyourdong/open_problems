# Certificate 001: Colchicine Efficacy in Recurrent Pericarditis

## Claim

Colchicine reduces the recurrence rate of pericarditis by approximately 50% compared to standard anti-inflammatory therapy alone. The number needed to treat (NNT) is approximately 4 (meaning 4 patients need to be treated with colchicine to prevent 1 recurrence). This has been demonstrated in 4 independent randomized controlled trials, all led by Imazio and colleagues, and confirmed by Cochrane meta-analysis.

## Sources

1. **Imazio M, Bobbio M, Cecchi E et al. (2005)** "Colchicine as first-choice therapy for recurrent pericarditis: results of the CORE (COlchicine for REcurrent pericarditis) trial." *Arch Intern Med* 165:1987-1991. Open-label RCT, n=84. Colchicine + aspirin vs aspirin alone for recurrent pericarditis. Recurrence at 18 months: 24% vs 51%. Relative risk reduction ~53%.
2. **Imazio M, Bobbio M, Cecchi E et al. (2005)** "Colchicine in addition to conventional therapy for acute pericarditis: results of the COlchicine for acute PEricarditis (COPE) trial." *Circulation* 112:2012-2016. Open-label RCT, n=120. Colchicine + aspirin vs aspirin alone for first episode of acute pericarditis. Recurrence at 18 months: 10.7% vs 32.3%. Relative risk reduction ~67%. NNT = 4.6.
3. **Imazio M, Brucato A, Cemin R et al. (2011)** "Colchicine for recurrent pericarditis (CORP): a randomized trial." *Ann Intern Med* 155:409-414. Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT, n=120. Recurrence at 18 months: 24% vs 55%. Relative risk reduction ~56%. NNT = 3.2.
4. **Imazio M, Belli R, Brucato A et al. (2014)** "Efficacy and safety of colchicine for treatment of multiple recurrences of pericarditis (CORP-2): a multicentre, double-blind, placebo-controlled, randomised trial." *Lancet* 383:2232-2237. Double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT, n=240. Recurrence at 18 months: 21.6% vs 42.5%. Relative risk reduction ~49%. NNT = 4.8.

## Cross-Verification

- **Four independent RCTs**: COPE (2005), CORE (2005), CORP (2011), CORP-2 (2014). All show consistent ~50% relative risk reduction. The later trials (CORP, CORP-2) are double-blind, placebo-controlled, eliminating bias concerns from the earlier open-label trials.
- **Cochrane meta-analysis**: Alabed et al. (2014) "Colchicine for pericarditis." Cochrane Database Syst Rev. Meta-analysis of all available RCTs confirms the ~50% reduction with high certainty.
- **Effect size consistency**: Across all 4 trials, the relative risk reduction ranges from 49-67%, with a pooled estimate of approximately 50%. This consistency across trials of varying design quality is strong evidence.
- **Guideline adoption**: ESC 2015 guidelines (Adler et al., Eur Heart J) give colchicine a Class I, Level of Evidence A recommendation for pericarditis. This is the highest level of guideline endorsement.
- **Mechanism understood**: Colchicine inhibits microtubule polymerization, suppressing NLRP3 inflammasome assembly and neutrophil migration. This is the same NLRP3 pathway targeted by BHB (ketosis) in the T1DM protocol.

## Confidence

**VERY HIGH** -- This is RCT-grade evidence, replicated in 4 independent trials (including 2 double-blind placebo-controlled), confirmed by Cochrane meta-analysis, and adopted into Class I clinical guidelines. The effect size is large, consistent, and clinically meaningful. This is the gold standard of clinical evidence.
