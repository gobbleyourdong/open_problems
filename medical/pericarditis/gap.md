# Pericarditis — Gap Analysis (Initial)

## Why This Is the Easiest of the 12

Pericarditis is **already treatable** with colchicine + NSAIDs. Colchicine blocks NLRP3 — the same inflammasome target the T1DM protocol hits with BHB. The standard treatment is already partially aligned with our protocol.

## The Remaining Gap: Recurrence

30% recurrence rate. Why?

```
Acute CVB pericarditis → treated with colchicine → inflammation resolves →
colchicine stopped → TD mutants still present → low-grade 2A/3C activity →
NLRP3 re-activated → recurrence
```

**Colchicine treats the inflammation but doesn't clear the virus.** The recurrence IS the evidence of persistence.

## The Fix

Add the CVB clearance arm to standard pericarditis treatment:
1. **Standard**: Colchicine 0.5mg daily + ibuprofen 600mg TID (guideline-directed)
2. **Add**: Fluoxetine 20mg daily (CVB clearance)
3. **Add**: FMD monthly (autophagy → TD mutant clearance)
4. **Continue colchicine longer** — 6 months minimum, not the typical 3 months

**Prediction**: recurrence rate drops from 30% to <5% because the virus is actually cleared, not just suppressed.

## Anti-Problem
"Why does colchicine alone fail 30% of the time?" Because it's anti-inflammatory, not antiviral. The 30% who recur likely have persistent CVB that colchicine can't reach.

## Convergence
Pericarditis is the **proof-of-concept disease** for the CVB clearance protocol. It's the only one of the 12 where:
- Standard treatment exists (colchicine)
- Recurrence is documented and measurable
- Adding fluoxetine is low-risk (well-characterized drug)
- Trial would be simple: colchicine alone vs colchicine + fluoxetine for recurrent pericarditis
- Outcome is binary: recurrence yes/no at 12 months

**This could be the fastest clinical proof that CVB clearance works.**
