## The Candidates

### 1. Soppela VLP-ΔVP4 (Most Promising)
- **Type**: Virus-like particle, VP4 deleted
- **Serotypes**: Multivalent CVB1-5
- **Key innovation**: VP4 deletion prevents ADE (antibody-dependent enhancement)
  - Traditional vaccines against CVB risk ADE: sub-neutralizing antibodies help virus enter cells
  - Without VP4, the VLP can't mediate ADE even with sub-optimal antibody titers
  - This solves the main safety concern that killed previous CVB vaccine attempts
- **Status**: Preclinical (animal studies)
- **Developer**: Soppela (Finnish biotech)
- **Timeline to human trials**: 3-5 years (if funded)

### 2. Inactivated CVB vaccine (traditional approach)
- **Type**: Formalin-inactivated whole virus
- **Precedent**: IPV (inactivated polio vaccine) works for a related enterovirus
- **Problem**: ADE risk not eliminated (whole virus retains VP4)
- **Problem**: Manufacturing requires BSL-2+ facilities for live virus growth
- **Advantage**: Proven technology, faster regulatory path
- **Status**: Various preclinical programs

### 3. mRNA CVB vaccine
- **Type**: mRNA encoding CVB capsid proteins (VP1-VP3, without VP4)
- **Advantage**: rapid manufacturing (proven with COVID-19 mRNA vaccines)
- **Advantage**: VP4 can be excluded by design → no ADE risk
- **Advantage**: multivalent by mixing multiple mRNAs
- **Status**: Not in development for CVB specifically (but platform exists)
- **This should be pursued**: mRNA platform + CVB capsid antigens (minus VP4) = fastest path

### 4. Recombinant protein vaccine
- **Type**: Recombinant VP1 protein (major neutralizing antigen)
- **Advantage**: simple, scalable, no ADE risk (VP1 alone doesn't mediate ADE)
- **Disadvantage**: may not generate as broad an antibody response as VLP
- **Status**: Various preclinical

## The ADE Problem (Why No CVB Vaccine Exists Yet)

Antibody-dependent enhancement is THE reason there's no CVB vaccine despite decades of research:

```
Normal antibody response:
  Antibody + virus → neutralization → virus destroyed ✓

ADE:
  Sub-neutralizing antibody + virus → Fc receptor on macrophage →
  virus ENTERS macrophage → replicates INSIDE macrophage →
  MORE virus, not less → disease WORSE than no vaccine ✗

This happened with:
  Dengue vaccine (Dengvaxia → ADE in seronegative children)
  RSV vaccine (1960s formalin-inactivated → ADE in infants)
  
CVB risk:
  If vaccine generates antibodies against CVB but titers are low,
  those antibodies could ENHANCE infection instead of preventing it
```

**VLP-ΔVP4 solves this**: Without VP4, the VLP cannot mediate Fc receptor-mediated entry. The antibodies generated are ONLY neutralizing, never enhancing.

## Vaccination Strategy

### Strategy A: Infant vaccination (prevent lifetime of CVB diseases)
- Age: 2, 4, 6 months (with standard vaccination schedule)
- Multivalent: CVB1-5 (all disease-causing serotypes)
- Booster: 12-18 months
- **Prevents**: all 12 diseases in the individual

### Strategy B: Maternal vaccination (prevent neonatal sepsis + seed protection)
- Age: 28-32 weeks gestation (third trimester)
- Transplacental IgG → neonate born with anti-CVB antibodies
- **Prevents**: neonatal sepsis (immediate), early childhood CVB infection (6-12 months passive immunity)
- Bridge until infant can be actively vaccinated

### Strategy C: Catch-up vaccination (adults with existing CVB disease)
- For T1DM, ME/CFS, DCM patients
- Boost neutralizing antibodies to help clear persistent CVB
- **Not a cure alone** (TD mutants evade antibodies via exosomal escape)
- But may help: higher antibody titers → better control of any non-TD viral particles
- Combined with protocol (fluoxetine + autophagy): vaccine handles extracellular virus, protocol handles intracellular virus

## The Development Roadmap

```
YEAR 1-2: Preclinical
  ├── mRNA CVB vaccine design (VP1-VP3, no VP4, all 5 serotypes)
  ├── VLP-ΔVP4 scale-up
  ├── Animal immunogenicity and challenge studies
  └── ADE safety testing (the CRITICAL gate)

YEAR 2-3: Phase 1
  ├── Safety in healthy adults (18-50)
  ├── Immunogenicity (neutralizing titers against CVB1-5)
  └── Dose finding

YEAR 3-4: Phase 2
  ├── Expanded safety
  ├── Immunogenicity in target populations (infants, pregnant women)
  └── Correlate antibody titers with protection in natural CVB exposure

YEAR 4-7: Phase 3
  ├── Efficacy against clinical CVB disease
  ├── Primary endpoint: neonatal CVB sepsis prevention (fastest signal)
  ├── Secondary endpoint: T1DM prevention in offspring (requires 5-10yr follow-up)
  └── Maternal vaccination arm: cord blood titers, neonatal protection

YEAR 7+: Approval and rollout
  ├── Infant vaccination schedule integration
  ├── Maternal vaccination recommendation
  └── Catch-up vaccination for high-risk adults
```
