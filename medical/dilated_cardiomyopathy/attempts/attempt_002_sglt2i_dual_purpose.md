# Attempt 002: SGLT2 Inhibitors as Dual-Purpose Therapy in CVB-DCM

## The Insight

SGLT2 inhibitors (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin) are standard heart failure therapy with 20-30% mortality reduction (EMPEROR-Reduced, DAPA-HF). But they also have properties that directly address CVB persistence:

```
Standard HF benefit:          CVB-clearance benefit:
├── Natriuresis/diuresis      ├── Autophagy induction (AMPK activation)
├── Reduced preload           ├── NLRP3 inflammasome suppression
├── Improved energetics       ├── Ketogenesis → BHB production
├── Anti-remodeling           ├── Anti-fibrotic (TGF-β suppression)
└── Mortality reduction       └── Anti-inflammatory (IL-6, TNF-α reduction)
```

**SGLT2 inhibitors are already part of GDMT AND they enhance the CVB clearance protocol.** This is not adding a drug — it's recognizing that an existing drug has dual benefit.

## The Mechanisms

### 1. AMPK activation → Autophagy
- SGLT2i → caloric loss via glycosuria → energy deficit → AMPK activation
- AMPK → ULK1 phosphorylation → autophagy initiation
- Autophagy → clears TD mutants from cardiomyocytes
- **This is the FMD mechanism achieved pharmacologically**
- Advantage over FMD: continuous (daily drug vs monthly fast), and DCM patients may not tolerate fasting

### 2. Ketogenesis → BHB
- SGLT2i → glycosuria → glucose depletion → hepatic ketogenesis
- BHB levels rise 0.2-0.6 mM (modest but sustained)
- BHB → NLRP3 suppression (same as fasting/keto)
- BHB → HDAC inhibition → NF-κB transcriptional dampening
- **This is the keto/BHB mechanism achieved pharmacologically**

### 3. Anti-fibrotic
- SGLT2i → reduced TGF-β1 signaling → less fibroblast → myofibroblast transition
- Less collagen deposition → slower fibrosis progression
- This addresses the DCM-specific gap that the T1DM protocol doesn't cover

### 4. Anti-inflammatory
- IL-6 reduction (15-30% in clinical studies)
- TNF-α reduction
- CRP reduction
- Mechanisms partially via AMPK, partially via NLRP3, partially independent

## The Protocol

For CVB-positive DCM (confirmed by biopsy enteroviral RNA or clinical suspicion):

### Core HF therapy (GDMT)
- SGLT2i: **empagliflozin 10mg daily** (choose this one — most autophagy data)
- ACE-I/ARB/ARNI: sacubitril/valsartan 97/103mg BID (if tolerated)
- Beta-blocker: carvedilol 25mg BID
- MRA: spironolactone 25mg daily

### CVB clearance (added)
- Fluoxetine 20mg daily
- Autophagy boost: empagliflozin IS the autophagy boost (daily, not monthly like FMD)
- If tolerated: time-restricted eating (16:8) to further enhance autophagy
- FMD: CAUTIOUS — only if LVEF >35% and no recent decompensation

### Anti-inflammatory (added)
- BHB: empagliflozin IS the BHB source (modest but continuous)
- Colchicine 0.5mg daily
- Omega-3 3g EPA/DHA daily
- Vitamin D 5000 IU daily

### Anti-fibrotic (DCM-specific)
- Empagliflozin already has anti-fibrotic properties
- If fibrosis progressing: consider pirfenidone (off-label, used in pulmonary fibrosis)
- Cardiac MRI with LGE at baseline, 6 months, 12 months to track fibrosis

## The Empagliflozin Triple-Count

Empagliflozin counts as THREE protocol components:
1. Heart failure drug (standard indication)
2. Autophagy inducer (replaces FMD for patients who can't fast)
3. BHB source (replaces ketogenic diet for patients who can't do keto)

**One drug, three mechanisms, already FDA-approved, $10/month generic.**
