# Attempt 001: Halt Progression via CVB Clearance + Standard HF Therapy

## Approach
Combine the T1DM CVB clearance protocol with guideline-directed heart failure therapy. The hypothesis: if you stop 2A protease production, dystrophin rebuilds, DGC complex reassembles, sarcolemma stabilizes, no further cardiomyocyte loss.

## The Protocol

### Layer 1: Stop the virus (from T1DM)
- Fluoxetine 20mg daily (CVB 2C ATPase inhibitor)
- Itraconazole 200mg daily (OSBP/PI4KB lipid trafficking block) — **CAUTION: QT prolongation, monitor with existing cardiac disease**
- FMD monthly (autophagy → TD mutant clearance)

### Layer 2: Standard HF therapy (GDMT)
- ACE-I or ARB (ramipril 10mg or valsartan 160mg BID) — afterload reduction, anti-remodeling
- Beta-blocker (carvedilol 25mg BID or bisoprolol 10mg) — anti-remodeling, anti-arrhythmic
- MRA (spironolactone 25mg or eplerenone 50mg) — anti-fibrotic, mortality reduction
- **SGLT2 inhibitor (empagliflozin 10mg or dapagliflozin 10mg)** — mortality reduction + autophagy enhancement + anti-inflammatory → triple-purpose in CVB-DCM

### Layer 3: Anti-inflammatory (from T1DM)
- BHB via ketogenic periods or exogenous ketones — NLRP3 suppression
- Colchicine 0.5mg daily — NLRP3 (proven in pericarditis, plausible in DCM)
- Omega-3 3g daily — resolvin production
- CoQ10 400mg daily — mitochondrial support

## The Key Drug Interaction Concern
**Itraconazole + cardiac disease**: itraconazole has negative inotropic effects and is contraindicated in heart failure (FDA black box). In DCM patients, fluoxetine alone for CVB clearance, with autophagy (FMD) as the second antiviral arm. Itraconazole ONLY if cardiac function is preserved (LVEF > 50%).

## What This Solves
- Halts viral replication → stops dystrophin cleavage
- Standard GDMT manages symptoms and slows remodeling
- SGLT2i pulls double duty (HF therapy + autophagy/anti-inflammatory)

## What This Doesn't Solve
1. Existing fibrosis (need anti-fibrotic strategy)
2. Lost cardiomyocytes (need regeneration strategy)
3. Diagnostic certainty (need to prove THIS patient's DCM is CVB-caused)

## Critical Diagnostic Step
Before treating: **endomyocardial biopsy with enteroviral RNA RT-PCR**. Only ~35% of DCM is CVB-caused. Don't treat what isn't there.

Alternative non-invasive: cardiac MRI pattern (mid-wall LGE pattern typical of viral DCM vs subendocardial in ischemic).

## Status: THEORETICAL — framework for CVB-positive DCM patients
