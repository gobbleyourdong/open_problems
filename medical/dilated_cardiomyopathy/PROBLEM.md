# Dilated Cardiomyopathy (CVB-induced)

## The Problem
Chronic sequela of viral myocarditis. CVB persists as TD mutants in myocardium, 2A protease continuously cleaves dystrophin, progressive loss of sarcolemma integrity → chamber dilation → heart failure. Responsible for ~30% of acquired DCM cases. No antiviral treatment. End stage requires transplant.

## Status: UNSOLVED

## Key Biology

| Component | What | Role |
|-----------|------|------|
| Dystrophin | 427 kDa cytoskeletal protein | Links actin to extracellular matrix via DGC complex |
| DGC complex | Dystrophin-glycoprotein complex | Maintains sarcolemma integrity during contraction |
| 2A protease | CVB viral protease | Cleaves dystrophin at hinge 3 region → DGC disruption |
| TD mutants | Persistently replicating CVB | Low-grade 2A production → chronic dystrophin cleavage |
| Enteroviral RNA | Detectable by RT-PCR in biopsy | Found in ~35% of DCM patients at explant/biopsy |
| sCAR | Soluble CAR receptor | Experimental: blocks CVB entry, prevents DCM in mice |

## The Mechanism (Unique to CVB-DCM)
1. Acute myocarditis → incomplete viral clearance
2. CVB evolves TD mutant form (5' terminal deletion)
3. TD mutants replicate 100,000x slower but persist indefinitely
4. Low-level 2A protease production continues
5. 2A cleaves dystrophin at hinge 3 → DGC complex falls apart
6. Without DGC: sarcolemma tears during each contraction cycle
7. Progressive cardiomyocyte death → fibrosis → chamber dilation
8. Years-to-decades progression → symptomatic heart failure

## Connection to T1DM
- Identical viral persistence mechanism
- Same 2A protease — different substrate (dystrophin vs beta cell targets)
- **Protocol prediction**: clearing CVB should halt dystrophin cleavage and stop DCM progression
- T1DM patients are at elevated risk for subclinical CVB cardiomyopathy

## Anti-Problem
"Can dystrophin cleavage rate be measured as a biomarker?" If so, it tracks viral activity directly.
