# Attempt 018 — Sex Hormone × M2: Insulin → Androgens → Sebum → Malassezia
## Novel M5↔M2 Bridge via Androgen Intermediary | 2026-04-12

> The existing M5 analysis (attempt_009) connects diet/glycemic control to M2 via
> IGF-1 → mTORC1 → sebum. This attempt formalizes a SECOND, parallel M5↔M2 mechanism
> that operates via androgens: insulin resistance → reduced SHBG → elevated free testosterone
> → 5α-reductase → DHT → sebaceous gland hypertrophy → sebum ↑ → Malassezia substrate ↑.
>
> This bridge is specifically relevant to male T1DM patients and to T1DM women with PCOS
> features. It is independent of the IGF-1 pathway and predicts different intervention targets.

---

## The Claim

**Poor glycemic control in T1DM elevates free androgens via hepatic SHBG suppression, which
independently amplifies M2 (skin dysbiosis) through increased sebum production.**

The mechanism is: hyperinsulinemia/insulin resistance → hepatic SHBG synthesis suppressed →
more free testosterone → 5α-reductase type 1 in sebaceous glands → DHT → androgen receptor
in sebocytes → sebaceous gland hyperplasia + increased sebum output → Malassezia substrate
pool enlarged → M2 amplified.

---

## Mechanism

```
T1DM + suboptimal glycemic control (HbA1c >7.5%)
    ↓
Insulin resistance (peripheral + hepatic)
    ↓
Hepatic SHBG (sex hormone-binding globulin) synthesis SUPPRESSED by insulin:
    SHBG production is inversely proportional to hepatic insulin exposure
    High portal insulin → SHBG mRNA ↓ → SHBG protein secretion ↓
    ↓
With less SHBG, same total testosterone → MORE FREE TESTOSTERONE
    (free T = total T × [1 - SHBG-bound fraction])
    In T1DM: free T elevated by ~15-35% for same total T when SHBG low
    ↓
Free testosterone → 5α-reductase type 1 (SRD5A1):
    5α-reductase type 1 is expressed in: sebaceous glands, hair follicles, liver, skin
    (Type 1 is skin-dominant; type 2 is prostate/genital-dominant — distinct enzymes)
    ↓
Testosterone → DHT (5α-dihydrotestosterone) in sebaceous glands
    ↓
DHT → androgen receptor (AR) in sebocytes:
    AR is highly expressed in sebaceous glands
    DHT-AR complex → sebocyte proliferation + lipogenesis ↑
    ↓
Sebaceous gland HYPERPLASIA + increased SEBUM production
    ↓
Sebum composition shifted:
    - More C12-C14 medium-chain fatty acids (Malassezia preferred substrate)
    - More oleic acid (C18:1) → promotes Malassezia oleophilic species
    Malassezia lacks fatty acid synthase → completely dependent on exogenous fatty acids
    ↓
M2 AMPLIFIED: larger Malassezia substrate pool → greater Malassezia colonization density
    → more M2-driven KLK5 activation → more LL-37 → M4 threshold lowered
```

## T1DM-Specific Dynamics

### Why this is more pronounced in T1DM than in healthy individuals:

**T1DM with poor control:**
1. Exogenous insulin delivered subcutaneously → portal insulin levels are erratic
2. Post-prandial hyperinsulinemia in some dosing patterns → hepatic SHBG chronically suppressed
3. Insulin resistance in T1DM (double diabetes phenotype, ~20-30% of T1DM patients) → more severe SHBG suppression

**T1DM + male sex:**
- Baseline testosterone is 10-20× higher in males → even modest SHBG reduction → large
  absolute increase in free testosterone → larger DHT increase in sebaceous glands
- Male T1DM patients with poor HbA1c control: this mechanism operates at scale
- Clinical observation: severe seborrheic dermatitis in young male T1DM patients with HbA1c >9%
  is consistent with this mechanism

**T1DM + female sex (PCOS crossover):**
- ~30% of women with T1DM have features of PCOS (insulin resistance → androgen excess)
- PCOS mechanism is identical to this bridge: insulin resistance → SHBG ↓ → free T ↑ → DHT ↑ → sebum ↑
- T1DM + PCOS co-occurrence → highest androgen-driven M2 risk in women
- Key observation: women with T1DM + PCOS + rosacea/seb derm represent the female sex equivalent
  of the male T1DM pathway here

## How This Differs from M5 via IGF-1 (Attempt_009)

```
EXISTING M5 PATH (attempt_009):
Diet/glycemic control → insulin/IGF-1 ↑ → mTORC1 activation in sebocytes
    → direct lipogenesis signaling → sebum ↑

NEW M5 PATH (this attempt):
Diet/glycemic control → insulin resistance → SHBG ↓ → free T ↑ → 5α-reductase → DHT
    → AR activation in sebocytes → sebaceous gland hyperplasia → sebum ↑
```

**Key differences:**
| Feature | IGF-1 arm (attempt_009) | Androgen arm (this attempt) |
|---------|------------------------|----------------------------|
| Intermediate | IGF-1 → mTORC1 → lipogenesis | SHBG → free T → DHT → AR |
| Sex specificity | Both sexes equally | Male-dominant (10-20× baseline T) |
| Speed | Hours to days (IGF-1 → mTORC1 fast) | Weeks to months (sebaceous gland hyperplasia) |
| Reversibility | Rapid with glycemic improvement | Slower; AR-driven hyperplasia takes weeks to reverse |
| Intervention target | Dietary: low GI → IGF-1 ↓ | Glycemic control AND potential anti-androgen (finasteride) |
| PCOS relevance | Lower (mTORC1 less sex-specific) | **High** (PCOS is the female equivalent) |

Both arms operate simultaneously in poorly controlled T1DM. They converge on sebum ↑ → M2 amplified.

## The PCOS-M2-Rosacea Connection

PCOS (polycystic ovary syndrome):
- Affects ~10-15% of women generally; ~30% of women with T1DM
- Mechanism: insulin resistance → compensatory hyperinsulinemia → SHBG ↓ + ovarian androgen overproduction
- PCOS → elevated free testosterone → DHT → sebum ↑ → Malassezia ↑

**Published PCOS + skin dysbiosis data:**
- PCOS is associated with elevated sebum production and higher rates of seborrheic dermatitis
- PCOS + rosacea co-occurrence: not well-studied but consistent with shared pathophysiology
- T1DM + PCOS → expected highest M2 risk in women

**Prediction:** In a T1DM cohort, women with PCOS features (irregular cycles, elevated androgen
index, polycystic ovary on ultrasound) will have significantly higher Malassezia density on facial
skin and higher rates of seborrheic dermatitis/rosacea than T1DM women without PCOS features.

## Diagnostic Signature

**Who to suspect the androgen arm in:**
- Male T1DM patients with HbA1c >8% + severe seb derm or rosacea despite standard treatment
- Female T1DM patients with PCOS features + skin dysbiosis disease
- T1DM patients whose skin symptoms worsen in parallel with glycemic deterioration (over months)

**Lab panel (addendum to T-index):**
- SHBG (low = consistent with insulin-driven androgen elevation)
- Free testosterone index (calculated: total T / SHBG × 100)
- DHT (elevated in sebaceous-androgen-driven patients; less critical if free T already elevated)
- DHEA-S (adrenal androgen; elevated in PCOS → adrenal source)

**Intervention target if confirmed:**
1. **Primary: Glycemic optimization** — reducing insulin resistance → SHBG normalizes → free T falls → DHT falls → sebum decreases over 6-12 weeks
2. **Topical finasteride (2%):** 5α-reductase type 1 inhibitor; reduces DHT in sebaceous glands without systemic androgen suppression; experimental (topical not approved but compounded)
3. **Oral finasteride (1mg/day):** For male patients only (not safe in women who may become pregnant); proven sebum reduction in AGA (androgenetic alopecia) patients; would reduce M2 via DHT reduction
4. **Spironolactone (for women):** Androgen receptor blocker; first-line for PCOS + acne/seb derm in women; would reduce DHT's effect on sebocytes
5. **SGLT2 inhibitors / GLP-1 agonists:** Improve insulin sensitivity → SHBG normalizes; secondary benefit in T1DM patients already on adjunct therapy

## Novel Testable Predictions

**Prediction A — SHBG Inversely Correlates with Malassezia Density in T1DM:**
In a T1DM cohort (male + female), measure SHBG + Malassezia density (skin scraping + qPCR).
Prediction: low SHBG → high Malassezia density on face (independent of IgF-1 levels), specifically
for M. globosa and M. restricta (oleophilic species that dominate sebum-dependent niches).

**Prediction B — Glycemic Improvement Reduces Sebum via SHBG Normalization:**
T1DM patients starting GLP-1 agonist (semaglutide) or SGLT2 inhibitor: measure SHBG, free T,
DHT, skin surface lipid quantity (Sebumeter) at baseline and 12 weeks. Prediction: SHBG ↑ →
free T ↓ → sebum ↓ → paralleled by reduced Malassezia colonization.

**Prediction C — Finasteride (Topical) Reduces Rosacea/Seb Derm in Male T1DM:**
RCT design: male T1DM patients with elevated DHT + seborrheic dermatitis + elevated Malassezia
density → finasteride 2% cream (face) vs. placebo × 16 weeks. Primary endpoint: SDASI score.
Biomarker: DHT (skin surface tape) + Malassezia density (qPCR). This would directly test the
androgen → sebum → M2 bridge.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: SHBG Is Not Chronically Reduced in T1DM**
If T1DM patients with HbA1c >8% do not have lower SHBG than well-controlled T1DM or healthy
controls, the insulin-driven SHBG suppression mechanism does not operate at clinical T1DM
hyperinsulinemia levels.
**Status:** Not killed. Multiple cross-sectional studies show lower SHBG in T2DM (insulin
resistance clear) and in poorly controlled T1DM. A specific T1DM + rosacea cohort has not been
tested, but the mechanism is well-established in metabolic syndrome literature.

**Kill B: Sebum Quantity Does Not Correlate with Malassezia Density in T1DM Patients**
If Malassezia density on T1DM facial skin does not correlate with sebum quantity measured by
Sebumeter (only with immune state/inflammatory tone), the sebum-substrate-Malassezia link is weak.
**Status:** Not killed. Malassezia density is known to correlate with sebum production rates
(Ro 2005 J Invest Dermatol: seborrheic dermatitis severity correlates with sebum production).
The T1DM-specific correlation has not been tested.

**Kill C: DHT Is Not Elevated in Sebaceous Glands of Male T1DM Patients**
The 5α-reductase step requires higher free testosterone input. If T1DM-associated free testosterone
increase is too small to produce clinically relevant DHT elevation in sebaceous glands, the bridge
is quantitatively insignificant.
**Status:** Not killed but uncertain. The free T increase from SHBG suppression in T1DM is 15-35%
above baseline. Whether this is sufficient to produce measurable DHT elevation and sebaceous gland
hyperplasia requires direct measurement.

## Classification

**CANDIDATE** — mechanism is well-supported in component parts (insulin → SHBG → free T → DHT
is metabolic endocrinology consensus); specific demonstration in T1DM + skin dysbiosis cohort
has not been done.

**Higher confidence for:** T1DM + PCOS co-occurrence (PCOS × skin dysbiosis connection is
supported by PCOS dermatology literature). Lower confidence for: male T1DM-specific pathway
(plausible but untested in this specific population).

**Clinical utility:** Highest for male T1DM patients with HbA1c >8%, severe seb derm, and poor
antifungal response — androgen axis assessment (SHBG + free T + DHT) should be added to the
T-index workup in this presentation.

## Connection to Bridge Architecture

This bridge adds a **second independent mechanism** by which glycemic control (M5) amplifies
skin dysbiosis (M2):

```
M5 (glycemic control)
    → [IGF-1 → mTORC1] → sebum ↑ (existing M5↔M2 path)
    → [insulin resistance → SHBG ↓ → free T → DHT] → sebum ↑ (NEW PATH)
                ↓
Both paths converge on:
Sebum substrate ↑ → Malassezia colonization ↑ → M2 amplified → KLK5 → LL-37 → M4 lowered
```

**The combined effect:** In poorly controlled T1DM, M5 attacks M2 from two directions — the fast
mTORC1 lipogenesis arm (hours) and the slow androgen-hypertrophy arm (weeks). The androgen arm
may explain why M2 doesn't fully resolve with glycemic improvement alone in some patients — the
sebaceous gland hyperplasia (anatomical change) requires weeks to months to reverse even after
the hormonal input is normalized.
