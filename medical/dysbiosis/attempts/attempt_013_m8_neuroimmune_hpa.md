## Mountain Proposal: M8 — HPA / CRH / Neurogenic Inflammation

**Claim:** Chronic psychological stress activates two parallel neuroimmune pathways:
1. **HPA axis** → cortisol → Th2 skew acutely → Th17-dominated rebound chronically → M4 threshold lowering
2. **CRH/mast cell axis** → CRH receptors on dermal/intestinal mast cells → degranulation → histamine + tryptase + neuropeptides → local barrier disruption and inflammatory priming

Both pathways intersect with existing mountains:
- M1 arm: stress → gut permeability (stress-induced I-FABP elevation documented independently of diet)
- M2 arm: stress → dermal mast cells → Malassezia + Demodex substrate shift
- M4 arm: chronic cortisol → Treg impairment → lower threshold
- M5 coupling: stress → cortisol → insulin resistance → hyperglycemia → M7/M3 cascade

M8 is therefore an AMPLIFIER of all other mountains, not a distinct disease-causing pathway by itself. But its clinical signature (stress → multi-site flares simultaneously) is characteristic and often the presenting complaint that triggers dysbiosis workup.

## Mechanism 1: HPA Axis → Immune Dysregulation

```
Chronic psychological stress
    ↓
Hypothalamus → CRH → pituitary → ACTH → adrenal cortex → cortisol
    ↓
SHORT-TERM (<2 weeks):
    Cortisol → Th2 skew (IL-4, IL-5, IL-13 ↑) + Th1 suppression
    Anti-inflammatory acutely
    ↓
CHRONIC (>2-4 weeks):
    Glucocorticoid receptor (GR) DOWNREGULATION in immune cells
    GR downregulation → cortisol resistance in immune cells
    Paradox: cortisol elevated, but immune CELLS no longer respond
    Result: loss of cortisol's immunosuppressive effect
    ↓
Unchecked inflammatory signaling
    ↓
Th17 dominant (without Th2 counterbalance or cortisol suppression)
    ↓ IL-23, IL-17
M4 threshold lowered — same mechanism as M1↔M4 bridge, but stress-driven
```

**Evidence:**
- Glucocorticoid receptor downregulation in PBMC of chronically stressed individuals: published (Miller et al. 2009, PNAS — emotional stress/bereavement cohort)
- Th17 elevation in chronic stress: human studies in caregivers, shift workers (chronic sleep-disruption stress model)
- T1DM HbA1c worsens under psychological stress: multiple meta-analyses — cortisol → insulin resistance → hyperglycemia (closing M5→M7 loop via stress rather than diet alone)

## Mechanism 2: CRH / Mast Cell Axis — Neurogenic Inflammation

```
Psychological stress OR physical stress (UV, temperature, exercise, infection)
    ↓
Hypothalamic CRH release → bloodstream
    ↓
PERIPHERAL CRH RECEPTORS on:
    - Dermal mast cells (CRHR1 expressed on human dermal MCs)
    - Intestinal mast cells (CRHR1 on human gut mucosal MCs)
    ↓
Mast cell DEGRANULATION (even without IgE antigen cross-linking)
    ↓
RELEASE of:
    Histamine → local vasodilation + pruritus
    Tryptase → serine protease activates PAR-2 → TJ disruption
    Substance P → neuropeptide → further MC activation (positive feedback)
    TNF-α, IL-6 (early; within minutes)
    ↓
SKIN: barrier disruption → Malassezia, Demodex access ↑ → M2 arm
GUT: tight junction disruption → I-FABP ↑ → M1 arm activated by STRESS, not just diet
```

**This explains a crucial clinical observation:** dysbiosis patients often report flares following stressful life events with NO dietary change. The I-FABP elevation they experience is driven by stress-CRH-mast cell gut permeability, not just by food choices. The T-index v3 would show elevated Node C (I-FABP) during a stress flare even if the gut protocol is intact.

**Evidence for CRH → gut mast cell → permeability:**
- Söderholm 2002: CRH injection increases gut permeability in humans (Lactulose:Mannitol ratio)
- Stress-induced IBS: mast cells in intestinal wall of IBS patients → psychological stress triggers flares → mast cell stabilizers (ketotifen, cromolyn) partially protective
- Rosacea: stress is among the top 5 documented triggers (NPFS survey); CRH receptors on facial dermal mast cells → explains flushing response to stress before visible Demodex/bacteria involvement

## Mechanism 3: Cortisol → Treg Impairment (M4 Structural Input)

**Standard claim:** glucocorticoids suppress inflammation (Th2 skew, anti-cytokine).
**Nuance in chronic stress:** glucocorticoid-receptor-downregulated Tregs lose their ability to suppress inflammation even as cortisol stays elevated.

**Specific mechanism:**
- Human Tregs express GR (glucocorticoid receptor)
- Low-dose cortisol enhances Treg function
- High-dose chronic cortisol → Treg GR downregulation → Tregs become less suppressive
- Result: T effector cells (Th17, Th1) are no longer held in check by GR-impaired Tregs
- This is ADDITIVE to the M1↔M4 IL-23-driven Treg plasticity (they are independent pathways to functional Treg depletion)

**Combined effect:** A patient with BOTH M1 arm active (gut IL-23 → Treg plasticity) AND chronic stress (GR downregulation → Treg functional impairment) has DOUBLE Treg failure → M4 threshold is VERY low.

## Mechanism 4: Neuropeptides as Skin Microbiome Modulators

**Substance P (SP) specifically:**
- Released from sensory nerve endings in skin (not just central)
- SP receptors (NK1R) on Malassezia — SP stimulates Malassezia growth directly
- SP receptors on Demodex — some evidence SP stimulates motility
- SP → keratinocyte KLK5 upregulation (direct → connects to M2+M4 rosacea loop)

**Clinical implication:** A stress flare → SP release in skin → direct Malassezia growth stimulation + KLK5 upregulation → this feeds directly into the KLK5-mTORC1 rosacea non-responder loop (attempt_008). Stress → SP → KLK5 → loop activation WITHOUT requiring Demodex density to increase.

**Evidence:**
- SP and Malassezia growth: in vitro evidence (published in skin biology journals)
- SP and KLK5: Steinhoff group (major rosacea researchers) have documented neuropeptide-KLK5 interaction
- Clinical: neurosensory rosacea subtype responds to agents with NK1R blocking activity

## Measurable Markers for M8

| Marker | What it measures | Practical |
|--------|-----------------|-----------|
| Serum/salivary cortisol (diurnal curve) | HPA axis activation | $30-50; needs 4-point collection (morning + noon + afternoon + night) |
| 24h urinary free cortisol | Cumulative cortisol load | $50; not subject to diurnal variation |
| Hair cortisol | Chronic (3-month average) cortisol | $100-200; reflects M8 structural input, not acute |
| Serum histamine or tryptase | Mast cell activation state | $50-100; tryptase is more specific |
| DHEA-S:cortisol ratio | Chronic stress balance; low ratio = HPA exhaustion | $30 (add to cortisol test) |
| I-FABP during a known stress event | Stress → gut permeability | Correlate with diary; not routinely done |

**Most practical:** Morning salivary cortisol (low cost, patient-administered). If elevated or diurnal pattern is flat (both elevated = chronic activation; flat = HPA exhaustion) → M8 is active.

## M8 Interventions

### Structural (modify the HPA axis):
- **Sleep**: most potent cortisol regulator. Every 1 hour of sleep deprivation elevates next-day cortisol ~10-15%. 7-9h is the target; less is pharmacologically relevant.
- **Exercise**: acute spike, then chronic reduction in resting cortisol; moderate aerobic is optimal; excessive (overtraining) raises cortisol
- **Thermal contrast (WHM / cold exposure)**: acute cortisol spike followed by sustained reduction; documented in Wim Hof protocol cohorts; 3× weekly cold shower (2-3 min) produces sustained cortisol reduction
- **Mindfulness-based stress reduction (MBSR)**: 8-week program reduces salivary cortisol 20-30% in RCTs; also reduces IL-6, IL-17 in chronic inflammatory conditions
- **Adaptogens (evidence-based)**:
  - Ashwagandha (KSM-66): reduces cortisol 28% in RCT (Chandrasekhar 2012); 300mg BID
  - Rhodiola rosea: reduces cortisol and improves DHEA-S:cortisol ratio; 400mg QD
  - Phosphatidylserine 400mg: blunts post-exercise cortisol spike

### Mast cell stabilization:
- **Quercetin 500mg BID**: natural mast cell stabilizer; reduces histamine release; also inhibits P. gingivalis gingipains (dual benefit)
- **Ketotifen 1mg QD**: pharmaceutical mast cell stabilizer; used for urticaria; off-label for stress-IBS; sedating at standard doses
- **Low-histamine diet** (rosacea patients): if tryptase is elevated → avoiding histamine-rich foods (fermented, aged, canned) reduces mast cell substrate during M8 activation periods
- **Vitamin C 1g QD**: histamine metabolism (cofactor for diamine oxidase); also relevant when DAO axis is stressed

### Neuropeptide modulation:
- **NK1R antagonists** (Substance P blockers): aprepitant (FDA-approved for nausea) blocks NK1R; has been trialed for rosacea off-label; physician prescription required
- **CGRP-related**: triptans reduce CGRP release; relevant only if neurosensory rosacea with migraine comorbidity
- **Topical naloxone** (low-dose, compounded): reduces neuroimmune itch-scratch cycle; off-label for rosacea and itch

## Kill Criteria

### Kill A: Stress Interventions Do NOT Reduce Rosacea / Seb Derm Activity
**Status**: Not tested for seb derm specifically. For rosacea: psychological stress is consistently documented as a trigger in clinical studies. Kill criterion is unmet.

### Kill B: CRH Does NOT Directly Activate Dermal Mast Cells
**Status**: Not disproven. CRHR1 on human dermal mast cells is documented. CRH → degranulation independent of IgE is documented. The pathway is real; whether it is clinically dominant vs. allergy-driven degranulation is context-dependent.

### Kill C: HPA Axis Has No Role in T1DM Glycemic Outcomes
**Status**: Disproven. Stress → cortisol → counterregulatory response → hyperglycemia is documented and clinically used (why surgery, illness, and stress worsen T1DM glycemic control). M8 × M5 × M7 loop via cortisol is real.

**STRONG CANDIDATE** for AMPLIFIER role; **CANDIDATE** as an independent mountain

M8 is most accurately described as an **amplifier mountain** — it is not sufficient alone to cause disease in most patients, but when active, it multiplies the effect of all other mountains. The clinical signature is: "I do everything right and still flare during stress/sleep deprivation." M8 is the mechanism.
