## Mountain 5 — Substrate shift

## Mountain
M5 — High glycemic / dairy / low-fiber diet → insulin/IGF-1 elevation → sebum substrate shift → pathogenic overgrowth

## Hypothesis
Chronically elevated insulin/IGF-1 (from modern dietary pattern) increases sebocyte lipogenesis (SREBP-1c activation) and alters sebum fatty acid composition (↑oleic acid C18:1, ↓linoleic acid C18:2), creating a substrate that selectively feeds Malassezia and Demodex while simultaneously reducing gut microbial diversity via low fiber. This substrate shift is the upstream driver connecting modern environment to multi-site dysbiosis.

- `run_001_mountain_kill_roi.md`: M5 Kill ROI = 6/10 (already validated ecologically; individual-level causation weaker)
- `run_005_scfa_omega3_references.md`: fiber → butyrate producers → gut health mechanistic chain confirmed

## Mechanistic Chain
```
High glycemic diet + dairy
     ↓
Insulin/IGF-1 chronically elevated
     ↓
SREBP-1c activation in sebocytes → increased triglyceride synthesis
     ↓
Sebum output ↑ + fatty acid composition shifts (↑C18:1 oleic, ↓C18:2 linoleic)
     ↓
PARALLEL:
Gut: Low fiber → butyrate producers depleted → M1 pathway activated
Skin: Malassezia preferentially cleaves C18:1 (oleic acid from triglycerides)
      → more substrate = more Malassezia growth
Skin: Demodex feeds on sebaceous secretions → more sebum = more Demodex
     ↓
BOTH gut + skin dysbiosis driven by the SAME dietary upstream
```

## Kill Test
**Kill criterion:** Individual-level dietary intervention (8-week low-GI + dairy elimination) does NOT reduce sebum output OR skin Malassezia density OR gut butyrate producers compared to control diet.

**Existing evidence (near-kill test):** Smith 2007 RCT (low-GI diet, acne patients) showed reduced sebum secretion rate. Effect size moderate. Not measured for Malassezia specifically.

**Status: PARTIALLY CONFIRMED at population level; individual-level mechanistic confirmation incomplete.**

## Evidence FOR: 3/3 (ecological), 2/3 (individual)
Kitavan zero-acne (N=1200). Inuit historical. IGF-1 → SREBP-1c → sebum: biochemically established. Within-individual dietary trials: modest but real effect sizes.

## Evidence AGAINST: 0/3
No contradicting evidence for the population claim. Individual-level effect sizes are modest (~50% improvement in acne with dietary intervention vs ~30% placebo) suggesting diet is ONE factor, not the only one.
