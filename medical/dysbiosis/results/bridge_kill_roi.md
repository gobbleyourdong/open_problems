## Layer 0: What Would Kill Everything

Before analyzing bridge-level kills, one upstream assumption would collapse the entire framework:

**Kill-0: The M4 threshold concept is false — disease is density-only.**

If disease onset is SOLELY a function of pathogen density (Demodex count, Malassezia CFU, CVB load) with NO host tolerance threshold variation:

- All 7 sky bridges become irrelevant
- M4 (THE WALL) dissolves — there is no threshold to lower
- T-index v3 is meaningless
- The IFN-α arm (M3→M4) and Th17 arm (M1→M4) have nothing to lower
- Rosacea in T1DM would be purely explained by higher pathogen density in T1DM patients, not lower threshold
- Ivermectin would be sufficient for all rosacea (eliminates Demodex → disease resolves)

**Evidence that Kill-0 is likely false:**
- 25% of rosacea patients do NOT respond to ivermectin despite Demodex reduction (attempt_008)
- High-Demodex carriers without rosacea exist in general population (~50% of adults have Demodex)
- T1DM patients do NOT have higher Malassezia/Demodex density — they have more disease at similar density
- These three observations collectively require a host threshold variable

**Kill-0 status: VERY UNLIKELY, but the most impactful kill in the framework.**

## Layer 1: Foundation Kills (Eliminate Multiple Bridges)

### Kill-1: P. Gingivalis Does NOT Translocate to Pancreatic Islets (PMC7305306 fails to replicate)

**Bridge directly killed:** 006 (M3↔M7) — the bridge exists BECAUSE P. gingivalis reaches islets

**Cascade kills:**
- 011 (M5↔M7) — the M5→M7→M3 chain depends on P. gingivalis reaching islets; if it doesn't, M5 still affects M7 (oral dysbiosis) but M7 no longer connects to M3 (CVB). The T1DM-specific feedback loop breaks.
- 012 (M7→M1) — this bridge is about gut colonization, NOT islet translocation; survives independently
- The four-mountain chain (M7→M3→M4→M2) collapses, removing the most novel cross-specialty prediction

**What survives Kill-1:**
- M1↔M4 (007) — gut Th17 trafficking is independent of P. gingivalis
- M2+M4 (008) — rosacea loop is independent
- M3↔M2 (009) — CVB → IFN-α → pDC still operates without P. gingivalis as entry mechanism; CVB enters via other means
- M6↔M4 (010) — early-life Tregs are independent
- M7→M1 (012) — oral-gut colonization survives if gut TLR2 effect is real

**Kill-1 score: HIGH** — collapses 2 bridges partially, severs the most novel inter-specialty chain. BUT the remaining framework (M1↔M4, M3↔M2, M2+M4, M6↔M4) survives substantially.

---

### Kill-2: IL-23 Does NOT Drive Treg Plasticity in Human Skin (PMID 31776355 fails)

**Bridge directly killed:** 007 (M1↔M4) — the gut→skin Treg depletion mechanism requires IL-23-driven plasticity

**Cascade kills:**
- 008 (M2+M4) — the rosacea loop uses the SAME IL-23→Th17 mechanism in skin. If IL-23→Treg plasticity is false, the Th17 input to the rosacea loop is weakened (Demodex still drives IL-23 → Th17, but the Treg depletion step is removed). The KLK5-mTORC1 component survives independently of Treg plasticity.
- 009 (M3↔M2) — IFN-α → pDC → IL-23 → Th17 is the mechanism; if IL-23 doesn't drive Treg plasticity in skin, the bridge is weakened but not fully killed (IFN-α → Th17 still operates, just without the Treg depletion amplification)

**What survives Kill-2:**
- 006 (M3↔M7) — P. gingivalis → CAR upregulation is Treg-independent
- 009 (M3↔M2) partially — IFN-α → pDC → Th17 survives; Treg plasticity was amplification, not the core step
- 010 (M6↔M4) — structural Treg floor is about Treg ABUNDANCE, not plasticity; survives
- 011, 012 — survives

**Kill-2 score: MEDIUM-HIGH** — collapses M1↔M4 and weakens M2+M4 and M3↔M2. Framework loses the GALT Th17 arm but retains IFN-α arm and structural M6 floor.

## Layer 2: Single-Bridge Kills (Isolated damage)

### Kill-3: CVB Chronic Persistence in T1DM Is Not the Source of Elevated IFN-α
(pDC expansion is real in T1DM, but driven by non-CVB IFN source)

**Bridge directly killed:** 009 (M3↔M2) — already partially killed by HLA. This would complete the kill.

**Cascade:**
- 006 (M3↔M7) weakened but not killed — CAR upregulation via P. gingivalis cytokines is independent of CVB; the P. gingivalis part survives; CVB entry via upregulated CAR still occurs, just without CVB being the primary IFN source
- M3 (CVB persistence as T1DM mechanism) itself would be weakened — but IFN-α in T1DM would still be real, just from non-CVB source
- The antiviral protocol rationale weakens but doesn't collapse (IFN-α is still the target; source matters less for treatment)

**What survives Kill-3:** Most of the framework. The IFN-α arm is real; whether CVB generates it is the fragile step.

**Kill-3 score: LOW-MEDIUM** — framework is already partially protected against this by the confirmation bias audit finding (IFN-α arm survives regardless of CVB specificity).

### Kill-4: Early-Life Treg Pool Is NOT Non-Redundant (Rudensky 2015 doesn't replicate in humans)

**Bridge directly killed:** 010 (M6↔M4)

**Cascade:** None significant. M6↔M4 is an isolated add-on to the main framework. Its kill doesn't change M1-M3, M7, or M5 connections.

**What changes:** M6 history loses its structural floor interpretation. Adult interventions can fully compensate for early-life Treg deficit. Clinical implication: M6 high-risk patients are less disadvantaged than the current framework suggests.

**Kill-4 score: LOW** — isolated kill, no cascade.

## The Framework's Load-Bearing Assumptions

In order of criticality (most load-bearing first):

1. **M4 host threshold exists** — if disease were density-only, the entire framework collapses. Evidence: ivermectin non-responders, high-density tolerant carriers. This assumption is well-supported.

2. **IL-23 → Treg plasticity in human skin** — if this fails, the M1↔M4 bridge collapses and M2+M4 loop loses the Treg depletion amplification. This assumption is supported by PMID 31776355 (human psoriatic skin) but needs extension to rosacea specifically.

3. **P. gingivalis translocates to pancreatic islets** — if PMC7305306 fails to replicate, the M3↔M7 bridge and four-mountain chain (M7→M3→M4→M2) collapse. This is the most novel single finding and is currently only one study (mice + human tissue). **This is the single most important finding to replicate.**

4. **IFN-α arm in T1DM lowers M4 skin threshold** — supported by pDC expansion data (PMID 24973447) and mechanistic plausibility. The CVB specificity is fragile (HLA explains part of the OR 2.59); the IFN-α mechanism itself is robust.

5. **Early-life Treg pool is non-redundant** — supported by Rudensky 2015. Lower-stakes assumption; its kill doesn't damage the main framework.

## Strategic Implication for Experimental Design

**If designing one experiment to stress-test the framework maximally:**
Kill-1 test: nPOD dual IHC (VP1 + gingipain on same tissue sections).
If P. gingivalis is NOT co-localized with CVB in islets → Kill-1 is confirmed → four-mountain chain breaks.
If co-localized → framework gains substantial support at the most novel and fragile point.

**If designing one experiment to stress-test at lowest cost:**
Kill-3 test: IFN-α2 Simoa stratified by rosacea severity WITHIN T1DM patients.
If IFN-α2 does NOT correlate with rosacea in T1DM (after controlling for HLA-DR3) → CVB-specific IFN-α arm is likely not operating.
Cost: $150-300 per patient. Feasible in existing T1DM cohort.

**If testing framework survival at maximum breadth:**
Simultaneous panel (P. gingivalis IgG + IFN-α2 Simoa + I-FABP + rosacea severity assessment):
- Tests Kill-1 indirectly (P. gingivalis serology — active or not)
- Tests Kill-3 (IFN-α2 — is it elevated?)
- Tests Kill-2 indirectly (I-FABP — is gut Th17 arm active, consistent with Treg plasticity?)
- Costs ~$250-500 total

## Summary

The framework has ONE catastrophic vulnerability (Kill-0: M4 threshold), ONE high-impact vulnerability (Kill-1: P. gingivalis in islets), and ONE medium-impact vulnerability (Kill-2: IL-23 → Treg plasticity in skin). All other kills are isolated.

The framework is most fragile at the P. gingivalis islet translocation finding (PMC7305306). This needs replication more urgently than any other element.

The framework is most robust in the Th17 arm (M1↔M4) because this rests on multiple independent lines: the GALT trafficking mechanism, the IL-23→Treg plasticity finding, and the ustekinumab dual-disease clinical data. Killing this bridge requires falsifying all three simultaneously.
