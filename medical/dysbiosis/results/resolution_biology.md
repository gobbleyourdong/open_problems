# Resolution Biology — When and How the Protocol Succeeds
## Protocol De-escalation Framework | 2026-04-12

> All prior documents define the problem, the mountains, and the intervention.
> None defines what SUCCESS looks like or when to stop.
> This document fills that gap: mechanistic definitions of remission, T-index
> normalization criteria, de-escalation sequence, and what "cured vs. managed"
> means in this framework.

---

## Why Resolution Biology Is Non-Trivial Here

Standard clinical remission = absence of symptoms. But the dysbiosis framework has
a structural problem: symptoms can resolve while the underlying mountain architecture
remains intact.

**Example:** Topical ketoconazole + dietary change → seb derm clears. But:
- M4 threshold is still low (gut dysbiosis not addressed)
- M7 still active (P. gingivalis still colonizing, IgA protease still cleaving sIgA)
- M3 still active (CVB persisting in islets, IFN-α still elevated)

Symptomatic resolution with structural risk intact = **MANAGED, not resolved.**
The next trigger event (stress, antibiotic course, viral illness) re-activates because
the mountains never came down. This predicts the clinical observation that dysbiosis
patients have recurrent flares over years despite effective acute treatment.

**True resolution requires mountain-by-mountain structural normalization,** not just
symptom absence.

## Mechanistic Definitions of Remission

### Level 1: Symptomatic Remission (Minimum — "Managed")
- No active seb derm, rosacea, or oral dysbiosis symptoms for ≥3 months
- Dietary adherence maintained
- T-index not yet measured or normalized

**What this does NOT guarantee:** Mountain architecture may be intact. Withdrawal of
topical treatment → likely recurrence within weeks to months.

**Appropriate response:** Not de-escalation — MEASUREMENT. This is when to run
T-index v3 baseline panel to determine which mountains are driving.

---

### Level 2: T-Index Normalization ("Mechanistically Managed")

T-index v3 normalization criteria:

| Node | Abnormal | Normalized | Action when normalized |
|------|----------|------------|------------------------|
| Node C (I-FABP) | >400 pg/mL | <200 pg/mL on two consecutive measures 4 weeks apart | M1 arm input resolved; gut barrier restored |
| Node D (IFN-α2 Simoa) | >5 pg/mL | <2 pg/mL | M3 arm input below activation threshold |
| Node B (hsCRP + IL-17A) | hsCRP >3 mg/L OR IL-17A >5 pg/mL | hsCRP <1 mg/L AND IL-17A <3 pg/mL | Systemic Th17/inflammatory state normalized |
| Node A (Foxp3+/RORγt- Tregs) | <5% of CD4+ T cells OR plasticity index >30% | >8% genuinely suppressive Tregs | M4 threshold restored; NOT just Foxp3 count |
| P. gingivalis IgG | Seropositive | Seronegative OR declining titer to <1:8 | M7 arm structurally resolved |

**Level 2 achieved = all 5 parameters normalized on two consecutive measurements ≥8 weeks apart.**

This is harder to achieve than Level 1 and requires active mountain protocol.

---

### Level 3: Structural Remission ("Resolved Until Re-Triggered")

Level 2 + the following:

1. **M6 history acknowledged:** The early-life Treg floor cannot be changed in adulthood.
   A patient with low M6 floor (C-section + antibiotics + formula) has a LOWER maximum
   achievable threshold. They may normalize T-index nodes but will re-trigger at lower
   provocation than a high-M6-floor patient.
   
   **Level 3 in a low-M6 patient = all nodes normalized + explicit understanding that:**
   - Higher maintenance dose of fiber/butyrate/Clostridia mix is required chronically
   - Reactivation threshold is lower → triggers (stress, antibiotics) cause faster decline

2. **M8 assessed and addressed:** If M8 (stress, sleep) is not addressed, the T-index
   will not hold normalization. Level 3 requires:
   - Morning cortisol in normal diurnal range OR hair cortisol in normal range
   - OR explicit acknowledgment that M8 is managed by non-pharmaceutical means (sleep 7-9h,
     MBSR completion confirmed, quercetin maintained)

3. **Seasonal stability confirmed:** T-index measurements during high-M8-risk periods
   (exam periods, winter, bereavement) remain within Level 2 bounds.

Level 3 is the operational definition of "the framework has achieved its goal."

## What "Cured" Means Here (And Doesn't)

### Not achievable (structural limits):
- M6 floor is permanent. Low-M6 patients will always have a lower threshold than high-M6 peers.
- Genetic HLA-DR3 floor is permanent. The T1DM → Th17 baseline phenotype is genetic.
- M3 (viral persistence): if CVB has established persistent replication-silent 5'UTR-deleted
  infection, clearance may require years of antiviral protocol. The exact timeline is unknown.

### Achievable:
- M1 arm: gut dysbiosis is fully reversible with dietary + Clostridia mix + Akkermansia protocol.
  Node C normalization confirms it. Maintenance: fiber ≥35g/day + LGG probiotic.
- M7 arm: P. gingivalis periodontal colonization is fully clearable with SRP + chlorhexidine
  rinse + oral hygiene. P. gingivalis IgG seroconversion to negative is the marker.
- M2 arm: symptomatic seb derm / rosacea controlled with topical ketoconazole + ivermectin.
  After M4 threshold normalization, lower topical maintenance dose is sufficient.
- M8 arm: stress → manageable chronically with sleep + MBSR + ashwagandha. Not cured,
  but M8 contribution to mountain load is reducible to near-zero.

**Practical answer to "am I cured?":**
The mountains M1, M7, M8 are manageable and maintainable.
The mountains M3, M4, M6 require ongoing maintenance because:
- M3 clearance timeline is unknown and IFN-α is the only proxy
- M4 threshold is a structural property set by M6 history + current inputs
- M6 floor is permanent

**"Cured" = stable Level 2/3 T-index + no medication required except maintenance dietary fiber,
Clostridia/Akkermansia probiotic, and oral hygiene. Everything else de-escalatable.**

## De-Escalation Sequence

**Protocol layers and their de-escalation order:**

### Tier 1 — De-escalate FIRST (acute/symptomatic layer):
Topical treatments (ketoconazole shampoo, ivermectin cream, azelaic acid, chlorhexidine rinse)
- **De-escalate when:** Level 1 (symptomatic remission) maintained for ≥3 months
- **How:** reduce frequency (daily → 2×/week → 1×/week → as-needed)
- **Watch for:** relapse within 4 weeks of frequency reduction
- **Do NOT de-escalate if:** Node D (IFN-α) or Node C (I-FABP) still elevated —
  symptomatic control is suppressive not curative while mountains remain active

### Tier 2 — De-escalate SECOND (pharmacological arms):
Itraconazole, fluoxetine (if used purely as antiviral, not for concurrent depression)
- **De-escalate when:** Level 2 (Node D IFN-α normalized to <2 pg/mL on two consecutive
  measurements ≥8 weeks apart)
- **Fluoxetine note:** if taken for depression, do NOT de-escalate on the basis of IFN-α
  normalization. Antidepressant benefit is independent and de-escalation follows psychiatric
  guidelines.
- **Itraconazole:** taper (not abrupt stop — fungal rebound risk). Reduce to alternate-day
  for 4 weeks then stop. Recheck Node D at 4 weeks post-taper.

### Tier 3 — Maintain INDEFINITELY (structural floor maintenance):
High-fiber diet, Clostridia mix probiotic, Akkermansia supplementation, butyrate (reduced dose
to 600mg-1g/day maintenance after HDAC induction phase), oral hygiene
- **Do not de-escalate.** These are maintenance behaviors, not acute treatments.
- The fiber and probiotic requirements are permanent because M6 floor does not rise.
  A patient with high M6 floor might tolerate gaps; low-M6 patients will relapse.
- **Minimum indefinite maintenance:**
  - Dietary fiber ≥30g/day (relaxed from therapeutic 40g+ after normalization)
  - LGG probiotic + Akkermansia
  - Oral hygiene: twice-daily brushing, flossing, 6-monthly periodontal maintenance

### Tier 4 — Conditionally maintain (M8 arm):
Sleep 7-9h, quercetin 500mg BID, ashwagandha KSM-66 300mg BID
- **Maintain during:** ongoing high-stress life periods, winter, identified M8-risk seasons
- **May reduce:** during sustained low-stress periods (salivary cortisol confirms)
- **Quercetin note:** maintain indefinitely if P. gingivalis history (seropositive ever) —
  the IgA protease self-sustaining loop means residual P. gingivalis risk persists even
  after treatment. Quercetin's gingipain-inhibiting effect provides residual protection.

## Resolution Kinetics — How Fast Does Each Mountain Come Down?

Based on mechanism:

| Mountain | Expected normalization timeline | Rate-limiting step |
|----------|--------------------------------|---------------------|
| M2 (skin symptoms) | 4-8 weeks with topical treatment | Malassezia/Demodex density reduction |
| M7 (P. gingivalis) | 8-12 weeks with SRP + chlorhexidine | Biofilm disruption + sIgA recovery |
| M1 (gut dysbiosis) | 3-6 months with dietary + probiotic | Akkermansia/F. prausnitzii recolonization |
| M8 (HPA normalization) | 6-12 weeks with MBSR + sleep | Cortisol diurnal pattern restoration |
| M4 (threshold normalization) | 6-12 months after M1+M7 resolution | Treg pool restoration (Rudensky 2015: slow turnover) |
| M3 (IFN-α normalization) | 1-3 years with antiviral protocol | Viral reservoir clearance; LAMP2/autophagy cycle |
| M6 (structural floor) | Permanent — cannot normalize | No mechanism to restore imprinted Foxp3 pool |

**Implication for T-index monitoring schedule:**
- Months 1-3: monitor Node C and Node B monthly (fastest-responding nodes)
- Months 3-6: add Node D monitoring (IFN-α, slower)
- Months 6-12: Node A (Treg quality; requires repeat measurement after upstream resolution)
- Year 1+: annual P. gingivalis IgG to confirm M7 remains resolved
- Year 2+: annual IFN-α Simoa to confirm M3 remains resolved or continues declining

## Relapse Detection and Response

### Relapse Pattern A — Diet Relapse (M5 → M1 activation)
**Signs:** Node C rise (I-FABP) + food diary shows fiber drop or GI index increase
**Response:** Return to therapeutic fiber protocol; recheck Node C in 4 weeks
**Not a framework failure** — this is expected if diet adherence lapses

### Relapse Pattern B — Stress Relapse (M8 → M1 + M7 activation)
**Signs:** Node C rise + Node B rise during documented stress event + diet unchanged
**Signs:** salivary cortisol elevated (flat diurnal = HPA exhaustion), sIgA low
**Response:** Add quercetin BID if not already maintained; prioritize sleep; short ashwagandha course;
do NOT add more dietary fiber — the permeability is M8-driven, not M1-driven
**Clinical trap to avoid:** Attributing stress-flare I-FABP to diet failure and adding unnecessary
dietary restriction during an already-stressful period — worsens quality of life, doesn't fix M8.

### Relapse Pattern C — M7 Recurrence (periodontal re-infection)
**Signs:** dental exam shows recurrent pocketing; P. gingivalis IgG titer rising; Node D rising
(P. gingivalis → EBV reactivation → IFN-α, third M7 arm)
**Trigger:** antibiotic course (disrupts oral microbiome, opens niche), new stress period (sIgA
drops, P. gingivalis re-establishes), dental trauma
**Response:** Repeat SRP + chlorhexidine course; confirm quercetin is maintained; assess M8 as driver

### Relapse Pattern D — Viral Re-activation (M3 return)
**Signs:** Node D (IFN-α) rising without explanation; EBV VCA IgM reappears
**Trigger:** immunosuppression (stress-induced or medication), new viral exposure
**Response:** Restart antiviral arm (fluoxetine if discontinued; FMD cycle; trehalose); check
selenium status (maintains viral RNA fidelity; low selenium → CVB virulence mutations → harder clearance)

## The Patient Conversation: What to Tell the Patient

**If T-index normalizes and symptoms resolve:**
"Your mountains are mostly managed. You'll need to maintain fiber, probiotics, and oral hygiene
indefinitely — these aren't drugs that you stop; they're the terrain that prevents the problem
from re-establishing. If the stress in your life stays high, we'll need to address that separately —
it has its own biological pathway that can reopen these mountains even when everything else is correct."

**On the question "am I cured?":**
"The gut dysbiosis, oral dysbiosis, and skin dysbiosis are all manageable to remission. What we
cannot change is your early-life immune baseline or your genetic background — those set your floor.
What that means practically is that your margin for error is smaller than someone who started with
higher baseline tolerance. You don't have less health — you have less redundancy. The protocol is how
you maintain health within that reality."

**On the question "what if I stop?":**
"The timeline for relapse depends on which mountains are still structurally reduced. If M3 (the viral
arm) is not fully resolved, stopping the antiviral protocol too early likely leads to IFN-α return
and reconstitution of M4 threshold lowering. Clinical experience (from the broader CVB campaign):
patients who stop antiviral protocol at 6 months often relapse by 18 months. The target is 2-3 years
of sustained IFN-α normalization before considering discontinuation."
