# Confirmation Bias Audit — Phase 4 Sigma Method
## Dysbiosis Problem | 2026-04-12

> Per sigma method Phase 4: identify what is selection bias and what is genuine signal.
> Question: is the cluster (T1DM + seb derm + chalazion + rosacea + son's POD)
> epidemiologically real, or assembled retroactively by the CVB/dysbiosis framing?
>
> The sigma method requires this audit BEFORE accepting a framework as explanatory.
> A framework that explains too much is evidence of confirmation bias, not of correctness.

---

## The Cluster Being Audited

The user has:
1. T1DM (diagnosis, primary)
2. Seborrheic dermatitis (documented, active)
3. Chalazion (eyelid cyst, resolved with olive oil cessation)
4. Possible rosacea (status: not formally diagnosed; spectrum question)
5. Son: perioral dermatitis (POD)

The claim being audited: these conditions form a coherent cluster driven by a shared mechanism (CVB → IFN-α → immune dysregulation → M4 threshold failure → multi-site dysbiosis disease).

- 0.004 × 0.075 × 0.15 ≈ 4.5 × 10^-5 (1 in 22,000)
- This is rare BEFORE adjusting for any shared pathophysiology
- But: these conditions are not independent — they share risk factors (Th17 immune type, skin barrier genetics, sebaceous-zone biology)

## Step 2: Co-Occurrence Data — What is Actually Published

### T1DM + Rosacea
**OR 2.59 (95% CI 1.41–4.73)** — Egeberg et al. JAAD 2016
- **Well-powered epidemiological observation. GENUINE.**
- BUT: GWAS audit (run_007_rosacea_hla_kill.md, attempt_009) reveals this is primarily explained by **shared HLA-DR3 haplotype (DRB1*03:01/DQA1*05:01/DQB1*02:01)**, not shared dysbiosis mechanism
- The co-occurrence is real; the CVB mechanistic explanation is not the primary driver at population level
- CVB mechanism may add a second layer for CVB-active individuals but is NOT the primary explanation for OR 2.59

**Status: GENUINE CO-OCCURRENCE, but HLA-mediated, not dysbiosis-mediated at population level.**

---

### T1DM + Seborrheic Dermatitis
**No published co-occurrence data found.**
- Seb derm is common in the general population (10-30%)
- T1DM patients are not known to have elevated seb derm rates compared to matched controls
- Shared mechanism: Th17 immune skewing (plausible) but not epidemiologically validated

**Status: UNCONFIRMED. May be coincidental or mechanistically real but unstudied.**

---

### Rosacea + Seborrheic Dermatitis
- Co-occurrence is clinically recognized (both sebaceous-zone, both Malassezia/Demodex-driven)
- No robust quantitative co-occurrence study found (OR/RR)
- Mechanistically: shared M4 threshold problem, shared substrate (sebum)

**Status: MECHANISTICALLY COHERENT but unquantified epidemiologically.**

---

### T1DM + Chalazion
**No published co-occurrence data.**
- Chalazion is Demodex/meibomian gland dysfunction related
- No epidemiological link to T1DM published
- User's specific case: olive oil on eyebrows → substrate shift → chalazion. This is a specific SUBSTRATE TRIGGER (anti-problem category), not a systemic clustering signal.

**Status: COINCIDENTAL in terms of epidemiology. User's case is explicable by specific trigger, not systemic disease connection.**

---

### Rosacea + Chalazion (Ocular Rosacea)
- Ocular rosacea (including meibomian gland disease) occurs in ~30-50% of rosacea patients
- Chalazion is a recognized ocular rosacea manifestation
- **IF rosacea is present**: chalazion is NOT coincidental — it is part of the rosacea spectrum

**Status: CHALAZION IS CONDITIONAL. If rosacea is confirmed → chalazion is part of the rosacea cluster, not a separate disease. If rosacea is absent → chalazion is likely coincidental.**

This changes the audit significantly:
- The question "does the user have rosacea?" determines whether chalazion is a meaningful cluster member or noise
- Unconfirmed rosacea + chalazion = possibly coincidental; confirmed rosacea + chalazion = same disease

---

### Son's POD + Parental T1DM
**No published epidemiological link.**
- POD in children: topical corticosteroid exposure, SLS toothpaste, Malassezia — multifactorial
- No studies showing children of T1DM parents have elevated POD rates
- However: POD in the user's son could reflect inherited skin barrier genetics (M4 floor is partially heritable) rather than inherited T1DM-specific immune state

**Status: NOT PROVEN to be part of the cluster. Likely coincidental or reflects inherited skin barrier genetics, not T1DM/CVB-specific mechanism.**

## Step 3: Framework Retroactivity Test

**Question: Would these conditions have been clustered together WITHOUT the CVB/dysbiosis framing?**

Before CVB framing:
- T1DM: managed primarily as endocrine/autoimmune disease
- Seb derm: managed topically (ketoconazole), typically treated as independent
- Chalazion: ophthalmic condition, managed by warm compresses/drainage
- Rosacea (if present): managed with ivermectin/metronidazole
- Son's POD: managed topically (SLS-free, no steroids)

**Each condition would have been treated by a different specialist with no assumed connection.**

After CVB framing: all five conditions are "explained" by the same convergent mechanism (CVB → IFN-α → M4 threshold → multi-site dysbiosis disease).

**The retroactivity test result: the cluster is ASSEMBLED by the framework, not discovered by it.**

This is the hallmark of confirmation bias: a framework that turns independent observations into a unified narrative. The question is whether the narrative is correct despite this, or precisely because the framework over-explains.

## Step 4: Protocol Response as Evidence

**The user's CVB/dysbiosis protocol** reportedly produces improvement across multiple conditions.

**Is this proof of a unified mechanism?**

**No.** The protocol implements:
- NF-κB suppression (curcumin, resveratrol, berberine)
- NLRP3 inhibition (intermittent fasting, BHB)
- mTOR suppression (fasting)
- Omega-3 (resolvin production, IL-17 attenuation)
- Vitamin D3/K2 (cathelicidin regulation, Treg support)
- Ketoconazole topical (Malassezia-specific)

**All of these would improve ANY Th17-driven inflammatory condition**, not specifically CVB/T1DM-linked conditions. A patient with psoriasis + allergic rhinitis + eczema on this protocol would also improve across multiple sites — without T1DM or CVB.

**The protocol is not a test of CVB specificity.** It is a broad anti-Th17 protocol that reduces inflammatory tone systemically. Cross-site improvement is expected from general Th17 suppression, not from CVB-specific mechanism.

## Step 5: What the Confirmation Bias Audit Shows

### Genuine Signal (not bias):
1. **T1DM + rosacea co-occurrence is real** — OR 2.59, epidemiologically confirmed
2. **Seb derm + rosacea mechanistic coherence** — shared Malassezia/Demodex/M4 basis; clinically recognized even if unquantified
3. **Chalazion is ocular rosacea** — IF rosacea confirmed, chalazion is part of the same condition
4. **T1DM is a Th17-skewed autoimmune disease** — shared immune phenotype with inflammatory skin disease is real

### Likely Confirmation Bias (assembled retroactively):
1. **T1DM + seb derm causal connection** — no published data; seb derm is common; may be coincidental
2. **Chalazion as independent cluster member** — only meaningful if ocular rosacea; if no rosacea → likely coincidental trigger (olive oil substrate)
3. **Son's POD as inherited T1DM/CVB dysbiosis** — not epidemiologically supported; POD is common, multifactorial
4. **CVB protocol → skin improvement proves CVB mechanism** — false; protocol is general anti-Th17

### The Bias Score:
- Cluster coherence is **partial** (~40% genuine, ~60% assembled)
- The genuine core: T1DM + inflammatory skin disease tendency (via HLA genetics + Th17 phenotype)
- The assembled overlay: the specific CVB mechanism, oral hygiene connection, chalazion/POD as cluster members

## Step 6: What This Means for the Framework

### Findings to RETAIN:
1. T1DM → Th17 immune phenotype → lower M4 threshold for skin dysbiosis disease (mechanism valid)
2. M1↔M4 and M3↔M2 bridges are mechanistically valid regardless of user's specific cluster
3. The convergent mechanism (IFN-α + Th17 → M4) is a valid research framework
4. Protocol elements addressing Th17/NF-κB/NLRP3 are appropriately targeted
5. Ketoconazole + ivermectin are disease-specific (not just general anti-inflammatory)

### Findings to DOWNGRADE:
1. The CVB mechanism is NOT the primary explanation for T1DM + rosacea co-occurrence at population level (HLA is dominant)
2. Seb derm is NOT proven to be mechanistically connected to T1DM — it may be coincidental
3. Son's POD should be treated as a standalone dermatology problem, not as a cluster marker of parental T1DM dysbiosis
4. Cross-site improvement on the protocol does NOT prove the specific CVB/dysbiosis framework

### The Sharpest Confirmation Bias Finding:
**The user's improvement on the protocol would be expected for ANY patient with multiple Th17-driven inflammatory conditions.** The CVB framing provides a compelling narrative, but the protocol would work for:
- A patient with psoriasis + eczema + seb derm (no T1DM, no CVB)
- A patient with T1DM + no skin disease who subsequently develops seb derm

The protocol tests Th17 modulation, not CVB specificity. The confirmation bias is in attributing improvement to the CVB mechanism specifically.

## Step 7: Recommended Reframing

**Current frame (bias-inflated):**
"CVB persistent infection drives IFN-α, which primes pDCs, which lowers M4 threshold, which allows Demodex/Malassezia to cause seb derm/rosacea/chalazion in T1DM patients. The pediatric POD case in the same family is part of the same cluster."

**Bias-corrected frame:**
"T1DM patients have a Th17-skewed immune phenotype (via HLA genetics and/or GALT dysbiosis) that lowers the threshold for skin dysbiosis disease at sites with Malassezia and Demodex. The T1DM + rosacea epidemiological link is real (HLA-driven) and the IFN-α/CVB mechanism adds a second layer in CVB-active patients specifically. Seb derm is possibly co-occurring due to shared M4 biology; chalazion is part of rosacea if rosacea is present; the pediatric POD case is a separate pediatric dermatology problem."

**The framework should be:**
- NARROW claim: T1DM → lower M4 threshold → increased rosacea risk (CONFIRMED, HLA-mediated + possible IFN-α layer)
- BROADER claim (less confirmed): T1DM → lower M4 threshold → increased risk across all sebaceous-site dysbiosis (POSSIBLE, unconfirmed epidemiologically)
- NOT claimed: the cluster is driven specifically by CVB at population level (HLA is dominant)

---

## Step 8: The Sigma Method Verdict

**Confirmation bias is operating at moderate intensity (estimated 40-60%).**

The bias does NOT invalidate the framework — the Th17 mechanism is real, the M4 threshold concept is valid, the sky bridges are mechanistically sound. But it inflates confidence in:
1. The specific CVB causal pathway vs. HLA genetics
2. The coherence of the full cluster (seb derm, chalazion, son's POD as integral members)
3. The protocol as a test of the CVB hypothesis (it is a test of Th17 modulation, not CVB-specific)

**The framework is most reliable where it is most specific:** predicting that IFN-α2 Simoa will be elevated, that I-FABP will be elevated, that P. gingivalis serology will be positive, and that these measurements predict rosacea severity within T1DM patients. If these predictions are wrong, the framework is wrong. If they are right, the CVB mechanism contributes beyond just the HLA genetics.

**The decisive test remains unchanged:** IFN-α2 Simoa stratifies rosacea risk WITHIN T1DM patients (controlling for HLA). This is the test that separates HLA mechanism from CVB mechanism.
