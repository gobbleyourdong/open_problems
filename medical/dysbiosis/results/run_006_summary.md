## High-Signal Findings

### 1. M1 STALL RESOLVED — gut-skin connection is GALT/T-cell, not LPS

**Previous claim:** Gut LPS translocates → primes skin TLR4 → lowers inflammatory threshold.

**New finding:** Published review (PMID 38654394) explicitly states: "acute systemic inflammation alone does not trigger skin-specific responses." The dominant mechanism is:
- GALT primes T cells in mesenteric lymph nodes
- Gut-primed Th17 cells acquire skin-homing markers (CLA/CCR4) — dual homing
- They traffic to skin, deplete local Tregs → lower skin disease threshold
- Same IL-23/Th17 axis drives both IBD and psoriasis

**Evidence:**
- Psoriasis in CD: 9.6-11.2% (4.4× population rate) — epidemiological support
- I-FABP in psoriasis: 243 vs 114 pg/mL, r=0.78 with PASI — gut enterocyte damage correlates with skin severity
- LBP in psoriasis: elevated ONLY in psoriasis + metabolic syndrome subgroup, NOT in isolated psoriasis — rules out LPS as primary skin driver
- DSS colitis mice: dual gut+skin-homing T cells confirmed experimentally

**New sky bridge: M1 ↔ M4**
Gut dysbiosis → Th17 trafficking → skin Treg depletion = DIRECTLY SETS M4 THRESHOLD.
This means M4 is partly a dynamic, gut-modifiable parameter, not only an intrinsic host property.

### 2. CXCL10 CASCADE — weak gate, protocol revised

**Problem:** CXCL10 elevation in T1DM is inconsistent. Only ~44-55% of T1DM patients show clear elevation (>2 SD above controls). Fold-change 1.3-1.5×. No validated cutoff.

**Protocol revision:** CXCL10 is now a SUPPORTIVE marker. Normal CXCL10 does NOT rule out CVB activity. IFN-α2 Simoa should be ordered based on clinical context (T1DM + CVB protocol), not gated exclusively on CXCL10 elevation.

**Gap discovered:** No published T1DM-specific IFN-α2 Simoa cohort data exists. Healthy controls: ≤5 fg/mL (Simoa). T1DM expected low fg/mL range, but NO population norms available. First measurement IS the baseline.

**Better gate than CXCL10:** MxA protein (IFN-α specific, not IFN-γ responsive) is a more specific confirmatory marker for type I interferon signature.

### 3. M5 SCOPE CORRECTED — Kitavan data = acne only

**Correction:** The Cordain 2002 paper surveyed ONLY acne. Seb derm, dandruff, and pityriasis versicolor were NEVER examined in Kitavan or any traditional population study. The "zero Malassezia-driven disease in Kitavan" claim has NO published support — it was never tested.

**What WAS confirmed from modern data:**
- IGF-1 elevated in seb derm patients, dose-response relationship (Kenawy 2021)
- Western diet → elevated seb derm risk (PMC10719884, 2023)
- M5 mechanistic chain (diet → IGF-1 → SREBP-1c → sebum C18:1 shift → Malassezia substrate) is now published in seb derm literature specifically

**PV removed from M5 prediction set:** Climate confounding is overwhelming (1% temperate, 40% tropical). Kitava is tropical — PV would be high there regardless of diet. Not a valid test of M5.
