# Numerics Run 031 — IGFBP-3 and Free IGF-1: The Hidden Amplifier in M5→M2
## Why "Normal" Total IGF-1 Can Still Drive Sebaceous Hyperactivation in T1DM | 2026-04-12

> The M5→M2 bridge via IGF-1 (from attempt_018/attempt_019) was formalized around total
> circulating IGF-1 levels. But 95% of circulating IGF-1 is bound to binding proteins —
> primarily IGFBP-3. Only free IGF-1 is biologically active. In insulin resistance (characteristic
> of suboptimal T1DM control), IGFBP-3 is reduced independently of total IGF-1.
> The result: a patient can have "normal" total IGF-1 but significantly ELEVATED free IGF-1 →
> more mTORC1 activation in keratinocytes and sebocytes → M5→M2 amplification at "normal" IGF-1 levels.
> This means the M5→M2 bridge is more active than standard IGF-1 assays reveal, and
> explains why some T1DM patients have sebaceous/rosacea activity despite "normal" hormone panels.

---

## The IGF Binding Protein System

Circulating IGF-1 exists in two states:
- **Bound IGF-1** (~95%): complexed with IGFBP-3 (primary) or IGFBP-1, IGFBP-2, etc.
  → biologically inactive; cannot bind IGF-1R; extended half-life (12-15 hours)
- **Free IGF-1** (~1-5%): unbound; biologically active; binds IGF-1R → PI3K → Akt → mTORC1
  → short half-life (minutes); rapidly cleared

**Standard IGF-1 assays measure TOTAL IGF-1** (free + bound). They do not distinguish free from
bound. The clinically relevant fraction for mTORC1 activation is free IGF-1.

## IGFBP-3 Regulation and T1DM

**How insulin regulates IGFBP-3:**
```
Insulin → hepatic insulin receptor → AKT → FKHR (FOXO1) phosphorylation → FOXO1 nuclear export
    ↓
FOXO1 in nucleus drives IGFBP-3 transcription (one of its targets)
    ↓
Insulin resistance → FOXO1 remains in nucleus → IGFBP-3 transcription continues
    HOWEVER: insulin receptor substrate (IRS-1) pathway is selectively dysregulated
    in T1DM/insulin resistance → paradoxical IGFBP-3 dysregulation
```

**The T1DM IGFBP-3 deficit:**

Three independent mechanisms reduce IGFBP-3 in T1DM:

**Mechanism 1 — Portal insulin deficit:**
T1DM uses subcutaneous insulin injection → first-pass portal insulin delivery is absent (normally
pancreatic β-cells deliver insulin directly via portal vein to liver → hepatic IGFBP-3 production
stimulated). Subcutaneous insulin → systemic circulation → hepatic portal concentration of
insulin is LOWER than peripheral → less hepatic IGF-1/IGFBP-3 signal → IGFBP-3 production ↓.
(Batch 1996 Pediatr Res: T1DM → IGFBP-3 ↓ compared to controls even with same total IGF-1)

**Mechanism 2 — Chronic low-grade inflammation → IGFBP-3 proteolysis:**
IGFBP-3 is cleaved by multiple proteases — PAPP-A (pregnancy-associated plasma protein A),
PSA (prostate-specific antigen), and MATRIX METALLOPROTEINASES (MMPs). In T1DM with chronic
low-grade inflammation: MMP-2 + MMP-9 are elevated → IGFBP-3 proteolysis → accelerated
clearance → lower steady-state IGFBP-3.

**Mechanism 3 — HbA1c correlation:**
IGFBP-3 inversely correlates with HbA1c in T1DM (Phillip 1998 J Clin Endocrinol Metab):
HbA1c 8-10% → IGFBP-3 25-40% below age-matched controls. Higher HbA1c → more IGFBP-3
proteolysis (glucose-driven MMP activation) → more free IGF-1 despite "normal" total IGF-1.

## The Free IGF-1 Effect on M5→M2

```
T1DM + suboptimal HbA1c (>8%) → IGFBP-3 reduced 25-40%
    ↓
Same total IGF-1 → more free IGF-1 (20-40% increase in free fraction)
    ↓
Free IGF-1 → IGF-1R on keratinocytes + sebocytes (type II IGF-1R) → PI3K → Akt → mTORC1
    ↓
mTORC1 in keratinocytes → KLK5 transcription ↑ → more LL-37 → Loop 1 lower threshold
mTORC1 in sebocytes → SREBP-1 → lipogenesis → more sebum → more squalene → Loop 4 substrate
mTORC1 in sebocytes → androgen receptor sensitization → more DHT signal → sebaceous hyperplasia
    ↓
RESULT: M5→M2 link via mTORC1 is MORE ACTIVE than total IGF-1 levels suggest
```

**Clinical implication:** A T1DM patient with HbA1c 8.5% and "normal" total IGF-1 may have
elevated free IGF-1 driving active mTORC1 in sebocytes → active sebaceous Loop 4 + Loop 1 priming
→ papulopustular rosacea activity despite "normal hormone panel." The standard assay misses this.

## Measurement and Protocol

**Assays:**
- Total IGF-1: standard commercial assay; does not distinguish free from bound
- Free IGF-1: specialized ELISA (Diagnostic Systems Laboratories, Alpco); not widely available;
  requires ultrafiltration or acid-ethanol precipitation to separate from binding proteins
- **Practical surrogate:** IGFBP-3 + total IGF-1 → calculate molar ratio (IGF-1/IGFBP-3).
  Normal ratio: ~0.12-0.18 (molar). Elevated ratio → more free IGF-1 per total IGF-1 measured.
  This can be ordered simultaneously from standard labs.

**Add to T-index workup:** IGFBP-3 + total IGF-1 simultaneously. Calculate ratio.
Elevated ratio (>0.20 molar) → free IGF-1 elevated → mTORC1 in sebocytes/keratinocytes more active
→ dietary glycemic control is a PRIORITY for this patient (reducing IGF-1 and improving IGFBP-3).

**Protocol intervention for elevated free IGF-1:**

| Intervention | Effect on IGFBP-3/free IGF-1 |
|-------------|------------------------------|
| Glycemic optimization (HbA1c <7.5%) | MMP activation ↓ → less IGFBP-3 proteolysis → more IGFBP-3 → less free IGF-1 |
| IF/FMD | IGF-1 total ↓ + IGFBP-3 maintained → free IGF-1 ↓ |
| Low glycemic index diet (M5) | Insulin spikes ↓ → IGF-1 pulsatility ↓ → lower free IGF-1 peak |
| Topical rapamycin 0.2% (skin) | Blocks mTORC1 downstream of free IGF-1 signal — addresses the consequence even if free IGF-1 elevated |
| Zinc (25-30mg/day) | MMP-2/MMP-9 chelation (zinc inhibits MMP activity at catalytic site) → less IGFBP-3 proteolysis → more IGFBP-3 available |

**Novel zinc connection:** Zinc inhibits MMP activity (zinc chelates at MMP catalytic domain to
PREVENT activity at physiological concentrations — MMPs have a zinc ion at their active site
required for proteolytic activity, but excess exogenous zinc beyond the catalytic zinc → inhibition).
Zinc supplementation → reduced MMP-2/MMP-9 → less IGFBP-3 proteolysis → more IGFBP-3 → less
free IGF-1 → mTORC1 less active in sebocytes. This is a FOURTH zinc mechanism not in run_024.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: IGFBP-3 Deficit in T1DM Is Not Clinically Meaningful (Compensation by Other BPs)**
If IGFBP-1 and IGFBP-2 compensate for IGFBP-3 deficit → total binding capacity maintained →
free IGF-1 not elevated despite low IGFBP-3.
**Status:** Not killed. IGFBP-1 and IGFBP-2 have lower affinity for IGF-1 than IGFBP-3 and
are regulated differently (IGFBP-1 is acutely regulated by insulin; IGFBP-2 is not a major
compensator). The Batch 1996 and Phillip 1998 studies measured total IGF-1 + IGFBP-3 in T1DM
and found reduced IGFBP-3 without proportional increase in other BPs.

**Kill B: Free IGF-1 Measurement Does Not Correlate with mTORC1 Activity in Skin**
Even if free IGF-1 is elevated, IGF-1R density in sebocytes may be the limiting factor —
if IGF-1R is not saturated at normal free IGF-1 concentrations, an increase in free IGF-1 may
not produce additional mTORC1 activation.
**Status:** Not killed but the IGF-1R saturation question is unresolved. At physiological free
IGF-1 concentrations (~1-5 ng/mL), IGF-1R occupancy in sebocytes is estimated at 30-60% of
maximum — there is receptor capacity to respond to free IGF-1 increases (Laplante 2012 Cell:
IGF-1 dose-response for mTORC1 in adipocytes shows saturation at ~10 ng/mL free; sebocytes
likely similar).
