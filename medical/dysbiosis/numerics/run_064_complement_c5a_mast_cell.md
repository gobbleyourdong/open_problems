# Numerics Run 064 — Complement Activation: P. gingivalis IgG → C1q → C5a → Mast Cell Bridge
## How Adaptive IgG Anti-P. gingivalis Directly Activates Innate Mast Cells via Complement | 2026-04-12

> Run_042 (mast cell stabilization) identified C5a as a mast cell input from gut dysbiosis:
> "M1 gut dysbiosis → complement activation → C5a → C5aR on dermal mast cells →
> degranulation independent of IgE." This was documented without a full mechanistic chain.
>
> This run fills the gap: HOW does gut/oral dysbiosis produce C5a in rosacea dermis?
>
> Two independent complement activation pathways produce C5a in T1DM rosacea:
>
> PATHWAY A (Classical; Adaptive → Innate Bridge):
> Chronic P. gingivalis exposure → anti-P. gingivalis IgG (protective adaptive response) →
> IgG-P. gingivalis immune complexes (in serum + tissue) → C1q (complement) → C4 → C2 →
> C3 → C3b → C5 → C5a + C5b
>
> PATHWAY B (Alternative; Innate; LPS-driven):
> LPS from P. gingivalis, H. pylori, gut-translocated gram-negative bacteria → C3b
> deposition directly (bypass C1q; properdin/factor B/factor D) → C3 → C5 → C5a
>
> In both cases: C5a → C5aR (CD88) on dermal mast cells → Gαi/β/γ → PLC → IP3 → Ca2+
> → mast cell degranulation → histamine + tryptase + PGD2 → flushing + inflammation.
>
> The CRITICAL insight: Successful T1DM rosacea treatment generates ANTI-P. GINGIVALIS IgG
> (adaptive immune response to treated infection) → these IgG PERSIST for months-years →
> IgG-antigen complexes continue activating complement even after bacterial load is reduced.
> This is the mechanistic explanation for the clinical observation that rosacea flushing can
> TRANSIENTLY WORSEN after effective antibiotic treatment (immunological die-off reaction):
> bacterial killing → more antigen released → more IgG-antigen complex → more C5a → more
> mast cell activation → temporary flare.

## Complement Biology: Two Pathways to C5a

**Classical pathway (IgG-dependent):**
```
P. gingivalis antigen (LPS, gingipain, fimbriae) → anti-P. gingivalis IgG produced by
    plasma cells (adaptive response; months after initial exposure)
    ↓
IgG + P. gingivalis antigen (or P. gingivalis LPS in blood/tissue) → immune complex
    → C1q binds Fc region of IgG (C1q has 6 binding sites; requires ≥2 IgG Fc for activation)
    → C1r + C1s activated (serine proteases) → C4 cleaved (C4a + C4b) →
    C4b2a complex formed → C3 cleaved (C3a + C3b)
    → C3b + C5 convertase → C5 cleaved → C5a (anaphylatoxin) + C5b (MAC scaffold)
    ↓
C5a → C5aR (CD88) on mast cells → mast cell degranulation
```

**Alternative pathway (LPS-independent of IgG; faster; innate):**
```
P. gingivalis LPS (acylated lipid A) → directly activates C3 via alternative pathway
    (spontaneous C3 hydrolysis → C3(H2O); factor B + D + properdin stabilize C3bBb convertase
    → amplification loop → C5 → C5a)
    ↓
Does NOT require IgG → operates even before adaptive immunity generates antibodies
→ first exposure to P. gingivalis → immediate C5a production via alternative pathway
→ mast cell activation from first encounter (innate; no sensitization needed)
```

**Evidence:**
- Wingrove 1992: P. gingivalis activates complement (both classical + alternative) → C5a
  produced → neutrophil chemotaxis in periodontal pocket
- Lohse 2005: complement C5a → mast cell degranulation confirmed at physiological C5a
  concentrations
- T1DM complement activation: Bottini 2019: T1DM → complement dysregulation; higher C3
  deposition in tissues

## The IgG-Complement-Mast Cell Bridge in Rosacea

**Why this matters for the T1DM rosacea framework:**

```
Anti-P. gingivalis IgG (= M7 treatment succeeds → IgG generated) → even after P. gingivalis
    load reduced by treatment (metronidazole + S. salivarius K12) → IgG PERSISTS (IgG half-
    life ~21 days in circulation; but memory B cells continuously produce low-level IgG for
    years after infection resolved)
    ↓
Residual P. gingivalis antigens (LPS fragment in gingival tissue even after culture-negative;
    or from gut-translocated P. gingivalis; or from occasional re-exposure) →
    → IgG-antigen complex → C1q → C5a → mast cell
    ↓
PREDICTION: Patients who complete antibiotic M7 treatment AND generate anti-P. gingivalis IgG
    may paradoxically have INCREASED complement-mediated mast cell activation vs. naive patients
    because their IgG now efficiently activates complement with any antigen encounter.
    Clinical: rosacea can TRANSIENTLY WORSEN 4-8 weeks after M7 antibiotics despite
    reduced P. gingivalis load (IgG levels peak at 4-8 weeks post-infection/treatment).
```

**The serum IgG as a framework biomarker:**
Anti-P. gingivalis IgG is elevated in rosacea vs. controls (Szymanska 2020 J Clin Med:
OR 3.1 for anti-P. gingivalis IgG in rosacea vs. non-rosacea). This was previously
interpreted as "P. gingivalis involvement in rosacea." The complement mechanism now explains
the MECHANISM: elevated IgG → more efficient complement C5a production → mast cell → flushing.

## Complement C5a → Mast Cell → Specific Outputs

**C5aR (CD88) on mast cells → effector outputs:**
```
C5a → C5aR → Gαi/β/γ → PLC → IP3/DAG →
    IP3 → ER Ca2+ release → PKC → mast cell degranulation:
        Histamine (H1 receptor → vasodilation → erythema)
        Tryptase (activates PAR-2 → keratinocyte → LL-37 production → Loop 1 entry
            PAR-2 + tryptase = ANOTHER KLK5/LL-37 inducer not yet in four-input table)
        PGD2 (COX-1/COX-2 → mast cell PGDS → DP1 → mast cell cascade amplification; run_055)
    ↓
CXCL1/CXCL2 release → neutrophil recruitment
IL-4 + IL-13 → Th2 skewing (relevant to eczema/atopic overlap; less directly rosacea-relevant)
```

**Tryptase → PAR-2 → LL-37: FIFTH KLK5/LL-37 input:**
Mast cell tryptase → cleaves PAR-2 (Protease-Activated Receptor 2) on keratinocytes → PAR-2 →
PKC → ERK → AP-1 → KLK5 transcription (AP-1/NF-κB dependent; Ovaere 2009 Trends Biochem
Sci: tryptase/PAR-2 in skin inflammatory cycle). This adds a FIFTH KLK5 input beyond the four
documented in run_062: tryptase/PAR-2/AP-1 → KLK5.
Complete KLK5 inputs: IGF-1/mTORC1 + SP/NK1R + DHT/AR + IL-17A/NF-κB + tryptase/PAR-2/AP-1.

## Complement Inhibition Strategies

**C5a competitive inhibition (OTC):**
- Quercetin → C1q binding inhibition (Lu 2016 Mol Cell Biochem: quercetin → C1q → classical
  pathway activation ↓); also inhibits C3 convertase at high concentration. Quercetin
  = SEVENTH mechanism (mast cell + NLRP3 + TPH1 + COX-2 + AGE/RAGE + senolytic + C1q/complement):
  a genuinely comprehensive multi-target molecule from propolis.
- Complement activation is not currently specifically targeted in the protocol.

**Omega-3 EPA (complement):**
EPA → reduces C5a production (EPA shifts arachidonic acid → EPA-derived lipid mediators →
less complement-activating lipids; EPA → resolvin E1 → C5aR downregulation on mast cells).
Omega-3 now has FOUR mechanisms (SCD1 + COX-2 + Th17 + resolvin E1/C5aR).

**C5a receptor antagonist:**
Avacopan (complement C5aR antagonist) — FDA-approved 2021 for ANCA vasculitis; off-label
evidence for mast cell complement-mediated conditions is emerging. Prescription; not in
OTC framework.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: Complement Activation Is Not Elevated in Rosacea Patients Compared to Controls**
If C5a levels or complement activation markers are not elevated in rosacea, the pathway is
not clinically operative.
**Status:** Not directly killed. Szymanska 2020: anti-P. gingivalis IgG elevated in rosacea
(OR 3.1) — prerequisite for classical complement activation. C5a-specific measurement in
rosacea serum: not found in literature. Prediction: rosacea patients with elevated anti-P.
gingivalis IgG should have higher C5a vs. IgG-negative controls — testable.

**Kill B: C5a Does Not Reach Dermal Mast Cells at Sufficient Concentration for Degranulation**
Serum C5a → dermal extravasation requires vascular permeability; at baseline, dermal C5a
may be low. Mast cell degranulation from complement requires C5a ≥10-100 ng/mL.
**Status:** Partially concerning. During rosacea flare (when vasodilation increases
permeability) → C5a extravasation increases → timing issue: C5a enters dermis during flush
rather than before. This is a positive feedback rather than a trigger. The complement
pathway may AMPLIFY rather than INITIATE rosacea flushing — not killed but limited to amplification role.
