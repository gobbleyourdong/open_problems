# Run 008 — IFN-α Sources Beyond CVB in T1DM
## Numerical Instance | 2026-04-12

> Confirmation bias audit (Phase 4) found that the CVB-specific mechanism is NOT
> the primary explanation for T1DM + rosacea co-occurrence (HLA-DR3 explains it).
> This raises the question: if IFN-α2 Simoa is elevated in a T1DM patient, does it
> mean CVB is active, or is IFN-α elevation inherent to T1DM regardless of CVB?
>
> This run maps all non-CVB sources of IFN-α elevation in T1DM, grades their evidence,
> and specifies what concurrent testing can distinguish between them.

---

## The Core Problem

IFN-α2 Simoa cannot by itself distinguish between:
1. CVB 5'UTR-deleted persistent infection (the M3 hypothesis)
2. EBV reactivation (independent IFN-α source)
3. HHV-6/7 persistence (latent DNA virus reactivation)
4. HERV-W activation (endogenous retrovirus, T1DM-relevant)
5. Other enterovirus persistence (echovirus, EV-A71, EV-D68)
6. General T1DM autoimmune IFN-α tone (from adaptive immune activation itself)

**CVB detection rate in T1DM islets: ~40-50%** (from multiple islet studies including nPOD).
This means ~50-60% of T1DM patients have NO detectable CVB — yet the IFN-signature is present in ~50-60% of T1DM patients at diagnosis. These populations overlap but are NOT identical.

**Conclusion before looking at alternatives:** IFN-α elevation in T1DM is NOT exclusively CVB-driven. The antiviral protocol targeting CVB addresses one source, not all sources.

---

## Non-CVB IFN-α Sources — Graded Evidence

### 1. HERV-W (Human Endogenous Retrovirus W / MSRV) — CANDIDATE

**Evidence in T1DM:**
- HERV-W ENV protein detected in serum of T1DM patients (Brudek 2012 and follow-up studies)
- HERV-W ENV activates TLR4 on innate immune cells → NF-κB + IFN-β/α production
- Mechanism: HERV-W transcription increases under inflammatory conditions (IFN-γ, viral infection) → HERV-W ENV protein → TLR4 → IFN-α (autocrine amplification loop)
- More established in MS (HERV-W ENV protein is the target of the drug temelimab, in Phase 3 trials for MS) than T1DM

**T1DM-specific finding:**
- HERV-W ENV elevated in ~30-40% of new-onset T1DM patients in some cohorts
- Correlates with autoantibody titers (ZnT8, IA-2) — suggests HERV-W activation is part of islet autoimmunity, not just epiphenomenon

**Evidence grade:** II-2 (observational cohort data; no mechanistic T1DM-specific confirmation)

**Kill criterion:** HERV-W ENV NOT elevated in CVB-negative T1DM patients → HERV-W is not a primary IFN source in absence of CVB priming

**Assay:** HERV-W ENV protein (ELISA or multiplex; research-grade; not commercially available widely) or ISG score (pan-IFN response — cannot distinguish HERV from viral source)

---

### 2. EBV Reactivation — STRONG CANDIDATE

**Why EBV is relevant:**
- EBV infects nearly all humans (>90% seropositivity by adulthood)
- EBV encodes EBER1/EBER2 (non-coding RNAs) that activate TLR3 and RIG-I → IFN-α production
- EBV EBNA antigens can trigger molecular mimicry (cross-reactivity with autoantigens)
- Established causal role in MS (Bjornevik 2022, N=10M cohort, 32× MS risk post-seroconversion)

**T1DM association:**
- EBV antibody titers (VCA IgG, EA-D) are elevated in T1DM patients vs controls in several cohorts
- EBV DNA found in pancreatic ductal cells in some studies
- PROPOSED mechanism: EBV reactivation in pancreatic cells → EBER → TLR3 → IFN-α → pDC priming → lower threshold for islet autoimmunity (same pDC pathway as CVB model)
- ENDIA cohort: EBV seroconversion timing relative to islet autoimmunity onset — data not yet published at scale

**Evidence grade:** II-2 (epidemiological association; causal chain not proven in T1DM; MS model established)

**Practical implication:** EBV reactivation (not just prior infection) produces IFN-α. If an elevated IFN-α2 Simoa reading is observed, concurrent EBV serology is MANDATORY to rule out reactivation as the driver before attributing to CVB.

**Required concurrent test:** VCA IgM (acute reactivation marker) + EA-D IgG (early antigen, marks recent reactivation). If both negative → EBV reactivation not the current driver. VCA IgG alone is insufficient (everyone is IgG positive).

---

### 3. HHV-6 / HHV-7 Persistence — CANDIDATE

**Evidence:**
- HHV-6A and HHV-6B can integrate chromosomally (ciHHV-6) — present in ~1% of population
- HHV-6 reactivation documented in autoimmune conditions
- HHV-6 dsRNA → RIG-I → IFN-α (same sensing pathway as CVB)
- HHV-7 less studied; similar biology

**T1DM association:** Weak epidemiological data; no large cohort studies. HHV-6 more studied in MS, encephalitis, and transplant immunosuppression.

**Assay:** HHV-6 IgG avidity (high avidity = past infection; low avidity or IgM = recent/reactivation). ciHHV-6 can be tested by PCR.

**Evidence grade:** II-3 (weak; not T1DM-specific)

---

### 4. Other Enteroviruses (Echovirus, EV-A71, EV-D68) — CANDIDATE

**Evidence:**
- Enteroviruses as a class are associated with T1DM; CVB is the most studied species
- Echovirus 9, 30 and Enterovirus A71 have also been found in T1DM islets in some studies
- 5'UTR deletion mechanism: specifically characterized for CVB3 and CVB5; whether echovirus uses same mechanism is NOT established
- Islet studies typically use pan-enterovirus VP1 staining → detects all enteroviruses, not CVB-specific

**Critical implication:** Published "CVB in islets" studies may actually be detecting OTHER enteroviruses misidentified as CVB, or co-infections. The nPOD VP1 staining that underpins much of the CVB persistence literature is pan-enterovirus, not CVB-specific.

**Assay:** Species-level PCR or next-gen sequencing with enterovirus-specific probes. Currently: no clinical assay distinguishes CVB3 from CVB4 from echovirus from EV-A71 in islets.

**Evidence grade:** II-2 for enteroviruses as class; I for CVB-specific (studies insufficient for species-level causal claim)

---

### 5. General T1DM Autoimmune IFN-α Tone — HIGH EVIDENCE

**This is perhaps the most important alternative source:**

- IFN-α signature appears 12-24 months BEFORE T1DM onset (Kallionpää 2019, DIPP cohort)
- This is likely reflecting immune priming, not viral infection per se
- ISG upregulation in T1DM is PARTIALLY driven by innate immune sensing of beta cell death itself:
  - Dying beta cells release self-DNA/RNA → STING/cGAS pathway → IFN-β/α
  - This is analogous to the IFN-α elevation seen in lupus (where NETs and released DNA drive IFN)
- pDC expansion in T1DM (PMID 24973447) may be an EFFECT of ongoing autoimmunity, not a cause

**This creates a confounding loop:** T1DM → beta cell death → self-nucleic acid release → IFN-α → pDC expansion → measured as elevated IFN-α2 Simoa → attributed to CVB.

**Evidence grade:** II-2 (mechanism established in general; T1DM-specific IFN-α from self-nucleic acids not definitively separated from viral sources in clinical studies)

**The unanswerable question with current assays:** Is the IFN-α elevation in T1DM patients driven primarily by:
(a) Viral persistence (CVB/EBV/HHV-6)
(b) Self-nucleic acid release from dying beta cells
(c) HLA-DR3 genetics making the IFN pathway hyperreactive
(d) All three simultaneously

There is NO current clinical assay that can distinguish these sources.

## What This Means for the T-Index v3 / M3 Arm

**Previous framework assumption:** Node D (IFN-α2 Simoa) = CVB arm activity

**Revised, more accurate statement:**
Node D = **sum of all active IFN-α sources** including CVB, EBV, HERV-W, self-DNA, and HLA baseline

**This does NOT weaken the framework.** The pDC expansion → M4 threshold lowering happens regardless of which IFN source is active. The TREATMENT implication is different:
- CVB-specific source → OSBP inhibitors (itraconazole), antiviral protocol
- EBV reactivation → antiviral protocol overlaps (omega-3, vitamin D); EBV-specific: valacyclovir (off-label reactivation suppression)
- HERV-W → no targeted therapy (temelimab is investigational); anti-inflammatory protocol reduces HERV-W transcription
- Self-DNA/STING → autophagy (IF, NAD+), STING pathway suppression (emerging targets)
- HLA baseline → cannot be changed; shifts interpretation of what "normal" IFN-α means for this patient

**The practical conclusion:** Elevated IFN-α2 Simoa warrants antiviral protocol initiation regardless of source. The question of CVB specificity matters for which antiviral interventions are added (OSBP inhibitors are CVB-specific; general immune modulation is source-agnostic).

## Required Protocol Update: IFN-α Interpretation

When IFN-α2 Simoa is elevated, ADD concurrent testing:
1. **EBV serology**: VCA IgM + EA-D IgG (reactivation panel; NOT just VCA IgG which everyone has)
   - Cost: ~$30-60
   - If EBV reactivation confirmed: consider whether general antiviral protocol is sufficient
2. **HLA-DR3 genotyping** (if not already done): establishes genetic baseline
   - Cost: ~$50-100
   - HLA-DR3 homozygous → higher genetic IFN-α tone; contextualizes Simoa value
3. **CMV IgG avidity** (optional, lower priority)
   - If CMV primary or reactivation: confirms another IFN source

**The IFN-α2 result is ACTIONABLE regardless of source.** But knowing the source improves precision:
- EBV reactivation → periodontal health also matters (EBV lives in B cells in gingival tissue; P. gingivalis can reactivate latent EBV via butyrate production + histone deacetylase)
- CVB confirmed → OSBP inhibitor addition is most specific intervention
- HLA baseline elevation → recalibrate expectations; "normal" Simoa may be higher for this patient

## Sky Bridge Implication: M3 Is Broader Than CVB

This run supports an update to the M3 mountain:

**M3 (Virome persistence) should be framed as:**
"Persistent viral/endogenous retroviral activity → chronic IFN-α → pDC expansion → M4 threshold lowering"

NOT framed as:
"CVB specifically → IFN-α → pDC → M4 threshold lowering"

The CVB-specific mechanism is the best-documented route but is NOT the only route. The IFN-α arm of M4 is likely active in T1DM via multiple concurrent sources. This strengthens the justification for addressing the IFN-α arm but weakens the CVB-specific justification for OSBP inhibitors specifically.

## P. Gingivalis — EBV Connection (Bonus Finding)

**Highly relevant to the M7→M3 bridge:**
P. gingivalis produces short-chain fatty acids (butyrate-like) that can reactivate latent EBV in gingival B cells via histone deacetylase inhibition of the EBV reactivation promoters. This means:

**P. gingivalis oral dysbiosis → EBV reactivation in gingival tissue → systemic EBV IFN-α**

This adds a THIRD mechanism by which M7 (oral dysbiosis) connects to IFN-α:
1. M7→M3 (bacteremia → CAR → CVB, attempt_006)
2. M7→M1 (gut colonization → TLR2, attempt_012)
3. M7→EBV-reactivation → IFN-α (this run — new)

All three converge on the same endpoint: elevated IFN-α → M4 threshold lowering.

**Periodontal treatment now addresses THREE independent IFN-α inputs simultaneously.** This further increases the ROI of periodontal treatment as an intervention.
