# Numerics Run 078 — Urolithin A: Gut-Produced Mitophagy Inducer Parallel to Spermidine
## Gordonibacter urolithinfaciens → Urolithin A → PINK1/Parkin → Mitophagy → mtROS ↓ | 2026-04-12

> Run_041 documented spermidine → EP300 inhibition → Beclin-1 → autophagy/mitophagy →
> removal of damaged mitochondria → mtROS (Signal 2) source eliminated → NLRP3 ↓.
> Spermidine is the fifth NLRP3 inhibition mechanism (of six total in the framework).
>
> Urolithin A (UA) is a gut-produced metabolite derived from dietary ellagitannins (pomegranate,
> raspberries, walnuts, strawberries) by gut bacteria — specifically Gordonibacter
> urolithinfaciens (formerly Bifidobacterium pseudocatenulatum; reclassified). UA induces
> mitophagy via a DIFFERENT molecular pathway (PINK1/Parkin) than spermidine (EP300/Beclin-1).
> The outcome is identical: damaged mitochondria cleared → mtROS ↓ → NLRP3 Signal 2 ↓.
>
> In T1DM gut dysbiosis: Gordonibacter urolithinfaciens is reduced (dysbiosis depletes
> Actinobacteria, which include Gordonibacter) → UA production ↓ → mitophagy ↓ →
> mtROS chronically elevated. This is an INDEPENDENT mitophagy deficit parallel to but
> mechanistically distinct from the spermidine/EP300 deficit.

## Urolithin A Biochemistry

**Ellagitannin → Urolithin A pathway:**
```
Dietary ellagitannins (pomegranate: punicalagin; raspberry: ellagitannin; walnuts: pedunculagin)
    → Gastrointestinal hydrolysis → Ellagic acid (ellagitannin → ellagic acid + sugar)
    ↓
Colon bacteria:
    Gordonibacter urolithinfaciens (primary producer)
    G. pamelaeae (secondary producer)
    → Decarboxylation + dehydroxylation: ellagic acid → urolithin A (UA; 3,8-dihydroxy-6H-dibenzo[b,d]pyran-6-one)
    → UA absorbed → liver conjugation (glucuronide + sulfate) → systemic circulation
    → Plasma UA: 0.1-5 µM after pomegranate consumption in UA producers
    ↓
Important: ~30-40% of adults produce no urolithin A despite consuming ellagitannins
    (UA non-producers: lack functional Gordonibacter strains → no metabolic conversion)
    → T1DM + dysbiosis patients may disproportionately be UA non-producers
```

**UA vs. Spermidine — parallel mitophagy pathways:**
```
Spermidine (run_041):
    EP300 (acetyltransferase) → inhibited by spermidine → Beclin-1 deacetylated
    → Beclin-1 → PI3K-III complex → autophagosome initiation → mitophagy
    Upstream: Beclin-1-dependent autophagy initiation

Urolithin A:
    UA → activates PINK1 (PTEN-Induced Kinase 1) kinase
    → PINK1 → phosphorylates Parkin (E3 ubiquitin ligase) → Parkin activation
    → Parkin → ubiquitinates mitochondrial outer membrane proteins
    → Ubiquitin → p62/SQSTM1 + LC3 recruitment → selective autophagosome engulfment
    → Damaged mitochondria engulfed → fusion with lysosome → degradation → mtROS source removed
    Upstream: PINK1/Parkin-dependent selective mitophagy (different from Beclin-1 path)
```

**Ryu 2016 Cell Metab (seminal UA mitophagy paper):**
UA → C. elegans and mammalian cells → PINK1/Parkin-dependent mitophagy ↑ → ATP production
efficiency ↑ + mtROS ↓. In aged mice: UA oral administration → muscle mitochondrial quality ↑
(age-related mitochondrial decline reversed). Mitomycin C stress + UA → mitophagy 3.5× vs.
control → damaged mitochondria cleared more rapidly.

## Urolithin A → NLRP3 Signal 2 Reduction

**PINK1/Parkin → mitophagy → mtROS ↓ → NLRP3 Signal 2 ↓:**
```
Damaged mitochondria (from T1DM ROS: eNOS uncoupling + AGE-RAGE NADPH oxidase + HIF-1α):
    → mtROS (superoxide O2•-) leakage from damaged Complex I/III
    → mtROS = primary NLRP3 Signal 2 in many cell types

UA → PINK1 → Parkin → mitophagy → damaged mitochondria cleared
    → Less mtROS generation → NLRP3 Signal 2 source eliminated
    → Same functional outcome as spermidine → mitophagy (run_041)
    → NLRP3 inflammasome assembly less frequently triggered from Signal 2

UA benefit is ADDITIVE to spermidine:
    Spermidine removes damaged mitochondria via Beclin-1 pathway
    UA removes damaged mitochondria via PINK1/Parkin pathway
    Both pathways contribute to mitochondrial quality control simultaneously
    → Combined: more complete mitophagy than either alone
```

**UA in T1DM context:**
```
T1DM: three concurrent sources of mitochondrial damage:
    AGE-RAGE → NADPH oxidase → O2•- → mitochondrial damage (run_060)
    eNOS uncoupling → O2•- (run_052)
    HIF-1α/reoxygenation → ROS bursts (run_050)
    ↓
Higher mitochondrial damage burden → more need for mitophagy → both spermidine and UA
    are relevant; depletion of either impairs mitochondrial quality control
    ↓
T1DM dysbiosis → Gordonibacter ↓ → UA ↓ → PINK1/Parkin mitophagy ↓
    → Damaged mitochondria accumulate → mtROS elevation → NLRP3 perpetually primed
```

## UA vs. Spermidine: Complementary Protocol Agents

| Feature | Spermidine (run_041) | Urolithin A (this run) |
|---------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Source | Dietary (wheat germ, fermented foods) or endogenous synthesis | Gut bacteria from dietary ellagitannins (pomegranate, berries) |
| Mitophagy pathway | EP300/Beclin-1 (autophagy initiation) | PINK1/Parkin (selective mitophagy) |
| NLRP3 target | Signal 2 source removal (mtROS) | Signal 2 source removal (mtROS) |
| Responder status | Universal (dietary absorption) | Non-producer issue (30-40% make no UA) |
| Protocol form | 1-2 mg/day spermidine supplement or dietary | Pomegranate juice/ellagitannin foods OR UA supplement (Mitopure) |
| T1DM depletion | Dysbiosis → less spermidine-producing bacteria | Gordonibacter ↓ → UA ↓ |

**Producer status consideration:**
UA requires Gordonibacter. ~60-70% of adults produce UA from dietary ellagitannins; 30-40%
do not (non-producers). For non-producers: UA supplement (synthetic urolithin A; Mitopure by
Amazentis) bypasses the gut bacteria requirement and delivers UA directly.

## Protocol

**Primary (food-based):** Daily ellagitannin-rich foods:
- Pomegranate juice/arils: 1 cup (8 oz) pomegranate juice provides ~500-1000 mg punicalagin
- Raspberries/strawberries: 100g/day
- Walnuts: 30g/day (ellagic acid + omega-3)
- Note: bioavailability depends on Gordonibacter producer status

**Secondary (if non-producer confirmed or suspected in T1DM dysbiosis):**
- UA supplement (Mitopure by Amazentis; Phase 2/3 data in elderly muscle; 500-1000mg/day)
- Availability: OTC in US/EU as nutritional supplement

**Gut microbiome support for Gordonibacter:**
Gordonibacter is an Actinobacteria; T1DM dysbiosis depletes Actinobacteria.
Prebiotic fiber + L. reuteri → microbiome diversification → Actinobacteria recovery → UA
production potentially restored over 8-12 weeks (no direct Gordonibacter-specific probiotic
available commercially).

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: UA → PINK1/Parkin Mitophagy Is Not Confirmed in Dermal Macrophages or Keratinocytes**
Ryu 2016 data is in C. elegans + murine skeletal muscle + NIH3T3 fibroblasts. Dermal-specific
PINK1/Parkin mitophagy via UA is not directly published.
**Status:** Not killed. PINK1/Parkin mitophagy is ubiquitous (expressed in all nucleated cells).
The mechanism is cell-type agnostic (requires only PINK1 + Parkin expression + damaged
mitochondria + UA). Dermal macrophages and keratinocytes express both. The C. elegans +
muscle data establishes the principle; tissue-specific confirmation is lacking but not
needed to establish mechanism validity.

**Kill B: UA Producer Status Cannot Be Determined Without Fecal Analysis; Framework Protocol Cannot Account for Non-Producers**
~30-40% of people don't produce UA. Without fecal microbiome analysis confirming Gordonibacter,
prescribing pomegranate juice may be ineffective in non-producers.
**Status:** Not killed. For food-first approach: recommend pomegranate consumption regardless
(no harm in non-producers from the food itself; other benefits: antioxidants, polyphenols).
For documented non-producers or suspected T1DM dysbiosis with Gordonibacter depletion:
recommend UA supplement (Mitopure) which bypasses producer status requirement entirely.
The protocol accommodates both scenarios.
