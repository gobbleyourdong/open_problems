## CRITICAL FINDING: GALT Mechanism > LPS Mechanism

The dominant published mechanism for gut-skin disease connection is:

```
Gut dysbiosis / leaky gut → gut inflammatory milieu
    ↓
GALT primes mucosal T cells in mesenteric lymph nodes
    ↓ (KEY STEP — T cell reprogramming)
T cells expressing α4β7/CCR9 (gut-homing) acquire skin-homing markers (CLA/CCR4)
    ↓ (gut-primed T cells now re-route to skin)
Skin-homing T cells (with gut-derived memory) traffic to skin-draining lymph nodes
    ↓
Deplete skin Tregs → lower threshold for local skin inflammatory response
    ↓
Skin dysbiosis triggers disease in Treg-depleted skin
```

DSS colitis mouse models: colitis expands CD4+ T cells co-expressing BOTH gut- and skin-homing markers. Gut-microbe-responsive T cells confirmed to migrate to skin-draining lymph nodes.

This is SHARED IL-23/Th17 AXIS — the same cytokine environment drives both IBD and psoriasis:
- IL-23 → Th17 → IL-17A/F + IL-22
- Same in gut (IBD) and skin (psoriasis, seb derm)
- The T cells are the carrier, not the LPS

## What Is NOT the LPS Gap Filler

LBP in psoriasis: only elevated with concurrent metabolic syndrome. If psoriasis were purely LPS-driven, LBP would be elevated across all psoriasis patients, not just the metabolic syndrome subset. This is evidence that LPS is a contributing amplifier in the context of metabolic dysfunction, not the primary driver of gut-skin disease connection.
