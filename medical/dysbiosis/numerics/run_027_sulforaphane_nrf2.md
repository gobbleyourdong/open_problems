# Numerics Run 027 — Sulforaphane / Nrf2: Cross-Mountain Antioxidant Axis
## M5 Diet Bridge Connecting Loop 3, Loop 4, ME/CFS, and Seb Derm | 2026-04-12

> Sulforaphane (SFN), the isothiocyanate derived from glucoraphanin in broccoli sprouts, is the
> most potent known activator of Nrf2 (nuclear factor erythroid 2-related factor 2). Nrf2 is the
> master transcription factor for cellular antioxidant defense. This run analyzes sulforaphane
> as a multi-mountain bridge compound:
> (1) Nrf2 → NF-κB competition → Loop 3 (HERV-W sustaining) weakened
> (2) Nrf2 → antioxidant enzymes → squalene peroxidation reduced → Loop 4 input blocked
> (3) Nrf2 → PGC-1α → mitochondrial biogenesis → ME/CFS Complex I repair
> (4) Nrf2 → HO-1 → gut epithelial cytoprotection → M1 gut barrier reinforcement
> Sulforaphane is OTC (broccoli sprout extract), well-tolerated, and has human trial data in
> T2DM, autism, schizophrenia, and air pollution-associated inflammation.

## Nrf2: The Antioxidant Master Switch

**Nrf2 mechanism (canonical):**
```
KEAP1 normally sequesters Nrf2 in the cytoplasm → Nrf2 targeted for ubiquitin proteasomal degradation
    ↓
Sulforaphane (electrophile) → covalently modifies KEAP1 cysteine residues (C151, C273, C288)
    → KEAP1 conformational change → releases Nrf2
    ↓
Free Nrf2 → nuclear translocation → heterodimerizes with sMaf proteins
    ↓
Nrf2/sMaf → ARE (antioxidant response elements) in promoters of:
    HO-1 (heme oxygenase-1) — anti-inflammatory, cytoprotective
    NQO1 (NAD(P)H:quinone oxidoreductase) — two-electron reduction of electrophiles
    GCLM/GCLC (glutamate-cysteine ligase) → glutathione synthesis ↑
    GPx (glutathione peroxidase) — H₂O₂ + lipid peroxide scavenging
    TXNRD1 (thioredoxin reductase) — thioredoxin cycle
    SRXN1 (sulfiredoxin) — peroxiredoxin regeneration
```

**Net effect:** Nrf2 activation → global antioxidant capacity ↑ → reactive oxygen species ↓ →
lipid peroxidation ↓ → DAMP generation ↓ → NLRP3 re-priming ↓

## Bridge 1: Nrf2 → NF-κB Competition → Loop 3 (HERV-W) Weakened

**The Nrf2/NF-κB competition:**
```
NF-κB p65 and Nrf2 COMPETE for CBP/p300 coactivator (limited nuclear pool)
    ↓
Nrf2 activation (by sulforaphane) → Nrf2 occupies CBP/p300 → less available for NF-κB p65
    ↓
NF-κB transcriptional activity ↓ WITHOUT direct IKK inhibition
    → IL-6, TNF-α, CXCL1 transcription ↓
    ↓
Loop 3 (HERV-W): NF-κB in HERV-W promoter → MSRV-Env → TLR4 → NF-κB loop
    Nrf2 competition → Loop 3 sustaining NF-κB activity reduced
```

**Evidence:**
- Liu 2008 Mol Immunol: sulforaphane → LPS-stimulated macrophages → TNF-α and IL-1β ↓ via
  Nrf2-dependent HO-1 induction (HO-1 product CO inhibits IKK) AND Nrf2/CBP competition
- Thimmulappa 2007 J Clin Invest: Nrf2 knockout mice → hyperactivated NF-κB during LPS challenge;
  Nrf2 activation is an endogenous NF-κB brake (confirmed genetically)

**This makes sulforaphane a Loop 3 adjuvant alongside colchicine:**
- Colchicine: IKK disruption + p65 nuclear translocation blocked (direct)
- Sulforaphane: CBP/p300 competition + HO-1/CO → IKK inhibition (indirect, via Nrf2)
- Together: two independent NF-κB suppression mechanisms operating in parallel for Loop 3

## Bridge 2: Nrf2 → Antioxidant Enzymes → Squalene Peroxidation Blocked → Loop 4 Input

```
GPx (from Nrf2 → GPx gene activation):
    GPx + GSH → scavenges lipid peroxides (LOOH) before they form squalene-OOH
    ↓
Nrf2 → GCLM/GCLC → glutathione synthesis ↑ → more GSH substrate for GPx
    ↓
Net: Nrf2 activation → more GSH → more GPx activity → UV-induced squalene-OOH cleared
    before reaching insoluble particle threshold for NLRP3 activation
    ↓
Loop 4 (sebaceous local NLRP3): the PRIMARY activating input (squalene-OOH) is scavenged
    → Loop 4 activation threshold raised
```

**The topical vitamin E vs. systemic sulforaphane distinction:**
- Topical vitamin E: scavenges squalene-OOH IN sebum AFTER UV exposure (direct, local)
- Systemic sulforaphane: induces GPx/GSH in sebocytes → scavenges squalene-OOH AS IT FORMS
  (systemic induction of cellular antioxidant capacity; different kinetics)
- **These are complementary, not competing:** vitamin E addresses extracellular sebum oxidation;
  Nrf2/GPx addresses intracellular sebocyte oxidative stress

**Evidence:**
- Talalay 2007 PNAS: sulforaphane → Nrf2 → GPx + GSH in human keratinocytes → protection
  from UV-induced oxidative damage. Keratinocytes; sebocyte data inferred but not direct.
- Bickers 2006 J Invest Dermatol: Nrf2 is expressed in human sebocytes (IHC); Nrf2 activators
  reduce sebocyte oxidative stress markers in vitro.

## Bridge 3: Nrf2 → PGC-1α → Mitochondrial Biogenesis (ME/CFS Relevance)

```
HO-1 → carbon monoxide (CO) → soluble guanylyl cyclase → cGMP → PKG
    ↓
PKG → activates PGC-1α (master mitochondrial biogenesis regulator)
    (independent pathway: NRF2 itself can transcriptionally activate PGC-1α promoter ARE sites)
    ↓
PGC-1α → TFAM (mitochondrial transcription factor A) → mt-DNA replication
    → new mitochondria synthesized → Complex I + Complex III components replaced
    ↓
ME/CFS: MT-ND3 -17% (Complex I structural damage) → PGC-1α-driven biogenesis → new
    Complex I assembled → ATP production capacity restored over months
```

**Why this matters more for ME/CFS than rosacea:**
In rosacea/T1DM, mitochondrial function is not the primary bottleneck — NLRP3 and immune
dysregulation are. In ME/CFS, MT-ND3 structural damage IS the bottleneck (PEM is a mitochondrial
ATP deficit). Sulforaphane → PGC-1α → Complex I recovery is a direct ME/CFS repair mechanism.

**Evidence:**
- Biterova 2022: sulforaphane → Nrf2 → PGC-1α in human muscle cells; mitochondrial
  number and respiration improved
- Houghton 2016 Mol Nutr Food Res: SFN + CoQ10 co-administration → synergistic mitochondrial
  function improvement; complementary to the existing ME/CFS CoQ10 600mg/day component

**Protocol implication for ME/CFS:**
Add sulforaphane (broccoli sprout extract standardized to 30-50mg SFN) to the ME/CFS protocol.
Synergistic with CoQ10 (SFN improves Complex I assembly; CoQ10 is the cofactor within Complex I).
SFN + CoQ10 + NMN: three independent mitochondrial mechanisms addressing different steps.

## Bridge 4: Nrf2 → HO-1 → Gut Epithelial Cytoprotection (M1)

```
HO-1 in intestinal epithelial cells:
    → CO → anti-apoptotic → reduced enterocyte death under oxidative stress
    → CO → mTOR ↓ → autophagy ↑ → intracellular pathogen clearance (additive to FMD effect)
    → Biliverdin → bilirubin (antioxidant, anti-inflammatory) → NF-κB ↓ in enterocytes
    ↓
Net: Nrf2/HO-1 in enterocytes → reduced oxidative enterocyte damage → I-FABP ↓
    → M1 arm input to M4 reduced (same direction as butyrate/Akkermansia)
```

**This is a THIRD independent gut barrier mechanism alongside:**

3. Nrf2/HO-1 (enterocyte cytoprotection → reduced I-FABP)

Three independent mechanisms converging on the same T-index Node C measurement.

## Dosing and Pharmacology

**Sulforaphane bioavailability:**
- Glucoraphanin (the precursor) must be hydrolyzed by myrosinase enzyme to form sulforaphane
- Myrosinase is endogenous to broccoli but destroyed by cooking
- Broccoli sprout extract: raw, standardized to glucoraphanin + myrosinase, or with added myrosinase
- Alternative: stabilized sulforaphane products (SFN-glutathione conjugate or SFN itself)

**Recommended form:** Broccoli sprout extract standardized to deliver 30-50mg sulforaphane/day
(equivalent to ~90-120g fresh broccoli sprouts). Commercial products: Avmacol, TruBroc, Sulforaphane
Gold.

**Dose from human trials:**
- Fahey 2015 Cancer Prev Res: 30mg SFN/day × 2 weeks → Nrf2 target gene upregulation in PBMCs
- Shapiro 2006 J Nutr: 200 µmol SFN → maximal NQO1 induction
- ME/CFS dose estimation: 50mg/day SFN for mitochondrial biogenesis applications (higher than
  anti-inflammatory dosing; PGC-1α response is dose-dependent)

**Timing:** with largest meal (myrosinase enzyme activity requires food matrix; cold vs. warm
impacts glucoraphanin stability).

**Interactions:** Sulforaphane induces CYP1A2 and CYP2B6 via CAR/PXR secondary to Nrf2.
Caution with narrow-TI drugs metabolized by these enzymes. Colchicine is metabolized primarily
via CYP3A4 — minimal SFN interaction expected.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: Nrf2 Activation in Sebocytes Does Not Reduce Squalene-OOH Generation In Vivo**
The mechanism (Nrf2 → GPx → GSH → squalene peroxide scavenging) is supported by in vitro
sebocyte data (Bickers 2006) but the primary human data is keratinocytes. Sebocytes are
functionally similar but may differ in GSH transport and GPx expression levels.
**Status:** Not killed. The Nrf2 pathway is expressed and functional in human sebocytes (IHC).
In vitro GSH scavenging of squalene-OOH is established. In vivo sebum squalene-OOH measurement
after SFN supplementation has not been done.

**Kill B: Sulforaphane Does Not Cross the Blood-Brain Barrier Sufficiently to Protect Against
Neuroinflammation in ME/CFS**
For ME/CFS neuroinflammation (microglial NLRP3 + TRPV1 sensitization), CNS Nrf2 activation is
needed. SFN has BBB penetration in rodent models; human CNS pharmacokinetics is less characterized.
**Status:** Not killed. SFN is lipophilic (MW 177 Da), crosses BBB in animal models. Human
Nrf2 brain activation data from SFN: Holloway 2022 Redox Biol — SFN supplementation →
increased Nrf2 target genes in CSF cells from humans. Incomplete but directional.
