# Numerics Run 020 — Omega-3 Specialized Pro-Resolving Mediators (SPM)
## Active Resolution vs. Passive Suppression — Distinct NLRP3/DAMP Mechanism | 2026-04-12

> Omega-3 (EPA+DHA 4g/day) is in the protocol as "anti-inflammatory."
> The framework has not distinguished between two mechanistically distinct effects:
> 1. SUPPRESSION: omega-3 → competitive COX-2 substrate → less PGE2/LTB4 (pro-inflammatory
>    eicosanoids) → inflammation blunted (passive negative regulation)
> 2. ACTIVE RESOLUTION: omega-3 → specialized pro-resolving mediators (SPMs: resolvins,
>    protectins, maresins) → ACTIVE termination signals → neutrophil clearance, DAMP
>    efferocytosis, tissue homeostasis restoration
>
> The distinction matters for the NLRP3/pyroptosis loop (run_017 Type 2 non-responder):
> BHB blocks NLRP3 activation (K+ efflux pathway) but does NOT clear the DAMPs
> (HMGB1, ATP, uric acid) that re-prime NLRP3 after pyroptosis.
> Only efferocytosis (SPM-driven macrophage clearance) clears DAMPs.
> SPMs at therapeutic omega-3 doses also independently block NLRP3 via GPR32 receptor —
> a DIFFERENT receptor from BHB's K+ efflux mechanism → dual NLRP3 blockade.

## SPM Classes: What They Are and Where They Come From

### From EPA (20:5 n-3):
**E-series resolvins (RvE1, RvE2, RvE3):**
- RvE1: most studied; activates ChemR23 (chemerin receptor 23) on macrophages + neutrophils
- RvE1 → stops neutrophil chemotaxis; promotes neutrophil apoptosis; enhances macrophage efferocytosis
- RvE2/E3: similar but less potent; shorter half-life

### From DHA (22:6 n-3):
**D-series resolvins (RvD1-D6):**
- RvD1: most potent; activates GPR32 (also called DRV1) on macrophages and sensory neurons
- RvD1 → blocks NLRP3 inflammasome assembly (GPR32 → cAMP ↑ → PKA → NLRP3 phosphorylation → cannot assemble)
- RvD1 → reduces HMGB1 secretion + promotes HMGB1 clearance (HMGB1 is the key DAMP in Loop 2)
- RvD2: GPR18 agonist; anti-bacterial at nanomolar concentrations

**Protectins (PD1/NPD1):**
- PD1 (neuroprotectin D1): from DHA; inhibits NF-κB; promotes neuronal survival
- Protects sensory neurons → relevant to M8/TRPV1 neurogenic axis (PD1 → reduces SP secretion from trigeminal neurons)

**Maresins (MaR1, MaR2):**
- Macrophage-derived; tissue homeostasis + wound healing
- MaR1 → reduces dermal fibrosis (relevant to rosacea rhinophyma/skin thickening)
- MaR1 → accelerates epithelial wound repair (important for gut barrier restoration in M1 arm)

### Aspirin-Triggered SPMs (AT-SPMs):
```
EPA + COX-2 → normally → PGE2 (pro-inflammatory)
    ↓ if aspirin acetylates COX-2 (even at 81mg/day low dose):
Aspirin-acetylated COX-2 → instead produces 18R-HEPE from EPA
    ↓
18R-HEPE → RvE1 precursor → AT-RvE1 (aspirin-triggered resolvin E1)
    AT-RvE1 is MORE POTENT than standard RvE1 (different stereochemistry → longer half-life; binds ChemR23 better)

DHA + aspirin-acetylated COX-2 → 17R-HDHA
    ↓
17R-HDHA → AT-RvD1, AT-RvD3 (aspirin-triggered D-series resolvins)
    More potent than standard RvD series; greater NLRP3-blocking efficacy per molecule
```

**Clinical implication:** T1DM patients already on aspirin 81mg/day (cardiovascular protection)
→ if also taking EPA+DHA 4g/day → AT-resolvin production is augmented → superior SPM effect
vs. omega-3 alone. This combination was not designed for rosacea but produces this benefit
as a secondary consequence.

## The Active Resolution Mechanism (vs. Suppression)

### Suppression (standard omega-3 understanding):
```
EPA competes with arachidonic acid (AA) for COX-2 and LOX enzymes
    ↓
Less AA available → less PGE2 (prostaglandin E2), less LTB4 (leukotriene B4), less TXA2
    ↓
Pro-inflammatory signal reduced → inflammation continues at lower amplitude but persists
    ↓
Result: blunted but ongoing inflammation; state is "muted," not resolved
```

### Active Resolution (SPM mechanism):
```
Resolvins/protectins bind GPR32, ChemR23, BLT1 receptors on macrophages and neutrophils
    ↓
ACTIVE SIGNALS SENT:
1. STOP SIGNAL → neutrophil stop-infiltration (ChemR23 → L-selectin shedding → neutrophils
   cannot adhere to vessel walls → neutrophil influx into tissue terminates)
2. CLEARANCE SIGNAL → macrophage efferocytosis activated (GPR32 → macrophage "eat me" response
   to apoptotic neutrophils + cellular debris including DAMPs)
3. REPROGRAMMING SIGNAL → macrophage M1→M2 phenotype switch (pro-resolution macrophages produce
   IL-10, TGF-β → anti-inflammatory; produce more SPMs themselves → resolution cascade)
4. BARRIER RESTORATION → epithelial tight junction reassembly promoted (RvD1 → claudin/occludin
   expression ↑ in gut/skin epithelial cells → barrier repair)
    ↓
Result: inflammation IS COMPLETED (not just blunted); tissue returns to homeostasis
```

**The critical difference for Loop 2 (NLRP3/pyroptosis):**

After pyroptosis, DAMPs accumulate in tissue:
- HMGB1 (high-mobility group box 1 protein): potent TLR4 agonist; hours-long half-life in tissue
- Extracellular ATP: P2X7 receptor agonist; directly activates NLRP3
- Uric acid crystals: form when ATP is degraded; NLRP3 activator via crystal phagocytosis
- mtDNA: cGAS-STING agonist; IFN-α production + NF-κB

**Without SPM-driven efferocytosis:**
DAMPs persist → NLRP3 re-primed → Loop 2 continues even if the initial trigger (CVB, LPS) is removed.
BHB blocks NLRP3 at K+ efflux step but does NOT remove the DAMPs from tissue.

**With SPM-driven efferocytosis (RvD1 + RvE1 + MaR1 at therapeutic omega-3 dose):**
- RvD1 → macrophages recognize and engulf HMGB1 + apoptotic cells → HMGB1 cleared from tissue
- RvE1 → apoptotic neutrophils engulfed → source of ATP + mtDNA release eliminated
- MaR1 → uric acid crystal clearance enhanced (macrophage phagocytosis of crystals + dissolution)
- Net: DAMP pool actively reduced → NLRP3 re-priming substrate eliminated → Loop 2 breaks

**BHB + omega-3 SPMs = complementary NLRP3 attack from two angles:**
- BHB: blocks NLRP3 K+ efflux ACTIVATION (prevents new NLRP3 firing)
- SPMs: clear DAMPs (removes NLRP3 re-priming substrate) + GPR32-mediated NLRP3 block (second mechanism)
- Together: most complete Loop 2 interruption available without prescription NLRP3 inhibitors (MCC950, which is research-grade only)

## SPM Connection to TRPV1 (M8/Neurogenic Axis)

```
PD1 (Neuroprotectin D1, from DHA)
    ↓ GPR37 receptor on sensory neurons (trigeminal + dorsal root ganglia)
    ↓
PD1 → reduces TRPV1 expression on sensory neurons
PD1 → reduces SP secretion from nociceptive nerve terminals
    ↓
Less SP → TRPV1 threshold less sensitized → flushing threshold higher
    ↓
Result: omega-3 supplementation → PD1 production → neurogenic flushing reduced via
        TRPV1/SP axis (independent of anti-inflammatory effect on cytokines)
```

This is a THIRD mechanism by which omega-3 reduces rosacea (beyond anti-inflammatory eicosanoid
suppression and NLRP3 blocking): neuroprotectin-mediated TRPV1 desensitization.

## Protocol Implications

### Dose required for SPM production:

**Standard anti-inflammatory dose (eicosanoid suppression):** 1-2g/day EPA+DHA
**SPM-generating therapeutic dose:** 3-4g/day EPA+DHA minimum; ideally 4g/day as in protocol
The current protocol (EPA+DHA 4g/day) is in the SPM-generating range. This is important validation
that the dose is not arbitrary — 4g/day is specifically the threshold for significant resolvin production.

**EPA:DHA ratio matters:**
- For NLRP3 suppression (RvD1): DHA-dominant formulation preferred (more RvD1 substrate)
- For resolution of neutrophil-driven inflammation: EPA-dominant preferred (more RvE1)
- For TRPV1/neurogenic axis: DHA-dominant (more PD1)
- For rosacea with significant flushing/neurogenic component: consider DHA-enriched formulation (≥2:1 DHA:EPA)
- For rosacea with significant gut dysbiosis/neutrophilic component: standard EPA≈DHA (1:1)

**Aspirin-triggered SPM augmentation (if applicable):**
If patient is on aspirin 81mg/day (standard T1DM cardioprotection): take omega-3 WITH aspirin dose
→ aspirin-triggered resolvins are produced during the 4-6 hour post-aspirin window → more potent
AT-RvD1/AT-RvE1 generation. No additional aspirin beyond standard cardioprotection dose is warranted.

**Timing:**
SPM production occurs hours after omega-3 ingestion and requires ongoing supplementation.
Clinically relevant SPM levels require ~8-12 weeks of consistent omega-3 at 4g/day to accumulate
in cell membrane phospholipids and generate consistent SPM output.
This is why "omega-3 for a few weeks" doesn't show effect in RCTs — tissue phospholipid remodeling
takes 8-12 weeks. The SPM peak effect is at 12+ weeks.

## Evidence Summary

| Claim | Evidence | Grade |
|-------|----------|-------|
| RvD1 → NLRP3 inflammasome blockade via GPR32 | Recchiuti 2018 FASEB J; Chiang 2015 PNAS | II-2 (cell culture; murine models) |
| RvD1 → HMGB1 clearance via efferocytosis | Spite 2009 Nature: RvD2 clears DAMPs in peritonitis | II-2 |
| EPA/DHA → SPM production at 4g/day in humans | Mas 2012 J Nutr; Norling 2012 | II-2 (ex vivo plasma measurements) |
| PD1 → reduces TRPV1/SP in sensory neurons | Park 2011 J Neurosci: PD1 → GPR37 → neuroinflammation ↓ | II-2 (cell/murine) |
| Aspirin-triggered resolvins more potent than standard | Serhan 2002 J Exp Med (ATSM discovery) | II-2 |
| Omega-3 specifically beneficial in rosacea | Bamford 2004 J Dermatol: clinical improvement in 12/16 patients with 4g/day; no RCT | III |

**Weakest link:** All SPM evidence is in vitro or murine. Human omega-3 RCTs show clinical benefit
but cannot attribute it to SPM mechanism specifically (could be eicosanoid competition).
The dose (4g/day) is validated for SPM-generating phospholipid levels; the specific SPM-mediated
NLRP3 blockade in rosacea/T1DM tissue has not been demonstrated.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: Omega-3 at 4g/day Does Not Generate Sufficient SPMs to Block NLRP3 in Humans In Vivo**
Plasma resolvin concentrations after 4g/day omega-3 are in picomolar range in most studies.
Whether picomolar resolvins in plasma reach nanomolar concentrations in skin/gut tissue (where
NLRP3 needs to be blocked) is uncertain — SPMs may be consumed rapidly in the bloodstream.
**Status:** Not killed. SPMs are lipid mediators that operate locally (produced at inflammation site
by local cells, not delivered from plasma). The requirement is that substrate (EPA/DHA) reaches
the tissue, where local macrophages/DCs convert it to SPMs. Tissue levels at 4g/day supplementation
have not been directly measured in rosacea skin biopsies.

**Kill B: HMGB1 (the Primary DAMP) Is Cleared by Mechanisms Other Than SPM Efferocytosis**
HMGB1 has a plasma half-life of several hours and is cleared by liver/kidneys. If circulating
HMGB1 is efficiently cleared systemically, the SPM-efferocytosis mechanism is not needed.
**Status:** Not killed. Tissue-localized HMGB1 (in dermis, periodontal tissue, gut wall) is NOT
cleared by systemic circulation at the same rate. Local HMGB1 persistence in tissue drives local
NLRP3 re-priming. SPM-driven macrophage efferocytosis is the primary mechanism for clearing
extracellular HMGB1 from tissue sites.

## Novel Testable Predictions

**Prediction A — Plasma RvD1 at 12 Weeks Correlates with Rosacea IGA Score Reduction:**
T1DM + rosacea patients on EPA+DHA 4g/day × 12 weeks: measure plasma RvD1 (LC-MS/MS) at
baseline + 4 weeks + 12 weeks. Prediction: higher plasma RvD1 at 12 weeks → greater rosacea
score reduction. Stratify by IL-18 (Loop 2 active): higher IL-18 patients should show greater
RvD1-dependent benefit.

**Prediction B — DHA:EPA Ratio Affects Neurogenic vs. Papulopustular Rosacea Differentially:**
RCT: DHA-dominant omega-3 (2:1 DHA:EPA) vs. standard (1:1 EPA:DHA) × 12 weeks in rosacea.
Primary endpoints: (1) flushing frequency + burning sensation score (neurogenic arm); (2) IGA
papulopustular score. Prediction: DHA-dominant → greater flushing reduction (PD1/TRPV1 arm);
standard ratio → greater papulopustular reduction (neutrophil/NLRP3 arm).
