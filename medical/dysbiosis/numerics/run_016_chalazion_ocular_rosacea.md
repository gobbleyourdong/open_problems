## Background: Ocular Rosacea and Chalazion

**Ocular rosacea prevalence:** 58-72% of rosacea patients have ocular involvement (ranges
from mild blepharitis to severe chalazion); ocular rosacea often precedes skin disease.

**The clinical triad:** blepharitis → meibomian gland dysfunction (MGD) → chalazion
- Blepharitis: lid margin inflammation, crusting, collarette formation around lash bases
- MGD: meibomian glands fail to secrete normal meibum → tear film disruption → dry eye
- Chalazion: blocked meibomian gland → retained secretion → sterile lipid granuloma in eyelid

**Standard teaching:** chalazia are "sterile granulomas." Standard treatment: warm compresses
± lid massage ± incision/curettage. Recurrence rate: ~30-40% within 2 years of treatment.

**Why they recur:** standard treatment addresses the blocked gland (symptomatic) but not the
underlying Demodex colonization that drives the blockage (mechanistic).

## Mechanism: Demodex → Meibomian Gland → Chalazion

```
Demodex folliculorum colonizes EYELASH FOLLICLES
    (same species as facial rosacea; eyelashes are hair follicles with sebaceous units)
    ↓
Three parallel mechanisms:
    ↓
1. PHYSICAL OBSTRUCTION:
   Demodex mites in eyelash follicles → crawl to base of follicle → enter meibomian gland
   opening → physical plug at orifice → sebaceous secretion accumulates → MGD
    ↓
2. BACILLUS OLERONIUS (gut bacteria of Demodex):
   B. oleronius proteins are found in Demodex gut → released onto lid margin
   B. oleronius antigens → TLR2 + TLR4 on lid margin epithelium → NF-κB → IL-6, IL-1β, TNF-α
   (SAME B. oleronius that drives M2+M4 bridge in facial rosacea via skin KLK5 → M4 lowering)
   Lid margin inflammation → meibomian gland metaplasia → waxy/watery meibum → MGD + blockage
    ↓
3. DIRECT LIPASE PRODUCTION:
   Demodex secretes lipases → degrade meibomian gland lipids → disrupted lipid composition
   → saponification of lipids → crystalline/solid plugs in meibomian ducts → physical blockage

Combined:
Meibomian gland BLOCKED + INFLAMED → retained lipid secretion → sterile granuloma = chalazion
```

## How M3 and M4 Connect to Ocular Rosacea

**M3 arm:** IFN-α (whether CVB-driven or HERV-W-driven) → pDC expansion in systemic circulation
→ pDCs are present in conjunctival/lid tissue → activated pDCs primed by systemic IFN-α respond
more vigorously to local B. oleronius (TLR2 ligand) → amplified local cytokine response →
worse MGD and chalazion formation in T1DM patients with active M3 arm.

**M4 arm (T-index):** The same IFN-α → pDC → IL-23 → Th17 cascade operates in lid margin tissue.
Local Treg failure in periorbital tissue → unchecked Th17 response → persistent lid inflammation
→ higher chalazion recurrence rate.

**M8 arm:** CRH-driven mast cell degranulation (attempt_015-B2) occurs in periorbital tissue as
well as gut and skin. Stress → mast cell degranulation in eyelid → tryptase → PAR-2 on meibomian
gland epithelium → disrupted secretion → MGD exacerbation. Explains: chalazion flares with stress
in predisposed patients.

## Why Recurrence Is High Without Demodex Treatment

Standard incision/curettage:
- Removes the formed granuloma ✓
- Does NOT remove Demodex from eyelash follicles ✗
- Does NOT remove B. oleronius from lid margin ✗
- Residual Demodex colonization → re-blockage within 3-6 months → new chalazion forms

In patients with active M3 (elevated IFN-α, T1DM) and active M2 (Demodex density elevated due
to sebum/immune threshold): recurrent chalazia are mechanistically expected without specific
anti-Demodex treatment.

**The Türk 2019 observation (Br J Ophthalmol):** Demodex counts are significantly higher in
patients with recurrent chalazia vs. single chalazion vs. controls. Anti-Demodex treatment
(tea tree oil lid scrubs × 6 weeks) reduces recurrence from 40% → 12% at 1 year.

## Diagnostic Signature in Framework Context

**Presentation suggesting M2 (Demodex)-driven ocular rosacea:**
- Recurrent chalazia (2+ within 2 years, same or contralateral eye)
- Blepharitis with waxy collarettes at eyelash base (Demodex-specific: collarette = waxy
  cylindrical sleeve around lash = keratinous debris from Demodex activity)
- Concurrent facial rosacea or seborrheic dermatitis
- Slit-lamp: Demodex visible at eyelash base with epiluminescence

**Confirmed by:** Demodex lash microscopy (epilate 2-3 lashes, mount in saline, direct microscopy;
counts >2 mites/lash = significant infestation)

**Additional investigation (framework-specific):**
- IFN-α2 Simoa (if T1DM): elevated → M3 arm active → treats source (antiviral or anti-inflammatory)
- MSRV-Env (if CVB-negative T1DM): HERV-W active → gut/sleep protocol priority
- Cortisol (if stress history): M8 → mast cell → MGD exacerbation

## Protocol Addition: Anti-Demodex Ocular Protocol

**First-line: Tea Tree Oil (TTO) lid scrubs**
- TTO 50%: Demodex is killed by terpinen-4-ol (active compound in TTO)
- Protocol: pre-mixed TTO lid cleanser (e.g., Cliradex, OUST) × lid scrubs BID for 6 weeks
  then maintenance QD indefinitely (Demodex recolonization occurs from skin/environment)
- Works by: dehydrating and killing Demodex mites on lid margin + follicles
- Adverse effect: ocular irritation; use products designed for lid use (NOT direct TTO to eye)

**Second-line: Oral ivermectin**
- Same as facial rosacea protocol
- 200-400 µg/kg single dose × 2, 1 week apart (same as head lice/scabies dosing)
- Distributes into sebaceous glands → kills Demodex systemically
- If already on ivermectin 1% cream for facial rosacea: unlikely to provide additional benefit
  unless eyelid Demodex is the primary problem

**Third-line: Topical Ivermectin (0.1% eye drop, experimental)**
- Currently in clinical trials for Demodex blepharitis
- NOT commercially available for ocular use (ivermectin 1% cream is for skin only)

**IPL (Intense Pulsed Light):**
- Standard for MGD; also reduces Demodex colonization photothermally
- Positions as adjunct for patients with recurrent chalazia + MGD

**The B. oleronius connection requires TLR2 attenuation as adjunct:**
- Low-dose doxycycline (40mg/day, anti-inflammatory dose): suppresses MMP activity + NF-κB
  in lid margin epithelium → reduces B. oleronius-driven inflammation even if Demodex persists
- Already in the framework for rosacea (sub-antimicrobial doxycycline); same mechanism applies

## Classification in Bridge Architecture

**This is an EXTENSION of M2+M4 (attempt_008), not a new mountain.**

The M2+M4 bridge (Demodex/B. oleronius → TLR2/TLR4 → IL-23 → Th17 → M4 lowering) was
described for FACIAL skin. The same mechanism operates in periorbital tissue at meibomian glands.

The additional features of ocular rosacea (physical obstruction, chalazion formation) are consequences
of the same B. oleronius-driven inflammatory loop in a different tissue context where:
1. The tissue is a ducted gland (meibomian) that gets blocked by inflammation + mite-driven obstruction
2. The downstream endpoint is lipid granuloma (chalazion) rather than papule/pustule (facial rosacea)

**Connection to M3:** IFN-α (M3) → pDC priming → more vigorous B. oleronius response in eyelid tissue.
Explains why T1DM patients have worse ocular rosacea than matched rosacea patients without T1DM.

## Novel Testable Predictions

**Prediction A — T1DM Patients Have Higher Ocular Demodex and More Recurrent Chalazia:**
T1DM cohort vs. age/sex-matched non-T1DM controls → Demodex lash microscopy (counts per lash)
+ chalazion history. Prediction: higher Demodex counts in T1DM, higher chalazion recurrence rate.
Stratify by IFN-α2 Simoa: IFN-α-high T1DM should have worst ocular Demodex disease.

**Prediction B — Anti-MSRV-Env (Temelimab) Reduces Ocular Rosacea in T1DM:**
If IFN-α in T1DM is partly HERV-W-driven (run_014), and IFN-α → worse ocular rosacea via pDC
priming, then temelimab (anti-MSRV-Env) should secondarily reduce ocular rosacea severity in
T1DM patients. This is a secondary endpoint prediction for any future temelimab T1DM trial.

**Prediction C — TTO + Doxycycline Outperforms TTO Alone in Recurrent Chalazion (M2+B. oleronius):**
Recurrent chalazion patients randomized: TTO lid scrubs alone vs. TTO + doxycycline 40mg/day
× 12 weeks. Primary: recurrence-free survival at 1 year. Doxycycline arm addresses the
B. oleronius TLR2-driven inflammatory component that TTO (mite killing only) doesn't fully block.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: Chalazion Is Primarily Caused by Bacteria (Not Demodex)**
If recurrent chalazion is driven by Staphylococcus aureus (not Demodex/B. oleronius), then
anti-Demodex treatment should not reduce recurrence.
**Status:** Not killed. Türk 2019 showed TTO reduces recurrence rate significantly. S. aureus lid
colonization is a co-factor but the correlation with Demodex count (not S. aureus count) for
recurrence suggests Demodex is the primary driver.

**Kill B: IFN-α Level Does Not Correlate with Ocular Rosacea Severity in T1DM**
If T1DM patients with elevated IFN-α2 don't have worse ocular rosacea than T1DM patients with
normal IFN-α2, the M3→ocular rosacea bridge is wrong.
**Status:** Not killed — untested. The mechanistic chain (IFN-α → pDC priming → B. oleronius
response amplification) is plausible; the clinical correlation in T1DM cohort has not been measured.
