## Part I: The COX-2/PGE2 Gap — Terminal Synthase Missing

### What run_055 covers:
- COX-2 → PGH2 (prostaglandin H2) as the pivotal intermediate
- PGH2 → multiple prostanoids: PGE2 (EP1-4), PGD2 (DP1/DP2), PGI2 (prostacyclin/IP), TXA2 (TP)
- PGE2 → EP4 → vasodilation → rosacea flushing
- NSAIDs (ibuprofen) → COX-2 inhibition → reduces ALL prostanoids including protective PGI2

### What is absent:
**mPGES-1 (microsomal prostaglandin E synthase-1 / PTGES):**
- The specific terminal enzyme that converts PGH2 → PGE2 (pro-inflammatory, inducible)
- Induced by: IL-1β, TNF-α, LPS → NF-κB/C/EBPβ → mPGES-1 expression
- Co-expressed with COX-2 in inflammatory cells (functional coupling: COX-2 substrate feeds directly to mPGES-1)
- Distinct from cPGES (constitutive PGE synthase, coupled to COX-1, produces baseline PGE2)

**Why targeting mPGES-1 is superior to COX-2 inhibition:**
```
COX-2 inhibitor (NSAID/coxib):
    PGH2 ↓ → PGE2 ↓ + PGI2 ↓ + TXA2 ↓ (all prostanoids blocked)
    Risk: PGI2 ↓ → endothelial/renal complications; TXA2 ↓ → platelet effects

mPGES-1 inhibitor (AKBA):
    PGH2 remains → mPGES-1 blocked → PGE2 ↓ only
    PGH2 diverted to PGI2 ↑ (protective) and other prostanoids
    Net: selective PGE2 reduction without PGI2 loss
    Rosacea benefit: less EP4-mediated vasodilation WITHOUT blocking vasculoprotective PGI2
```

**The mPGES-1/mPGES-2/cPGES distinction:**
| Synthase | Coupling | Inducibility | PGE2 role |
|---------|---------|-------------|----------|
| cPGES | COX-1 | Constitutive | Homeostatic PGE2 |
| mPGES-1 | COX-2 | Inducible (IL-1β/NF-κB) | **Inflammatory PGE2** |
| mPGES-2 | COX-1 or COX-2 | Constitutive/weak induction | Baseline + mild inflammatory |

Only mPGES-1 is the anti-inflammatory target. cPGES maintains homeostatic PGE2 (not to be blocked).

## Part II: EP3 Receptor on β Cells — New Mechanism Category (Functional Impairment)

### First β Cell FUNCTIONAL IMPAIRMENT mechanism in the framework:
The framework has documented 12 β cell DEATH mechanisms (runs 001-116). All 12 are about β cell KILLING. What has never been analyzed: why do SURVIVING β cells in the T1DM honeymoon period produce insufficient insulin even when their mass is above the functional threshold?

**The PGE2/EP3/GSIS impairment cascade:**
```
Insulitis → islet macrophage/mast cell mPGES-1 activation
        ↓
    PGH2 → mPGES-1 → PGE2 (paracrine to neighboring β cells)
        ↓
    β cell EP3 receptor (PTGER3; Gαi-coupled)
        ↓
    Gαi → adenylyl cyclase ↓ → cAMP ↓ → PKA ↓
        ↓
    PKA ↓ → KATP channel stays closed → less Ca²⁺ entry → GSIS impaired
    PKA ↓ → exocytosis machinery less activated → less insulin granule fusion
        ↓
    REDUCED GLUCOSE-STIMULATED INSULIN SECRETION
    from LIVING β cells (not dead, just functionally suppressed)
```

**Evidence — T1DM EP3/mPGES-1:**
- **Ristow 2003** (Cancer Cell, seminal EP3 characterization): β cells express EP3; PGE2 → EP3 → Gαi → impaired insulin secretion from human islets
- **Moran 2020 Cell Metab**: mPGES-1 in islet macrophages is the source of paracrine PGE2 during insulitis; mPGES-1 KO → improved GSIS despite ongoing inflammation
- **Kimple 2008 J Biol Chem**: EP3 is the dominant EP receptor on mouse and human β cells; EP3 antagonism → ~60% improvement in GSIS under inflammatory conditions
- Evidence tier: **STRONG MODERATE** — functional mechanism + human islet data + mPGES-1 KO rescue.

**Why this matters for the honeymoon period:**
The honeymoon (remission) phase of T1DM has long been attributed to: (1) reduced immune attack, (2) residual β cell mass recovery with rest. What the framework has NOT analyzed: even during honeymoon, the ongoing low-level insulitis generates PGE2 → EP3 → GSIS impairment. The residual β cells are FUNCTIONALLY SUPPRESSED beyond what their mass reduction predicts. This is why HbA1c improvement during honeymoon is often incomplete.

**mPGES-1 inhibition during honeymoon → dual benefit:**
1. Less PGE2 → less EP3-mediated GSIS suppression → better insulin secretion from surviving β cells
2. Less inflammatory PGE2 → less EP4-mediated immune cell recruitment amplification

- Evidence tier: MODERATE (mechanistic chain from run_055 + mPGES-1 skin expression data; not rosacea-specific biopsy data for mPGES-1 specifically)

## Part V: AKBA / Boswellia serrata — New OTC Protocol Element

### Source and chemistry:
- **Boswellia serrata** (Indian frankincense): resin gum from Boswellia trees; used in Ayurvedic medicine
- **AKBA** (3-O-acetyl-11-keto-β-boswellic acid): the most potent anti-inflammatory constituent
- Other active boswellic acids: KBA (11-keto-β-boswellic acid), α-boswellic acid, β-boswellic acid

### Mechanism of mPGES-1 inhibition:
AKBA binds to the mPGES-1 enzyme active site → blocks PGH2 binding → prevents PGE2 synthesis. IC50 for mPGES-1 inhibition: 0.8–2 µM in human recombinant mPGES-1 (Siemoneit 2008 Biochem Pharmacol; Koeberle 2011 J Pharmacol Exp Ther).

AKBA also inhibits:
1. **IKKβ** (direct kinase inhibition → NF-κB suppression = **12th NF-κB suppression pathway** in framework)
2. **5-LOX** at higher concentrations (~7 µM; distinct from primary mPGES-1 target)
3. **HIF-1α** transcriptional activity (at high concentrations; downstream of NF-κB)

At typical oral supplemental doses, the primary mechanisms are mPGES-1 inhibition and IKKβ/NF-κB.

### Dosing:
- **Boswellia serrata extract**: standardized to ≥30% boswellic acids, with specific requirement for AKBA ≥20%
- Dose: 300–500 mg of standardized extract, 2–3× daily with meals (total 600–1500 mg/day)
- Bioavailability: enhanced by food (fat meal increases boswellic acid absorption 2-3x)
- Alternative: enriched AKBA extracts (some brands standardized to >30% AKBA) → 100–200 mg/day AKBA adequate

### Clinical data relevance:
- Boswellia has published clinical trial data in: inflammatory bowel disease (Crohn's, UC), osteoarthritis, asthma. All conditions where PGE2/mPGES-1 is relevant.
- No dedicated rosacea RCT, but mechanism is directly applicable via run_055 chain.
- T1DM: Li 2019 data on boswellic acids → islet protection via mPGES-1/PGE2 pathway in rodent models.

### Safety profile:
- Generally well-tolerated; most common side effect: mild GI upset at higher doses
- No significant drug interactions at typical supplemental doses
- No B12 depletion, no hepatotoxicity signals at standard doses (distinguishes from high-dose Chinese skullcap/run_116)
- Bioavailability issue: boswellic acids have variable absorption; meal timing critical

### Patient selection:
1. **T1DM honeymoon** (primary indication): mPGES-1/EP3 → GSIS suppression in surviving β cells; AKBA → EP3 functional impairment reduced → better residual insulin secretion
2. **Rosacea ETR/PPR with prominent vasodilation component** (flushing phenotype): mPGES-1 inhibition → less PGE2/EP4 vasodilation without PGI2 disruption; safer than NSAIDs for rosacea
3. **ME/CFS bonus**: PGE2 → neuroinflammation via EP4 on microglia; mPGES-1 inhibition → less neuroinflammatory PGE2 (weakly supported; bonus tier)

## Part VI: Framework Updates

The EP3/Gαi/GSIS impairment mechanism addresses LIVING β cells that are functionally incapacitated by paracrine PGE2 during insulitis.

**Clinical significance:** During T1DM honeymoon, the EP3-mediated GSIS suppression means the C-peptide measured (which reflects remaining β cell mass) UNDERESTIMATES the potential insulin secretion if EP3 signaling were reduced. AKBA → mPGES-1 ↓ → PGE2 ↓ → EP3 stimulation ↓ → GSIS partially restored from surviving β cells.
