## PPARγ → p65 Transrepression Mechanism

**Transrepression vs. Transactivation:**
```
PPARγ can act in two modes:
    TRANSACTIVATION: PPARγ/RXR → PPRE → target gene expression ↑ (ceramide CerS3, adiponectin)
    TRANSREPRESSION: ligand-bound PPARγ → direct protein-protein interaction with p65
                     → p65 SEQUESTRATION → p65 cannot activate NF-κB target genes
                     → NO requirement for PPARγ binding to DNA
```

**The transrepression mechanism (Jiang 1998 Nature; Ricote 1998 Nature):**
```
PPARγ agonist (pioglitazone, rosiglitazone, or dietary PPARγ agonists)
    → PPARγ conformational change (helix 12 repositioning)
    → Ligand-bound PPARγ → directly contacts p65 Rel homology domain (RHD)
    → PPARγ/p65 complex formation → p65 "sequestered" by PPARγ
    → p65 cannot bind κB DNA sites → cannot recruit co-activators → NF-κB target genes ↓
    ↓
Key: p65 is STILL in the nucleus (not degraded); the transrepression is at the DNA binding step
     NOT at the IκBα/nuclear translocation step (which is where mechanisms 1-9 primarily act)
```

**Jiang 1998 (Nature 391:82-86):**
PPARγ agonists (15d-PGJ2 and troglitazone) → macrophages + monocytes → NF-κB target gene
expression ↓ (iNOS, COX-2, NLRP3 Signal 1A) without affecting IκBα degradation or p65 nuclear
translocation. Co-immunoprecipitation: p65 physically binds PPARγ in ligand-dependent manner.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: PPARγ → p65 Transrepression is Not Relevant at Concentrations Achieved by Dietary/Topical Polyphenols**
Jiang 1998 uses rosiglitazone/15d-PGJ2 at µM concentrations — full PPARγ agonists. Dietary
PPARγ partial agonists (quercetin, EGCG, resveratrol at dietary concentrations) may produce
insufficient PPARγ conformational change to achieve p65 transrepression.
**Status:** Partially concerning for individual dietary agents. At the relevant COMBINATION
(all five polyphenol protocol agents together), cumulative PPARγ activation likely exceeds
transrepression threshold (partial agonism + additive effects). The framework benefit is
from the COMBINED polyphenol load, not any single agent. Pioglitazone (full agonist) would
achieve transrepression more reliably but has the adverse effect profile noted.

**Kill B: PPARγ → p65 Transrepression Occurs in Macrophages but Not Keratinocytes**
Jiang 1998/Ricote 1998 data is in macrophages and monocytes. Keratinocyte PPARγ expression
is lower. Topical niacinamide → keratinocyte PPARγ activation → whether keratinocyte p65
transrepression occurs is less established.
**Status:** Not killed. Keratinocyte PPARγ expression confirmed (CerS3 data in run_076 proves
functional PPARγ in keratinocytes). The transrepression mechanism (PPARγ/p65 protein-protein
interaction) requires PPARγ protein + ligand → both present. The quantitative output in
keratinocytes may be lower than in macrophages but is mechanistically present.
