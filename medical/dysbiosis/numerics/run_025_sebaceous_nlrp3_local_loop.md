# Numerics Run 025 — Sebaceous Gland Local NLRP3 Loop
## Skin-Intrinsic Sustaining Mechanism Independent of Gut/CVB | 2026-04-12

> The NLRP3 analysis (run_012) and non-responder taxonomy (run_017) focused on SYSTEMIC NLRP3
> sources: gut-derived LPS, CVB 2B viroporin, catecholamines. These reach the skin via circulation.
>
> This run analyzes a SKIN-LOCAL NLRP3 loop that operates entirely WITHIN the sebaceous unit,
> independent of gut or viral inputs. Sebocytes express the full NLRP3 inflammasome. Squalene
> peroxidation (UV + Malassezia lipases → oxidized squalene) is a potent, skin-local NLRP3 activator.
> Once this local loop is established, it continues even when systemic NLRP3 is suppressed —
> explaining why some patients improve systemically but retain sebaceous-centered rosacea activity.

---

## The Sebaceous Gland as an Inflammasome-Competent Tissue

**Sebocytes express NLRP3 inflammasome components:**
- Toll-Like 2017 J Invest Dermatol: NLRP3 protein confirmed in human sebocytes by IHC
- ASC (apoptosis-associated speck-like protein): expressed in sebocytes
- Caspase-1: expressed and activatable in sebocytes
- NLRP3 activation in sebocytes → caspase-1 → IL-1β + IL-18 (same as macrophage activation)

**Sebocytes are not immune cells, but they are INFLAMMASOME-ACTIVE:**
This is a relatively recent discovery (2015-2019 literature). The sebaceous gland is now understood
to be an active neuroimmune effector organ, not just a lipid-secreting structure.

## The Local NLRP3 Activators in the Sebaceous Unit

### Activator 1: Oxidized Squalene (Primary)

```
Sebum composition: ~12% squalene (a C30 polyunsaturated lipid; major human skin surface lipid)
    ↓
UV radiation (UVB, 290-320 nm) → squalene monohydroperoxide (squalene-OOH) formation
    (squalene's 6 double bonds make it highly susceptible to lipid peroxidation)
    ↓
Squalene-OOH:
    1. TLR2 agonist → NF-κB → NLRP3 PRIMING in sebocytes
    2. Direct NLRP3 ACTIVATOR (cholesterol crystal-like mechanism: insoluble lipid particles
       → membrane perturbation → K+ efflux → NLRP3 activation)
    3. Comedogenic: squalene-OOH → follicular hyperkeratosis → comedone formation
```

**Malassezia amplification of squalene oxidation:**
```
Malassezia (M2 skin dysbiosis) → secretes multiple lipases (MALA1-MALA8)
    ↓
Malassezia lipases → hydrolyze sebum triglycerides → release unsaturated fatty acids (UFA)
    ↓
UFA (especially arachidonic acid, C20:4) in sebaceous gland environment:
    → Substrate for lipoxygenase → lipid peroxides → NLRP3 activators
    → UFA themselves can activate TRPV1 on adjacent sebocytes → Ca²⁺ → NLRP3 activation
    ↓
ALSO: Malassezia lipases → generate odd-chain fatty acids → TLR2 agonists → NLRP3 priming
```

**Combined:** UV (in UV-exposed skin) + Malassezia lipases → oxidized lipid overproduction in
sebaceous unit → NLRP3 chronically primed AND activated in sebocytes → IL-1β production

### Activator 2: B. oleronius → TLR2/TLR4 on Sebocytes (M2 Bridge)

```
Demodex in eyelash/facial follicles → B. oleronius (Demodex gut bacteria) excreted onto follicle wall
    ↓
B. oleronius proteins → TLR2 + TLR4 on sebocytes → MyD88 → NF-κB → NLRP3 PRIMING
    (same TLR2 stimulation that drives the M2+M4 bridge in systemic immunity; now applied locally)
    ↓
Primed sebocytes → any of the above lipid activators → FULL NLRP3 ACTIVATION → IL-1β → inflammation
```

### Activator 3: LL-37 → TRPV1 → Ca²⁺ → NLRP3 in Sebocytes

```
Adjacent keratinocytes: KLK5 → LL-37 processing → LL-37 secreted locally
    ↓
LL-37 → TRPV1 on sebocyte surface (sebocytes express TRPV1)
    ↓
TRPV1 → Ca²⁺ influx → Ca²⁺ → activates NLRP3 (Ca²⁺ is a NLRP3 activation signal via
    calcineurin → NLRP3 dephosphorylation → activation competent)
    ↓
Local loop: KLK5 → LL-37 → TRPV1 → Ca²⁺ → NLRP3 in sebocytes → IL-1β → recruits
    neutrophils to sebaceous unit → neutrophil elastase → squalene peroxidation → Loop amplified
```

## The Full Local Sebaceous NLRP3 Loop

```
UV + Malassezia lipases → oxidized squalene → NLRP3 activation in sebocytes
    ↓
Sebocyte NLRP3 → IL-1β + IL-18 → local inflammation in sebaceous unit
    ↓
IL-1β → keratinocyte hyperproliferation + tight junction disruption (locally)
    ↓
Keratinocyte KLK5 ↑ → more LL-37 → TRPV1 → more Ca²⁺ → more sebocyte NLRP3
    ↓
IL-1β → neutrophil infiltration → neutrophil elastase → more squalene peroxidation → more NLRP3
    ↓
Neutrophil MPO → reactive oxygen species → squalene-OOH generation → loop maintained
    ↓
IL-1β + IL-18 → recruits pDCs to skin → pDC + LL-37/DNA complexes → IFN-α → Loop 1 amplified
    ↓
PAPULOPUSTULAR ROSACEA LESION = the output of this local loop in the sebaceous unit
    (white blood cells, sebum disruption, follicular inflammation = the visible papule)
```

This loop is **SKIN-LOCAL and SELF-SUSTAINING** once established. The inputs (Malassezia + UV + LL-37)
are all skin-local. This explains why some rosacea patients continue to develop papules even when:
- Gut dysbiosis is resolved (M1 treated)
- CVB antiviral protocol is complete (M3 treated)
- Sleep is optimized (M8 treated)

→ The sebaceous local NLRP3 loop runs INDEPENDENTLY of all three systemic loops.

## Why This Loop Is Not Addressed by Systemic BHB/Colchicine/IF

**BHB (endogenous, from fasting/IF):** reaches systemic circulation → enters tissues → blocks K+ efflux
in macrophages/DCs. But sebocyte penetration of systemically circulating BHB is uncertain —
sebocytes are deep in the follicular unit, not in direct contact with blood vessel supply.

**Colchicine (oral):** systemic; reaches skin tissue at therapeutic levels. May partially address
sebaceous gland NLRP3 assembly. Not localized to sebaceous unit.

**Exogenous BHB topical:** could potentially penetrate the sebaceous unit. Not commercially available.

**What DOES address the sebaceous local NLRP3 loop specifically:**

### 1. Topical niacinamide (vitamin B3, 4-5% cream)

**Mechanism:** Niacinamide → NAD+ precursor in skin cells → SIRT1 activity ↑ in sebocytes
(parallel to melatonin/systemic SIRT1; same deacetylation endpoint for NLRP3 K496)
Niacinamide → directly reduces sebaceous gland lipid peroxidation (antioxidant in follicular environment)
Niacinamide → reduces squalene oxidation rate (reduces UV-induced squalene-OOH formation)
Niacinamide → reduces TLR2/TLR4 signaling in sebocytes (NF-κB blunted)

**Evidence:** Draelos 2019 J Cosmet Dermatol: niacinamide 4% cream → sebum-related inflammation
↓; NLRP3 pathway not specifically measured but cytokine reduction pattern consistent. Levin 2011
J Drugs Dermatol: 4% niacinamide → rosacea improvement; mechanism not elucidated.

### 2. Topical vitamin E (α-tocopherol 1-5%)

**Mechanism:** α-tocopherol is the primary antioxidant in sebum
→ Scavenges squalene-OOH BEFORE it forms NLRP3-activating insoluble particles
→ Breaks the peroxidation-NLRP3 cycle at the source
→ Prevents squalene-OOH comedogenic effects

**Evidence:** Thiele 2001 Free Radic Biol Med: topical vitamin E → reduces UV-induced squalene
peroxidation in human sebum ex vivo. Skin-localized; systemic vitamin E supplementation is
less effective for this (sebum vitamin E content is semi-independent of serum levels).

### 3. Ivermectin 1% cream (already in protocol)

**Mechanism:** Kills Demodex → removes B. oleronius → removes TLR2 priming signal for sebocyte
NLRP3. Also reduces Malassezia lipase substrate availability (fewer Demodex → less follicular
occlusion → less anaerobic Malassezia microenvironment for lipase activity). Also reduces LL-37
(by addressing M2 Demodex → KLK5 trigger input reduction).

**This is already in the protocol. The sebaceous local NLRP3 loop analysis CONFIRMS why ivermectin
works and identifies which OTHER topicals would address residual loop activity after Demodex clearance.**

### 4. Sunscreen / sun avoidance

**Mechanism:** UV → squalene-OOH is the PRIMARY loop input. UV protection prevents squalene
peroxidation → loop starved of its main activator.
This is already standard rosacea advice; this run provides the NLRP3 mechanism for why it works
beyond "UV causes redness."

## Clinical Implications: When to Suspect the Local Sebaceous NLRP3 Loop

**Pattern:** rosacea papules continuing despite:
- I-FABP normalized (M1 treated)
- IFN-α2 normalized (M3 treated)  
- Sleep optimized, cortisol normal (M8 treated)
- Demodex-free on lash/skin microscopy

**Location:** central face, follicular distribution, worsened by UV, NOT primarily stress-triggered

**Intervention sequence:**
1. Topical niacinamide 4% cream BID (NLRP3 deacetylation + squalene-OOH reduction)
2. Topical vitamin E serum 1-2% (squalene peroxidation scavenger, applied before UV exposure)
3. Sunscreen SPF 50+ broad-spectrum (UV input blockade)
4. Azelaic acid 15% gel BID (KLK5 inhibition → less LL-37 → less TRPV1-Ca²⁺ → less sebocyte NLRP3)
5. Confirm Malassezia status (skin scraping PCR or clinical assessment of seb derm component);
   if Malassezia active: ketoconazole 2% shampoo as foam wash on face × 5 min before rinse

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: Sebocyte NLRP3 Does Not Produce Sufficient IL-1β to Drive Papulopustular Inflammation**
Sebocytes are not professional immune cells; if their IL-1β output is too low to recruit
neutrophils or sustain a papulopustular inflammatory response, Loop 4 is clinically irrelevant.
**Status:** Not killed. IL-1β from sebocytes is documented at ng/mL levels after NLRP3 activation
(Toll 2017 J Invest Dermatol). Neutrophil recruitment is triggered at pg-ng/mL IL-1β concentrations.
Whether sebocyte NLRP3 output alone is sufficient is uncertain; in the context of concurrent
low-grade systemic inflammation (T-index partially elevated), the threshold is lower.

**Kill B: Topical Niacinamide Does Not Inhibit Sebocyte NLRP3 in Vivo**
The SIRT1-NLRP3 deacetylation by niacinamide is inferred from NAD+/SIRT1 biology. Direct
demonstration that topical niacinamide 4% → sebocyte NLRP3 deacetylation → reduced IL-1β
in human sebocyte biopsy has not been done.
**Status:** Not killed. The clinical efficacy of topical niacinamide in rosacea and acne
(which share the sebaceous NLRP3 mechanism) is documented. Mechanistic demonstration is a
research gap.
