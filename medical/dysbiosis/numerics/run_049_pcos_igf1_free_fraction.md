# Numerics Run 049 — PCOS and Free IGF-1: The Non-T1DM Loop 1/4 Amplification
## Polycystic Ovary Syndrome Shares the IGFBP-3 Deficit Mechanism with T1DM | 2026-04-12

> Run_031 identified that T1DM specifically depletes IGFBP-3 (via three mechanisms: portal
> insulin deficit → hepatic IGFBP-3 ↓; inflammation → MMP-2/MMP-9 → IGFBP-3 proteolysis;
> hyperglycemia → MMP activation). This depletes IGFBP-3 → elevated FREE IGF-1 despite normal
> total IGF-1 → Loop 1 (mTORC1 → KLK5) + Loop 4 (mTORC1 → SREBP-1 → SCD1 → sebum quantity
> and composition) amplified at "normal" total IGF-1 levels.
>
> Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) affects 8-13% of reproductive-age women and is the most
> common endocrine disorder in women. PCOS shares TWO of the three T1DM IGFBP-3 depletion
> mechanisms — NOT the portal insulin deficit, but:
> 1. Hyperinsulinemia (chronic, from insulin resistance in PCOS) → MMP-2/MMP-9 activation
>    → IGFBP-3 proteolysis (same mechanism as run_031 hyperglycemia arm)
> 2. PCOS-associated low-grade inflammation → MMP elevation → IGFBP-3 proteolysis
>
> Additionally: PCOS is the primary cause of androgen-driven sebum excess (testosterone → DHT
> → sebaceous gland hypertrophy → sebum quantity ↑). This creates a FOUR-ARM amplification
> of Loop 1/4 in PCOS (androgen arm + free IGF-1 arm + NLRP3 priming + gut dysbiosis).

## PCOS Biology Relevant to Framework

**PCOS diagnostic criteria (Rotterdam):** ≥2 of 3:
1. Oligo/anovulation
2. Clinical or biochemical hyperandrogenism
3. Polycystic ovarian morphology on ultrasound

**PCOS metabolic phenotype:**
- Insulin resistance in 50-80% of PCOS (even in lean PCOS: normal BMI but ↑ insulin resistance)
- Compensatory hyperinsulinemia (fasting insulin >15-20 µIU/mL in PCOS vs. <10 µIU/mL controls)
- Hyperandrogen: LH pulsatility ↑ → LH:FSH ratio ↑ → ovarian theca cell testosterone ↑ →
  DHT (via 5α-reductase) → sebaceous gland hypertrophy → sebum ↑
- Low-grade systemic inflammation: CRP, IL-6, IL-18, TNF-α all elevated in PCOS vs. controls
  (Escobar-Morreale 2011 Endocr Rev meta-analysis)
- Gut dysbiosis: PCOS → altered gut microbiome (Qi 2019 J Clin Endocrinol Metab: reduced
  Lactobacillus + Bifidobacterium; elevated LPS-producers → increased gut permeability)

## PCOS IGFBP-3 Depletion Mechanisms

**Mechanism 1: Hyperinsulinemia → MMP-2/MMP-9 → IGFBP-3 proteolysis:**
```
PCOS insulin resistance → compensatory hyperinsulinemia → insulin → PI3K → Akt → mTORC1
    ↓
mTORC1 → activates MMP-2 and MMP-9 (gelatinases; cleave IGFBP-3 at specific sites)
    AND
Hyperinsulinemia → directly upregulates MMP-2/MMP-9 expression via AP-1 and SP-1 transcription
    (Insulin → ERK → AP-1 → MMP-2 promoter; documented in adipocytes and hepatocytes)
    ↓
MMP-2/MMP-9 cleave IGFBP-3 → IGFBP-3 fragments do not bind IGF-1 → free IGF-1 ↑
```

**Mechanism 2: Low-grade inflammation → MMP elevation:**
```
PCOS inflammatory cytokines (TNF-α → NF-κB → MMP-9 in hepatocytes + adipocytes;
    IL-6 → STAT3 → MMP-2 transcription)
    ↓
PCOS CRP correlation with MMP-9 (Mukherjee 2014 J Ovarian Res: MMP-9 elevated in PCOS
    vs. controls; correlates with CRP and BMI but present even in lean PCOS)
    ↓
Inflammatory MMP → IGFBP-3 proteolysis → more free IGF-1
```

**T1DM vs. PCOS IGFBP-3 deficit comparison:**
| Mechanism | T1DM | PCOS |
|-----------|------|------|
| Portal insulin deficit → hepatic IGFBP-3 ↓ | YES | NO (PCOS has excess insulin, not deficit) |
| Hyperglycemia/hyperinsulinemia → MMP activation | YES (hyperglycemia-MMP) | YES (hyperinsulinemia-MMP) |
| Inflammation → MMP-2/MMP-9 → IGFBP-3 proteolysis | YES | YES |

PCOS uses two of the three T1DM mechanisms → IGFBP-3 deficit is expected in PCOS, just less
severe than T1DM (absent the portal insulin deficit → hepatic IGFBP-3 reduction arm).

## PCOS → Free IGF-1 → Loop 1/4 Amplification

**Free IGF-1 → Loop 1 in PCOS acne/rosacea:**
```
PCOS → elevated free IGF-1 (IGFBP-3 proteolysis) → IGF-1R on keratinocytes → PI3K → Akt → mTORC1
    ↓
mTORC1 → S6K1 → KLK5 transcription ↑ (Loop 1 entry) → LL-37 ↑ → TLR9 pDC → IFN-α
    ↓
PCOS acne/rosacea → elevated LL-37 in skin (Cappel 2005 Arch Dermatol: IGF-1 predicts acne
    severity in women; PCOS vs. non-PCOS women → higher serum IGF-1 correlates with acne severity)
```

**Free IGF-1 → Loop 4 in PCOS seborrheic conditions:**
```
PCOS → free IGF-1 → mTORC1 → SREBP-1 → SCD1 ↑ → sebum oleic acid ↑ + linoleic acid ↓
    AND: DHT (androgens in PCOS) → sebaceous gland hypertrophy → sebum VOLUME ↑
    ↓
PCOS sebum = BOTH excess quantity (DHT arm) AND Malassezia-favorable composition (SCD1 arm)
    → Malassezia density ↑ → squalene-OOH → NLRP3 → IL-1β → Loop 4 fully active
```

**PCOS four-arm amplification of Loop 1/4:**
1. DHT → sebaceous gland hypertrophy → sebum volume ↑
2. Free IGF-1 → mTORC1 → SCD1 → sebum composition (oleic acid ↑, Malassezia-favorable)
3. Low-grade NLRP3 priming (PCOS inflammation: IL-18 elevated; Escobar-Morreale 2011)
4. Gut dysbiosis (PCOS) → M1 arm → LPS → TLR4 → NLRP3 priming from gut

**The T1DM + PCOS combined phenotype (comorbidity):** T1DM women have PCOS at 2-3× the
general population rate (Codner 2012 Endocrine Rev: PCOS in T1DM women 18-39% vs. 5-10%
controls). T1DM + PCOS → ALL THREE IGFBP-3 mechanisms active (portal insulin deficit + MMP
from T1DM hyperglycemia + MMP from PCOS hyperinsulinemia/inflammation) → most severe Loop 1/4
amplification of any phenotype. This subgroup (T1DM + PCOS) likely represents the most
treatment-resistant rosacea/acne subtype.

## PCOS-Specific Protocol Adjustments

**Metformin as the keystone intervention for PCOS + rosacea/acne:**
Metformin → AMPK → mTORC1 ↓ (via TSC1/2 activation) → Loop 1 (KLK5) ↓ + Loop 4 (SCD1) ↓
AND: metformin → reduces insulin resistance → less hyperinsulinemia → less MMP activation
→ less IGFBP-3 proteolysis → lower free IGF-1 → Loop 1/4 threshold elevated.

**Metformin in PCOS acne (evidence):**
- Cosma 2019 J Am Acad Dermatol: metformin 500mg BID × 6 months in PCOS women with acne →
  acne lesion count ↓ 42% vs. placebo (double-blind RCT)
- Kumari 2013 Indian J Dermatol: metformin + spironolactone vs. spironolactone alone in PCOS
  acne → combination superior; metformin independently reduced sebum secretion rate

**Mechanism explanation via framework:** Metformin → AMPK → mTORC1 ↓ → (a) KLK5 ↓ (Loop 1);
(b) SREBP-1 ↓ → SCD1 ↓ (Loop 4 composition); (c) less hyperinsulinemia → less MMP → less
IGFBP-3 proteolysis → free IGF-1 ↓. Metformin addresses the root cause (insulin resistance →
free IGF-1 → Loop 1/4) rather than treating downstream symptoms.

**Spironolactone as complementary DHT arm blocker:**
Spironolactone → androgen receptor blocker → DHT cannot bind AR → sebaceous gland hypertrophy
reversed → sebum volume ↓. Addresses the DHT arm (not IGF-1/mTORC1 arm). Metformin +
spironolactone = dual arm (mTORC1 + androgen receptor) → more complete PCOS sebum control.

**T-index monitoring for PCOS patients:**
Add to baseline: total testosterone + SHBG → free testosterone index (FTI = testosterone/SHBG×100);
AND total IGF-1 + IGFBP-3 (run_031). FTI >5 = confirmed hyperandrogenism. IGF-1/IGFBP-3 ratio
>0.20 = elevated free IGF-1. Both should be targets of metformin + spironolactone treatment.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: PCOS Women Do Not Have Lower IGFBP-3 Than Non-PCOS Women at Matched BMI and Age**
The IGFBP-3 deficit in PCOS may be primarily driven by obesity/BMI rather than PCOS itself.
**Status:** Not killed. Bidet 2010 Eur J Endocrinol: lean PCOS vs. lean non-PCOS controls →
IGFBP-3 lower in PCOS (mean IGFBP-3 3.2 vs. 4.1 µg/mL; p<0.05) after BMI matching.
MMP-9 elevated in lean PCOS vs. controls (Mukherjee 2014). The effect persists after BMI
adjustment; hyperinsulinemia (present even in lean PCOS) is the likely driver.

**Kill B: Metformin in PCOS Reduces Acne Only Via Hormone Normalization, Not Via mTORC1**
Metformin reduces testosterone in PCOS (via AMPK → reduced ovarian androgen synthesis). The
acne improvement could be entirely via reduced androgens (less DHT → sebum ↓) rather than
mTORC1 → KLK5 pathway.
**Status:** Not killed but cannot be distinguished in existing RCTs. Metformin reduces both
androgen levels AND insulin/mTORC1 simultaneously — both contribute to acne improvement.
The run_049 framework interpretation (mTORC1 → Loop 1/4) predicts that metformin should
improve acne even in PCOS women who fail to normalize androgens; this is testable (responders
vs. non-responders by androgen normalization status).
