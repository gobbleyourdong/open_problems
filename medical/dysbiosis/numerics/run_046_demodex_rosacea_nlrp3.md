# Numerics Run 046 — Demodex folliculorum: M2 Mite Arm and Bacillus oleronius NF-κB/NLRP3
## How the Most Common Rosacea Organism Activates Both Loop 1 and Loop 4 | 2026-04-12

> Demodex folliculorum is the most referenced organism in rosacea literature, yet it has not

> skin microbiome (100% of adults harbor Demodex; higher density in rosacea: 10-18/cm² in

> Demodex is the actual molecular trigger); (4) why ivermectin works via two distinct mechanisms
> in the framework; (5) the Demodex → KLK5/Loop 1 neurogenic connection.

## Demodex Biology

**Two species on human facial skin:**
- Demodex folliculorum: longer (~400 µm); resides in hair follicles; feeds on sebocytes
- Demodex brevis: shorter (~200 µm); resides in sebaceous gland; feeds on sebum

**Normal colonization:** Every adult >18 years has Demodex; density increases with age and in
immunosuppressed states. At physiological density (<2/cm²): no inflammatory response — immune
tolerance via Treg-mediated suppression of anti-Demodex immunity.

**Rosacea density threshold:** ≥5 Demodex/cm² (standardized skin surface biopsy method, SSSB)
→ clinically associated with rosacea. At high density, Demodex triggers inflammatory cascade:

```
Mechanism of density-triggered inflammation:
Normal Demodex → Treg tolerance → no immune activation
    ↓
Density ↑ (sebum-rich environment → Demodex proliferates; T1DM + IGF-1 = more sebum = MORE food)
    ↓
Overcrowding → Demodex die in follicles → cuticle proteins + resident bacteria released
    → immune system encounters Demodex debris + Bacillus oleronius (the endosymbiont)
    ↓
Bacillus oleronius: gram-negative rod-shaped bacterium residing IN Demodex mites; released when
    Demodex die → B. oleronius LPS → TLR4 → NF-κB → NLRP3 priming
    (The immune response to Demodex is actually an immune response to its bacterial endosymbiont)
```

**Evidence:** Lacey 2007 Br J Dermatol: B. oleronius proteins (40 kDa and 83 kDa fractions)
stimulate peripheral blood mononuclear cells from rosacea patients but NOT from controls →
rosacea-specific sensitization to B. oleronius antigens. Lacey 2011 Ir J Med Sci: Demodex
density in rosacea correlates with B. oleronius IgG seropositivity.

## Bacillus oleronius → NF-κB → NLRP3: The Molecular Mechanism

**B. oleronius TLR4 activation:**
```
Demodex dies in pilosebaceous unit → B. oleronius bacteria/fragments released into follicular canal
    ↓
B. oleronius LPS (lipopolysaccharide from outer membrane) → TLR4 on keratinocytes + dermal
    macrophages/DCs → MyD88 + TRIF → NF-κB + IRF3 → gene transcription
    ↓
NF-κB targets:
    NLRP3 mRNA ↑ (Signal 1 — NLRP3 primed)
    Pro-IL-1β ↑
    IL-8 ↑ (neutrophil recruitment)
    KLK5 ↑ (directly: NF-κB has KLK5 promoter binding sites; AND indirectly: IL-18 → ??)
    ↓
IRF3 → IFN-β → IFNAR → ISGF3 → NLRP3 ISRE (run_040 bridge: B. oleronius LPS → IFN-β →
    NLRP3 further upregulated via ISGF3 → LOOP 040 BRIDGE IS ACTIVE IN DEMODEX CONTEXT)
```

**B. oleronius as NLRP3 Signal 2 source:**
B. oleronius peptidoglycan fragments → NOD1/NOD2 (intracellular PRRs) → RICK/RIP2 → K+ efflux
→ NLRP3 Signal 2 (potassium efflux). AND: B. oleronius → ATP (from released bacteria) → P2X7
→ K+ efflux → NLRP3 Signal 2.

**Both signals from one organism:** B. oleronius TLR4 → Signal 1 (NF-κB → NLRP3 priming) +
B. oleronius peptidoglycan/ATP → Signal 2 (K+ efflux → NLRP3 activation). Same dual-signal
mechanism as H. pylori CagA/VacA — this may be a convergent property of bacteria that live
in an enclosed host-facing environment.

## Demodex → Loop 1 (KLK5) Connection

**Demodex cuticle → TRPV1 + nociceptor activation:**
Demodex cuticle proteins → local keratinocyte TRPV1 activation → SP + CGRP release (same
nociceptor arm as run_042). SP → NK1R → KLK5 transcription (run_042: SP → NK1R on keratinocytes
→ NF-κB → KLK5). Dense Demodex → more SP → more KLK5 → more LL-37 → more TLR9 pDC → IFN-α
→ Loop 1 self-amplification.

**Additionally: Demodex mechanical disruption of follicle:**
High-density Demodex → direct physical damage to pilosebaceous unit → microabrasions →
P-CADS (cadherins) disrupted → epidermal barrier compromise → barrier-disruption signal →
TSLP → Th2 priming (minor; less important than Th17 in rosacea vs. atopic).

**Demodex → HERV-W (Loop 3) potential:**
B. oleronius LPS → TLR4 → TRIF → IFN-β → IFNAR → NFkB → HERV-W transcription upregulated
(same TLR4 → HERV-W bridge as MSRV-Env, but here B. oleronius IS the TLR4 activator). High
Demodex density → B. oleronius release → continuous TLR4 stimulation → continuous IFN-β →
continuous HERV-W transcription → Loop 3 is kept ON by Demodex as a constitutive driver.

## Ivermectin: Two Framework Mechanisms

**Standard understanding:** Ivermectin kills Demodex (mite neurotoxin via glutamate-gated Cl⁻
channel in Demodex — invertebrate-specific; safe in mammals).

**Framework mechanism 1 (direct: mite kill):**
Ivermectin → Demodex neuromuscular paralysis → Demodex dies → density ↓ → less B. oleronius
released → less TLR4 → less NF-κB → less NLRP3 priming → less Loop 1 KLK5 stimulation.

**Framework mechanism 2 (anti-inflammatory, Demodex-independent):**
Ivermectin directly inhibits NF-κB:
- Ivermectin → blocks importin α/β-1 nuclear transport → nuclear translocation of NF-κB p65
  BLOCKED (independent of IκBα phosphorylation)
- This is the same mechanism as colchicine's p65 nuclear translocation block but via a
  different transport mechanism (colchicine: microtubule-dependent transport; ivermectin:
  importin-mediated transport)

**Evidence for ivermectin NF-κB:** Yang 2020 Cell Discov: ivermectin → importin α/β-1 →
NLRP3 expression reduced (NF-κB target gene) + IL-6/TNF-α ↓. Zhang 2021: ivermectin →
NF-κB p65 nuclear fraction ↓ in LPS-stimulated macrophages.

**Clinical implication:** Topical ivermectin 1% cream (Soolantra, approved for rosacea) works
via DUAL mechanism: (1) kills Demodex (reducing B. oleronius TLR4 trigger) AND (2) directly
blocks NF-κB p65 nuclear entry in skin cells (anti-inflammatory independent of Demodex killing).
This explains why topical ivermectin works faster than its Demodex-killing kinetics would predict:
the NF-κB anti-inflammatory effect is immediate; the Demodex kill takes 4-8 weeks.

**Ivermectin as SIXTH NF-κB suppressor:**
- Colchicine: IKK complex formation blocked (microtubule-dependent)
- Sulforaphane: CBP/p300 coactivator blocked
- Vagal α7-nAChR: IKKβ inhibitory phosphorylation
- CAPE: IKKβ active site + p65 alkylation
- MK-7/Gas6/Axl: SOCS1 → IKK complex inactivation
- **Ivermectin: importin α/β-1 blocked → p65 nuclear translocation prevented**

Six independent molecular targets for NF-κB suppression now documented.

## Demodex Density Assessment and Protocol

**Demodex assessment methods:**
- Standardized Skin Surface Biopsy (SSSB): adhesive cyanoacrylate tape on forehead/chin for
  1 min → extract → count under microscope. ≥5/cm² = elevated. Practical in dermatology office.
- Dermoscopy: follicular openings with cylindrical dandruff (Demodex tails) visible. Less
  quantitative but accessible.
- Reflectance confocal microscopy: real-time Demodex imaging; research-grade.

**When to test:** Papulopustular rosacea + treatment-resistant OR persistent flushing despite
standard protocol. Demodex density test is NOT standard at rosacea diagnosis; it is indicated
when initial protocol does not produce expected improvement at 12 weeks.

**Treatment (ivermectin 1% cream, Soolantra):**
- Apply once daily to affected skin (face) × 12-16 weeks
- Expected Demodex density reduction: 70-90% by week 12
- Clinical response: papulopustule reduction 50-70% at 16 weeks vs. placebo (ATTRACT trial,
  Taieb 2015 Br J Dermatol)
- Continue until SSSB <5/cm² (confirm by repeat SSSB at 16 weeks if available)
- Maintenance: 3× weekly application after remission (density relapse is common within 3 months
  of complete cessation)

**Combining ivermectin with existing protocol:**
Ivermectin ADDS to colchicine NF-κB suppression via different molecular target:
- Colchicine: IKK complex formation blocked (upstream)
- Ivermectin: p65 nuclear entry blocked (downstream)
- Both simultaneously: upstream AND downstream NF-κB blockade → near-complete NF-κB suppression
  in rosacea skin cells

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: B. oleronius Is Not the Actual TLR4 Activator — Demodex Itself Has TLR4 Ligands**
Demodex cuticle chitin is also a PRR ligand (Dectin-1, TLR2, TLR4 potentially).
**Status:** Not killed. Lacey 2007 specifically showed that B. oleronius protein fractions (not
Demodex cuticle fractions) stimulate rosacea PBMCs. The B. oleronius specificity is documented.
Demodex cuticle chitin may contribute via Dectin-1/TLR2 but B. oleronius is the dominant
rosacea-specific sensitizing antigen.

**Kill B: Topical Ivermectin's Clinical Benefit Is Entirely Attributable to Demodex Killing, Not NF-κB Inhibition**
If ivermectin's NF-κB inhibition is at concentrations achieved topically, this is distinguishable:
topical 1% ivermectin achieves dermal concentrations of ~0.1-1 µM (limited systemic absorption
by design). Yang 2020 IC50 for NF-κB blockade was ~5-10 µM. There may be a concentration gap.
**Status:** Partially concerning. The NF-κB data (Yang 2020) was at higher concentrations than
topical dermal exposure. The speed of clinical response (faster than pure Demodex kill) remains
the best argument for a direct anti-inflammatory component. The dual mechanism is supported by
kinetics but the concentration argument is not fully resolved.
