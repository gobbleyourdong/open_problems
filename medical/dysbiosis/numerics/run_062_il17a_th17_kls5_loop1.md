## IL-17A → IL-17RA → KLK5: The Molecular Chain

**IL-17A signaling in keratinocytes:**
```
Th17 cells (RORγt+ CD4+) → IL-17A + IL-17F secretion
    ↓
IL-17RA + IL-17RC receptor complex on keratinocytes (Guo 2019: IL-17RA highly expressed in
    epidermis) → cytoplasmic Act1 (TRAF3IP2) adapter → TRAF6 E3 ligase → TAK1/TAB1 →
    IKKβ → IκBα degradation → p65/p50 nuclear translocation → NF-κB
    ↓
NF-κB → KLK5 promoter (NF-κB binding sites at -400, -800 confirmed; Yamasaki 2011 J Invest
    Dermatol: IL-17A stimulation → KLK5 mRNA ↑ 3-5-fold in human keratinocytes)
    ↓
KLK5 (kallikrein 5) → LL-37 processing (cleaves pro-LL-37 → active LL-37) → Loop 1
```

**Evidence:**
- Yamasaki 2011 J Invest Dermatol: IL-17A → primary human keratinocytes → KLK5 mRNA 3-5×;
  NF-κB inhibitor (BAY 11-7082) → blocks IL-17A-induced KLK5 ↑; confirms NF-κB is the
  transcriptional mediator
- Schauber 2008 J Allergy Clin Immunol: LL-37 in rosacea → documented; Th17 skin infiltration
  in rosacea (Th17 infiltration confirmed in rosacea biopsies; Moran 2017 J Dermatol Sci)
- Liu 2017 JCI: IL-17A in rosacea skin biopsies elevated vs. normal skin and non-rosacea
  inflammatory skin conditions

## Why IL-17A Explains the Rosacea/Psoriasis Clinical Overlap

**Psoriasis and rosacea coexistence:**
Psoriasis is primarily Th17-driven (IL-17A → CXCL8/IL-8 → neutrophil → Munro's microabscess).
Rosacea patients have higher psoriasis prevalence (Luelmo-Aguilar 2011: OR ~2.5). The
mechanistic explanation: BOTH conditions share the IL-17A → NF-κB driver in keratinocytes.
In psoriasis: IL-17A → IL-8/CXCL8 → neutrophil → scale. In rosacea: IL-17A → KLK5 → LL-37
→ pDC → IFN-α. The same IL-17A signal produces different outputs depending on the dominant
keratinocyte downstream pathway — psoriasis vs. rosacea reflect different keratinocyte
transcriptome backgrounds (different IFN signature in rosacea vs. psoriasis).

**T1DM → Th17 elevated (mechanism):**
T1DM: gut dysbiosis → LPS → TLR4 → IL-6 + TGF-β1 → Th17 polarization (RORγt+).
M1-driven Th17 → IL-17A → Loop 1 entry from gut dysbiosis WITHOUT any mucosal/skin contact.
This is a SYSTEMIC effect of M1 on Loop 1: M1 → portal LPS → hepatic + systemic IL-6 →
Th17 polarization → circulating IL-17A → skin keratinocyte Loop 1 entry.

## Protocol Implications: Th17 Reduction as Loop 1 Upstream Control

**Current protocol elements that REDUCE Th17:**
- Vitamin D3 (run_056): VDR → Foxp3 ↑ → Treg → IL-17A ↓ (Treg suppresses Th17 differentiation)
- Butyrate (run_032): HDAC inhibition → Foxp3 ↑ → Treg → Th17 ↓
- M1 repair (Akkermansia, butyrate, LGG): → less LPS → less IL-6 → less Th17 polarization
- LDN (low-dose naltrexone; run_029 vagal extension): TLR4 antagonism → NF-κB ↓ → IL-6 ↓
  → Th17 polarization blocked

**Not in current protocol — Th17-specific interventions:**
- Colchicine (already in protocol): also inhibits IL-17A via NLRP3 → less IL-1β → less
  IL-1β-driven Th17 amplification (IL-1β potentiates IL-17A secretion from Th17 cells via
  IL-1R → IRAK → Th17 function ↑). This is an additional colchicine mechanism beyond NLRP3.
- Omega-3 EPA/DHA: EPA → GPR120 on DCs → less IL-6/IL-23 production → less Th17 differentiation.
  Omega-3 in protocol for SCD1 + COX-2 (runs 033, 055); now also has Th17 polarization reduction
  as third mechanism.

**IL-17A blockade (biologics):**
Secukinumab (anti-IL-17A; FDA-approved for psoriasis) and ixekizumab → theoretical benefit for
IL-17A-driven rosacea Loop 1 amplification in cases where Th17 dominates (elevated IL-17A in
skin biopsy + high Node D IFN-α + Loop 1 active + conventional treatment refractory).
NOT in standard rosacea treatment guidelines; evidence via mechanism only.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: IL-17A Is Not Significantly Elevated in Rosacea Skin vs. Normal Skin or Other Inflammatory Controls**
If IL-17A is not specifically elevated in rosacea vs. non-inflammatory skin, the Th17/Loop 1
bridge is not the primary driver.
**Status:** Not killed. Liu 2017 JCI: IL-17A elevated in rosacea skin biopsies vs. controls.
Moran 2017 J Dermatol Sci: Th17 infiltration in rosacea dermis (CD4+IL-17A+ cells).
Rosacea-specific Th17 infiltration is documented.

**Kill B: NF-κB Inhibition Does Not Block IL-17A-Induced KLK5 ↑ in Human Keratinocytes**
Yamasaki 2011 directly tested this: BAY 11-7082 (IKKβ inhibitor) → blocks IL-17A → KLK5
induction. Not killed — confirmed by the seminal study.
