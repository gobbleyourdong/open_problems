## Kill Finding: Rosacea Shares the Exact DR3 Haplotype With T1DM

**Chang et al. 2015 (rosacea GWAS, N~52,000, PMC4434179):**
Genome-wide significant rosacea HLA associations:
- **HLA-DRB1*03:01** (P=1.0×10⁻⁸)
- **HLA-DQB1*02:01** (P=1.3×10⁻⁸)
- **HLA-DQA1*05:01** (P=1.4×10⁻⁸)

The paper explicitly states: "The DRB1*03:01-DQB1*02:01-DQA1*05:01 haplotype has been
associated with type I diabetes." This is the canonical T1DM DR3 haplotype. Not adjacent
to it — IDENTICAL.

**Secondary rosacea loci (23andMe GWAS 2018, PMC6822543):**
- IRF4 (rs12203592): lymphocyte differentiation, Th17/plasma cell biology — NOT pDC/IFN-α
- IL13 (Th2 cytokine) — NOT type I IFN axis
- No IFNA, IRF7, MxA, or pDC-specific gene associations found in rosacea GWAS

---

## Kill Assessment

**What this kills:**
The claim that the T1DM/rosacea OR 2.59 is EVIDENCE FOR the CVB→IFN-α→pDC functional mechanism.

The OR 2.59 is **primarily explained by shared HLA-DR3 genetics** — the same classical shared
autoimmune genetics pattern seen in T1DM + celiac disease, T1DM + autoimmune thyroid disease, etc.
Shared HLA-DR3 haplotype = shared adaptive immune response tendency = correlated disease risk.
CVB→IFN-α mechanism is not needed to explain the population-level association.

**What this does NOT kill:**
1. The CVB→IFN-α mechanism itself — this may still operate in individual patients with active CVB infection
2. pDC expansion in T1DM (PMID 24973447; 18835928) — still confirmed
3. pDC type I IFN → rosacea loop (JCI Insight 2023) — still confirmed as the skin mechanism
4. **Prediction C of attempt_009 — SURVIVES**: "IFN-α stratifies rosacea risk WITHIN T1DM cohort"

## The Surviving Prediction

**Prediction C (attempt_009) — MOST IMPORTANT, SURVIVES HLA KILL:**
Within T1DM patients (all share HLA-DR3 floor equally):
- T1DM + rosacea patients should have HIGHER IFN-α2 (Simoa) than T1DM without rosacea
- T1DM + rosacea patients should have HIGHER pDC frequency than T1DM without rosacea

This test removes HLA confounding by design (comparing within T1DM, not T1DM vs non-T1DM).
If IFN-α level does NOT stratify rosacea risk within T1DM → CVB mechanism is not operating
(HLA fully explains T1DM/rosacea association).
If IFN-α level DOES stratify → functional CVB mechanism is operating ABOVE the HLA baseline.

This is now the DECISIVE TEST, not the OR 2.59 endpoint.

## What This Changes in the Protocol

**Protocol implication unchanged:**
For a T1DM+rosacea patient, IFN-α2 Simoa is still worth measuring — but for a DIFFERENT reason.
- Old reason: "elevated IFN-α caused the rosacea through CVB→pDC"
- New reason: "elevated IFN-α above HLA baseline suggests ACTIVE CVB infection, and treating it may reduce rosacea"

The protocol action (measure IFN-α, treat CVB) is identical. The mechanistic interpretation is more precise.

**What IS changed:**
If a T1DM+rosacea patient has IFN-α in the NORMAL range → CVB mechanism is not the driver
→ rosacea is likely HLA-determined immune dysregulation, not CVB-specific
→ focus rosacea treatment on the local loop (ivermectin + azelaic acid) not antiviral

Knowing IFN-α level now DISCRIMINATES between the two mechanisms in the INDIVIDUAL patient.
