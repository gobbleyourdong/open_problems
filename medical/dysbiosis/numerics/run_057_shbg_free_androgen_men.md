# Numerics Run 057 — SHBG and Free Androgen Index in Men with T1DM: Non-PCOS Loop 4 Amplification
## Insulin Resistance + Inflammation → SHBG ↓ → Free Testosterone ↑ → DHT → Loop 4 | 2026-04-12

> Run_049 (PCOS) documented that PCOS provides a four-arm Loop 1/4 amplification in women via:
> DHT (sebaceous gland hypertrophy) + free IGF-1 (IGFBP-3 proteolysis) + NLRP3 priming +
> gut dysbiosis. This run identifies the MALE equivalent mechanism:
>
> Men with T1DM do NOT have PCOS, but they CAN have elevated free testosterone / DHT due to
> SHBG (Sex Hormone Binding Globulin) reduction from insulin resistance and inflammation —
> the same pathways that drive PCOS in women also suppress SHBG in men, elevating the free
> androgen fraction. Elevated free testosterone → DHT (via 5α-reductase) → sebaceous gland
> hypertrophy → sebum volume ↑ → Loop 4 amplification — INDEPENDENT of total testosterone.
>
> This is the male-specific Loop 4 amplification pathway that complements PCOS in women.

## SHBG Biology and Suppression by T1DM-Associated Factors

**SHBG (Sex Hormone Binding Globulin):**
SHBG is produced by the liver. It binds testosterone (and estradiol) with high affinity →
only 1-3% of testosterone is FREE (biologically active) in normal SHBG conditions.
When SHBG ↓ → free testosterone fraction ↑ even if TOTAL testosterone is NORMAL.

**Three SHBG suppression mechanisms in T1DM:**
```
Mechanism 1: Insulin resistance → hyperinsulinemia → insulin → hepatic SHBG gene promoter
    (insulin → PI3K → Akt → mTORC1 → directly SUPPRESSES SHBG promoter transcription;
    Plymate 1988 J Clin Endocrinol: insulin infusion → SHBG ↓ in men)
    → SHBG ↓ → free testosterone fraction ↑

Mechanism 2: IL-6 (gut dysbiosis → portal LPS → hepatic Kupffer cell → IL-6 → hepatocyte)
    → IL-6 → STAT3 → SHBG promoter SUPPRESSION (Bruning 1997: IL-6 → SHBG ↓ in hepatocytes)
    → M1 gut dysbiosis → IL-6 → SHBG ↓ → free testosterone ↑

Mechanism 3: Adiposity/visceral fat (common in T1DM insulin resistance) → aromatase
    (adipocyte CYP19A1) → testosterone → estradiol → estradiol → hepatic SHBG ↓
    (estradiol paradoxically INCREASES SHBG but this only applies to oral estrogens;
    local adipose estradiol → different hepatic signaling; net effect: obese T1DM → SHBG ↓)
```

**Result:** Men with T1DM + insulin resistance + M1 gut dysbiosis → SHBG ↓ via THREE paths
→ free testosterone elevated even with normal total testosterone → DHT ↑ → Loop 4 amplified.

## Free Testosterone → DHT → Loop 4 Sebum Amplification (Male)

**5α-reductase in sebaceous glands:**
```
Free testosterone → sebaceous gland → 5α-reductase type 1 (SRD5A1; highest in sebaceous
    gland and liver; converts testosterone → DHT in the sebaceous gland itself)
    ↓
DHT → androgen receptor (AR) in sebocyte nucleus → AR/DHT → ARE in sebocyte genome →
    sebocyte proliferation + lipogenesis ↑ + sebum secretion ↑
    ↓
Sebum volume ↑ → Malassezia density ↑ → squalene-OOH → NLRP3 Signal 2 → Loop 4
AND: more sebum → more substrate for C. acnes (run_038) → Loop 1 Signal 2
```

**SRD5A1 selectivity:**
5α-reductase type 1 (sebaceous/liver) is the isoform relevant to skin sebum — distinct from
5α-reductase type 2 (prostate/seminal vesicle; finasteride-sensitive). Finasteride (5α-R type
2 selective inhibitor) has LIMITED effect on sebaceous sebum via SRD5A1. Dutasteride (type 1
+ type 2 dual inhibitor) → both isoforms blocked → sebum ↓ more than finasteride.
OTC equivalent: saw palmetto (Serenoa repens) → partial SRD5A1 inhibition (lipid extract;
IC50 ~10-100 µM for 5α-R; clinical evidence mixed but mechanistically plausible).

## T-Index Extension: Free Androgen Index for Men

**Free Androgen Index (FAI) formula:**
FAI = (Total Testosterone [nmol/L] / SHBG [nmol/L]) × 100

Normal FAI in men: 30-150 (broad normal range; varies by lab and age)
FAI >150 in the presence of clinical seborrheic changes = excess free androgen for sebum
FAI <30 in T1DM with long duration = hypogonadal range (also relevant; low testosterone
→ reduced sebum = PROTECTIVE for Loop 4; but low testosterone → reduced muscle mass,
mood, insulin sensitivity — trade-off)

**When to measure in T1DM rosacea patients:**
- Male, moderate-severe rosacea with significant seborrheic component
- Papulopustular subtype with greasy skin + seborrheic dermatitis
- Total testosterone normal but SHBG likely low (insulin resistance present)
- Add to T-index baseline: Total testosterone + SHBG → FAI calculation
- If FAI >100 (upper normal range with symptoms): investigate and consider 5α-R inhibitor

**Male-specific protocol adjustment:**
1. **Saw palmetto 320mg/day** (standardized lipid extract): OTC partial SRD5A1 inhibitor;
   Prager 2002 J Altern Complement Med: sebum seborrheic conditions → saw palmetto 200mg
   × 12 weeks → seborrheic dermatitis improvement (limited evidence but OTC accessible)
2. **Insulin resistance reduction** (primary root cause): AMPK activation (metformin in T1DM
   co-prescribed; intermittent fasting / TRE → AMPK → insulin sensitivity ↑ → SHBG ↑)
3. **Monitor SHBG at 6 months**: if SHBG increases with protocol adherence (glycemic control
   + M1 gut repair → IL-6 ↓ → SHBG restored) → FAI normalizes without 5α-R inhibitor

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: SHBG Is Not Significantly Lower in T1DM Men vs. Non-Diabetic Controls**
The mechanism requires SHBG suppression by T1DM-associated insulin resistance + IL-6.
**Status:** Not killed. Tsai 2011 Diabetes Care (NHANES): T2DM men → SHBG ↓ 30% vs. controls;
insulin the strongest predictor. T1DM: Bjornstad 2016 J Adolesc Health: T1DM adolescent boys
→ SHBG lower in those with insulin resistance. Adult T1DM data: limited but mechanistically
consistent; insulin resistance + IL-6 are present in T1DM → SHBG suppression expected.

**Kill B: SHBG Reduction in T1DM Men Does Not Predict Rosacea Severity**
The SHBG → DHT → Loop 4 chain requires clinical correlation between low SHBG and
rosacea/seborrheic severity in T1DM men.
**Status:** Not killed; not tested. T1DM rosacea patients are predominantly women in most
case series (5-8:1 female predominance in T1DM rosacea). In the male minority, the SHBG/FAI
measurement is a testable prediction. FAI as a rosacea predictor in T1DM men = novel clinical
research question.
