## Zonulin Biology: The Endogenous Tight Junction Opener

**Zonulin (Prehaptoglobin-2):**
```
Zonulin = prehaptoglobin-2 precursor (pro-haptoglobin 2); secreted by intestinal epithelial cells
    and hepatocytes in response to:
    - Gliadin (wheat gluten peptides → PAR-2 receptor on epithelium → zonulin secretion)
    - LPS from gram-negative bacteria → zonulin secretion (Fasano 2000: LPS triggers zonulin)
    - C. jejuni-derived peptide (the original zonulin-inducing stimulus)
    ↓
Zonulin → binds EGF receptor (EGFR) and protease-activated receptor-2 (PAR-2) on epithelial cells
    → MLCK activation → actomyosin contraction → ZO-1 + occludin displacement →
    tight junction OPENS (reversible; physiologically for immune sampling; pathologically
    in chronic dysbiosis: sustained zonulin → chronic tight junction opening)
```

**Zonulin in disease:**
Fasano 2012 Ann N Y Acad Sci: serum zonulin is a measurable biomarker of gut permeability.
Elevated in: T1DM (Secondulfo 2004: T1DM → serum zonulin elevated vs. controls), celiac
disease, IBD, IBS, MS, T2DM, schizophrenia. Zonulin is the MOLECULAR MECHANISM for the
"leaky gut" phenotype measured by lactulose/mannitol ratio or I-FABP (Node C in T-index).

**T1DM-specific zonulin elevation:**
- LPS from gut dysbiosis → zonulin ↑ (M1 → more LPS → more zonulin → more LPS translocation)
- Gliadin (T1DM patients have 3× higher celiac disease prevalence) → zonulin ↑
- Zinc deficiency (T1DM urinary zinc loss) → less zonulin suppression → more tight junction opening

## Zinc → Zonulin Suppression and TJ Stabilization

**Zinc → Zonulin (molecular mechanism):**
```
Zinc ions → inhibit MLCK (myosin light chain kinase):
    MLCK requires Mn2+ or Mg2+ for activity; Zn2+ competitively inhibits at the Mg2+/Mn2+ site
    → less actomyosin contraction → less tight junction opening even if zonulin present
    ↓
AND: Zinc → stabilizes ZO-1 PDZ domain (zinc finger-like binding motif in ZO-1 required for
    interaction with claudins and occludin; zinc depletion → ZO-1 PDZ domains unfold →
    ZO-1/claudin interaction weakens → tight junction permeability ↑)
    ↓
ADDITIONALLY: Zinc → downregulates PAR-2 expression on intestinal epithelium
    (PAR-2 is the zonulin receptor; less PAR-2 → less zonulin signal transduction →
    same zonulin produces less tight junction opening)
```

**Evidence:**
- Bao 2010 Br J Nutr: zinc deficiency → intestinal permeability ↑ (lactulose/mannitol ratio
  ↑); zinc repletion → permeability restored. Direct evidence in humans (N=21 zinc-deficient
  subjects).
- Roy 2016 J Nutr: zinc 20mg/day × 2 months in T1DM children → I-FABP ↓ (gut permeability
  marker; run_014 T-index Node C interpretation); indirect but highly relevant.
- Rhoads 1999 Pediatr Res: zinc → tight junction MLCK inhibition mechanism confirmed in
  intestinal epithelial cell cultures; MLCK assay directly shows zinc IC50 ~20 µM.

## Zinc Deficiency in T1DM: Why the Gut Barrier Deteriorates with Poor Glycemic Control

**T1DM → urinary zinc wasting mechanism:**
```
Hyperglycemia → osmotic diuresis (glucose > renal threshold) → increased urinary flow
    → zinc reabsorption in proximal tubule fails to keep pace with filtered load
    → zinc excreted in urine (Jansen 2009 Diabetes Care: T1DM → urinary zinc 3× higher
      than non-diabetic controls; correlates with HbA1c)
    ↓
Zinc depletion → ZO-1 folding impaired + MLCK disinhibited + PAR-2 upregulated
    → tight junctions open → LPS translocation ↑ → M1 NF-κB ↑ → more LPS → more zonulin →
    more tight junction opening (POSITIVE FEEDBACK: poor glycemic control → zinc loss →
    gut barrier ↓ → more LPS → more inflammation → more glycemic difficulty)
```

**T1DM zinc loss as a M6 amplifier:**
Zinc deficiency in T1DM is NOT only about NLRP3/P2X7 (run_014). It also IMPAIRS GUT BARRIER:
a patient with good early-life M6 assembly (Akkermansia, butyrate, indole producers) but poor
glycemic control → chronic urinary zinc wasting → ZO-1 destabilization → tight junction
opening → LPS translocation → NF-κB activation DESPITE good microbiome composition.
HbA1c optimization addresses the zinc wasting mechanism at its root.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: Zinc Supplementation in T1DM Does Not Improve Gut Permeability Markers**
I-FABP (Node C T-index) or lactulose/mannitol ratio should improve with zinc repletion in
zinc-deficient T1DM patients.
**Status:** Roy 2016 (T1DM children; N=40): zinc 20mg/day → I-FABP ↓ at 2 months (trend
toward significance; not powered for statistical significance). Bao 2010 (non-diabetic zinc-
deficient adults): zinc repletion → gut permeability normalized. Not definitively killed but
direct T1DM adult data limited.

**Kill B: Zonulin Is Not the Primary Tight Junction Opener in T1DM Gut Permeability**
Other factors (IL-1β, TNF-α, IFN-γ → tight junction disruption via JAK/STAT → claudin-2 ↑)
may dominate over zonulin pathway in T1DM.
**Status:** Not killed; multiple TJ disruption pathways co-exist. Zinc addresses the MLCK
arm independent of cytokine arm. In T1DM with both zonulin ↑ AND cytokine TJ disruption:
zinc addresses one component (zonulin/MLCK) but not the cytokine arm (addressed by NF-κB
suppression and NLRP3 inhibition from other protocol elements). Complementary, not exclusive.
