# Numerics Run 030 — Oral Red Complex: T. denticola + T. forsythia Completing M7
## Triple Colonization Mechanism and Why M7 Is Worst When All Three Are Present | 2026-04-12

> M7 (oral dysbiosis) has been analyzed around P. gingivalis as the primary organism — its
> CAR (complement-alternative pathway evasion), IgA protease (sIgA-protease self-amplifying loop),
> and fimbriae (TLR2 priming). The full M7 mechanism requires completing the analysis with the
> other two members of the "red complex" (Socransky 1998): T. denticola and T. forsythia.
> These two organisms have INDEPENDENT virulence mechanisms that are ADDITIVE to P. gingivalis
> in producing the most severe periodontal and systemic inflammatory phenotype.
> Understanding the triple colonization mechanism explains why single-organism targeting fails
> and why the full M7 treatment approach requires addressing all three.

---

## The Red Complex: Why It's a Complex, Not Just Three Bacteria

Socransky 1998 Infect Immun identified the red complex as the strongest periodontal pathogen
cluster — the three co-colonize at significantly higher rates than expected by chance, forming
a synergistic pathogenic unit:

```
P. gingivalis + T. denticola + T. forsythia — synergistic co-colonization
    ↓
P. gingivalis: CAR activation + IgA protease + gingipains + TLR2/TLR4 (fimbriae)
    ↓ ↓
T. denticola: dentilisin (metalloprotease) + IgA protease activity + CTLP (chymotrypsin-like protease)
    ↓ ↓
T. forsythia: BspA (TLR2 agonist) + sialidase + lytic enzymes
    ↓
Combined: triple synergy in periodontal pocket:
    (a) Complement evasion (all three have different mechanisms)
    (b) IgA degradation (P. gingivalis + T. denticola = double IgA protease)
    (c) Tissue invasion (P. gingivalis invasins + T. denticola motility + T. forsythia sialidase)
    (d) TLR2/TLR4 stimulation (all three have TLR-activating surface components)
```

**Why single-organism targeting is insufficient:**
If P. gingivalis is suppressed (by antibiotic or immune clearance), T. denticola and T. forsythia
maintain: IgA degradation, TLR2 stimulation, and tissue invasion. The sIgA-protease self-amplifying
M7 loop (IgA → P. gingivalis IgA protease → sIgA destroyed → less P. gingivalis control → more
IgA protease) has an EQUIVALENT loop for T. denticola. Single-organism treatment does not break
the multi-organism loop.

## Treponema denticola: Independent M7 Mechanisms

### T. denticola Virulence 1: Dentilisin (CTLP) and IgA Protease Activity

```
Dentilisin (T. denticola major protease, a chymotrypsin-like protease):
    → Cleaves IgA1 (the primary mucosal immunoglobulin in saliva/gingival crevicular fluid)
    → Same effect as P. gingivalis IgA protease: destroys local IgA → less opsonization
    → Also cleaves fibronectin, laminin, collagen IV (basement membrane degradation → invasion)
    ↓
Second IgA protease in the periodontal pocket: P. gingivalis + T. denticola = DOUBLE IgA degradation
    → Total sIgA in gingival fluid ↓↓ → immune surveillance further impaired
```

**M7 loop amplification:** The sIgA-protease self-amplifying loop (from attempt_015 / M7 analysis)
runs for BOTH P. gingivalis (lysine-specific IgA protease) AND T. denticola (dentilisin). Even
if P. gingivalis is cleared, the T. denticola dentilisin loop continues to suppress local IgA.
This is why some patients with periodontitis recur quickly after scaling + root planing (SRP) —
T. denticola IgA protease re-establishes before IgA levels recover.

### T. denticola Virulence 2: Outer Sheath Proteins → TLR2 → NF-κB

```
T. denticola outer sheath complex (MOSP/TprK proteins):
    → TLR2 agonism on gingival fibroblasts and PDL cells
    → NF-κB → IL-1β, IL-6, IL-8 (CXCL8) in periodontal tissue
    → ADDITIVE to P. gingivalis fimbriae TLR2 stimulation
    → Also: MOSP → complement evasion (binds factor H → alternative pathway blocked)
```

### T. denticola Virulence 3: Motility in Anaerobic Periodontal Pocket

T. denticola is motile (flagella) — it actively migrates through the periodontal pocket to
deep anoxic zones where antibiotics penetrate poorly. P. gingivalis is non-motile. T. denticola
acts as a "scout" — its motility enables colonization of zones that P. gingivalis then secondarily
colonizes. **Killing P. gingivalis without addressing T. denticola leaves the colonization
infrastructure intact for P. gingivalis recolonization.**

## Tannerella forsythia: Independent M7 Mechanisms

### T. forsythia Virulence 1: BspA — the Primary TLR2/TLR4 Agonist

```
BspA (Bacteroides-like surface protein A) on T. forsythia outer surface:
    → TLR2 AND TLR4 dual agonist (unusual: most organisms activate one or the other)
    → TLR2 → MyD88 → NF-κB → IL-1β, IL-6, TNF-α in macrophages/DCs
    → TLR4 → TRIF → IRF3 → IFN-β (type I IFN) → M3 arm INPUT from oral source
    ↓
T. forsythia BspA is the ORAL MICROBIOME contribution to the M3 arm IFN-α load:
    BspA → TLR4 → TRIF → IFN-β → pDC IFN-α response amplified
    (pDCs in gingival tissue and in regional lymphatics)
```

**This is a direct oral → M3 connection for T. forsythia specifically.** P. gingivalis primarily
uses TLR2; T. forsythia BspA also activates TLR4 → TRIF → IFN pathway. Triple red complex
colonization provides TLR2 (P. gingivalis + T. denticola + T. forsythia BspA) + TLR4 (T. forsythia
BspA) + IFN-β → pDC amplification. This is a more complete M3 arm activation than P. gingivalis
alone.

### T. forsythia Virulence 2: Sialidase → Mucus Degradation → Access

```
T. forsythia sialidase (NanH):
    → Cleaves sialic acid from mucin glycoproteins in gingival crevicular fluid
    → Mucin de-sialylation → mucus gel collapses → physical barrier thinner
    → Exposes underlying glycan substrates → supports T. forsythia + P. gingivalis colonization
    → ALSO: sialic acid released → nutrient for T. forsythia (it uses sialic acid as carbon source)
```

This is the ORAL EQUIVALENT of Malassezia lipase activity in the sebaceous gland (M2): both
organisms degrade the host's protective barrier to access nutrients and create a colonization niche.

### T. forsythia Virulence 3: Complement Evasion via Factor H + C4b-Binding Protein

T. forsythia binds both factor H AND C4b-binding protein → inhibits alternative AND classical
complement pathways simultaneously. P. gingivalis uses CAR (C3-subversion trick). T. forsythia
uses direct factor H + C4bBP binding. Together: triple complement evasion from all three
organisms → complete complement surveillance failure in deep periodontal pockets.

## The Additive Mechanism Map

```
M7 IFN-α input from triple red complex:
    P. gingivalis fimbriae (FimA) → TLR2 → MyD88 → IL-6/TNF-α → GALT Th17 priming
    T. denticola MOSP → TLR2 → IL-1β, IL-8
    T. forsythia BspA → TLR2 + TLR4 → IL-6 + IFN-β → pDC → IFN-α
    TOTAL: triple TLR2 stimulation + TLR4/IFN arm = highest M3 arm loading from M7 source

M7 IgA destruction (sIgA-protease loop):
    P. gingivalis: lysine-specific IgA1 protease
    T. denticola: dentilisin (chymotrypsin-like IgA cleavage)
    TOTAL: double IgA protease → sIgA virtually eliminated in deep pockets

M7 complement evasion:
    P. gingivalis: CAR (converts C3b to C3d → anti-opsonic, pro-inflammatory)
    T. denticola: factor H binding
    T. forsythia: factor H + C4bBP (classical + alternative blocked)
    TOTAL: all complement activation pathways blocked by different mechanisms

M7 tissue invasion:
    P. gingivalis: invasins (RgpA/B) → epithelial cell invasion
    T. denticola: motility → deep pocket colonization
    T. forsythia: sialidase → mucus degradation → physical access
    TOTAL: multi-mechanism tissue penetration and pocket deepening
```

**The synergy: each organism does what the others cannot.** P. gingivalis leads with complement
subversion; T. denticola pioneers deep pocket colonization via motility; T. forsythia degrades
the last physical barrier (mucus) and provides the TLR4/IFN arm that P. gingivalis avoids (P.
gingivalis actually SUPPRESSES TLR4 activation to avoid IFN-β, which would activate NK cells
against it; T. forsythia's BspA TLR4 activation may actually benefit the other two organisms by
driving more IL-6 locally while reducing TLR4-competent immune surveillance).

## Revised M7 Treatment Framework

**Current M7 protocol:** Scaling + root planing (SRP) → physical removal. Doxycycline 40mg/day
(sub-antimicrobial dose MMP inhibition) + LGG 90-day (sIgA restoration). M8 cortisol normalization
first (sIgA recovery requires HPA not depressed). Phageome: ΦPgI endolysins (pre-clinical, 3-5 years).

**What this misses with T. denticola + T. forsythia:**
1. T. denticola motility → survives SRP in deep pockets → re-colonizes after SRP (explains recurrence)
2. T. denticola IgA protease → LGG sIgA restoration is countered AGAIN by T. denticola dentilisin
   (not just P. gingivalis IgA protease)
3. T. forsythia sialidase → mucus layer doesn't recover if T. forsythia is still active (NanH
   continuously degrades regenerating mucin)

**Additional M7 interventions for triple-colonized patients:**

| Organism | Targeting approach | Evidence |
|---------|-------------------|---------|
| T. denticola | Metronidazole 400mg BID × 7 days (anaerobe-specific; P. gingivalis is also susceptible; doxycycline 200mg/day × 14 days as alternative) | Socransky 2002: metronidazole + amoxicillin reduces red complex in periodontal pockets |
| T. forsythia | Essential oils (thymol, menthol — Listerine components) → outer sheath disruption → T. forsythia is relatively susceptible; twice-daily mouthwash | ADA review: essential oil mouthwash reduces T. forsythia counts 60-70% at 6 months |
| All three | Chlorhexidine 0.12% mouthwash BID × 4 weeks | Reduces all three red complex species; 4-week course post-SRP to reduce re-colonization from saliva |

**Sequencing:**
1. M8 cortisol normalization FIRST (sIgA recovery requires non-suppressed HPA)
2. SRP (physical debridement — still essential; removes subgingival biofilm mechanically)
3. Chlorhexidine 0.12% BID × 4 weeks post-SRP (prevent immediate recolonization)
4. Metronidazole 400mg BID × 7 days (if T. denticola/T. forsythia specifically targeted)
5. LGG 90 days (sIgA restoration — now more likely to succeed with T. denticola IgA protease reduced)
6. Doxycycline 40mg/day × 3 months (sub-antimicrobial MMP inhibition — ongoing collagen protection)

**Biomarker for triple red complex:** Salivary DNA PCR panel for all three species (commercial
labs: OralDNA Labs, OmniGen). Baseline + 3-month post-treatment. If T. denticola/T. forsythia
persist after P. gingivalis clearance, add metronidazole + essential oil mouthwash.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: T. forsythia BspA TLR4 → IFN-β Does Not Measurably Contribute to Systemic IFN-α**
BspA-driven TLR4 → TRIF → IFN-β in gingival tissue is a local mechanism. Whether this local
IFN-β production is sufficient to activate systemic pDCs (vs. being handled by local NK cells)
depends on T. forsythia load and gingival vascularization.
**Status:** Not killed. Severe periodontitis is associated with elevated systemic IFN-α (not
specifically attributed to T. forsythia, but the red complex as a whole). BspA-specific IFN
contribution is an extrapolation from in vitro data.

**Kill B: Adding Metronidazole to SRP Does Not Reduce Red Complex Recurrence vs. SRP Alone**
**Status:** Not killed. Berglundh 2004 J Clin Periodontol: metronidazole + SRP vs. SRP alone →
significantly lower T. denticola counts at 6 months (p<0.05). Benefit is documented; duration
of benefit before re-colonization is the question.
