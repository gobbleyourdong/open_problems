# Numerics Run 039 — Vitamin K2 (MK-7): Fifth NF-κB Suppressor via Gas6/Axl TAM Signaling
## Menaquinone-7 as Multi-Mechanism Framework Compound in T1DM | 2026-04-12

> Current NF-κB suppression has four independent pathways documented:
> 1. Colchicine: IKK complex assembly blockade (microtubule-dependent)
> 2. Sulforaphane: Nrf2/CBP competition → p300 unavailable for NF-κB coactivation
> 3. Vagal α7-nAChR: JAK2/STAT3 → IKK-β inhibitory phosphorylation
> 4. CAPE: IKKβ catalytic inhibition + p65 Cys38 alkylation (dual)
>
> Vitamin K2 (menaquinone-7, MK-7) is documented to suppress NF-κB via a FIFTH mechanism:
> Gas6 (growth arrest-specific protein 6) → Axl/Mer TAM receptor tyrosine kinases → SOCS1/3
> → NF-κB p65 inhibitory phosphorylation. This is distinct from all four existing pathways.
> Additionally, K2 in T1DM has two specific relevances: (1) gut dysbiosis depletes the primary
> K2 source (Bacteroidetes/Prevotella — both depleted in T1DM gut); (2) K2 activates Matrix Gla
> Protein (MGP) → vascular protection specifically relevant in T1DM arterial calcification risk.
> This run evaluates whether K2/MK-7 merits inclusion in the framework as a fifth NF-κB arm
> and/or as a T1DM-specific micronutrient protocol addition.

## Vitamin K2 Biology

**K2 forms and sources:**
- MK-4 (menaquinone-4): short-chain; produced by tissue conversion of K1 (phylloquinone); found
  in animal-source foods (egg yolk, butter, liver); not produced by gut bacteria
- MK-7 (menaquinone-7): long-chain; produced by gut bacteria (primarily Bacteroides + Prevotella
  in Bacteroidetes phylum) via menaquinone biosynthesis pathway; also in natto (fermented soybean)
- MK-7 has longer serum half-life (72h vs. 1-2h for MK-4) → more sustained activity; preferred
  for supplementation

**T1DM-specific K2 depletion mechanism:**
```
T1DM → gut dysbiosis (M1 arm) → Bacteroidetes ↓ (documented in T1DM gut microbiome studies;
    Brown 2011 PLoS ONE; Alkanani 2015 Diabetes)
    ↓
Bacteroides/Prevotella (MK-7 producers) ↓ → less intestinal MK-7 synthesis
    ↓
T1DM patients → MK-7 status often borderline-deficient even without dietary K2 restriction
    (Szulc 2001 documented K2 status in inflammatory disease; direct T1DM+K2 data limited)
    ↓
Low K2 → Gas6 not carboxylated → TAM receptor signaling impaired → NF-κB less restrained
    AND: Matrix Gla Protein not carboxylated → vascular calcification uninhibited
```

**This creates a GUT DYSBIOSIS → K2 DEFICIT → NF-κB DISINHIBITION loop specific to T1DM:**
M1 gut dysbiosis simultaneously causes: (1) gut leakage → systemic LPS → NLRP3 priming;
AND (2) Bacteroidetes depletion → K2 deficit → NF-κB restraint lost. Two NF-κB consequences
from the same M1 event.

## Gas6/Axl TAM Receptor NF-κB Suppression Mechanism

**Gas6 (Growth Arrest-Specific protein 6) requires K2-dependent gamma-carboxylation:**
```
Gas6 protein has Gla domain (N-terminal, 12-15 gamma-carboxyglutamic acid residues)
    ↓
Gamma-carboxylation requires vitamin K-dependent gamma-glutamyl carboxylase (GGCX enzyme)
    + vitamin K2 (cofactor, reduced to KH2 during reaction)
    ↓
Un-carboxylated Gas6 (ucGas6): cannot bind phosphatidylserine on apoptotic cells + cannot
    bind TAM receptors (Axl, Mer, Tyro3) with full affinity
    ↓
K2 deficiency → Gas6 not fully carboxylated → Gas6/TAM receptor signaling IMPAIRED
```

**Gas6 → Axl/Mer → SOCS1/3 → NF-κB inhibition:**
```
Fully carboxylated Gas6 → binds Axl (Tyrosine Kinase With Immunoglobulin And EGF Homology Domains)
    or Mer (MERTK) → receptor dimerization → kinase domain autophosphorylation → intracellular
    signaling cascade:
    ↓
Axl/Mer → PI3K → Akt (pro-survival) + simultaneously:
Axl/Mer → SOCS1 + SOCS3 (Suppressors Of Cytokine Signaling 1 and 3) upregulation
    ↓
SOCS1 → directly inhibits IKK complex by binding NEMO (NF-κB essential modulator) → blocks
    IKK-β activation → IκBα not phosphorylated → NF-κB p65/p50 stays bound to IκBα
    (this is distinct from colchicine which blocks IKK complex FORMATION — SOCS1 blocks its
    ACTIVATION even after the complex has assembled)
SOCS3 → blocks JAK/STAT3 signaling → limits NF-κB-activating cytokine amplification
    ↓
Net: Gas6/Axl → SOCS1 → IKK-β inactivated → NF-κB p65 not released → less NLRP3 priming
    + less HERV-W transcription (Loop 3 branch)
```

**This is the FIFTH mechanistically distinct NF-κB suppression pathway:**
- Colchicine: IKK complex FORMATION blocked (microtubule scaffold disruption)
- Sulforaphane: p300/CBP COACTIVATION blocked (NF-κB binds DNA but cannot recruit p300)
- Vagal α7-nAChR: IKK-β catalytic INHIBITION via JAK2/STAT3 (inhibitory phosphorylation)
- CAPE: IKKβ active site INHIBITION + p65 Cys38 alkylation (dual downstream block)
- **K2/Gas6/Axl: IKK complex ACTIVATION blocked by SOCS1 (assembled complex, inactivated)**

Five distinct molecular targets: assembly, coactivation, inhibitory phosphorylation, active site, activation.

## TAM Receptor Anti-Inflammatory Role in Autoimmunity (Direct T1DM Relevance)

**TAM receptors (Axl/Mer/Tyro3) function as innate immune CHECK points:**
TAM receptors are expressed on dendritic cells, macrophages, and NK cells. Their ligands (Gas6,
Protein S) are K2-dependent. TAM receptor signaling RESTRAINS innate immune activation:
- Mer on macrophages → efferocytosis of apoptotic cells → NO inflammatory signal; also Mer →
  SOCS1 → TLR4 signal suppression
- Axl on dendritic cells → SOCS1/3 → type I IFN production suppressed (TRIF pathway)
- Tyro3 on T cells → Gas6 binding → Treg stability (Tregs express Tyro3)

**In T1DM with Bacteroidetes depletion → K2 deficit:**
- Gas6 under-carboxylated → Axl/Mer/Tyro3 signaling reduced
- Dendritic cells: less SOCS1 → TLR4 → NF-κB MORE active (same LPS input → more IL-12, IL-6)
- Macrophages: less Mer → less efferocytosis → apoptotic cell debris accumulates → DAMPs → NLRP3
- T cells (Tyro3): less Gas6 → Treg stability reduced → immune regulatory capacity ↓

**Autoimmune amplification:** K2 deficiency → TAM receptor hypoactivity → innate immune check
points released → lower threshold for islet autoimmunity progression. This is a T1DM-specific
K2 loss-of-function hypothesis that connects gut dysbiosis (M1) → K2 deficit → autoimmune
amplification at multiple innate immune levels.

## Matrix Gla Protein (MGP) — Vascular Relevance in T1DM

**MGP requires K2-dependent carboxylation:**
Matrix Gla Protein is the primary inhibitor of vascular calcium deposition. Un-carboxylated
MGP (ucMGP) cannot bind calcium → calcium phosphate crystals deposit in arterial wall →
arterial calcification. T1DM has the highest arterial calcification rate of any metabolic disease
(independent of age/cholesterol; driven by hyperglycemia-driven AGER activation + RANKL).

**K2 + MGP + T1DM connection:**
- T1DM dysbiosis → Bacteroidetes ↓ → MK-7 ↓ → MGP under-carboxylated → vascular calcification
  UNINHIBITED → arterial stiffness → end-organ disease (renal, retinal, cardiac)
- ucMGP blood levels are a validated marker of K2 functional status (dp-ucMGP assay):
  dp-ucMGP >400 pmol/L = K2 insufficient; dp-ucMGP <200 pmol/L = K2 sufficient
- Supplementation 100-180µg MK-7/day → dp-ucMGP normalizes in 3-6 months (Knapen 2012 Osteoporos
  Int: 180µg MK-7 × 3y → significant reduction in arterial stiffness as measured by pulse wave
  velocity in postmenopausal women; not T1DM-specific but vascular mechanism confirmed)

**For T1DM framework:** MK-7 180µg/day addresses TWO framework-relevant deficits from a single
intervention: (1) Gas6 carboxylation → TAM receptor NF-κB restraint restored; (2) MGP
carboxylation → vascular calcification inhibited. The vascular benefit is directly relevant to
T1DM complication prevention independent of the inflammatory framework.

## Osteocalcin → Insulin Sensitization (Third K2 Benefit)

**Osteocalcin requires K2-dependent carboxylation:**
Osteocalcin is a bone Gla protein; carboxylated osteocalcin (cOC) binds hydroxyapatite in bone.
Under-carboxylated osteocalcin (ucOC) is released into circulation where it has ENDOCRINE FUNCTION:
```
ucOC → binds GPRC6A receptor on pancreatic β cells → insulin secretion ↑
ucOC → binds GPRC6A on skeletal muscle → glucose uptake ↑ (insulin-sensitizing effect)
ucOC → adipose tissue → adiponectin ↑ → further insulin sensitization
```

**K2 paradox for osteocalcin:** K2 carboxylates osteocalcin → REDUCES ucOC → reduces osteocalcin's
endocrine/insulin-sensitizing activity. This appears to create a trade-off. However:
- The primary clinical benefit of K2 for insulin resistance operates through IMPROVED β cell
  function (Gas6/Axl → NF-κB suppressed → less β cell IL-1β → β cells protected) rather than
  through ucOC increase
- K2 trials in T2DM → improved insulin sensitivity despite osteocalcin carboxylation (Sato 2020
  Nutrients review): net effect is insulin sensitization, not desensitization
- The NF-κB suppression effect on β cells (reducing chronic inflammasome-driven β cell loss)
  outweighs any ucOC reduction from K2 supplementation

**For T1DM:** β cell protection is less relevant (most β cells already destroyed at T1DM diagnosis).
The osteocalcin insulin sensitization is peripheral (muscle/fat) → relevant for insulin resistance
in T1DM; improves T-index node B (inflammatory tone) indirectly.

## Dosing and Formulation

**MK-7 supplementation:**
- Target: 180µg/day MK-7 (menaquinone-7, not menaquinone-4 — MK-7 has 72h half-life vs. 1-2h)
- Form: MK-7 from natto extraction (Bacillus subtilis var. natto fermentation product); synthetic
  all-trans MK-7 is equivalent; avoid MK-4 for gut dysbiosis context (gut bacteria produce MK-7,
  not MK-4; supplementing the form the gut microbiome normally makes is the goal)
- Timing: with dietary fat (K vitamins are fat-soluble; absorption requires bile acids and fat)
- Cost: ~$10-20/month for 180µg MK-7
- Duration: dp-ucMGP monitoring at baseline + 3 months; target <200 pmol/L

**Drug interaction:** Warfarin (vitamin K antagonist — anticoagulant mechanism via GGCX inhibition).
MK-7 directly antagonizes warfarin effect. Contraindicated with warfarin. Check anticoagulant
status before prescribing K2 in any T1DM patient on anticoagulation.

**Deficiency assessment:** dp-ucMGP assay (LabCorp available; VitaK assay kit). Not standard in
most clinical panels. Can be ordered as specialty lab. If unavailable, population-level assumption:
T1DM + dysbiosis + diet low in natto/fermented foods → high probability of functional K2 insufficiency.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: Gas6/Axl Signaling Does Not Suppress NF-κB in Human Innate Immune Cells at Physiological K2 Concentrations**
The SOCS1 → NF-κB inhibition mechanism is confirmed in murine macrophages and cell lines.
Human primary macrophage Gas6/Axl → SOCS1 → NF-κB suppression at K2-supplementation concentrations
is not directly demonstrated in RCT.
**Status:** Not killed. Rothlin 2007 Cell: TAM receptor SOCS1/3 → innate immune restraint confirmed
in macrophages and DCs (mouse + human cell lines). Lumeng 2010: Gas6 deficiency → macrophage
NF-κB hyperactivation. Wang 2014 J Immunol: Mer → SOCS1 → TLR4 signal suppressed in human
macrophages. Human evidence is cell-line based; in vivo RCT with NF-κB endpoint not done.

**Kill B: MK-7 Supplementation Does Not Raise dp-ucMGP to Sufficient Levels to Affect TAM Signaling**
TAM receptors bind Gas6, not MGP. The functional K2 status proxy (dp-ucMGP) reflects overall
K2 activity but Gas6 carboxylation was not measured in K2 supplementation trials.
**Status:** Not killed but the link is indirect. dp-ucMGP → K2 status sufficient → Gas6
carboxylation also sufficient (same GGCX enzyme carboxylates both). This is a reasonable
biochemical inference. Direct Gas6 carboxylation measurement with K2 supplementation would
be required to confirm — technically possible but not done in published trials.

**Kill C: T1DM Patients Actually Have Lower K2 Status Than Non-T1DM Controls**
Bacteroidetes depletion in T1DM gut → reduced MK-7 production is the proposed mechanism. Direct
MK-7 measurement in T1DM gut or dp-ucMGP comparison T1DM vs. non-T1DM controls is limited.
**Status:** Not killed. Brown 2011 PLoS ONE (T1DM gut microbiome): Bacteroidetes ↓ in T1DM
children vs. controls. Bacteroidetes are the primary gut MK-7 producers. The mechanistic
inference is sound; direct K2 status measurement in T1DM dysbiosis is an unfilled gap.
