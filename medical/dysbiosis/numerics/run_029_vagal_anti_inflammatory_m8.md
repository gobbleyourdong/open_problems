## The Cholinergic Anti-Inflammatory Pathway (CAP)

Tracey 2002 Nature identified the vagus nerve as a rapid, hard-wired anti-inflammatory output.
The mechanism:

```
Vagus nerve (CNS origin: dorsal motor nucleus/nucleus tractus solitarius)
    ↓ efferent vagal fibers
Celiac ganglion → splenic nerve (noradrenergic, not cholinergic at this step)
    ↓
Splenic nerve terminals release norepinephrine → β2-adrenergic receptor on splenic T cells
    ↓
Choline acetyltransferase (ChAT)-expressing T cells (CD4+ T cell subset in spleen)
    → activated by β2-AR stimulation → release acetylcholine
    ↓
Acetylcholine → α7 nicotinic acetylcholine receptor (α7-nAChR) on splenic macrophages
    ↓
α7-nAChR → JAK2 → STAT3 → reduces HMGB1 secretion from macrophages
α7-nAChR → inhibits IKK-β → NF-κB p65 nuclear translocation blocked
    ↓
TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6, HMGB1 secretion from splenic macrophages ↓
    ↓
Systemic anti-inflammatory effect via reduced splenic cytokine output
```

**This pathway is the neural anti-inflammatory reflex — it operates within milliseconds to minutes,
while cortisol/HPA feedback operates over hours.** It is the rapid-response arm of M8.

## Framework Connections

### Connection 1: CAP → Loop 3 (HERV-W/NF-κB) via α7-nAChR → IKK-β

The same IKK-β that colchicine disrupts (Loop 3 treatment) is the target of α7-nAChR signaling.
Vagal tone → α7-nAChR → IKK-β inhibited → NF-κB ↓ → HERV-W promoter less active.
**Cold exposure (already in protocol) → vagal tone → α7-nAChR → NF-κB ↓ = a THIRD NF-κB
suppression pathway alongside colchicine (IKK) and sulforaphane (CBP competition).**

### Connection 2: CAP → HMGB1 Clearance (Loop 2 Re-Priming Prevention)

HMGB1 is a DAMP released during pyroptosis (Loop 2). HMGB1 re-primes NLRP3 (via RAGE → NF-κB →
NLRP3 priming). The RvD1/omega-3 pathway clears HMGB1 via macrophage efferocytosis (run_020).
α7-nAChR on macrophages directly reduces HMGB1 secretion (Tracey 2002).
**Vagal activation → HMGB1 ↓ → Loop 2 re-priming reduced = third HMGB1 suppression mechanism
alongside omega-3 (efferocytosis) and melatonin (SIRT1 → HMGB1 deacetylation and retention).**

### Connection 3: CAP → GI Motility → M1 (Gut Barrier Maintenance)

Vagal efferent → enteric nervous system → gut motility maintained. Gut motility is essential
for bacterial transit and prevents SIBO (small intestinal bacterial overgrowth). M8 sympathetic
dominance (stress → sympathetic → vagal withdrawal → gut dysmotility → SIBO → I-FABP rises).

**Vagal tone restoration → gut motility normalized → SIBO prevented → I-FABP maintained → M1
arm not fed by gut dysmotility as a secondary mechanism.** This is particularly relevant in
ME/CFS where autonomic dysfunction (POTS, orthostatic intolerance) includes vagal withdrawal.

### Connection 4: CAP → M3 (IFN-α Dampening via Splenic DC)

pDCs (the primary IFN-α producers) in the spleen express α7-nAChR.
α7-nAChR → STAT3 → IRF7 suppression (the same IRF7 suppression that melatonin achieves via
a different pathway — see run_022).
**Vagal activation → splenic pDC α7-nAChR → IRF7 suppression → less IFN-α per TLR stimulus →
M3 arm damped.** Synergistic with melatonin's pDC IRF7 suppression (different receptor → same
IRF7 endpoint).

## Cold Exposure: The Protocol Component That Activates CAP

The Wim Hof method (WHM) — already in the protocol as M8 + metabolic component — activates
vagal tone via the DIVING REFLEX:

```
Cold water (face/full body submersion or cold shower) → cutaneous cold receptors activated
    ↓
Trigeminocardiac reflex + diving reflex → brainstem → dorsal motor nucleus of vagus
    ↓
Vagal efferent output ↑ → heart rate ↓ (measurable) → splenic nerve activation
    ↓
Cholinergic anti-inflammatory pathway activated (Tracey mechanism)
    ↓
α7-nAChR → NF-κB ↓ + HMGB1 ↓ + IFN-α ↓ in spleen
```

**This is the molecular mechanism for WHY cold exposure has anti-inflammatory effects beyond
"brown fat activation" or "catecholamine release."** The catecholamine arm (cold → norepinephrine)
is sympathetic. The vagal arm (cold → diving reflex → vagal → α7-nAChR) is parasympathetic.
Both operate simultaneously during cold exposure, but on different timescales and via different
pathways.

**Evidence:**
- Rosas-Ballina 2011 Science: CAP requires the ChAT+ T cell intermediate in spleen; vagal
  electrical stimulation → α7-nAChR → TNF-α suppression (in LPS challenge)
- Borovikova 2000 Nature: vagal stimulation in rodents → LPS-induced TNF-α ↓ 70%
- WHM specific: Kox 2014 PNAS (Wim Hof group): WHM-trained volunteers → voluntarily
  attenuated innate immune response to endotoxin challenge (LPS IV infusion) → TNF-α ↓,
  IL-6 ↓, IL-10 ↑. Mechanism partly attributed to HPA (adrenaline); vagal arm not specifically
  measured but compatible.

## HRV (Heart Rate Variability) as CAP Proxy Measurement

Heart rate variability (HRV) is the established clinical proxy for vagal tone:
- High HRV → strong vagal modulation of heart rate → high vagal tone → CAP active
- Low HRV → sympathetic dominance → vagal withdrawal → CAP suppressed

**Protocol measurement addition:** HRV monitoring via wearable (Oura ring, Garmin, Apple Watch)
provides a daily estimate of vagal tone. Low morning HRV → that day's inflammatory tone is
higher (vagal withdrawal → less α7-nAChR → less NF-κB/HMGB1 suppression).

**Clinical correlation prediction:** In T1DM + rosacea patients:
- HRV lowest in week before rosacea flare (sympathetic dominance → CAP withdrawn → NF-κB/NLRP3
  less suppressed → flare threshold crossed)
- HRV restoration after WHM protocol → parallel reduction in flare frequency

This is a testable prediction from the CAP mechanism.

  already in ME/CFS protocol for TRPV1/PEM; also relevant for rosacea with high M8 burden.

## Why This Matters for Non-Responder Phenotyping

**Observation:** Some Loop 2/Loop 3 non-responders are "flare-and-calm" phenotype — they have
frequent episodic flares that partially resolve between episodes. This is consistent with
oscillating vagal tone rather than a fixed "always-inflammatory" state.

**Low-HRV patients** (chronic sympathetic dominance): vagal withdrawal → CAP consistently
suppressed → NF-κB/NLRP3 low-level constant activation → grinding low-grade inflammation
rather than discrete flares.

**WHM + breathing + HRV monitoring = M8 parasympathetic restoration protocol** — separate from
the M8 cortisol/CRH/HPA arm (sleep, adaptogens, MBSR) but operating on the same M8 mountain
from the opposing side.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: α7-nAChR Activation Does Not Suppress NF-κB in Human Rosacea Skin**
The CAP evidence is from systemic inflammation models (LPS challenge, endotoxemia). Whether
vagal → splenic α7-nAChR → systemic NF-κB reduction translates to DERMAL NF-κB reduction
at the level of keratinocyte Loop 3/HERV-W is an extrapolation.
**Status:** Not killed. Systemic NF-κB suppression would reduce circulating cytokines (TNF-α,
IL-6) that sustain HERV-W transcription systemically. Local dermal NF-κB may require local
mechanisms (topical sulforaphane, colchicine penetration). Both systemic + local approaches
are additive.

**Kill B: Cold Exposure Does Not Measurably Increase HRV in T1DM Patients**
T1DM autonomic neuropathy (common in long-standing T1DM) reduces HRV baseline and may impair
the diving reflex / vagal activation response to cold.
**Status:** Not killed but important caveat. Autonomic neuropathy in T1DM is correlated with
poor glycemic control (HbA1c >8% chronically). If HbA1c is being optimized, autonomic neuropathy
progression is slowed. HRV measurement would identify whether WHM cold exposure actually
increases HRV in a specific patient — if it doesn't, autonomic neuropathy is present and CAP
activation via cold is limited.
