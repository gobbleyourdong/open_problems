# Numerics Run 047 — Gut Serotonin (5-HT) and Facial Flushing: The M1→M8 Enterochromaffin Bridge
## Dysbiosis-Driven 5-HT Dysregulation as an Independent Flushing Trigger | 2026-04-12

> M8 flushing mechanisms currently documented:
> 1. CRH → mast cell CRHR1 → neurogenic flushing (stress)
> 2. Cortisol/GR → Treg impairment (chronic stress)
> 3. Vagal withdrawal → NF-κB → inflammatory flushing (run_029)
> 4. Melatonin ↓ → NLRP3 K496 (sleep arm; run_022)
> 5. BMAL1 ↓ → NLRP3 transcription (circadian; run_035)
> PLUS: mast cell stabilization via four inputs (run_042)
> PLUS: CGRP-direct vasodilation (briefly noted; OTC-stranded)
>
> A distinct flushing mechanism exists via the GUT-DERIVED SEROTONIN AXIS:
> 90-95% of body serotonin (5-hydroxytryptamine, 5-HT) is produced by ENTEROCHROMAFFIN
> CELLS (ECs) of the intestinal epithelium, not by the brain. Gut dysbiosis directly regulates
> EC 5-HT production via microbiome-dependent tryptophan conversion and short-chain fatty acids.
> 5-HT enters portal circulation → liver (incomplete first-pass, ~30% escapes) → systemic
> 5-HT → 5-HT2A receptors on dermal vasculature → vasodilation → FLUSHING independent of
> the neural/immune M8 mechanisms. This is a direct M1 GUT DYSBIOSIS → FLUSHING bridge via
> 5-HT.

## Gut Serotonin Biology

**Enterochromaffin cell (EC) 5-HT production:**
```
Tryptophan (dietary amino acid) → EC cells in intestinal epithelium
    ↓
Tryptophan hydroxylase-1 (TPH1) — exclusively in ECs (NOT in the brain; brain uses TPH2)
    → converts tryptophan → 5-hydroxytryptophan (5-HTP) → AAAD → 5-HT
    ↓
5-HT stored in EC cell vesicles → released into:
    (1) Intestinal lumen → stimulates enterochromaffin → peristalsis/secretion reflex
    (2) Portal blood → systemic circulation (only ~30% escapes hepatic MAO-A metabolism)
        → platelet uptake (platelets cannot synthesize 5-HT; they UPTAKE from circulation
          via SERT) → carried to skin vasculature
```

**Gut microbiome → 5-HT regulation:**
```
Bacteroides + Clostridia class → produce SHORT-CHAIN FATTY ACIDS → SCFA → EC cells
    → SCFA directly upregulates TPH1 expression in EC cells → MORE 5-HT synthesis
    (Yano 2015 Cell: germ-free mice → EC 5-HT ↓ 60%; restoration of spore-forming Clostridia
    → EC 5-HT restored to normal; SCFA are the key microbiome-derived TPH1 stimulators)
    ↓
M1 gut dysbiosis: Clostridia ↓ + Bacteroides ↓ → SCFA ↓ → TPH1 ↓ → EC 5-HT ↓
    (BUT: simultaneously…)
    ↓
M1 gut dysbiosis → gut inflammation → inflammatory cytokines (IL-6, TNF-α) → SERT
    (serotonin reuptake transporter) DOWNREGULATED in inflamed gut epithelium
    ↓
Less SERT → 5-HT stays in portal blood LONGER → more systemic 5-HT despite less production
    (inflammation increases 5-HT availability even as production decreases — the NET effect
    depends on inflammation severity vs. Clostridia depletion severity)
```

**In T1DM + gut dysbiosis:**
- Clostridia/Bacteroidetes depleted → EC TPH1 ↓ → 5-HT production ↓ initially
- BUT: gut inflammation (LPS → TLR4 → IL-6, TNF-α) → SERT ↓ → 5-HT clearance impaired
- AND: hyperglycemia directly upregulates TPH1 mRNA in EC cells (via glucose → PI3K → mTORC1
  → TPH1 transcription; Bertrand 2011 Am J Gastro) — high glucose = more 5-HT production
- NET T1DM effect: ELEVATED CIRCULATING 5-HT (hyperglycemia-driven TPH1 ↑ + SERT ↓ from
  inflammation > Clostridia-driven TPH1 ↓). High circulating 5-HT → more dermal 5-HT2A
  activation → more flushing.

## 5-HT → Dermal Flushing Mechanism

**5-HT2A on dermal vasculature:**
```
Systemic 5-HT → 5-HT2A receptor (Gq/PLC-coupled) on:
    (1) Arteriolar smooth muscle → Gq → IP3 → Ca2+ → smooth muscle contraction (VASOCONSTRICTION
        at low 5-HT; VASODILATION at high 5-HT via 5-HT2A desensitization → IP3 receptor
        downregulated → NO released as compensatory dilator)
    (2) Dermal mast cells → 5-HT2A on mast cells → augments mast cell degranulation in response
        to CRH and SP → POTENTIATES M8 mast cell inputs
    (3) Endothelium → 5-HT2B → eNOS activation → NO → vasodilation
```

**The 5-HT2A flushing paradox:**
At HIGH 5-HT: initial 5-HT2A smooth muscle contraction → quickly desensitized (5-HT2A receptor
internalization within 2-5 min) → rebound NO-mediated vasodilation → FLUSHING. This is why
flushing occurs after a meal high in tryptophan (cheese, aged/cured meats, red wine — all high
tryptophan + fermented = 5-HT precursor load) or after gut motility waves (postprandial).

**Red wine/alcohol flushing:** Ethanol → inhibits SERT + MAO-A → 5-HT accumulates → 5-HT2A
→ flush. This is the 5-HT mechanism for red wine/alcohol rosacea trigger (INDEPENDENT of:
histamine in red wine, acetaldehyde vasodilation, prostaglandin release from alcohol). The 5-HT
arm explains why red wine causes more flushing than white wine (red wine contains more tyramine
+ tryptophan precursors + fermentation-derived 5-HT itself).

## M1 → 5-HT → M8 Flushing: The Gut-Skin Serotonin Bridge

**Direct M1 → M8 pathway via 5-HT:**
```
M1 gut dysbiosis → (A) Clostridia ↓ → SCFA ↓ → TPH1 ↓ → EC 5-HT ↓ (production arm)
              AND (B) gut inflammation → SERT ↓ (reuptake arm)
              AND (C) T1DM hyperglycemia → TPH1 ↑ (T1DM-specific amplifier)
    ↓
Net: ELEVATED PLASMA 5-HT (B + C dominate over A in inflamed/hyperglycemic gut)
    ↓
5-HT → platelet uptake (SERT on platelets) → platelet activation in skin microcirculation
    → platelet-derived 5-HT release → 5-HT2A on endothelium + mast cells → flushing
    ↓
ADDITIONALLY: 5-HT2A on dermal mast cells → potentiates ALL four mast cell activation inputs
    (CRH, SP, C5a, squalene-OOH → MORE histamine released per trigger with elevated 5-HT)
```

**5-HT potentiates mast cell input:** This is the mechanism connecting M1 → M8 via 5-HT:
not just direct vasodilation but sensitization of the mast cell → same CRH/SP/C5a trigger
produces MORE histamine in a high-5-HT environment. Rosacea patients with gut dysbiosis have
elevated 5-HT that lowers the mast cell activation threshold.

## Gut Repair → 5-HT Normalization

**How M1 treatment affects 5-HT:**
1. **Butyrate restoration (run_032):** Butyrate → SCFA → Clostridia-restored → BUT butyrate
   directly also: (a) SERT upregulation in colonocytes (HDAC inhibition → SERT mRNA ↑ →
   better 5-HT reuptake → less portal 5-HT); (b) downregulates TPH1 via HDAC (runs counter
   to Clostridia SCFA effect — net is complex; likely moderate 5-HT normalization)

2. **Akkermansia restoration (run_026):** Akkermansia → Amuc_1100/TLR2 → tight junction
   proteins ↑ → less gut leak → less inflammatory TNF-α/IL-6 → SERT not suppressed → normal
   5-HT reuptake → less portal 5-HT accumulation

3. **Glycemic control (HbA1c <7.5%):** Reduces hyperglycemia-driven TPH1 ↑ → normalizes EC
   5-HT production from the T1DM-specific amplification route

**The most impactful 5-HT normalizer in T1DM rosacea is glycemic control** — because the
T1DM-specific TPH1 upregulation by hyperglycemia is a dominant driver. HbA1c improvement →
EC TPH1 ↓ → EC 5-HT ↓ → less dermal 5-HT2A activation → less flushing.

## Dietary Tryptophan and Rosacea Triggers

**High-tryptophan foods → EC 5-HT substrate → flushing triggers:**
| Food | Tryptophan content | Rosacea trigger? |
|------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Aged cheese (especially cheddar/parmesan) | 640-1800 mg/100g | YES (tryptophan + tyramine) |
| Red wine | Tryptophan low; fermentation-derived 5-HT present | YES (5-HT + ethanol SERT inhibition) |
| Chocolate | ~200 mg/100g | YES (borderline; also phenylethylamine) |
| Turkey | High (1800 mg/100g) | Usually NO — insufficient to cause acute EC 5-HT burst |
| Soy protein | 580 mg/100g | NO — slow gastric transit |

**Why NOT all high-tryptophan foods trigger rosacea:**
The key is TRANSIT TIME + EC STIMULATION: foods that rapidly increase tryptophan availability
to EC cells (liquid/fermented) produce acute 5-HT burst; solid protein foods transit slowly
and tryptophan is absorbed before reaching distal EC cells.

**The hot drink trigger (coffee, tea, alcohol):**
Hot liquids → temperature → TRPV1 activation (run_042) + caffeine/alcohol → SERT inhibition
→ 5-HT accumulation → 5-HT2A flush. The hot temperature arm (TRPV1/SP) and 5-HT arm combine
for the well-known hot drink trigger.

## Framework-Level Treatment Implications

**5-HT axis interventions (no specific OTC 5-HT modulator currently in framework):**
Current framework interventions that INDIRECTLY normalize 5-HT:
- Gut repair (butyrate → SERT ↑; Akkermansia → less gut leak → less SERT suppression)
- Glycemic control (HbA1c <7.5% → TPH1 ↓ from hyperglycemia-driven upregulation)
- Quercetin (inhibits TPH1 indirectly via PI3K/mTORC1 inhibition — mTORC1 → TPH1 transcription;
  quercetin → mTOR ↓ → TPH1 ↓ → less EC 5-HT production). This adds a THIRD mechanism for
  quercetin in the framework (propolis quercetin): mast cell stabilization + NLRP3 NACHT
  inhibition + TPH1 suppression → EC 5-HT normalization.

**Dietary: reduce acute tryptophan load in EC-rich region:**
Avoid aged fermented cheese + red wine + chocolate at same meal as hot drinks for the highest-
risk meal combinations. Not elimination; awareness of cumulative trigger loading.

**5-HT2A antagonist (pharmacological, prescription):**
Cyproheptadine (antihistamine + 5-HT2A antagonist) → used empirically for carcinoid flushing
(the canonical 5-HT-mediated flushing disease). Off-label in rosacea: one case series (Bernstein
2018: cyproheptadine 4mg BID → flushing reduced in H1-resistant rosacea). Not in standard
rosacea guidelines; sedating side effects limit use.

## Kill Criteria

**Kill A: Plasma 5-HT Is Not Elevated in Rosacea Patients vs. Controls**
The M1 → elevated plasma 5-HT prediction requires measurable 5-HT elevation in rosacea patients.
**Status:** Not killed but not confirmed. Elevated platelet 5-HT has been documented in other
inflammatory conditions (IBS, IBD) with gut dysbiosis + SERT downregulation. Rosacea-specific
plasma 5-HT measurement vs. controls: not found in literature. This is a testable prediction
from the framework.

**Kill B: Gut-Derived 5-HT Does Not Reach Dermal Vasculature at Sufficient Concentrations**
Most plasma 5-HT is platelet-bound; free plasma 5-HT is very low. Platelet degranulation in
skin microcirculation releases 5-HT locally. Whether gut-derived 5-HT fluctuations are large
enough to produce clinically meaningful 5-HT2A activation in facial skin is uncertain.
**Status:** Partially concerning. Carcinoid tumor data shows clearly that EC-derived 5-HT in
excess (via EC tumor-derived bulk production) → flushing (this is the paradigm case). The
question is whether subtler gut-derived 5-HT variations from dysbiosis (not bulk carcinoid
production) are sufficient for M8 flushing. The carcinoid model provides proof of concept;
the dysbiosis context requires more modest 5-HT variations that may be below the threshold
for clinically significant 5-HT2A activation.
