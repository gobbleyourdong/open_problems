> "Kill ROI" = if this mountain's core prediction is falsified, how much does it constrain the remaining problem space?

## Scoring Method

**Kill ROI = (Constraint on other mountains if falsified) × (Probability testable with existing tools) × (Probability not already resolved)**

All factors 0-1, score 0-10 after scaling.

**Key question: which mountain, if its CORE PREDICTION is shown FALSE, most changes what we do next?**

## M1 — Gut dysbiosis → systemic inflammation (LPS / leaky gut)

**Core prediction to falsify:** Gut-derived LPS does NOT produce clinically significant systemic inflammatory tone. Systemic inflammation in dysbiosis-linked disease is from local sources, not gut translocation.

**How to falsify:** Selective gut barrier repair (without changing composition or local inflammation) does NOT reduce systemic CRP/cytokine levels. Animal: gnotobiotic model where LPS translocation is blocked but gut composition is identical.

**If falsified:**
- M2 (skin) operates independently — no gut contribution to skin disease
- M3 (virome) operates independently — no gut inflammatory priming
- M4 threshold question still stands (but gut contribution drops out)
- M5 (diet→substrate) still matters for SKIN substrate, less for systemic pathway
- Treatment implication: gut-targeted interventions (probiotics, FMT) have no systemic anti-inflammatory value

**If confirmed:**
- Gut health is load-bearing for ALL site-specific dysbiosis
- Gut intervention is the highest-leverage single point
- FMT / prebiotic / dietary fiber = systemic anti-inflammatory tools

**Kill ROI score: 7/10**
- High constraint: changes treatment priorities for M2, M3 if gut pathway proven/disproven
- Moderate testability: gnotobiotic models + selective barrier repair compounds (e.g., butyrate, Akkermansia-mediated mucin restoration)
- NOT fully resolved: LPS mechanism known but systemic clinical significance contested

## M2 — Skin dysbiosis → local inflammatory dermatoses

**Core prediction to falsify:** Antifungal/antiparasitic monotherapy FAILS to resolve seb derm / rosacea. Microbial density is not causally related to disease.

**Status: This prediction is ALREADY FALSIFIED in reverse** — monotherapy SUCCEEDS (~75-80% response rates). So M2 is confirmed, not a priority kill target.

**If the reverse were somehow found (monotherapy fails):** Would imply host-only disease, no microbial component. Extremely unlikely given the treatment response data.

**More useful kill test for M2:** Why does ~20-25% of patients NOT respond to monotherapy? What predicts non-response? This is the M4 overlap.

**Kill ROI score: 3/10**
- Low: M2 core is already validated. Not a productive kill target.
- The 20-25% non-responders are the interesting frontier — that's an M4 question.

## M3 — Virome persistence drives chronic autoimmune/inflammatory disease

**Core prediction to falsify (T1DM specific):** CVB variants are NOT detectable at higher rates in T1DM islets than controls, OR detection is artifact of sequencing method, OR removal of CVB does not reduce islet inflammation.

**How to falsify:** Large-scale virome-enriched sequencing of T1DM vs healthy islet biopsies with rigorous controls (n≥100, matched). If CVB detection rates are equal → M3 weakens significantly.

**Core prediction to falsify (MS specific):** Despite EBV seroconversion, MS risk is not different in EBV+ vs EBV- cohorts after controlling for confounders.

**If M3 falsified (CVB-T1DM link):**
- T1DM etiology shifts entirely to gut dysbiosis (M1) + genetic susceptibility
- User's antiviral component of CVB protocol loses mechanistic rationale
- Autoimmune disease → no viral component

**If M3 confirmed + mechanism found:**
- Antiviral therapy becomes first-line T1DM prevention
- Virome profiling becomes a clinical diagnostic priority
- Massive treatment implications

**Kill ROI score: 8/10**
- High: T1DM and MS are major disease burdens; viral causation completely changes treatment paradigm
- Moderate testability: EBV-MS evidence is already very strong (P3.2); CVB-T1DM needs larger studies with better methods
- Partially resolved: EBV-MS 3/3, CVB-T1DM ~2/3

## M4 — Host-microbe threshold (tolerance problem)

**Core prediction to falsify:** ALL hosts with density-X of Malassezia/Demodex get disease — there are no true tolerant carriers. High-density non-diseased individuals simply haven't been exposed long enough or measured precisely enough.

**How to falsify:** Longitudinal study following high-density asymptomatic carriers for 10 years. If all eventually develop disease → threshold may just be time-exposure, not individual tolerance. If persistent asymptomatic carriers exist indefinitely → threshold is real.

**If M4 falsified (threshold is not real):**
- Disease is purely a function of microbial density and exposure time
- Treatment = reduce density below disease threshold in ALL hosts
- Host-targeted interventions (NLRP3 block, NF-κB suppression) have no disease-modifying effect
- M1/M2/M3 are sufficient explanatory models

**If M4 confirmed (threshold is real + mechanistically explained):**
- Biomarker design (NLRP3 priming, cathelicidin processing, Treg frequency) becomes the priority
- Host-targeted therapy is disease-modifying even WITHOUT microbiome change
- "Threshold-raising" becomes a therapeutic target class
- Explains the 20-25% antifungal/antiparasitic non-responders

**Kill ROI score: 10/10**
- MAXIMUM: if threshold is real, it integrates all mountains. If not, enormously simplifies the problem.
- Currently untestable at scale (no biomarker for threshold state)
- Entirely unresolved — no study has directly tested this

**THE WALL IS HERE.**

## M5 — Modern diet alters microbial substrate

**Core prediction to falsify:** High glycemic diet does NOT change sebum composition or gut microbial community in humans in ways that promote pathogenic overgrowth. Kitavan data is explained by genetic differences, not diet.

**How to falsify:** Dietary crossover RCT (Western → traditional diet, n≥50, 12 weeks) with sebum composition measurement + skin microbiome + gut microbiome + clinical disease outcomes. If composition doesn't shift → M5 weakens.

**If M5 falsified:**
- Dietary interventions are off the table as mechanistic tools
- Treatment must be pharmacological (microbial/host-targeted)
- Kitavan data = genetic selection, not environmental

**If M5 confirmed at individual level:**
- Dietary prescription becomes precision intervention
- IGF-1 pathway drugs (metformin, mTOR inhibitors) become dysbiosis treatments
- Prevention via diet achievable without pharmaceuticals

**Kill ROI score: 6/10**
- Moderate: already epidemiologically validated at population level; individual-level mechanistic proof would upgrade interventions
- Testable: dietary crossover RCTs feasible (but require compliance + multimodal measurement)
- Partially resolved: P5.1, P5.2, P5.6 all strong; individual-level causation weaker

## M6 — Early-life microbiome assembly

**Core prediction to falsify:** C-section delivery + formula feeding do NOT produce persistent microbiome deficits at age 10. Microbiome self-corrects with environmental exposure by age 3.

**If M6 falsified:**
- Early-life obstetric/feeding interventions have no long-term microbiome value
- Adult interventions are sufficient for correction
- Pediatric prevention strategies lose priority

**If M6 confirmed + window precisely defined:**
- FMT/microbiome intervention in first 3 years becomes prevention standard
- C-section protocols change (seeding, microbiome rescue)
- Antibiotic stewardship in pediatrics becomes a public health priority

**Kill ROI score: 5/10**
- Moderate: intervention window already closed for adults; relevant for prevention strategy, not current patients
- Well-studied but mechanistic confirmation (which early exposures matter most?) would change pediatric protocols
- Long time horizon for falsification (need to follow cohorts 20-40 years)

## Dead Ends (what NOT to spend numerical time on)

1. **Re-proving Malassezia/Demodex causation** — it's done. Monotherapy works. Move on.
2. **Accumulating more Kitavan-style epidemiological data** — sufficient to validate M5. More of the same doesn't help.
3. **Correlation studies of gut microbiome composition** — field is saturated. 10,000+ papers. More 16S genus-level correlation adds nothing.
4. **Zonulin as gut permeability marker** — marker is contested. Don't build on it without validation via L:M ratio or independent method.
5. **Probiotics for systemic inflammation** — effect sizes consistently small and heterogeneous. Low ROI to study more of the same.
