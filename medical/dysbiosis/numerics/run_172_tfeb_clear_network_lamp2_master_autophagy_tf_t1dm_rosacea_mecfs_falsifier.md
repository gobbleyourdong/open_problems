## Central Mechanism

```
mTORC1 active (nutrient-rich, anabolic state)
    ↓
TFEB Ser211 phosphorylated by mTORC1 (lysosomal-membrane-tethered
    mTORC1, requires Rag GTPases + Ragulator)
    ↓
Phospho-Ser211 → 14-3-3 binding → TFEB cytoplasmic sequestration
    ↓
NO TFEB nuclear activity → CLEAR network silenced → low baseline
    autophagy/lysosomal gene expression

----- STARVATION / mTORC1 INHIBITION / LYSOSOMAL STRESS -----

mTORC1 inactivated (FMD per run_021, rapamycin per run_158, BHB
    per Youm 2015 PMID 25686106 → indirect, AMPK→TSC→mTOR-suppression
    per run_069)
    ↓
TFEB Ser211 dephosphorylated by calcineurin (Ca²⁺-activated;
    lysosomal Ca²⁺ release triggers this — closes feedback loop)
    ↓
14-3-3 dissociates → TFEB free to nuclear-translocate
    ↓
TFEB binds CLEAR motif (10-bp GTCACGTGAC) in promoters of ~500
    lysosomal/autophagy genes:
    → LAMP1, LAMP2 (lysosomal membrane proteins; **LAMP2 IS THE
        FRAMEWORK FALSIFIER per gap.md 2026-04-18**)
    → Cathepsins (B, D, F, S, K, etc.) — lysosomal proteases
    → MCOLN1 (lysosomal Ca²⁺ channel) — feedback
    → ATG genes (5, 7, 9b, 16L1) — autophagosome formation
    → Bcl2 (anti-apoptotic) — TFEB also pro-survival
    → MITF/TFE3 — paralogues; TFEB cross-regulates the family
    ↓
Autophagosome formation ↑ + lysosomal biogenesis ↑ + lysosome-
    autophagosome fusion ↑ + cargo degradation ↑
    ↓
Mitophagy: damaged mitochondria cleared (PINK1/Parkin per run_090
    SIRT3 mitochondrial; required substrate: functional lysosomes
    delivered by TFEB-driven biogenesis)
    ↓
Cellular stress relieved → mtISR signal reduces → ATF4/CHOP/GDF15
    pathway (run_146 + run_171) downregulates
    ↓
Cell returns to homeostasis OR (if stress is chronic) maintains
    sustained TFEB activation as adaptive program
```

**Why TFEB is the framework-integration node**:

The dysbiosis cross-disease integration framework (gap.md) has
identified ~13 dysbiosis-linked diseases sharing common substrate
(NLRP3, NF-κB, mtISR, autophagy deficits). TFEB is the **single
transcriptional master switch** that, when activated, simultaneously
addresses:
- Lysosomal capacity deficit (LAMP2 ↑)
- Autophagy flux deficit (ATG genes ↑)
- Mitophagy deficit (lysosomal capacity required)
- Damage-clearance deficit (cathepsin ↑ for protein aggregates)
- Cellular survival under stress (Bcl-2 ↑)

A protocol intervention that drives TFEB nuclear translocation hits
ALL of these simultaneously. This is the molecular substrate for
"Convention Beats Instruction" applied to therapeutics: rather than
multi-target druggable interventions, target the master coordinator.

**Run_172 supplies the molecular mechanism for this prediction**:

1. Full-protocol engagement reduces inflammatory load (NLRP3 block
   via BHB run_037, NF-κB block via multiple, oral microbiome shift,

   → mTORC1-mediated TFEB Ser211 phosphorylation reduces

   protein up over weeks
5. Measurement window of 3-6 months matches the lag of transcriptional
   adaptation + protein accumulation + PBMC sampling

**Sharper falsifier per CITATION_DISCIPLINE Phase 4b standard**:

If LAMP2 rises at 3-6 months, the next prediction layer is that it
should be CO-ELEVATED with other CLEAR network targets (cathepsin D,
MCOLN1, ATG7) in the same PBMC samples. A LAMP2 rise WITHOUT
co-elevated CLEAR network siblings would falsify the TFEB-mediated
explanation (rules out direct LAMP2-only induction by alternative
pathways).

Additional measurable: nuclear TFEB protein in PBMC nuclei (IHC or
nuclear/cytoplasmic fractionation) should rise BEFORE the LAMP2
mRNA rise, by ~1-2 weeks. This is a more direct, earlier-detection
falsifier than waiting on LAMP2 protein.

## Kill Challenges + Defenses

### Kill A: TFEB activation also activates apoptosis pathways — net effect on β-cell viability is unclear

Some literature (TFEB Beyond Autophagy review) shows TFEB can drive
autophagic cell death in tumor contexts via CASP8/CASP3 induction.

**Defense**: tissue context matters. In β cells specifically,
Settembre et al. and downstream literature show TFEB activation is
PROTECTIVE under stress (hypoxia, STZ, ER stress) — apoptotic effects
are context-dependent on baseline autophagic flux + cell type. The
β-cell-specific Tfeb-KO mouse phenotype (increased STZ susceptibility)
disambiguates: TFEB net effect in β cells is protective, not
death-promoting. The dual role is a calibrated response — same TF,
different outputs depending on cellular state. Cancer-cell autophagic
death contexts use chronic-supraphysiologic TFEB activation, not the
stress-adaptive levels predicted by protocol intervention.
**Status**: Not killed for β cells. Important caveat for any future
direct TFEB-agonist therapy: dose + duration calibration needed to
stay in protective regime.

### Kill B: PBMC TFEB activity does not represent tissue-resident β-cell or keratinocyte TFEB

PBMCs are accessible (the framework's measurement substrate per
LAMP2 falsifier) but are NOT the disease-target tissue. PBMC TFEB
might rise without corresponding tissue rise.

**Defense**: this is a known measurement-substrate limitation, not
a mechanism kill. PBMC LAMP2 is used as a SURROGATE for systemic
mTORC1/TFEB tone — the prediction is that systemic protocol effects
(reduced systemic inflammatory cytokines: IL-6, TNF-α, IL-1β) will
shift the systemic mTORC1/TFEB balance, detectable in any reporter
tissue including PBMCs. The biology supports the surrogate: systemic
inflammatory load is a system-level driver of mTORC1 activity, and
any cell type will experience reduced mTORC1 phosphorylation of
TFEB-Ser211 under reduced systemic inflammation. Tissue-specific
local effects (β-cell intrinsic IL-1β/IFN-γ) may add additional
local TFEB regulation, but the SYSTEMIC component should be
detectable in PBMCs. The framework's PBMC measurement choice is
specifically because LAMP2 is in the universal CLEAR target set.
**Status**: Not killed. The PBMC surrogate is a methodological choice
with biological grounding, not a mechanism-claim of identity.

  exposure. **First author: Pasquier A, NOT Pastore N — corrected
