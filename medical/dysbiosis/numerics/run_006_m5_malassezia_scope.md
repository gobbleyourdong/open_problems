## What the Kitavan Data Actually Measured (Scope Correction)

**Cordain 2002 (Arch Dermatol 138:1584):** Targeted acne survey ONLY.
Conditions measured: acne papulopustulosa, acne comedonica, acne conglobata.
Conditions NOT measured: seborrheic dermatitis, dandruff, pityriasis versicolor, or ANY other skin condition.

**Lindeberg 1994 (J Intern Med):** Cardiovascular endpoints, metabolic markers, anthropometrics, insulin/leptin. Zero dermatology.

**Consequence for M5:**
The "Kitavan zero-disease" claim is specifically zero ACNE. The broader claim that traditional diets produce zero Malassezia-driven skin disease has NO published epidemiological support — positive OR negative. The data gap is total.

---

## M5 Mechanistic Chain Extended to Seb Derm (NEW Evidence)

The M5 mechanistic chain (diet → IGF-1 → SREBP-1c → sebum composition shift → Malassezia substrate) was established for acne. It now extends to seborrheic dermatitis specifically:

**IGF-1 in seb derm patients (Kenawy & Mikhael 2021, ResearchGate):**
- N=50 seb derm patients vs N=30 controls
- IGF-1 significantly elevated in seb derm (p=0.045)
- Higher in SEVERE seb derm vs mild (p=0.005)
- Dose-response relationship: IGF-1 level correlates with disease severity

**Western diet + seb derm (PMC10719884, 2023 cross-sectional):**
- Western dietary pattern: increased seb derm risk
- Fruit intake: protective against seb derm
- This is the same dietary pattern → insulin/IGF-1 chain, applied to seb derm

**JMIR Dermatology 2024 systematic review on nutrition + seb derm:**
States the M5 mechanistic path explicitly: high-GI diet → insulin/IGF-1 → SREBP-1c → sebum C18:1 shift → Malassezia substrate.
This means M5 is now PUBLISHED as a unified mechanism across acne AND seb derm.

## Pityriasis Versicolor — Climate Confounding Problem

PV prevalence by climate:
- Temperate regions: ~1%
- Humid tropical regions: up to 40%

Kitava IS tropical and humid → if substrate alone drove PV, it would be expected high there regardless of diet.
This makes PV a WEAK prediction for M5 — climate is a major confounder that diet studies cannot easily separate.

**Assessment:** PV should be REMOVED from the M5 prediction set as a test case for diet-substrate connection. The signal-to-noise ratio from climate confounding is too high.

## IGF-1 as Pharmacological Target — Updated Assessment

Theory phase (attempt_005) asked: "Is there a threshold insulin/IGF-1 level below which sebum composition normalizes?"

**Best evidence from seb derm data:**
- Kenawy 2021: dose-response relationship between IGF-1 level and seb derm severity
- IGF-1 in mild seb derm vs severe seb derm are DIFFERENT (both elevated vs controls)
- This suggests: the DEGREE of IGF-1 elevation correlates with disease severity — consistent with a threshold below which disease doesn't manifest

**What exists as IGF-1 modulation evidence:**
- Metformin: reduces IGF-1 signaling via mTOR pathway; published case reports of acne improvement in PCOS patients using metformin
- Low-GI diet (Smith 2007): reduced sebum secretion rate in acne patients — effect size ~50% vs 30% placebo
- Dairy elimination: removes exogenous IGF-1; clinical effect on acne moderate

**The threshold question remains OPEN.** No study has measured:
- What absolute IGF-1 level (in ng/mL) correlates with the onset of sebum composition shift
- Whether reducing IGF-1 from elevated to normal normalizes sebum C18:1/C18:2 ratio

This is a gap for a small N=10 metabolic ward study: dietary crossover, measure IGF-1 kinetics + sebum fatty acid composition simultaneously.

## M5 Kill Test Revision

**Previous kill criterion:** Individual-level dietary intervention (8-week low-GI + dairy elimination) does NOT reduce sebum output OR skin Malassezia density OR gut butyrate producers.

**Revised — add seb derm specific arm:**
The kill test now has a new positive control: IGF-1 measurement alongside sebum fatty acid composition.
If dietary intervention → IGF-1 reduction → NO change in sebum C18:1/C18:2 ratio → M5 mechanistic chain is wrong.
If dietary intervention → IGF-1 reduction → C18:1 DECREASES, C18:2 INCREASES → M5 mechanistic chain confirmed at individual level.

This would be a metabolic study (3 measurements: serum IGF-1, sebum fatty acid profile by GC-MS, skin Malassezia density by quantitative PCR), dietary crossover design, N=20 would be informative.
