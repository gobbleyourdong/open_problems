# Dysbiosis — Anti-Problem

## The Anti-Problem Question

**What interventions would make dysbiosis-linked disease WORSE?** Per sigma Phase 4, identifying the load-bearing structures (things that MUST be true for the current system to work) via their inversion is a powerful check. Things that worsen dysbiosis are the things whose absence makes health possible.

This document catalogs iatrogenic and lifestyle patterns known or strongly suspected to worsen dysbiosis, organized by mechanism. Acting on these (avoiding them) is often higher ROI than adding interventions.

## Category 1 — Antibiotic Overuse

### Broad-spectrum antibiotic courses in first 2 years of life
- Evidence: IBD risk up ~3× in cohorts with early antibiotic exposure (Shaw 2011 Am J Gastroenterol)
- Asthma risk up ~1.5× per course (Marra 2009 Pediatrics)
- T1DM risk ~1.3× per course (Mikkelsen 2017)
- Mechanism: ablation of microbiome diversity during the developmental window → permanent community structure deficits
- Avoidance: use antibiotics only when clearly indicated, narrow-spectrum when possible, document exposure for pediatric records

### Broad-spectrum antibiotics in adults (repeated)
- Evidence: each course reduces bacterial diversity, with incomplete recovery over months
- C. difficile risk rises exponentially with cumulative exposure
- Clostridium scindens and butyrate producers particularly sensitive
- Avoidance: narrow-spectrum when possible, probiotic rescue (Saccharomyces boulardii) during course, probiotic recolonization (high-dose multi-strain) after

### Topical antibiotics used indiscriminately
- Mupirocin for minor skin irritation selects for resistant Staph
- Neomycin in triple-antibiotic ointment is a common contact allergen AND selects against beneficial skin flora
- Fluoroquinolones (eye drops, ear drops) systemic absorption + broad spectrum = disproportionate effect

## Category 2 — Diet Patterns That Feed Pathogenic Overgrowth

### High refined-sugar / high-glycemic diet
- Drives insulin/IGF-1 chronically elevated
- Increases sebum production + alters fatty acid composition → Malassezia, Demodex food supply
- Feeds Candida and other sugar-dependent fungi
- Promotes bacterial species that thrive on simple sugars (Clostridia subtypes, enterococci)
- Suppresses fiber-fermenting butyrate producers

### Dairy (especially skim)
- Elevates IGF-1 independently of glycemic load
- Adds exogenous bovine growth factors
- Case-control evidence linking to acne severity
- Whey > casein for IGF-1 effect; organic vs conventional not protective

### Seed oils / high omega-6 PUFAs
- Arachidonic acid substrate → pro-inflammatory prostaglandins/leukotrienes
- Shifts omega-6:omega-3 ratio away from resolvin production
- Specific skin: rosacea flares documented with high linoleic acid intake
- Evidence mixed for direct causal role, clear role in amplifying other triggers

### Alcohol (especially high-glycemic: beer, sweet wine, cocktails)
- Direct gut barrier disruption (tight junction degradation)
- Selects for pro-inflammatory gut microbes
- Documented rosacea trigger
- Disrupts hepatic metabolism of hormonal regulators

### Emulsifiers and food additives (carrageenan, polysorbate-80, CMC)
- Murine evidence: disrupt mucus layer, promote colitis
- Human evidence emerging: Chassaing 2022 showed CMC shifts gut microbiome in short-term feeding trial
- Ubiquitous in ultra-processed foods

## Category 3 — Topical / Personal Care Products

### Heavy occlusive oils on sebaceous zones
- User's olive-oil-on-eyebrows case study: fed Malassezia → seb derm flare, fed meibomian glands → chalazion
- Comedogenic oils (coconut oil, olive oil, wheat germ oil) on face
- Lanolin on already-inflamed skin
- Pattern: oils that are fine on dry body skin are inflammatory when applied over sebaceous zones

### Products with common Demodex/Malassezia food
- Fatty acid esters, oleic acid, cetearyl alcohol — all common moisturizer ingredients that selectively feed Malassezia
- "Malassezia-free" formulations (Cerave's Malassezia-approved line, La Roche-Posay) specifically avoid these

### Topical corticosteroids on facial / mucosal skin long-term
- Rebound dermatitis (the POD mechanism)
- Local immune suppression → Demodex + Staph overgrowth
- Skin atrophy + telangiectasia → permanent scarring
- Even "safe" mild-potency (hydrocortisone 1%) produces problems over months

### Antimicrobial soaps (triclosan-based)
- Select for resistant skin flora
- Triclosan banned in US hand soaps 2016 but persists in some products
- Reduce beneficial commensals along with pathogens

### Alcohol-heavy lid scrubs / toners
- Disrupt skin barrier
- Force oil production upregulation as compensation
- Long-term worsen the dysbiosis they aim to treat

### Sodium lauryl sulfate (SLS) in toothpaste
- Documented perioral dermatitis trigger (especially children)
- Disrupts oral mucin layer
- Switching to SLS-free resolves 30%+ of refractory POD

## Category 4 — Lifestyle Amplifiers

### Chronic sleep deprivation
- Elevates cortisol baseline → insulin resistance + sebum production
- Impairs immune regulation (Treg function depends on sleep)
- Skin barrier repair happens during deep sleep phases

### Chronic psychological stress
- Sympathetic activation → skin vasodilation (rosacea triggers)
- Cortisol elevation → immune dysregulation
- Gut motility changes → altered microbial community structure

### Sedentary lifestyle
- Reduces gut motility → altered transit time → different community selection
- Lower body temperature variability → reduced immune stimulation
- Sweating reduction → reduced skin commensal regulation

### Indoor-only environment
- Vitamin D deficiency → cathelicidin dysregulation, reduced Treg support
- Reduced microbial diversity exposure → under-educated immune system
- Low UV → no natural skin antimicrobial effect

### Over-sanitization
- Hand sanitizer overuse → skin microbiome depletion
- Antibacterial wipes on everything
- Children raised in overly clean environments: higher allergic disease rates (hygiene hypothesis)
- NOT advocating dirt — advocating appropriate microbial exposure (pets, outdoors, fermented foods, human contact)

## Category 5 — Iatrogenic Patterns in Medical Care

### Unnecessary PPI (proton pump inhibitor) use
- Gastric acid is the first-line microbial filter
- PPI use → altered gut microbiome + SIBO + C. difficile risk
- ~25% of US adults on chronic PPI; many could taper
- Rebound acid on discontinuation perpetuates use

### Long-term oral contraceptive use
- Alters gut microbiome in animal + human studies
- Estrogen metabolism loop involves gut bacteria (β-glucuronidase producers)
- Not advocating avoidance — advocating awareness + probiotic support

### Over-treatment of Helicobacter pylori
- H. pylori may be protective against asthma, esophageal cancer, eosinophilic esophagitis
- Eradication benefit must be weighed against loss of this ancient commensal
- Not all H. pylori infection needs treatment

### Early introduction of formula + late introduction of diverse solids
- Formula microbiome differs persistently from breastfed infants
- Delaying allergenic foods (peanut, egg) past 6-12 months INCREASES allergy risk (LEAP trial reversed decades of guidance)
- Current best practice: early diverse exposure, breastfeed if possible

### Cesarean delivery without vaginal seeding
- C-section babies have distinct microbiome (more skin flora, less gut flora) persisting for months-years
- Vaginal seeding (swab + transfer) is controversial but being studied
- Relevant if the user's children were born via C-section (risk factor for son's POD)

## The Load-Bearing Insight

Inverting the anti-problem list:

**The load-bearing structures for health are:**
1. An intact and diverse early-life microbiome assembly
2. An unperturbed mucosal barrier (gut, skin, oral)
3. A diet that doesn't chronically elevate insulin/IGF-1
4. Minimal exposure to broad-spectrum antimicrobials
5. Appropriate (not excessive) hygiene
6. Vitamin D sufficiency (sun, supplement, or both)
7. Sleep
8. Immune tolerance calibration (Treg-favoring signals: fiber, probiotics, adequate nutrition, not chronic stress)

These are the preconditions. Adding interventions (supplements, treatments) without removing the amplifiers is lower ROI than removing amplifiers first.

## Sigma Method Note

The anti-problem exercise here is particularly useful for dysbiosis because:

- The "do X to fix it" answers are mostly unknown (mechanism unclear)
- The "don't do Y or you make it worse" answers are mostly known (iatrogenic harm well-documented)
- Acting on what we know (anti-problem) is higher ROI than guessing at what we don't (the wall)

This is a behavioral wall OVERLAY on the mechanistic wall: even if we can't yet mechanistically close the main wall, we can eliminate amplifiers with high confidence. The user's protocol already implements most of Category 6; the scaffolding for future mountain attempts should reference the anti-problem list as the baseline to preserve.

## Post-Phase 3 Additions — 2026-04-11

### New Iatrogenic Pattern: Dupilumab (IL-4/IL-13 blocker) → Rosacea Flares

Dupilumab suppresses Th2 signaling (IL-4/IL-13). This releases the Th17 arm from Th2 counterbalance.
In patients with latent Demodex-driven rosacea risk, Th17 release → B. oleronius → IL-23 → Th17 loop engages.
Published: dupilumab causes rosacea-like flares in atopic dermatitis patients; this is documented as a class effect.

**Clinical risk:** T1DM + atopic derm + rosacea triad. If dupilumab is initiated for atopic derm,
monitor for rosacea activation. If rosacea develops: don't discontinue dupilumab — add ivermectin +
azelaic acid to address the Th17/Demodex loop that was unmasked, not caused, by dupilumab.

### New Iatrogenic Pattern: Periodontal Neglect as CVB Susceptibility Amplifier

P. gingivalis oral dysbiosis (active periodontal disease) → translocation to pancreatic islets (PMC7305306)
→ CAR (CVB receptor) upregulation on beta cells (PMC5129002) → easier CVB entry → more persistent infection.

**Clinical risk:** In T1DM patients on a CVB-targeting protocol, untreated periodontitis actively
INCREASES CVB susceptibility at the receptor level. Periodontal neglect undermines the antiviral protocol.
Chlorhexidine 0.12% rinse + professional scaling is not optional adjunctive care — it is a
PRECONDITION for reducing CVB susceptibility (if P. gingivalis seropositive).

### New Iatrogenic Pattern: Itraconazole CYP3A4 Interactions

If itraconazole is used as an OSBP inhibitor for CVB (antiviral application), it is a potent CYP3A4
inhibitor. Drug interactions include:
- Statins (simvastatin, lovastatin) → rhabdomyolysis risk; avoid combination
- Calcium channel blockers → elevated plasma levels
- Immunosuppressants (cyclosporine, tacrolimus) → elevated levels
- Warfarin → elevated INR

**Clinical risk:** Itraconazole should not be initiated without a full medication interaction review.
Standard antifungal dosing (200mg BID) achieves plasma concentrations within CVB antiviral EC50 range,
but any concurrent CYP3A4 substrate requires pharmacist or physician review.

**CRITICAL ADDITION (from ../DRUG_SAFETY_MATRIX.md):**
- Colchicine (NLRP3 microtubule blocker) → itraconazole raises colchicine levels 2-3× via CYP3A4 inhibition → **NARROW THERAPEUTIC INDEX → DOCUMENTED FATALITIES** in literature. NEVER use itraconazole + colchicine together at standard doses.
- Heart failure → itraconazole is a negative inotrope, CONTRAINDICATED in HF. If T1DM has progressed to CVB-related DCM (dilated cardiomyopathy), itraconazole cannot be used. Baseline echocardiogram recommended before initiating.
- **Preferred alternative:** Fluoxetine 20-60mg QD (CVB 2C ATPase inhibitor) is the primary antiviral in the broader CVB campaign — no cardiac contraindication, no colchicine interaction, ~$4-8/month generic. Should be considered FIRST-CHOICE over itraconazole for CVB suppression.

### New Iatrogenic Pattern: IL-17 Blockers in IBD Patients

IL-17 blockers (secukinumab, ixekizumab) approved for psoriasis/rosacea ARE CONTRAINDICATED in IBD.
They worsen IBD via Th17 depletion in the gut (different from skin — Th17 is protective in gut barrier).
In T1DM + IBD + rosacea patients, IL-17 blockade treats rosacea but worsens gut disease.
Anti-IL-23 (risankizumab, guselkumab) is the correct agent for the combination — it's approved
for both IBD AND psoriasis.
