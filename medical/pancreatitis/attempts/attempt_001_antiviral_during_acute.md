# Attempt 001: Antiviral During Acute CVB Pancreatitis to Prevent T1DM

## Approach
Add fluoxetine to standard supportive care during acute viral pancreatitis. The goal is NOT to treat the pancreatitis (it's self-limiting) — the goal is to prevent CVB persistence in islets and thereby prevent downstream T1DM.

## The Hypothesis
```
Acute CVB pancreatitis → CVB infects acinar cells AND islet cells simultaneously →
Acinar infection resolves (self-limiting) → Islet infection persists (TD mutants) →
Years later: T1DM

INTERVENTION: fluoxetine during acute phase → clears CVB from islets before
TD mutants establish → T1DM prevented
```

## The Protocol

### Standard care (both arms)
- IV fluids, bowel rest, pain management
- NPO until pain resolves, then gradual diet advancement

### Intervention arm (add)
- Fluoxetine 20mg daily × 3 months (starting during acute admission)
- Rationale: 3 months covers the window where TD mutant establishment occurs
- Butyrate 300mg TID × 3 months (gut barrier support during pancreatic recovery)

### Monitoring (both arms)
- Islet autoantibodies at baseline, 3, 6, 12, 24 months:
  - GAD65, IA-2, ZnT8, insulin autoantibodies (IAA)
- Fasting glucose + HbA1c at same intervals
- CVB serology (VP1, neutralizing antibodies by serotype)
- Stool enteroviral RNA at baseline and 3 months

### Primary endpoint
- Seroconversion to islet autoantibody positivity at 24 months

### Secondary endpoints
- Number of autoantibodies (risk stratification: 2+ autoantibodies = Stage 1 T1DM)
- Fasting glucose trajectory
- Time to autoantibody appearance

## The Problem
**Patient selection**: How do you know the pancreatitis is CVB-caused?
- Test for CVB: stool enteroviral RNA, serum CVB IgM, throat swab PCR
- Clinical clues: summer/fall, young patient, concurrent symptoms (pleurodynia, rash)
- Exclude: gallstone, alcohol, drug-induced, hypertriglyceridemia

## Prediction
- If CVB pancreatitis truly seeds islet infection → ~5-15% of CVB pancreatitis patients develop islet autoantibodies within 2 years (untreated)
- If fluoxetine clears CVB during acute phase → <2% seroconversion (intervention arm)
- Long-term: this becomes a T1DM prevention trial disguised as a pancreatitis add-on study

## Limitation
- Very long follow-up needed (autoantibody → clinical T1DM takes years)
- Small effect size requires large sample OR enriched population (HLA-DR3/DR4 carriers)
- Pancreatitis patients rarely get CVB testing → enrollment requires protocol change in ED

## Status: THEORETICAL — high-impact prevention concept, challenging trial design
