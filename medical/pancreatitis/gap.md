# Viral Pancreatitis — Gap Analysis (Initial)

## The Relationship to T1DM

Pancreatitis and T1DM are **the same organ, different cell types**:
- Pancreatitis: CVB destroys exocrine pancreas (acinar cells → digestive enzyme release → autodigestion)
- T1DM: CVB destroys endocrine pancreas (beta cells → insulin loss → hyperglycemia)

They can co-occur. Acute pancreatitis may be the **initial seeding event** that establishes CVB persistence in islets → later T1DM.

## The Gap

### For acute viral pancreatitis: no gap
Acute pancreatitis is managed supportively (fluids, pain control, bowel rest). Most cases resolve. The gap is **recognizing the viral etiology** — CVB is rarely tested in pancreatitis workups because treatment is the same regardless of cause.

### For the T1DM connection: the gap is timing
If acute CVB pancreatitis seeds persistent islet infection, then:
1. Diagnosing CVB pancreatitis EARLY
2. Treating with antivirals DURING the acute phase (before TD mutants establish)
3. Could PREVENT the downstream T1DM cascade entirely

**This is a prevention gap, not a treatment gap.**

## The Anti-Problem
"Do patients who had documented CVB pancreatitis have higher rates of later T1DM?"

If yes: pancreatitis is the sentinel event, and antiviral treatment during acute pancreatitis is T1DM prevention.
If no: the seeding happens silently (subclinical infection), and pancreatitis is a parallel manifestation, not a precursor.

## Protocol Transfer
- Acute phase: supportive care (standard) + fluoxetine 20mg (antiviral, novel)
- Post-acute: full T1DM protocol to prevent persistent infection
- Monitoring: islet autoantibodies (GAD65, IA-2, ZnT8) at 3, 6, 12 months post-pancreatitis
- If autoantibodies appear: aggressive immune modulation (the T1DM protocol IS this)

## Convergence
Pancreatitis is the **earliest intervention point** in the T1DM timeline. Catch CVB here, clear it, and T1DM may never develop.
