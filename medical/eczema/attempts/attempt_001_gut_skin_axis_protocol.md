# Attempt 001: Gut-Skin Axis Protocol for Atopic Dermatitis

## Approach
Target the gut-skin axis — the upstream driver of Th2 skewing in atopic dermatitis. Restore gut microbiome → increase butyrate → induce Tregs → suppress Th2 → reduce eczema.

## The Mechanism

```
HEALTHY GUT:
  Diverse microbiome → high butyrate → FOXP3+ Tregs → Th1/Th2 balance → no eczema

ECZEMA GUT:
  Dysbiosis (low Faecalibacterium, Roseburia) → low butyrate → fewer Tregs →
  Th2 dominant → IL-4/IL-13 → suppress filaggrin → barrier defect → eczema

THE FIX:
  Restore butyrate → restore Tregs → restore Th1/Th2 balance → eczema improves
```

## The Protocol

### Tier 1: Gut restoration (the foundation)
| Intervention | Mechanism | Dose | Cost/mo | Evidence |
|-------------|-----------|------|---------|----------|
| **Butyrate (sodium/calcium)** | Direct SCFA supplementation → FOXP3 → Tregs | 300mg TID | $25 | Strong (mechanism), moderate (eczema-specific) |
| **Prebiotic fiber (GOS/FOS)** | Feed butyrate-producing bacteria | 5-10g/day | $15 | Moderate (PREBALD trial showed benefit in infants) |
| **Probiotic (L. rhamnosus GG)** | Best-studied probiotic strain for eczema | 10B CFU/day | $20 | Moderate (meta-analyses show modest benefit, strongest in prevention) |
| **Fermented foods** | Diverse SCFA production + microbial diversity | Kefir, sauerkraut, kimchi daily | $20 | Epidemiological (traditional diets correlate with lower eczema) |

### Tier 2: Immune modulation (same as CVB protocol)
| Intervention | Mechanism | Dose | Cost/mo |
|-------------|-----------|------|---------|
| **Vitamin D** | Treg induction + cathelicidin (anti-S. aureus) + anti-proliferative on keratinocytes | 5,000 IU/day (adult), 1,000 IU/day (child) | $10 |
| **Omega-3 (EPA/DHA)** | Resolvin production → resolve skin inflammation | 3g/day (adult), 500mg-1g/day (child) | $30 |
| **BHB (exogenous ketones)** | NLRP3 suppression in keratinocytes | Per protocol (adults); not established in children | $30 |

### Tier 3: Barrier repair (eczema-specific)
| Intervention | Mechanism | Cost/mo |
|-------------|-----------|---------|
| **Ceramide-containing moisturizer** | Restores lipid lamellae in stratum corneum | $15 (CeraVe, Vanicream) |
| **Dilute bleach baths** | S. aureus decolonization (0.005% NaOCl) | $0 (household bleach) |
| **Wet wrap therapy** | Emollient + damp layer + dry layer → hydration + barrier repair (for flares) | $0 (gauze + moisturizer) |

### Tier 4: Flare management (conventional)
| Intervention | Mechanism | Notes |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| **Topical corticosteroid** | Anti-inflammatory (acute flares only) | Use lowest potency for shortest duration |
| **Topical calcineurin inhibitor** (tacrolimus/pimecrolimus) | Steroid-sparing, safe for face/folds | Maintenance for flare-prone areas |
| **Dupilumab** (if severe) | Anti-IL-4Rα → blocks IL-4 and IL-13 | $3,000/mo; for moderate-severe refractory |

## Pediatric Considerations
- Gut microbiome is still FORMING in young children → interventions may be more effective
- Probiotic prevention: L. rhamnosus GG during pregnancy + first 6 months of life reduces eczema incidence by ~30% (Kalliomäki 2001, Lancet)
- Vitamin D: 1,000 IU/day is safe and recommended for children
- Omega-3: age-appropriate dosing (fish oil, or algal DHA for young children)
- NO fasting, NO BHB, NO fluoxetine in children — gut restoration + vitamin D + barrier care only
- **Breastfeeding** (if still applicable): transfers IgA + shapes microbiome

## What This Solves
- Gut dysbiosis → butyrate restoration → Tregs → Th2 suppression
- Barrier defect → ceramide moisturizers + dilute bleach (S. aureus)
- NLRP3 amplification → BHB (adults only)
- Vitamin D deficiency → supplementation

## What This Doesn't Solve
1. **FLG mutations** — genetic barrier defect can't be fixed by gut restoration
2. **Established IgE sensitization** — allergen-specific IgE persists even if Th2 suppressed
3. **Neural itch circuit** — IL-31 → sensory neurons → itch may persist independently
4. **Severe/refractory disease** — may need dupilumab regardless

## Prediction
- Mild-moderate eczema: 40-60% SCORAD improvement within 3 months on Tiers 1-3
- Severe eczema: 20-30% improvement (helpful but likely insufficient alone)
- Pediatric eczema: potentially stronger response (microbiome still plastic)
- Flare frequency: reduced by 30-50% (fewer triggers reaching Th2 threshold)

## Measurement
- SCORAD (Scoring Atopic Dermatitis) — validated severity index
- EASI (Eczema Area and Severity Index)
- Patient/parent-reported itch (NRS 0-10)
- Flare frequency (episodes per month requiring topical steroid)
- Stool calprotectin (gut inflammation marker)
- Stool microbiome sequencing (if available — track butyrate producer abundance)
