# Eczema — Gap Analysis (Initial)

## How Eczema Fits the Campaign

Eczema is NOT a CVB disease. It breaks the "one virus, twelve diseases" pattern. But it shares enough mechanistic overlap with the CVB protocol's anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating components that it belongs in the campaign as a **co-beneficiary** — a disease that improves as a side effect of treating CVB.

```
CVB diseases:     Virus → Persistence → Inflammation → Organ damage
Eczema:           Barrier defect → Immune dysregulation → Inflammation → Skin damage

SHARED:           Inflammation driven by NLRP3, gut dysbiosis, Treg insufficiency
SHARED PROTOCOL:  BHB, butyrate, vitamin D, omega-3 → address inflammation in BOTH
```

## The Gap

### For eczema specifically
The gap is the **itch-scratch cycle**. Even with perfect immune modulation, mechanical scratching damages the barrier → re-exposes antigens → re-triggers inflammation. Breaking the cycle requires:
1. Reduce itch (IL-31 blockade — nemolizumab, or antihistamines)
2. Restore barrier (emollients, ceramides, FLG restoration)
3. Kill S. aureus (antimicrobial — vitamin D → cathelicidin helps here)
4. Suppress Th2 (dupilumab blocks IL-4/IL-13, or Tregs suppress Th2 naturally)

### For the campaign
The gap is whether the CVB protocol's anti-inflammatory stack provides SUFFICIENT benefit for eczema without eczema-specific interventions. The protocol addresses:
- ✅ NLRP3 (BHB, colchicine)
- ✅ Gut dysbiosis (butyrate)
- ✅ Tregs (butyrate, vitamin D)
- ✅ Resolvins (omega-3)
- ✅ Antimicrobial peptides (vitamin D → cathelicidin)
- ❌ Barrier repair (not in protocol — needs emollients/ceramides)
- ❌ Direct IL-31/IL-4/IL-13 blockade (not in protocol — needs dupilumab or similar)
- ❌ S. aureus decolonization (partially addressed by cathelicidin, but may need topical)

## The Fork

### Fork A: Eczema improves as a side effect of the T1DM protocol
- Butyrate + vitamin D + omega-3 + BHB → improved gut-skin axis → eczema flares reduce
- No eczema-specific intervention needed
- Prediction: flare frequency decreases 30-50% within 3 months of protocol start
- Measurement: SCORAD or EASI score tracked alongside T1DM markers

### Fork B: Eczema needs targeted additions
- Add: ceramide-containing emollients (CeraVe, Vanicream — $15/month)
- Add: dilute bleach baths 2x/week (S. aureus decolonization — $0)
- Add: if severe, dupilumab 300mg q2wk ($3,000/month with insurance, or biosimilar)
- These are eczema-specific, not part of the CVB protocol

### Fork C: Eczema is entirely separate
- If no improvement from the protocol → eczema and T1DM share risk factors but not mechanism
- Treat independently per dermatology guidelines

## Priority

Eczema is a quality-of-life disease, not life-threatening. Within the campaign, it's lowest priority — but if the protocol improves it as a side effect, that's a meaningful bonus and supports the "immune dysregulation is the root cause" thesis.
