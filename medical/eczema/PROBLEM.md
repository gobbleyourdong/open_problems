# Atopic Dermatitis / Eczema

## The Problem
Chronic inflammatory skin disease characterized by pruritus (itching), erythema, xerosis (dryness), and recurrent flares. Affects ~15-20% of children and ~7-10% of adults worldwide. No cure. Management is topical steroids, calcineurin inhibitors, and newer biologics (dupilumab). Quality of life impact is enormous — comparable to diabetes and heart disease in severity.

## Status: UNSOLVED (treatable, not curable)

## Key Biology

| Component | What | Role |
|-----------|------|------|
| Filaggrin (FLG) | Epidermal barrier protein | Loss-of-function mutations in ~30% of eczema patients → barrier defect |
| Th2 cells | CD4+ T helper subset | Produce IL-4, IL-13, IL-31 → drive eczema inflammation |
| IL-4 / IL-13 | Th2 cytokines | Suppress filaggrin expression, drive IgE, impair barrier |
| IL-31 | "Itch cytokine" | Directly activates sensory neurons → pruritus |
| IgE | Immunoglobulin E | Elevated in ~80% of eczema; sensitization to allergens |
| TSLP | Thymic stromal lymphopoietin | Epithelial alarm cytokine → initiates Th2 cascade |
| Staphylococcus aureus | Skin pathobiont | Colonizes 90%+ of eczema skin; drives flares via toxins |
| NLRP3 inflammasome | IL-1β/IL-18 platform | Activated in eczema keratinocytes; drives inflammation |
| Skin microbiome | Diverse commensal bacteria | Dysbiosis (S. aureus dominance) correlates with severity |
| Gut microbiome | Intestinal bacteria | Gut dysbiosis → Th2 skewing → "gut-skin axis" |

## The Disease Cascade
1. Genetic susceptibility (FLG mutations + immune gene variants)
2. Barrier defect → allergens/microbes penetrate epidermis
3. Keratinocytes release TSLP, IL-25, IL-33 (alarm cytokines)
4. Dendritic cells activate → prime Th2 responses
5. Th2 cells produce IL-4/IL-13 → further suppress filaggrin → barrier worsens (vicious cycle)
6. IL-4/IL-13 → B cells switch to IgE → allergic sensitization
7. IL-31 → sensory neuron activation → itch → scratch → barrier damage → more inflammation
8. S. aureus colonizes damaged barrier → toxins → superantigen activation → flare
9. NLRP3 activation in keratinocytes → IL-1β → amplifies inflammation
10. Chronic phase: Th2 + Th22 + some Th1 → epidermal thickening (lichenification)

## Possible Connection to CVB / The Campaign

### Direct CVB connection: WEAK
- Eczema is not traditionally caused by CVB
- No enteroviral RNA found in eczema skin lesions (not studied specifically)
- The Th2-dominant immune profile is OPPOSITE to the Th1/inflammatory profile seen in CVB diseases

### Indirect connections: MODERATE TO STRONG

**1. Shared NLRP3 pathway**
- NLRP3 inflammasome activated in eczema keratinocytes (Dai 2011, Keller 2017)
- BHB suppresses NLRP3 → should reduce eczema flare severity
- Colchicine (NLRP3 blocker) is NOT standard eczema treatment but mechanistically plausible

**2. Gut-skin axis**
- Gut dysbiosis → reduced butyrate → impaired Treg induction → Th2 skewing → eczema
- Butyrate supplementation → FOXP3+ Tregs → restore Th1/Th2 balance → reduce eczema
- Eczema patients have lower gut Faecalibacterium and Roseburia (butyrate producers)
- This is the SAME gut dysbiosis pattern seen in T1DM (Hit 2 in attempt 035)

**3. Vitamin D**
- Vitamin D deficiency correlates with eczema severity
- Vitamin D → cathelicidin (antimicrobial peptide) → kills S. aureus on skin
- Vitamin D → Treg induction → Th2 suppression
- Already in the protocol at 5,000 IU/day

**4. Omega-3**
- EPA/DHA → anti-inflammatory resolvins in skin
- Omega-3 supplementation reduces eczema severity in some trials
- Already in the protocol at 3g/day

**5. Th1/Th2 balance and CVB**
- CVB infection drives Th1 response (IFN-γ, TNF-α)
- Eczema is Th2-dominant
- HYPOTHESIS: persistent CVB infection may paradoxically PROTECT against eczema (hygiene hypothesis) OR CVB-driven immune dysregulation may contribute to Th2 skewing in some patients
- The "hygiene hypothesis" originally came from eczema/allergy research

**6. Shared protocol components**
- BHB → NLRP3 suppression (both diseases)
- Butyrate → Tregs → immune balance (both diseases)
- Vitamin D → antimicrobial + Tregs (both diseases)
- Omega-3 → resolvins (both diseases)
- Gut microbiome restoration → reduces both T1DM and eczema risk

## Anti-Problem
"What do people who never develop eczema despite carrying FLG mutations look like?"
~30% of FLG mutation carriers never develop eczema. They have: intact gut microbiome, adequate vitamin D, Th1/Th2 balance maintained by Tregs. The protocol builds this phenotype.
