# Eczema (Atopic Dermatitis) — THE WALL

## Classification Reminder

Eczema is **NOT** a CVB disease. It is a Category 2 co-beneficiary: an immune dysregulation disease that shares enough mechanistic overlap with the CVB protocol that it improves as a side effect.

The campaign addresses eczema through the **shared pathway** (NLRP3, gut dysbiosis, Treg insufficiency) rather than through an antiviral mechanism.

## The Wall

**The wall for eczema is patient expectation management.**

Most eczema patients pursue:
1. Topical steroids (symptom suppression)
2. Dupilumab (IL-4/IL-13 blockade, $2,400/month)
3. Newer biologics (tralokinumab, lebrikizumab)

The campaign protocol addresses eczema through mechanisms that are **upstream** of these drug targets:

```
What biologics do:         What the campaign does:
Dupilumab → block IL-4/13  Butyrate → gut barrier → less allergen translocation
                           Butyrate → FOXP1 → Treg suppresses Th2 (the cell making IL-4/13)
                           VitD → cathelicidin → antimicrobial (kills S. aureus on skin)
                           BHB → NLRP3 suppression → less IL-1β amplifying Th2
                           Omega-3 → resolvins → pro-resolution of inflammation
                           FMD → gut microbiome reset → butyrate producers restored
```

## The Expected Effect

From eczema/attempts/attempt_002 + attempt_004 (bioinformatics):
- Protocol addresses 5 of 8 mechanistic components of eczema (butyrate, VitD, NLRP3, resolvins, cathelicidin)
- Missing: barrier repair (ceramide emollients, not in protocol), direct IL-4/13 blockade
- **Predicted**: 40-60% reduction in flare frequency within 3 months of protocol

The FOXP1 mechanism (attempt_004): at 4-6g/day butyrate (the dose required for HDAC inhibition in non-gut tissues), FOXP1 is upregulated in Treg precursors → Tregs suppress Th2 → less IL-4/IL-13 produced → eczema improvement. This is the mechanism that runs PARALLEL to dupilumab's effect on the same cytokines.

## The Protocol Addition for Eczema Patients

Standard campaign protocol PLUS:
1. **Ceramide emollients** (CeraVe, Vanicream, etc.): OTC, $15/month. Restores skin barrier — this is the ONE eczema-specific missing piece in the core protocol.
2. **Dilute bleach baths** 2× weekly: S. aureus decolonization. Vitamin D → cathelicidin helps, but bleach baths are the gold standard. OTC, ~$0/month.
3. If severe: dupilumab can be used CONCURRENTLY with the protocol. The protocol's Treg restoration may reduce the required dupilumab dose over time.

## What Patients Should Expect

Timeline:
- **Month 1-2**: gut microbiome beginning to shift (butyrate + FMD reset). Some improvement in gut-skin axis.
- **Month 3-4**: FOXP1/Treg restoration starting (butyrate 4-6g + VitD 5000 IU). Th2 response modulating.
- **Month 4-6**: measurable reduction in flare frequency expected if the eczema has significant gut-Th2 component.
- **Not expected**: complete remission in severe eczema without direct IL-4/IL-13 blockade.

**How to measure improvement**: SCORAD or EASI score at baseline and monthly. Itch VAS (visual analog scale) daily. Topical steroid usage (should decrease if protocol is working).

## The Wall Is Expectation

Eczema patients who expect the protocol to work like dupilumab will be disappointed. The protocol works **upstream** — slower (months, not weeks) and more systemically (through gut-Th2 axis) rather than the immediate cytokine blockade that biologics provide.

Patients who understand that the protocol is targeting root cause (gut dysbiosis → Treg failure → Th2 dominance) rather than symptoms (IL-4/IL-13 blockade) will set appropriate expectations.

**For the patient**: if you have both T1DM and eczema, the T1DM protocol should improve your eczema as a side effect over months 3-6. Track your flare frequency alongside your C-peptide. The improvement in eczema will be evidence that the Treg restoration arm is working.
