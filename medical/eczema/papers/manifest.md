# Eczema / Atopic Dermatitis — Paper Manifest

## TIER 1: Foundational

| Paper | What it proves | Used in |
|-------|---------------|---------|
| **Weidinger & Novak 2016, Lancet** — "Atopic dermatitis" | Comprehensive review: FLG, Th2, barrier, S. aureus | All |
| **Palmer et al. 2006, Nature Genetics** — FLG mutations and eczema | FLG loss-of-function as primary genetic risk | PROBLEM.md, anti-problem |
| **Nakatsuji et al. 2017, Nature Medicine** — "Antimicrobials from human skin commensal bacteria protect against S. aureus and are deficient in AD" | Commensal transplant reduces S. aureus and improves eczema | Attempt 003 |
| **Kalliomäki et al. 2001, Lancet** — "Probiotics in primary prevention of AD" | L. rhamnosus GG reduces eczema incidence by ~30% in at-risk infants | Attempt 001 |
| **Stein et al. 2016, NEJM** — "Innate immunity and asthma risk in Amish and Hutterite farm children" | Same genetics, different microbial environment → 4x difference in allergic disease | Anti-problem |

|-------|-------------|

## TIER 3: Vitamin D / Cathelicidin

| Paper | What it shows |
|-------|-------------|
| **Hata et al. 2008, J Allergy Clin Immunol** — Vitamin D and cathelicidin in AD | Oral vitamin D increases cathelicidin and improves AD |

| **Camargo et al. 2014, JAAD** — Vitamin D supplementation in winter-related AD | RCT: vitamin D 1000 IU reduced winter eczema severity in children |
