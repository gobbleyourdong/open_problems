## PHASE 1: FOUNDATION (Months 1-3)

### Goal: Reduce Destruction

The model says your beta cells are being destroyed faster than they regenerate. Phase 1 aims to cut the destruction rate by ~50%:
- Fluoxetine clears the coxsackievirus that has been smoldering in your islets for decades
- Vitamin D reduces bystander inflammatory damage
- Butyrate expands regulatory T cells (the immune system's brakes)
- Fasting silences beta cells (fewer neoantigens = less immune targeting)

### Month 1, Week 1: START

**Fluoxetine 10mg/day**

- Take in the morning, with breakfast
- Half dose for 2 weeks to minimize side effects
- Common side effects (expect these, they pass):
  - Nausea (most common, usually resolves week 2-3)
  - Headache (days 1-5)
  - Insomnia or vivid dreams (if insomnia: take in AM, never PM)
  - Increased anxiety (paradoxical, temporary, resolves week 2-3)
  - GI upset (take with food)

**IMPORTANT for T1DM patients:** Fluoxetine can mask hypoglycemia symptoms (tremor, anxiety) by about 15-20%. This is because it blunts the sympathetic nervous system response that produces those warning signs. **Raise your CGM low alarm to 80 mg/dL** (from the typical 70).

**Vitamin D3 4000 IU/day**

- Take with your fattiest meal of the day (absorption improves ~50% with fat)
- If your baseline vitamin D was below 20 ng/mL: start at 5000-6000 IU for the first 2 months
- Target blood level: 50-70 ng/mL
- If any blood test shows >100 ng/mL: reduce to 2000 IU

### Month 1, Week 2: Add Butyrate

**Sodium butyrate 300mg, 2x/day**

- Start with ONE capsule (300mg) with dinner for 3 days
- Then add the second capsule with breakfast
- MUST be enteric-coated capsules or you will experience the infamous butyrate smell (rancid butter)
- Common early effects: mild GI discomfort, slightly loose stools. Usually resolves in 1 week.
- If GI symptoms persist: reduce to 1x/day for another week, then try again

### Month 1, Week 3: Increase Fluoxetine

**Fluoxetine 20mg/day** (the target therapeutic dose)

- If side effects from 10mg were tolerable: increase to 20mg
- If side effects were severe: stay at 10mg for another 2 weeks, then try 20mg
- 20mg achieves steady-state brain concentration of ~4.5 uM (15x plasma), which is 4.5x above the CVB 2C ATPase IC50 of 1.0 uM
- Steady state takes 4-6 weeks from dose change

### Month 1, Week 4: YOUR FIRST 24-HOUR FAST

**THIS IS THE MOST IMPORTANT SAFETY EVENT IN THE PROTOCOL.**

You are a T1DM patient fasting for 24 hours. This requires preparation, monitoring, and clear abort criteria. Do not wing this.

**The Day Before:**
- Eat a normal dinner at 6:00 PM. This is your last meal.
- Take your normal bolus insulin with this meal.
- Prepare your fasting kit:
  - CGM sensor (confirmed working)
  - Blood ketone meter + strips
  - Glucose tabs (at least 8)
  - Juice box (at least 2)
  - Water bottle
  - Log sheet (time / BG / BHB / notes)
  - Phone charged (emergency contact programmed)
- Tell someone you are fasting. Give them your location.

**Insulin Adjustments:**
- Basal insulin: take 80% of your normal dose at your normal time
- Bolus insulin: NONE during the fast (no meals)
- If blood glucose goes above 250: take a CORRECTION dose at 50% of your normal correction factor. If it stays above 300 for 2 hours, break the fast.

**During the Fast (the next 24 hours):**

| Time | Action |
|------|--------|
| 6 PM (hour 0) | Last meal. Log starting BG and BHB. |
| 10 PM (hour 4) | Check BG (CGM). Check BHB (finger stick). Log. |
| 6 AM (hour 12) | Check BG. Check BHB. This is where BHB typically starts rising above 0.5. |
| 10 AM (hour 16) | Check BG. Check BHB. |
| 2 PM (hour 20) | Check BG. Check BHB. You may feel hungry but otherwise fine. |
| 6 PM (hour 24) | Check BG and BHB one last time. BREAK FAST with a normal meal. Take normal bolus. |

**What to drink:** Water (as much as you want), black coffee (no sugar, no cream), plain tea. Zero calories.

**Activity level:** Light walking is fine. No gym, no vigorous exercise. Exercise increases glucose variability and accelerates ketogenesis.

**ABORT the fast IMMEDIATELY if:**
1. Blood glucose drops below 60 mg/dL (or below 70 with symptoms)
2. Blood BHB rises above 3.0 mM
3. You feel nauseous, have abdominal pain, or start vomiting
4. You feel confused or unable to self-monitor
5. Blood glucose goes above 300 AND BHB is above 1.5 (DKA prodrome)

**If you abort:**
- Eat 15-30g of fast carbs immediately (glucose tabs, juice)
- If BG was below 54: Rule of 15 (15g carbs, wait 15 min, recheck, repeat)
- If BHB was above 3.0 with symptoms: GO TO THE EMERGENCY ROOM
- If BHB above 5.0: CALL 911 -- this is DKA
- Wait at least 48 hours before attempting another fast
- Review what went wrong with your doctor

**Expected outcome:** BHB peaks at 1.0-1.5 mM. This is the therapeutic sweet spot. Enough BHB to suppress the NLRP3 inflammasome, not enough for DKA. Blood glucose stays 70-150 for most of the fast with 80% basal insulin.

### Month 2: First 5-Day FMD

The fasting-mimicking diet is the ENGINE of regeneration. Each cycle has three acts:

**Act 1: Clearing (Days 1-2)**
- Your body depletes glycogen and switches to fat burning
- AMPK activates, mTOR shuts off
- Autophagy begins -- your cells start cleaning house
- Beta cells begin to quiet down (less antigen display to the immune system)

**Act 2: Deep Reset (Days 3-5)**
- BHB peaks at 2-3 mM on ~800 kcal/day (lower than a water fast)
- Deep autophagy: viral replication complexes are digested
- Beta cells are fully quiescent -- invisible to the immune system
- Old inflammatory immune cells die and are replaced

**Act 3: Rebirth (Days 6-8, Refeeding)**
- mTOR reactivates within 6 hours of normal eating
- IGF-1 rebounds (temporarily overshoots normal)
- Ngn3+ pancreatic progenitors differentiate into NEW BETA CELLS
- Fresh immune cells emerge from bone marrow
- If your Tregs are strong (from butyrate + vitamin D): the new beta cells SURVIVE

## PHASE 2: AMPLIFY (Months 3-6)

### Goal: Boost Regeneration

Phase 1 reduced D. Now we add components to boost R:

### Month 3: START GABA

**GABA 250mg, 3x/day (750mg total to start)**

GABA does two things for you:
1. **Anti-inflammatory:** Reduces T effector cell expansion by ~20% (less immune attack)
2. **Transdifferentiation:** Activates GABA-A receptors on alpha cells, triggering Pax4 expression, which causes alpha cells to convert into beta cells (Soltani et al., PNAS 2011)

Your pancreas has plenty of alpha cells (~95% of normal mass). GABA essentially recruits them to switch teams.

Start with 250mg with each meal. After 2 weeks, if tolerated, increase to 500mg 3x/day (1500mg total).

Side effects: mild drowsiness (week 1), slight dizziness. GABA does not cross the blood-brain barrier well in adults, so central nervous system effects are minimal. If drowsiness is an issue, take the largest dose at bedtime.

### Month 4: Talk to Your Doctor About Semaglutide

This is Tier 3 (premium). The protocol works without it, but semaglutide provides:
- 25% boost to beta cell replication rate (GLP-1R -> cAMP -> CREB)
- 40% enhanced glucose-stimulated insulin secretion per cell
- 30% improved peripheral insulin sensitivity
- Suppressed glucagon (lower fasting glucose)
- All of which mean: you need LESS total insulin, and what your beta cells produce goes FURTHER

**What to tell your doctor:**

"I'd like to try low-dose semaglutide as an adjunct to my insulin. The ADJUNCT ONE and TWO trials showed it reduces insulin dose by 20-30% in T1DM, improves time-in-range, and reduces HbA1c. I have CGM and I'm monitoring closely. I'd like to start at 0.25mg weekly and go no higher than 0.5mg."

**If they agree:** Start 0.25mg subcutaneous weekly. Proactively reduce basal insulin by 10%. Watch CGM for lows.

**If they decline:** Fine. Continue without it. GABA + FMD are the main regeneration drivers.

**If cost is the issue:** Check compounding pharmacies ($150-300/mo), Canadian pharmacies with valid Rx ($250-350/mo), or manufacturer patient assistance programs.

### Months 3-6: Continue Monthly FMD

One 5-day FMD every month. Same protocol as Month 2. The cumulative effect is what matters -- each cycle adds ~0.3% beta cell mass during the refeeding window.

### Month 4-5: Titrate Semaglutide (if started)

After 4 weeks at 0.25mg, increase to 0.5mg weekly if tolerated.
- Reduce basal insulin by another 10% (total 20% reduction from pre-semaglutide)

- Watch your weight: semaglutide causes weight loss. If BMI drops below 20, discuss with doctor.
- Do NOT go above 0.5mg. You are not trying to lose weight -- you want the beta cell effects.

### Month 6: Decision Point #2 -- The Big One

**OUTCOME A: C-peptide is rising**

Celebrate. This is evidence of beta cell recovery. The model is working.

If stimulated C-peptide is above 0.4 nmol/L, you are responding strongly. Move to Phase 3 with full confidence.

**OUTCOME B: hs-CRP dropped but C-peptide is flat**

The anti-inflammatory arm is working. The immune attack is reduced. But regeneration has not caught up yet. This is consistent with the model -- the inequality reversal is predicted at month 8-10. You may be on the slower end.

Action: Increase GABA to 1500mg/day if not already there. Continue 3 more months. Do not panic.

**OUTCOME C: Nothing has changed**

Check compliance first. Were all FMD cycles completed? Was fluoxetine taken daily? Was vitamin D at target?

If compliance was perfect: the model predicts some patients take 12+ months. Continue. Consider teplizumab (see Phase 3).

**OUTCOME D: C-peptide dropped**

Uncommon. Check for: recent illness, severe stress, medication changes, infection. Repeat in 4 weeks. A single drop is not failure -- the trajectory over 3+ time points matters.

### If C-Peptide Is Rising: The Insulin Dose Reduction Ladder

This is the most dangerous part of the protocol. As your beta cells wake up, your total insulin (endogenous + exogenous) may become too high, causing hypoglycemia. CGM is non-negotiable.

**NEVER reduce insulin without both CGM data showing recurrent lows AND C-peptide confirmation of rising endogenous production.**

| C-Peptide (nmol/L) | Milestone | Insulin Action | CGM Alarm |
|---------------------|-----------|----------------|-----------|
| 0.10-0.19 | Baseline | No change. Document. | 80 mg/dL |
| 0.20 | First signal | No change. Celebrate. Watch for nighttime lows. | 80 mg/dL |
| 0.40 | Early response | Reduce BOLUS by 25% | 75 mg/dL |
| 0.80 | Moderate recovery | Reduce BASAL by 30%, BOLUS by 50%. DANGER ZONE. | 80 mg/dL |
| 1.20 | Strong recovery | Reduce BASAL by 50%, BOLUS by 75% | 75 mg/dL |
| 1.80 | Near independence | Safety minimum basal (0.1 U/kg). Trial: discontinue bolus. | 75 mg/dL |
| 2.50 | Independence | Trial complete discontinuation (supervised, 2-week period). | 70 mg/dL |

**The Danger Zone (C-peptide 0.4-1.2):** When endogenous insulin provides 30-70% of your needs, small fluctuations in either source can cause hypoglycemia or hyperglycemia. Your glucose variability will INCREASE before it improves. This is expected. Carry glucose tabs at all times. Have a glucagon kit accessible.

### If C-Peptide Is Flat: Consider Teplizumab

Teplizumab (Tzield) is an anti-CD3 monoclonal antibody. FDA-approved for delaying T1DM onset. Off-label for established disease but biologically rational -- it depletes the exhausted autoreactive T cells that are attacking your beta cells.

This requires:
- Specialist: academic endocrinologist or immunologist
- Setting: infusion center (14-day IV course)
- Cost: ~$13,000 per course (check insurance, manufacturer assistance)
- Side effects: cytokine release syndrome (flu-like symptoms days 1-5), transient lymphopenia, rash

**CRITICAL RULE:** Do NOT fast during teplizumab infusion. Resume FMD 4 weeks after completing the course.

This is Tier 3+ (premium). Most patients should exhaust the supplement protocol for 9-12 months before considering teplizumab.

## PHASE 4: MAINTENANCE (Months 12-24)

### Goal: Consolidate Gains

If responding, continue all components. As C-peptide rises and insulin doses drop, you can gradually reduce the intensity:

- FMD: can reduce to every 6-8 weeks (instead of monthly) if C-peptide is steadily rising
- Fluoxetine: consider tapering at month 18 if all biomarkers stable (see below)
- Supplements: continue indefinitely (low cost, ongoing benefit)
- Semaglutide: continue if tolerated and affordable

### Month 18: Labs + Fluoxetine Taper Decision

Labs: C-peptide, HbA1c, ALT, Vitamin D (~$120)

**Fluoxetine taper decision:** At 18 months, the viral clearance model predicts all 8 compartments (including CNS and testes) are cleared. Consider tapering IF:
- C-peptide has been rising for 6+ months
- Autoantibodies declining or stable
- hs-CRP normal

**Taper schedule (NEVER stop abruptly):**
- Week 1-2: 20mg -> 10mg daily
- Week 3-4: 10mg every other day
- Week 5: Stop

If discontinuation symptoms occur (dizziness, "brain zaps," irritability, flu-like): slow the taper, extend each step to 3-4 weeks.

If C-peptide is still flat at month 18: continue fluoxetine. There is no clinical harm from longer-term use.

### Month 24: Final Assessment -- The Verdict

Full comprehensive panel (everything from baseline). Cost: ~$725

- Beta cell mass: ~28% (up from 8%)

**Q: What about my current insulin? Do I change it?**

Not at the start. You continue your current insulin regimen exactly as-is. Insulin adjustments happen ONLY when C-peptide rises and ONLY per the milestone ladder. Premature insulin reduction is the single most dangerous mistake you can make.

**Q: How do I know if I have residual beta cells?**

If your fasting C-peptide is above 0.03 nmol/L, you have residual beta cells. If it is above 0.10, you have meaningful residual function. Butler (2005) showed 88% of T1DM patients have SOME beta cells even after 50+ years. the patient has detectable C-peptide at 0.12 nmol/L after 67 years.

**Q: What if my doctor says fasting is dangerous for T1DM?**

They are not wrong -- unmonitored fasting in T1DM IS dangerous. That is why this protocol includes: insulin dose adjustments (80% basal), CGM monitoring, blood ketone monitoring, strict abort criteria, and clear emergency protocols. Show them the safety analysis. If they still refuse, ask them to help you design a safer fasting approach (e.g., start with 16:8 IF before graduating to 24h or FMD).

**Q: What about colchicine? I see it mentioned in the safety profile.**

Colchicine (anti-inflammatory, NLRP3 inhibitor) was evaluated as a potential protocol component but is NOT included in the primary protocol due to its narrow therapeutic index and CYP3A4 interaction with fluoxetine. If your doctor wants to discuss it, the safe dose with concurrent fluoxetine is 0.5mg/day maximum.

**Q: Is this protocol appropriate for newly diagnosed T1DM?**

Yes -- and likely MORE effective. Newly diagnosed patients have more residual beta cell mass (typically 20-40%). The earlier you intervene, the more cells you have to protect and build on. The same protocol applies, with the caveat that autoimmune activity may be more aggressive in early-onset disease.

**Q: What about children/adolescents?**

Pediatric endocrinology supervision is mandatory. Fluoxetine dosing differs for children. FMD may need modification (no full fasting, only FMD at 1000+ kcal). GABA and butyrate doses should be weight-adjusted. The principles apply but the execution needs pediatric expertise.

## EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS

### HYPOGLYCEMIA (BG below 70 mg/dL)

**Mild (54-70, you're aware):**
1. Eat 15g fast carbs: 4 glucose tabs, 4 oz juice, or 1 tbsp honey
2. Wait 15 minutes. Recheck.
3. If still below 70: repeat.
4. Once above 70: eat a snack with protein + fat.
5. Log it. Figure out why it happened.

**Moderate (40-54, confused/symptomatic):**
1. Eat 30g fast carbs NOW
2. If you cannot eat safely: glucagon nasal spray (Baqsimi) -- tell your housemate where it is
3. Call someone to be with you
4. If no improvement in 15 minutes: CALL 911
5. After recovery: full meal. Report to your doctor. Review insulin doses.

**Severe (below 40 or unconscious):**
1. CALL 911
2. Bystander: administer glucagon
3. Do NOT put food in the mouth of an unconscious person
4. Place on side (recovery position)

### DKA (Diabetic Ketoacidosis)

Warning signs: BHB above 3.0, glucose above 250 + ketones above 1.5, nausea/vomiting, abdominal pain, fruity breath, rapid deep breathing, confusion.

1. STOP FASTING. Eat carbs.
2. Check BG and BHB.
3. BHB above 3.0 with symptoms: GO TO ER.
4. BHB above 5.0: CALL 911. This is DKA.
5. Take correction insulin (50% of normal).
6. Drink water aggressively.
7. Do NOT exercise. Do NOT take exogenous BHB.

### CHEST PAIN

CVB can cause myocarditis/pericarditis. The protocol monitors for this.

1. STOP all exercise.
2. Severe chest pain or difficulty breathing: CALL 911.
3. Mild/intermittent: urgent cardiology within 24-48h.
4. Get troponin + BNP blood test immediately.
5. Do NOT fast until cardiac evaluation is complete.
6. Continue medications (fluoxetine and supplements are not cardiac-risky).

### SEVERE DRUG SIDE EFFECTS

**STOP fluoxetine immediately if:** Rash/hives/swelling (allergic), jaundice (liver), severe agitation + tremor + fever (serotonin syndrome -- RARE but LETHAL), seizure.

**STOP semaglutide immediately if:** Severe abdominal pain that will not stop (pancreatitis), vision changes, allergic reaction, vomiting for more than 24 hours.

**General rule:** When in doubt, contact the endocrinologistcrinologist. If they are unavailable and symptoms are severe, go to the ER. Bring your medication list. Tell them you are on a supplement protocol for T1DM beta cell recovery.
