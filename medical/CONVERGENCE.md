# Cross-Disease Convergence Model — One Virus, One Protocol, Twelve Diseases

## The Hierarchy

Not all 12 diseases are equal. They form a hierarchy based on timing, severity, and intervention opportunity:

```
PREVENTION LAYER (stop the virus before it seeds)
├── Neonatal sepsis prevention → maternal CVB vaccine (prevents ALL downstream)
├── Pleurodynia surveillance → earliest clinical detection of CVB exposure
└── Pancreatitis intervention → prevent islet seeding during acute infection

ACUTE LAYER (treat the active infection)
├── Encephalitis → emergency antiviral (minutes matter, neurons die)
├── Myocarditis → early antiviral (weeks matter, cardiomyocytes barely regenerate)
├── Hepatitis → monitor liver function (self-limiting but affects drug dosing)
├── Aseptic meningitis → antiviral to prevent CNS persistence
└── Pericarditis → colchicine + antiviral (best proof-of-concept trial)

CHRONIC LAYER (clear persistent virus, reverse damage)
├── T1DM → the primary protocol (63 attempts, 5 mountains, THEWALL.md)
├── DCM → halt dystrophin cleavage + standard HF therapy
├── ME/CFS → PEM-safe adapted protocol (largest population)
└── Orchitis → confirm reservoir, verify drug penetration
```

**Protocol update (post-bioinformatics):**
- Trehalose added: addresses LAMP2 block confirmed in CVB-infected human cells (GSE184831). Critical for testes (κ_effective = 0.15 without trehalose → 0.40 with), CNS (0.22 → 0.50), muscle (0.26 → 0.55).

- CoQ10/NAD+ added: MT-ND3 Complex I dysfunction confirmed in 93 ME/CFS patients (GSE293840).

## The Clinical Trial Roadmap

Ranked by feasibility and speed to proof:

### Tier 1: Can start now (existing drugs, clear endpoints)
1. **Pericarditis (RCT v3)**: 3-arm trial — colchicine alone | colchicine+fluoxetine | full protocol (adds trehalose + FMD). **9-month treatment duration** (LAMP2-corrected pericardial clearance = 8 months). cfRNA NLRP3 as biomarker endpoint. n=65/arm, 195 total. See pericarditis/attempts/attempt_003 for full design.
2. **T1DM**: the patient protocol (n=1, stimulated C-peptide at 3-6 months)

### Tier 2: Can start with modest infrastructure
3. **ME/CFS**: PEM-safe CVB clearance protocol (SF-36 + NK cell function, n=30 pilot)
4. **Pleurodynia surveillance**: add CVB testing to ED chest pain workup (observational, n=500)
5. **Hepatitis diagnostic**: CVB testing in T1DM patients with elevated LFTs (observational, n=100)

### Tier 3: Requires specialized infrastructure
6. **Myocarditis**: antiviral during acute phase (cardiac MRI endpoint, n=60)
7. **Pancreatitis**: antiviral to prevent T1DM (autoantibody endpoint, 2-year follow-up, n=200)
8. **Meningitis → ME/CFS**: prospective cohort (5-year follow-up, n=100)

### Tier 4: Requires drug/vaccine development
9. **Neonatal sepsis**: maternal CVB vaccination (requires VLP-ΔVP4 vaccine)
10. **Encephalitis**: pocapavir availability (requires expanded access program)

## The Non-CVB Co-Beneficiaries (Diseases 13-14)

Eczema and psoriasis are NOT caused by CVB. They are included because they share enough mechanism with the protocol's anti-inflammatory and immune-modulating components to improve as co-beneficiaries.

```
THE CAMPAIGN NOW COVERS THREE CATEGORIES:

CATEGORY 1: CVB-CAUSED (diseases 1-12)
  Virus → persistence → inflammation → organ damage
  Protocol: antiviral (fluoxetine, autophagy) + anti-inflammatory + immune modulation
  
CATEGORY 2: IMMUNE DYSREGULATION CO-BENEFICIARIES (diseases 13-14)
  Genetic susceptibility → trigger → inflammatory amplification loop → tissue damage
  Protocol: anti-inflammatory + immune modulation (same components, no antiviral needed)
  
CATEGORY 3: THE SHARED MECHANISM
  All 14 diseases involve:
  ├── NF-κB / TNF-α amplification
  ├── NLRP3 inflammasome → IL-1β
  ├── Treg insufficiency (different T helper pathways: Th1, Th2, Th17)
  ├── Gut dysbiosis → immune dysregulation
  └── Bistable attractor dynamics (disease and health are both stable states)
  
  The protocol attacks the SHARED MECHANISM.
  CVB diseases get the antiviral arm on top.
```
