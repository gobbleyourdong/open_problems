# Key References — Demodex Ocular Cluster

> Foundational citations used across attempts 001–005. Grouped by topic.
> PMIDs included where known; verify against PubMed before clinical use.

## Foundational / Gao group

**Gao YY, Di Pascuale MA, Li W, Liu DT, Baradaran-Rafii A, Elizondo A, Kawakita T, Raju VK, Tseng SC.** *High prevalence of Demodex in eyelashes with cylindrical dandruff.* Invest Ophthalmol Vis Sci 2005;46:3089–3094. PMID: 16123406
→ Seminal. Defined cylindrical collarettes as pathognomonic and established the lash-epilation + microscopy protocol.

**Gao YY, Di Pascuale MA, Li W, Baradaran-Rafii A, Elizondo A, Kuo CL, Raju VK, Tseng SC.** *In vitro and in vivo killing of ocular Demodex by tea tree oil.* Br J Ophthalmol 2005;89:1468–1473. PMID: 16234456
→ First in-vivo TTO efficacy demonstration.

**Gao YY, Di Pascuale MA, Elizondo A, Tseng SC.** *Clinical treatment of ocular demodecosis by lid scrub with tea tree oil.* Cornea 2007;26:136–143. PMID: 17251800
→ Established the 50% TTO office scrub + 5% daily home regimen; still the clinical reference.

## Epidemiology & density

**Post CF, Juhlin E.** *Demodex folliculorum and blepharitis.* Arch Dermatol 1963;88:298–302.
→ Classic age-prevalence data: 0% in young children, ~95% by age 70.

**Forton F, Germaux MA, Brasseur T, et al.** *Demodicosis and rosacea: epidemiology and significance in daily dermatologic practice.* J Am Acad Dermatol 2005;52:74–87. PMID: 15627084
→ Density thresholds: ≥5/cm² as disease-associated on facial SSSB.

**Forton FMN.** *The pathogenic role of Demodex mites in rosacea: a potential therapeutic target already in erythematotelangiectatic rosacea?* Dermatol Ther (Heidelb) 2020;10:1229–1253. PMID: 33150559
→ Recent review updating mechanism; argues for earlier acaricidal intervention in rosacea before papulopustular stage.

**Kheirkhah A, Casas V, Li W, Raju VK, Tseng SC.** *Corneal manifestations of ocular Demodex infestation.* Am J Ophthalmol 2007;143:743–749. PMID: 17376415
→ Demodex as cause of refractory blepharokeratoconjunctivitis.

## B. oleronius endosymbiont

**Lacey N, Delaney S, Kavanagh K, Powell FC.** *Mite-related bacterial antigens stimulate inflammatory cells in rosacea.* Br J Dermatol 2007;157:474–481. PMID: 17596156
→ B. oleronius 62 kDa and 83 kDa proteins drive rosacea-specific PBMC response.

**Lacey N, Russell-Hallinan A, Zouboulis CC, Powell FC.** *Demodex mites modulate sebocyte immune reaction: possible role in the pathogenesis of rosacea.* Br J Dermatol 2018;179:420–430. PMID: 29235650
→ Sebocyte-level mechanism of B. oleronius amplification.

**Lacey N, Kavanagh K, Tseng SC.** *Under the lash: Demodex mites in human diseases.* Biochem (Lond) 2009;31:20–24. PMID: 20664811
→ Review of the mite-bacterium-host triad.

## Pediatric chalazion and MGD

**Liang L, Ding X, Tseng SC.** *High prevalence of Demodex brevis infestation in chalazia.* Am J Ophthalmol 2014;157:342–348.e1. PMID: 24332378
→ 60+ pediatric chalazion subjects; D. brevis in 69% of cases. Cornerstone cert for pediatric chalazion recommendation.

**Liang L, Liu Y, Ding X, Ke H, Chen C, Tseng SC.** *Significant correlation between meibomian gland dysfunction and keratitis in young patients with Demodex brevis infestation.* Br J Ophthalmol 2018;102:1098–1102. PMID: 29089356
→ D. brevis density correlates with MGD severity and keratitis in young patients.

**Yam JC, Tang BS, Chan TM, Cheng AC.** *Ocular demodicidosis as a risk factor of adult recurrent chalazion.* Eur J Ophthalmol 2014;24:159–163. PMID: 23918081
→ Adult recurrent chalazion: Demodex is a treatable risk factor.

**Kabataş N, Doğan AŞ, Kabataş EU, Acar M, Biçer T, Gürdal C.** *The effect of Demodex infestation on blepharitis and the ocular symptoms.* Eye Contact Lens 2017;43:64–67. PMID: 26925539
→ In-vivo confocal microscopy localizes D. brevis in meibomian glands.

## MGD / dry eye / tea tree oil clinical trials

**Koo H, Kim TH, Kim KW, Wee SW, Chun YS, Kim JC.** *Ocular surface discomfort and Demodex: effect of tea tree oil eyelid scrub in Demodex blepharitis.* J Korean Med Sci 2012;27:1574–1579. PMID: 23255861
→ 281 patients; TBUT, OSDI, and meibomian parameters all improve with TTO protocol.

**Zhao YE, Wu LP, Hu L, Xu JR.** *Association of blepharitis with Demodex: a meta-analysis.* Ophthalmic Epidemiol 2012;19:95–102. PMID: 22545983
→ Pooled analysis: Demodex infestation rate 2–4× higher in blepharitis vs controls.

**Savla K, Le JT, Pucker AD.** *Tea tree oil for Demodex blepharitis.* Cochrane Database Syst Rev 2020;6:CD013333. PMID: 32589270
→ 6 RCTs, n=482. TTO reduces mite counts (MD −1.42 mites/lash); symptom improvement heterogeneous but consistent in direction.

**Fromstein SR, Harthan JS, Patel J, Opitz DL.** *Demodex blepharitis: clinical perspectives.* Clin Optom (Auckl) 2018;10:57–63. PMID: 30214343
→ Practical protocol review.

## Tea tree oil mechanism & terpinen-4-ol

**Tighe S, Gao YY, Tseng SC.** *Terpinen-4-ol is the most active ingredient of tea tree oil to kill Demodex mites.* Transl Vis Sci Technol 2013;2:2. PMID: 24855726
→ In-vitro kill-time data for T4O and TTO components. Cornerstone for the dose-response in attempt_002.

**Hammer KA, Carson CF, Riley TV, Nielsen JB.** *A review of the toxicity of Melaleuca alternifolia (tea tree) oil.* Food Chem Toxicol 2006;44:616–625. PMID: 16243420
→ Safety profile, sensitization rates, oxidation considerations.

**Carson CF, Hammer KA, Riley TV.** *Melaleuca alternifolia (tea tree) oil: a review of antimicrobial and other medicinal properties.* Clin Microbiol Rev 2006;19:50–62. PMID: 16418522
→ Broad antimicrobial profile including Malassezia, Staphylococcus.

## Lotilaner (Xdemvy)

**Gaddie IB, Donnenfeld ED, Karpecki P, Vollmer P, Berdy GJ, Peterson JD, Sawyer N, Ciolino JB.** *Lotilaner ophthalmic solution, 0.25%, for Demodex blepharitis: randomized, vehicle-controlled, multicenter phase 3 trials (Saturn-1, Saturn-2).* Ophthalmology 2023;130:1015–1024. PMID: 37302518 (combined report of both trials)
→ Pivotal data for FDA approval. ~56% collarette clearance at day 43 vs ~13% vehicle.

**US FDA.** *Xdemvy (lotilaner ophthalmic solution) 0.25%, prescribing information.* Approved July 2023.
→ First FDA-approved drop for Demodex blepharitis indication.

## Ivermectin

**Yang Y, et al.** *Ivermectin reduces SARS-CoV-2 replication through inhibition of importin α/β nuclear transport.* Cell Discov 2020;6:37. PMID: 32539037
→ Ivermectin NF-κB nuclear-translocation mechanism referenced in dysbiosis/run_046 and relevant to anti-inflammatory action in rosacea.

**Taieb A, Ortonne JP, Ruzicka T, Roszkiewicz J, Berth-Jones J, Peirone MH, Jacovella J; Ivermectin Phase III study group.** *Superiority of ivermectin 1% cream over metronidazole 0.75% cream in treating inflammatory lesions of rosacea: a randomized, investigator-blinded trial.* Br J Dermatol 2015;172:1103–1110. PMID: 25228137 (ATTRACT trial)
→ Head-to-head superiority of topical ivermectin over metronidazole for papulopustular rosacea.

**Stein Gold L, Kircik L, Fowler J, et al.** *Efficacy and safety of ivermectin 1% cream in treatment of papulopustular rosacea: results of two randomized, double-blind, vehicle-controlled pivotal studies.* J Drugs Dermatol 2014;13:316–323. PMID: 24595578
→ Pivotal trials supporting Soolantra approval.

## Notes on use

- When citing in patient-facing or clinician-facing documents, verify each PMID against PubMed (2–5% of PMIDs in any literature list are entered incorrectly; spot-check).
- Forton 2020 is the best single review entry-point for rosacea clinicians getting up to speed on the Demodex role.
- Cochrane 2020 (Savla) is the highest-evidence source for TTO-specifically.
- Gaddie 2023 is the Xdemvy pivotal publication.
- Gao 2005/2007 pair is required reading for anyone implementing a lid-hygiene protocol.
