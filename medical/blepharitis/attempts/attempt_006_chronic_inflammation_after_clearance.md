# attempt_006 — Chronic Inflammation After Demodex Clearance

> Scope: the patient whose mite burden has been reduced (confirmed by
> collarette exam + epilation count) but who still has symptoms —
> redness, lid irritation, foreign-body sensation, recurrent MGD. This
> is the *ocular rosacea* phenotype, and it requires non-acaricidal
> management layered on top of the acaricide work.
>
> Addresses: gap.md Type B4 (KLK5/LL-37 normalization), Type D1 (ocular
> rosacea as its own axis).

---

## The clinical picture

A subset of patients completes a full TTO or lotilaner course,
slit-lamp shows collarettes cleared, epilation counts are normal — and
they still report:

- Lid redness and lid-margin telangiectasia
- Burning / foreign-body sensation / photophobia
- Tear-film instability (persistent evaporative dry eye)
- Recurrent styes or chalazia on different lash lines
- Facial flushing triggers (alcohol, spicy food, heat, sun)

This is not treatment failure. It is the **chronic inflammatory state
that Demodex initiated but has developed independence**. In facial
rosacea terms: the patient has transitioned from Demodex-driven to
KLK5/LL-37-driven inflammation. Acaricide alone does not address the
downstream protease cascade.

---

## The KLK5 / LL-37 mechanism (why acaricide is insufficient)

From Yamasaki 2007 (Nat Med) and subsequent work:

```
Chronic skin inflammation (any cause, but Demodex + B. oleronius is the
lid-margin initiator)
    ↓
Kallikrein-5 (KLK5) upregulated in lid-margin keratinocytes
    ↓
KLK5 cleaves cathelicidin precursor (hCAP18) into abnormal LL-37 fragments
(not the normal antimicrobial form — pro-inflammatory variants)
    ↓
Abnormal LL-37 → TLR2 activation → NF-κB → IL-8, IL-6, VEGF, MMP-9
    ↓
Telangiectasia (VEGF-driven), inflammation (NF-κB), tissue remodeling
(MMP-9), neovascularization (severe cases)
    ↓
The process becomes self-sustaining: NF-κB upregulates KLK5 transcription
→ more abnormal LL-37 → more TLR2 → more NF-κB. Even after Demodex is
gone, the KLK5/LL-37 loop runs on its own momentum.
```

The operator already has deep background on this — see
`../dysbiosis/numerics/run_046_demodex_rosacea_nlrp3.md` and the
cross-referenced NLRP3 mechanism. Same biology plays out on the
eyelid substrate.

**Practical implication:** 6 weeks of acaricide reliably clears most
mites but does not immediately normalize KLK5. The published rosacea
literature shows KLK5 / LL-37 return to baseline over 3–6 months with
continued anti-inflammatory management. Patients stopped at the 6-week
clearance mark often regress because the KLK5 loop is still active.

## Non-acaricidal management options

### Azelaic acid 15–20%

**Mechanism:**
- Directly inhibits KLK5 activity (independent of LL-37)
- Inhibits kallikrein 7, meprin α/β
- Anti-inflammatory via NF-κB down-regulation
- Comedolytic

**Evidence:**
- FDA-approved for rosacea at 15% (Finacea gel) and 20% (Azelex cream)
- First-line for papulopustular rosacea; reduces KLK5 activity over 8–12 weeks
- Well-tolerated on face; **not formulated for ocular use** — periocular skin only, not lid margin

**Role:**
- Concurrent facial rosacea management while acaricide handles the lid margin
- 1–2× daily on papulopustular zones

### Doxycycline sub-antimicrobial (40 mg modified-release, Oracea)

**Mechanism:**
- At 40 mg/day, doxycycline concentration is below antimicrobial MIC but:
  - Directly inhibits matrix metalloproteinases (MMP-9 especially)
  - Reduces ROS production in neutrophils
  - Inhibits protein kinase C
  - Reduces angiogenic signaling (VEGF)
- Does NOT induce bacterial resistance at this dose (pivotal differentiation from traditional doxycycline)

**Evidence:**
- FDA-approved for rosacea 2006; Oracea brand (Galderma)
- Reduces papulopustular lesions without antibiotic pressure
- Benefit in ocular rosacea specifically well-documented (Sobolewska 2014 review; Arman 2015)

**Role:**
- **The single most useful systemic agent for chronic ocular rosacea**
- 40 mg daily × 3 months, reassess
- Can be continued long-term if symptom control requires
- Avoid in pregnancy, children <8, photosensitivity risk

### Omega-3 fatty acids (EPA + DHA)

**Mechanism:**
- Substrate-shift for prostaglandin synthesis: omega-3 → series-3 PGs + series-5 LTs (anti-inflammatory)
- Competes with arachidonic acid for COX/LOX enzymes
- Incorporated into meibomian-gland lipid output — improves meibum quality directly

**Evidence:**
- DREAM study (N Engl J Med 2018) — omega-3 vs placebo for dry eye, modest effect
- Smaller meibography trials show improved meibomian gland expression on imaging with long-term (3–6 month) supplementation

**Role:**
- Adjunct for MGD component of ocular rosacea
- Target: EPA 1000–2000 mg + DHA 500–1000 mg daily for ≥3 months
- Triglyceride forms (not ethyl ester) have better absorption
- Not a replacement for acaricide; additive for lipid-layer stability

### Topical calcineurin inhibitors (tacrolimus, pimecrolimus)

**Mechanism:**
- Calcineurin inhibition → reduced T-cell cytokine production
- Anti-inflammatory without steroid side effects (no skin atrophy, no cataract/glaucoma risk at low doses)

**Evidence:**
- Off-label for periocular rosacea and lid dermatitis
- Tacrolimus 0.03% ointment twice daily on periocular skin (not directly on lid margin) — reduces inflammation over 8–12 weeks
- Risk: potential for conjunctival irritation; "burning" sensation on application

**Role:**
- Refractory rosacea with lid-periocular component, especially if steroids were failing
- Pediatric rosacea / POD-spectrum cases

### Intense pulsed light (IPL)

**Mechanism:**
- 500–1200 nm pulsed light targets hemoglobin in telangiectatic vessels → vessel coagulation
- Secondary effects: thermal coagulation of meibomian gland contents (improved meibum), reported mild direct Demodex effect
- 3–4 sessions, 3–4 weeks apart

**Evidence:**
- Toyos 2015 J Clin Exp Ophthalmol — IPL for MGD with significant TBUT and symptom improvement
- Prieto 2002 Lasers Surg Med — earlier observation of skin improvement + mite reduction
- Increasingly offered in dry-eye clinics (Optilight, Lumenis M22)

**Role:**
- Refractory ocular rosacea with telangiectasia or chronic MGD
- $300–500/session, 3–4 sessions typical
- Adjunct to acaricide + doxycycline, not a substitute

### Clonidine / beta-blocker for flushing

**Mechanism:**
- Sympathetic output reduction → less vasodilatory flushing

**Evidence:**
- Clonidine 0.05–0.1 mg BID has been used anecdotally for severe flushing rosacea
- Beta-blockers (propranolol, carvedilol) also have flush-reduction literature
- Brimonidine 0.33% topical gel (Mirvaso) is FDA-approved for erythematotelangiectatic rosacea — α2-adrenergic agonist; rebound erythema is a known concern

**Role:**
- Flushing-dominant rosacea phenotype
- Consider after acaricide + doxycycline are optimized

### Hypochlorous acid lid sprays (reminder from attempt_004)

Not directly anti-inflammatory but reduces lid-margin bacterial biofilm
(Staphylococcus spp., Cutibacterium), which contributes to sustained
inflammation independent of Demodex.

## The ocular rosacea phenotype — specific management

**Diagnostic criteria (NROSCO working definition / AAO guidance):**

- Lid-margin telangiectasia
- Anterior + posterior blepharitis
- Meibomian gland dysfunction
- Recurrent chalazion/hordeolum
- ± corneal involvement (in severe cases: peripheral vascularization, marginal infiltrates, thinning)

**Often under-diagnosed** because patients present with "dry eye" and
ocular rosacea is a recognized differential only in dry-eye subspecialty
practices.

**Stepped protocol:**

**Week 0–6 (acaricidal induction):**
- TTO / Xdemvy on lids (attempt_002)
- Warm compress + lid massage 2×/day
- Omega-3 supplementation started
- Household decontamination (attempt_005)

**Week 2+ (layer anti-inflammation):**
- Doxycycline 40 mg sub-antimicrobial daily
- Topical metronidazole or azelaic acid on facial rosacea component
- Hypochlorous acid lid spray daily for bacterial biofilm

**Week 6–12 (consolidate):**
- Maintenance TTO 2–3×/week
- Continue doxycycline 40 mg — reassess at week 12
- Omega-3 continues
- IPL considered if telangiectasia persists

**Week 12+ (maintenance):**
- Doxycycline can often taper if symptoms stable
- TTO wipes 2×/week indefinite
- Omega-3 continues
- Escalate to IPL / calcineurin inhibitor if breakthrough

## Why this matters for the "treatment failure" question

The Xdemvy 44% non-response gap (Type B1 in gap.md) almost certainly
includes a large fraction of **correctly-cleared patients with ongoing
rosacea inflammation.** They read as "non-responders" on collarette +
symptom composite endpoints because the symptoms persist, but they are
mite-cleared. The clinical question isn't "why didn't the acaricide
work?" — it's "which downstream inflammation layer are we also treating?"

Reframe: Demodex is a **triggering mechanism**, not a sustaining
mechanism, in chronic rosacea-blepharitis. Acaricide removes the trigger.
Anti-inflammatory layered management addresses the sustainment.
Protocols that only do the first half regress.

---

## What this adds to the framework

1. **Acaricide is step 1, not the whole protocol.** Even a perfectly-
   executed TTO or Xdemvy course needs an anti-inflammatory overlay for
   chronic disease.
2. **Doxycycline 40 mg sub-antimicrobial is the highest-leverage single
   systemic agent** for ocular rosacea, independent of Demodex status.
3. **The "recurrent chalazion" signal means both things at once:** there's
   still mite reservoir (acaricidal intensification needed) AND there's
   sustained KLK5/LL-37 activity (anti-inflammatory adjunct needed). Treat
   both.
4. **IPL for refractory cases** is a legitimate third-line option; most
   practices will not suggest it before 6 months of medication trials.
