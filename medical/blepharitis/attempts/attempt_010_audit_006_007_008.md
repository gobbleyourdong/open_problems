## GREEN findings

- **G416** attempt_006 **KLK5/LL-37 self-sustaining loop** (L38-55):
  Demodex+B.oleronius → KLK5↑ → abnormal LL-37 → TLR2 → NF-κB →
  IL-8/IL-6/VEGF/MMP-9 → telangiectasia+inflammation+remodeling →
  NF-κB → KLK5↑ → loop persists after mites cleared. Mechanism
  explains the clinical puzzle (mites gone, symptoms persist).
  Matches therapeutic_convergence.md framework.
- **G417** attempt_006 **6 non-acaricidal agents** each with
  Mechanism/Evidence/Role structure: azelaic acid 15-20% (KLK5
  inhibitor, FDA Finacea/Azelex), doxycycline 40mg (MMP-9, FDA
  Oracea 2006), omega-3 EPA+DHA (meibum quality, DREAM study NEJM

## Non-audit observations

- **Blepharitis attempts 006-008 are the newest clinical content
  in the non-math corpus** (all untracked per git status). They
  show the highest citation-discipline of any disease subdir:
  FDA approval dates, brand names, formulation details, dose
  ranges, and gap.md cross-references per-attempt. The PMID gap
  (Y296-Y300) is the only systematic weakness — the author+year+
  journal citations are specific enough to find PMIDs but don't
  thread them.
- **attempt_007's route-reachability matrix** is the kind of
  clinical-decision tool that makes a protocol document
  operationally useful rather than academic. A clinician choosing
  between TTO wipe, Xdemvy drop, and oral ivermectin can read the
  matrix and match treatment to the patient's specific species
  profile. This pattern should be replicated wherever multi-agent
  multi-target decisions exist (e.g., t1dm/antiviral+immunomodulator
  +autophagy agent selection).
- **attempt_008 connects directly to biology/evolution/attempt_010**
  (Malassezia+C.acnes per-organism evolutionary framework) — the
  same organisms described at evolutionary scale in biology/ are
  described at clinical-treatment scale in blepharitis/. The
  framework's cross-directory integration works: evolutionary
  understanding → clinical-decision support.
