## Verified entries

### Gao 2005 ✅ (foundational Demodex-blepharitis paper)

**Gao YY, Di Pascuale MA, Li W, Liu DT, Baradaran-Rafii A, Elizondo A,
Kawakita T, Raju VK, Tseng SC.**
*High prevalence of Demodex in eyelashes with cylindrical dandruff.*
**Invest Ophthalmol Vis Sci** 2005;46(9):3089–3094.
**DOI: 10.1167/iovs.05-0275**

PMID verification deferred (direct search did not return the PMID
explicitly; can be looked up at PubMed via the DOI). Per-run files
may cite by DOI until PMID is PubMed-confirmed.

**Use in corpus**: landmark paper establishing **collarettes (cylindrical
dandruff) as a specific clinical sign of Demodex infestation** —
the methodology foundation for all downstream Demodex-blepharitis
work. Primary source for the "every cylindrical-dandruff case had
Demodex" framing in attempts.

**Cross-subdir**: blepharitis/ primary; persistent_organisms/
(Demodex as persistent-organism exemplar); dysbiosis/ (skin-microbiome
cross-refs).

### Koo 2012 ✅ — tea tree oil lid-scrub RCT (also corpus's "Zhao 2012" — **same paper**, author-drift)

**Koo H, Kim TH, Kim KW, Wee SW, Chun YS, Kim JC.**
*Ocular surface discomfort and Demodex: effect of tea tree oil eyelid
scrub in Demodex blepharitis.*
**J Korean Med Sci** 2012;27(12):1574–1579.
**PMID: 23255861**  **DOI: 10.3346/jkms.2012.27.12.1574**

⚠️ **Author-drift flag**: corpus citations of **both "Koo 2012" and
"Zhao 2012" resolve to this same paper**. The first author is Koo H,
not Zhao. The "Zhao 2012" appearances in per-run files are
**FM1-class author-drift** (corpus-memory-substitution of a different
author name for the real first author). This is a new finding beyond
year-drift and journal-drift: **FM1d author-drift**. Adds to the
failure-mode catalog alongside FM1a/b/c. Per-run files citing "Zhao
2012" should be corrected to "Koo 2012" with PMID 23255861.

**Use in corpus**: 335 patients with ocular discomfort; 84% positive
for Demodex; randomized eyelid-scrub (n=106 TTO vs n=54 control for
1 month); TTO group Demodex count reduced 4.0→3.2 (p=0.004), OSDI
34.5→24.1 (p=0.001). Control group: no significant mite reduction.
Foundation for tea-tree-oil / T4O as efficacious against ocular
Demodex.

**Cross-subdir**: blepharitis/ primary; persistent_organisms/ (Demodex
treatment); dysbiosis/ (skin-microbiome treatment cross-refs).

### Lacey 2007 ✅ — Demodex/B. oleronius symbiosis

**Lacey N, Kavanagh K, Tseng SC.**
*Mite-related bacterial antigens stimulate inflammatory cells in rosacea.*
**Br J Dermatol** 2007;157(3):474–481.
**PMID: 17596156**  **DOI: 10.1111/j.1365-2133.2007.08028.x**

**Use in corpus**: establishes that **B. oleronius antigens** (derived
from the Demodex-symbiont bacterium, first isolated from termite
hindgut) stimulate inflammatory cells in papulopustular rosacea
patients. Primary reference for the "Demodex + bacteria together
drive rosacea" framing, linking the mite presence to B. oleronius-
mediated immune activation.

**Cross-subdir**: blepharitis/ + persistent_organisms/ (Demodex-bacteria
symbiosis); dysbiosis/ (skin-microbiome cross-refs).
