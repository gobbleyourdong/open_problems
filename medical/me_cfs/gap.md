### Gap 2: The LAMP2 Block — New Mechanistic Gap
Pattern 015 (GSE184831, persistent CVB1) showed LAMP2 down -2.7x in infected cells. Pattern 016 confirmed this is consistent across datasets. This means:

**Autophagy initiation alone is not sufficient.** The virus blocks lysosomal fusion. Fasting induces autophagosome formation (ATG7 up) but without LAMP2, lysosomes cannot fuse — "zombie autophagy." The kill step is blocked.

**Protocol implication**: Add lysosomal enhancement to the ME/CFS arm:
- **Trehalose** (1–3 g/day): activates TFEB → lysosomal biogenesis → more lysosomes → overcomes per-lysosome LAMP2 deficit
- **Sulforaphane** (in broccoli sprouts): TFEB activator, also NRF2 → mitochondrial protection
- Addresses both the LAMP2 gap AND the MT-ND deficit simultaneously

### Gap 3: Mitochondrial Recovery Timeline
MT-ND3 is DOWN 17% (p=0.002) in ME/CFS cfRNA. Post-exertional malaise = ATP failure. Recovery requires:
1. Reducing the viral burden driving mitochondrial dysfunction (fluoxetine + fasting)
2. Restoring Complex I expression (CoQ10 600mg, NAD+ precursors, mitophagy)
3. Membrane repair (omega-3, high-dose EPA/DHA)

**Timeline gap**: how long does MT-ND3 expression take to normalize after viral clearance? No data. This is the rate-limiting step for functional recovery (PEM won't resolve until Complex I normalizes). Estimate: 6–12 months after viral clearance, based on mitochondrial dynamics data from other conditions.

### Gap 4: NK Cell Impotence Mechanism
NK cells are ARMED (perforin +52%) but patients are still sick. The problem is not NK activation but target recognition:
- CVB TD mutants downregulate CXADR (CVB receptor -32x)
- Downregulated MHC-I on infected cells could further impair NK recognition
- Result: NK cells cannot identify infected cells → cannot kill them

**This shifts the ME/CFS therapeutic strategy**: NK enhancement (IL-15, cold exposure) is unlikely to help if targets are invisible. Cell-autonomous autophagy — not immune killing — is the correct path.

## The Anti-Problem

"What do post-viral patients who recover fully look like?"

Most enteroviral infections resolve. The 42% who develop ME/CFS differ in:
1. **NK cell dysfunction** — reduced CD56bright cells, impaired cytotoxicity
2. **Interferon deficiency** — blunted IFN-alpha response during ACUTE phase (prevents TD clearance)
3. **Gut dysbiosis** — reduced butyrate-producing bacteria → reduced Tregs
4. **Autonomic dysfunction** — suggests viral persistence in autonomic ganglia
5. **FOXP1 suppression** (new) — tissue-local Treg impairment via FOXP1 axis

The protocol addresses #2 (WHM → cold shock → IFN induction), #3 (butyrate supplementation), partially #1 (vitamin D + autophagy), and #5 (butyrate at high dose promotes FOXP1 via HDAC inhibition).
