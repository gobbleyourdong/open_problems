## What We Know Now

1. **ME/CFS is the hardest of the 12 diseases** — multi-organ, no biomarker, heterogeneous etiology. But it's also the largest affected population (2.5M Americans) with the greatest unmet need.

2. **PEM makes standard interventions dangerous.** Exercise, which is beneficial in every other CVB disease, triggers crashes in ME/CFS. The protocol MUST clear the virus before reintroducing activity. This is the opposite of the deconditioning advice patients typically receive.

3. **NK cell dysfunction is the rate-limiting step.** Without functional NK cells, the body can't clear CVB-infected cells regardless of what antivirals you add. NK restoration (cold exposure, sleep, selenium, zinc, CoQ10) must come FIRST.

4. **The vicious cycle model (6 coupled variables) explains why ME/CFS doesn't spontaneously resolve.** Disease and health are both stable attractors. Single interventions fail because the system snaps back. Must push all variables simultaneously to cross the threshold.

5. **ME/CFS may be only ~42% CVB-driven.** Stratification by enteroviral status is essential. The anti-inflammatory stack helps regardless; the antiviral stack helps only the CVB subset.

6. **Long COVID is the same disease with better funding.** A dual-arm trial (CVB-ME/CFS + Long COVID) leverages $1.15B in NIH RECOVER funding. The ME/CFS community may get treatment faster through the Long COVID door.

## The Gap (Updated)

The original gap was "no diagnostic test, no treatment, no biomarker."

After 3 attempts: **the gap is STRATIFICATION.** Which ME/CFS patients are CVB-driven? The protocol helps them. Which are EBV-driven, autoimmune, or metabolic? They need different protocols.

Enteroviral RT-PCR in stool or muscle biopsy is the stratification test. If positive → CVB protocol. If negative → investigate other triggers.
