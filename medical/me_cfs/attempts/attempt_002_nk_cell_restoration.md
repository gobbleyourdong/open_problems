# Attempt 002: NK Cell Restoration — The ME/CFS-Specific Target

## The Insight

T1DM attempt 062 (Strategy 7) identifies NK cells as "snipers" that kill CVB-infected cells via missing-self recognition. In T1DM, NK cells are one of seven strategies. In ME/CFS, NK cell dysfunction is **THE defining immunological abnormality**.

```
ME/CFS patients:
- NK cell cytotoxicity: 40-60% of normal (Brenu 2011, Hardcastle 2015)
- CD56bright NK cells: reduced (Brenu 2012)
- Perforin/granzyme expression: reduced
- Correlates with symptom severity (sicker patients = worse NK function)
```

**If you fix NK cells, you fix ME/CFS.** Strong NK cells clear CVB-infected cells → viral persistence breaks → inflammation resolves → symptoms improve.

## Why NK Cells Fail in ME/CFS

### Hypothesis 1: Exhaustion
- Chronic viral stimulation → NK cell anergy (like T cell exhaustion in chronic viral infections)
- The cells are THERE but functionally impaired
- Analogous to PD-1+ exhausted CD8+ T cells in chronic HCV/HIV
- **Test**: check NKG2A (inhibitory receptor) and Tim-3/PD-1 expression on NK cells

### Hypothesis 2: Supply failure
- Chronic inflammation depletes NK cell precursors in bone marrow
- Fewer new NK cells generated → reduced circulating pool
- **Test**: NK cell counts (not just function) + bone marrow IL-15 (NK maturation factor)

### Hypothesis 3: Metabolic impairment
- Mitochondrial dysfunction in ME/CFS affects ALL cells including NK cells
- NK cell killing requires energy-expensive granule release
- Mitochondrial defect → insufficient ATP for degranulation
- **Test**: NK cell mitochondrial membrane potential (JC-1 assay), oxygen consumption rate

## The NK Restoration Protocol

### Tier 1: From T1DM arsenal (attempt 062, Strategy 7)
| Intervention | NK mechanism | Evidence |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| Cold exposure (2-3 min) | Norepinephrine → β2-AR → NK mobilization from spleen | Brenner 1999: 6-fold NK increase post-cold |
| Exercise (moderate, 30 min) | Catecholamines → NK mobilization + activation | Nieman 2019: meta-analysis |
| Sleep (7-9 hours) | NK activity peaks during slow-wave sleep | Irwin 2015: 1 night deprivation = 70% NK drop |
| Selenium (200 μg/day) | Selenoprotein P → NK granule function | Broome 2004 |
| Zinc (15 mg/day) | NK cell maturation via zinc-finger transcription factors | Prasad 2008 |

**THE ME/CFS PROBLEM**: exercise triggers PEM. Cold exposure is safe. Sleep optimization is critical. Selenium/zinc are easy.

### Tier 2: ME/CFS-specific additions
| Intervention | NK mechanism | Evidence |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| **IL-15** (if available) | Primary NK cell survival/proliferation cytokine | Carson 1994: IL-15 KO = no NK cells |
| **Low-dose naltrexone (LDN) 4.5mg** | Upregulates endorphins → opioid growth factor receptor → immune modulation including NK cells | Younger 2014: ME/CFS pilot, 30% symptom improvement |
| **CoQ10 (300mg/day)** | Mitochondrial support → NK cells can degranulate properly | Castro-Marrero 2015: ME/CFS CoQ10 trial |
| **D-ribose (5g TID)** | ATP precursor → energy for NK cell function | Teitelbaum 2006: ME/CFS energy study |
| **Nicotinamide riboside (NR, 300mg)** | NAD+ precursor → mitochondrial function | Preclinical: NAD+ depletion in ME/CFS |

### Tier 3: Checkpoint release (experimental)
If NK exhaustion is the mechanism:
- **Anti-NKG2A** (monalizumab) — blocks the inhibitory receptor → reactivates exhausted NK cells
- Used in oncology (head and neck cancer)
- Repurposing for ME/CFS: release the brakes on NK cells that are being held back
- **Highly experimental but mechanistically sound**

## The Sequencing (PEM-Safe)

```
Week 1-4:   Selenium + Zinc + CoQ10 + D-ribose + sleep optimization
            (Build the metabolic foundation for NK function)

Week 5-8:   Add LDN 1.5mg → 3mg → 4.5mg
            (Immune modulation, NK upregulation)

Week 9-12:  Add cold face immersion (30 sec) → cold shower (30 sec → 1 min)
            (NK mobilization without muscular exertion)

Week 13+:   Add NR if available. Measure NK cytotoxicity.
            If improved: cautiously introduce walking (the exercise payoff)
```

## Measurement
- NK cell cytotoxicity assay (K562 target cell killing) at baseline, week 8, week 16
- CD56bright vs CD56dim ratio (flow cytometry)
- NKG2A expression (exhaustion marker)
- Perforin/granzyme B intracellular staining
- Correlate with symptom scores (SF-36, Chalder fatigue scale)

## The Prediction
If NK function improves by >50% relative to baseline:
- CVB-infected cells cleared more effectively
- Viral persistence breaks
- Inflammation subsides
- Symptoms improve 4-8 weeks after NK restoration

**NK restoration is the RATE-LIMITING STEP in ME/CFS recovery.** The virus can't be cleared without functional NK cells. The protocol must fix NK cells FIRST, then the antiviral stack can finish the job.
