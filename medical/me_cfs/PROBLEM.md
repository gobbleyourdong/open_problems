# Myalgic Encephalomyelitis / Chronic Fatigue Syndrome (ME/CFS)

## The Problem
Debilitating chronic illness characterized by post-exertional malaise, cognitive dysfunction, unrefreshing sleep, and autonomic dysfunction. No FDA-approved treatment. No diagnostic test. Enteroviral persistence (CVB) found in 42% of ME/CFS patients vs 9% controls. Affects ~2.5M Americans.

## Status: UNSOLVED

## Key Biology

| Component | What | Role |
|-----------|------|------|
| CVB persistence | TD mutants in muscle/CNS tissue | Chronic low-grade inflammation driver |
| Enteroviral RNA | Detectable in muscle biopsies | Found at much higher rates in ME/CFS than controls |
| NK cell dysfunction | Reduced cytotoxicity | Can't clear persistently infected cells |
| Neuroinflammation | Microglial activation in brain | PET imaging shows widespread neuroinflammation |
| Mitochondrial dysfunction | Impaired oxidative phosphorylation | May result from chronic viral protease activity |
| Autoantibodies | Anti-β2-adrenergic, anti-muscarinic | Autonomic dysfunction component |
| Post-exertional malaise | Symptom worsening after exertion | Hallmark symptom, unique to ME/CFS |

## The Disease Model
1. Acute CVB infection (often "just a cold")
2. Incomplete clearance → TD mutant persistence in muscle, CNS, gut
3. Chronic low-grade inflammation → immune exhaustion
4. NK cell dysfunction → can't clear infected cells → persistence loop
5. Mitochondrial damage from chronic viral protease activity
6. Neuroinflammation → cognitive dysfunction ("brain fog")
7. Autoantibody development → autonomic dysfunction
8. Self-reinforcing: fatigue → less exercise → less AMPK → less autophagy → more viral persistence
