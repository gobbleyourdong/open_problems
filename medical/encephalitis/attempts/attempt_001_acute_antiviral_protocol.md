# Attempt 001: Acute CVB Encephalitis — Emergency Antiviral Protocol

## Approach
Design an emergency antiviral protocol for acute CVB encephalitis. This is the only one of the 12 diseases where minutes matter and where the T1DM protocol's "slow and steady" approach is insufficient.

## The Protocol (Escalating Urgency)

### Hour 0-6: Diagnosis + First-Line
1. **Confirm enteroviral encephalitis**: CSF enteroviral RT-PCR (results in 2-4 hours)
2. **Start empiric treatment while awaiting PCR**:
   - IVIG 1g/kg (passive anti-CVB antibodies — works immediately)
   - Fluoxetine 20mg via NG tube (2C ATPase inhibitor — onset in hours)
   - Dexamethasone 10mg IV (cerebral edema — controversial but standard for bacterial meningitis)
3. **Supportive**: ICU admission, seizure prophylaxis (levetiracetam), ICP monitoring if needed

### Hour 6-24: Escalation if PCR Positive
4. **Pocapavir** (capsid inhibitor) — compassionate use/expanded access
   - Contact FDA for emergency IND if not available
   - Directly blocks CVB entry into new cells
5. **Repeat IVIG** at 24 hours if clinical deterioration
6. **Continuous EEG monitoring** — seizure detection

### Days 2-14: Sustained Treatment
7. Continue fluoxetine 20mg daily
8. Taper dexamethasone over 5-7 days
9. **BHB via exogenous ketones** (NLRP3 suppression → reduce microglial activation)
   - Ketone ester 25g TID or IV sodium BHB if available
10. **Omega-3 emulsion IV** if oral not possible (neuroprotective)
11. Serial MRI at day 3, 7, 14 (track inflammation, edema, necrosis)

### Weeks 2-12: Recovery Phase
12. Transition to oral protocol (full T1DM-adapted protocol)
13. Fluoxetine continue × 6 months
14. FMD when medically stable (autophagy → clear residual TD mutants from CNS)
15. Neurorehabilitation as needed
16. ME/CFS monitoring protocol (see me_cfs/attempts/attempt_001)

## Critical Considerations

### Dexamethasone controversy
- PRO: reduces cerebral edema, prevents herniation
- CON: immunosuppression may impair viral clearance
- RESOLUTION: use short course (5-7 days) with concurrent antivirals. The antivirals handle clearance; steroids handle edema. Both are needed.

### Neonatal modification
- IVIG dose: 500mg/kg (neonatal dosing)
- Fluoxetine: NOT established in neonates — use pocapavir + IVIG only
- No dexamethasone in neonates (risk of NEC, perforation)

## What This Solves
- Acute viral replication (IVIG + pocapavir + fluoxetine — three antivirals)
- Cerebral edema (dexamethasone)
- Neuroinflammation (BHB, omega-3)
- Chronic persistence prevention (fluoxetine continuation + FMD)

## What This Doesn't Solve
- Neurons already dead → permanent deficits
- This is damage limitation, not reversal
- The real solution is prevention (vaccine, or early antiviral during meningitis phase)
