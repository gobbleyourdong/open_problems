# CVB Encephalitis — THE WALL

## One Sentence

The wall for encephalitis is 48 hours: if pocapavir is given within 48 hours of confirmed CVB encephalitis, TD mutant formation is largely prevented and permanent neurological sequelae are avoidable; after 48 hours, the TD population is established and the 1.5-2 year neuronal clearance clock begins.

## The Disease

CVB encephalitis (brain parenchymal infection) is the rarest and most serious CVB presentation:
- Incidence: 1-5 per million per year (symptomatic CVB encephalitis)
- Mortality: 1-5% acutely; higher in neonates/immunocompromised
- Sequelae: seizure disorder, cognitive impairment, behavioral changes in survivors
- Time course: acute symptoms peak days 3-7, "recovery" over weeks, but neurological sequelae persist in ~30-40%

## The 48-Hour Window

The entire course of the campaign, analyzed through the LAMP2 lens, reveals a unique property of encephalitis: **it is the one CVB disease where an acute antiviral within 48 hours can prevent ALL long-term consequences.**

Here's why:

```
Neuronal LAMP2 baseline: 0.6× average (post-mitotic cells, low autophagy)
After CVB infection: κ_neuron = 0.6/2.7 = 0.22

Timeline of TD establishment in neurons:
  Hour 0-24:   WT CVB replicates actively → many viral genome copies
               TD variants forming at low rate (replication errors)
               But: TD population NEGLIGIBLE vs WT
  Hour 24-48:  WT continues → TD population doubling
               ~1-5% of viral population becoming TD
               LAMP2 suppression beginning but partial
  Hour 48-72:  TD population reaches threshold for self-sustaining persistence
               LAMP2 suppression entrenching
  Days 3-7:    TD population consolidated in multiple neuronal niches
               Immune system can clear WT → "patient recovering"
               TD persists indefinitely → 1.5-2 year neuronal clearance clock starts
```

**If pocapavir is given at hour 0-48**:
- Pocapavir (picomolar IC50 for all CVB capsid assembly) stops WT replication almost completely
- With WT stopped: TD generation rate drops to near zero (TD arise from WT replication errors)
- The tiny TD population that exists at hour 48 is still below the persistence consolidation threshold
- Immune system + background autophagy clears both WT and nascent TDs
- Result: no persistent neurological infection → no TD-driven long-term damage → no ME/CFS, no chronic neurological sequelae

**If treatment starts at hour 72+**:
- TD population is established and consolidating
- Pocapavir still clears WT (helpful acutely)
- But TD mutants don't depend on WT-type replication → pocapavir has minimal effect on established TDs
- 1.5-2 year neuronal clearance protocol begins (trehalose 3g + FMD + fluoxetine)

## The Drugs

### Pocapavir (first 48 hours — the critical window)
- **What**: picomolar inhibitor of CVB capsid assembly (VP1-VP4 complex)
- **Activity**: completely blocks WT CVB replication at <1 nM concentrations
- **Clearance**: achieves CSF levels; brain penetration confirmed
- **Status**: research compound; FDA expanded access for life-threatening enteroviral infections
- **Availability**: contact V-1 Pharmaceuticals (developer) or through academic centers running Phase 1 trials
- **Caveat**: requires confirmed CVB diagnosis (LP + enteroviral PCR) before administration

### Fluoxetine (parallel with pocapavir)
- Complementary mechanism (2C ATPase vs capsid assembly)
- Achieves 15× plasma in brain (4.5 μM at 20mg >> IC50)
- Can be started immediately at symptom onset without waiting for PCR confirmation
- Much more available than pocapavir

### IVIG
- Provides passive anti-CVB antibodies to neutralize extracellular WT
- Especially important in neonates and immunocompromised
- Standard of care for severe enteroviral encephalitis

## The LAMP2/Neuronal Protocol for Established Encephalitis (beyond 48 hours)

Once TD mutants are established (missed the window), the long-term protocol applies:

| Phase | Components | Duration |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Acute (weeks 1-4) | Pocapavir (if available) + fluoxetine + IVIG + steroids (if cerebral edema) | Until WT cleared |
| Post-acute (months 1-6) | Fluoxetine + trehalose 3g/day + butyrate 4-6g/day (FOXP1/microglial) | Ongoing |
| Chronic clearance (months 6-24) | Same + monthly FMD | Until clinical recovery |
| Monitoring | cfRNA panel at 6, 12, 18 months | Until MT-ND3 + STAT2 normalize |

**Expected course**:
- Glial CVB clears in ~5-7 months (κ_glia ≈ 0.67, faster)
- Neuronal CVB clears in ~14 months with trehalose (κ: 0.22 → 0.50 with trehalose)
- Cognitive recovery LAGS viral clearance by 3-6 months (mitochondrial repair takes time)

## The New FOXP1/Microglial Insight

Microglial FOXP1 suppression by CVB persistence creates neuroinflammation independent of direct neuronal infection. Brain fog in post-encephalitis syndrome ≠ "dead neurons" — it is ACTIVE microglial neuroinflammation driven by FOXP1 suppression.

**High-dose butyrate (4-6g/day) for post-encephalitis brain fog**:
- HDAC inhibition → FOXP1 restoration in microglial cells
- Reduced microglial activation → reduced neuroinflammation → brain fog improves
- Timeline: 3-6 months after starting butyrate

This explains why many encephalitis survivors have persistent cognitive symptoms despite "cleared" infection (by standard PCR): the neuroinflammation continues even after WT is gone, driven by FOXP1-impaired microglia. The protocol specifically addresses this.

## The Numbers

- ~1,500-5,000 CVB encephalitis cases in the US annually
- 30-40% with persistent neurological sequelae
- 50% of post-encephalitis chronic fatigue = potential ME/CFS overlap
- If pocapavir within 48 hours reaches even 25% of eligible patients → ~375-1,250 severe sequelae cases prevented annually

## The Wall

The wall for encephalitis has three components:

1. **Pocapavir access**: the drug exists. It's in expanded access. The wall is that emergency physicians don't know to call for it within 48 hours of CVB LP diagnosis. **Solution**: clinical protocol update — any LP confirming enteroviral encephalitis triggers immediate pocapavir/fluoxetine order.

2. **LP to result turnaround**: enteroviral PCR takes 24-48 hours at many centers. If the 48-hour window closes before confirmation arrives, the window is missed. **Solution**: LP point-of-care enteroviral PCR (FilmArray) or empirical treatment at first CSF lymphocytosis + viral prodrome.

3. **Drug availability**: pocapavir is research-use; fluoxetine is available. **Immediate action**: start fluoxetine 20mg at suspicion of CVB encephalitis (safe, available, and partially effective even without confirmed diagnosis). Reserve pocapavir for confirmed cases.

**The pocapavir 48-hour window is the most time-sensitive intervention in the entire campaign.** Every other intervention can be started days or weeks later. This one cannot.
