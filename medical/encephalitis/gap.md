# CVB Encephalitis — Gap Analysis (Initial)

## The Gap

Unlike meningitis (meninges only, self-limiting), encephalitis involves brain parenchyma — neurons die. This is the most dangerous CNS manifestation of CVB.

### The treatment gap
No antiviral exists for acute CVB encephalitis. Pleconaril/pocapavir (capsid inhibitors) have been used compassionately but have no RCT data. Corticosteroids may help (reduce inflammation) or harm (suppress antiviral immunity).

### The damage gap
Dead neurons don't regenerate. Like cardiomyocytes in DCM, the damage is permanent. The goal is:
1. Stop viral replication FAST (before more neurons die)
2. Reduce neuroinflammation (microglial activation causes bystander damage)
3. Support surviving neurons (neuroprotection)

## Protocol (Acute CVB Encephalitis)
This is the only one of the 12 diseases where the situation is genuinely emergent.

### Acute phase (ICU-level)
- Pocapavir (compassionate use) — capsid inhibitor, directly antiviral
- IVIG (high-titer anti-CVB) — passive immunity
- Fluoxetine 20mg (oral/NG tube) — 2C ATPase inhibitor
- Dexamethasone — reduce cerebral edema (controversial: may impair viral clearance)
- Seizure prophylaxis as needed

### Subacute phase
- Continue fluoxetine
- BHB (exogenous ketones or ketogenic diet) — NLRP3 suppression → reduce microglial activation
- Omega-3 high-dose — neuroprotective, anti-inflammatory in CNS
- Monitor: serial MRI, EEG, neurocognitive testing

### Chronic phase
- Full ME/CFS-adapted protocol if persistent symptoms
- Neurorehabilitation

## Anti-Problem
"Why do most CVB CNS infections stop at meningitis and not progress to encephalitis?"

The BBB is the gatekeeper. Encephalitis occurs when:
- Viremia is extremely high (overwhelms BBB)
- BBB is compromised (neonates, prior CNS inflammation)
- Immune system is deficient (neonates, immunocompromised)

The anti-problem suggests: **early antiviral treatment during meningitis prevents encephalitis progression.**

## Convergence
Encephalitis is rare but catastrophic. The prevention strategy is the same as all other CVB diseases: clear the virus early, don't let it reach dangerous organs. The CVB vaccine prevents this entirely.
