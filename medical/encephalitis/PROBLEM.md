# Viral Encephalitis (Coxsackievirus B)

## The Problem
Inflammation of the brain parenchyma caused by CVB infection. Rarer and more serious than meningitis. Presents with confusion, seizures, altered consciousness. Can cause permanent neurological damage. More common in neonates and immunocompromised.

## Status: UNSOLVED (supportive care only, no antiviral)

## Key Biology

| Component | What | Role |
|-----------|------|------|
| Brain parenchyma | Neurons, glia | Direct viral infection → neuronal death |
| Microglia | Brain-resident macrophages | Neuroinflammatory response to CVB |
| CVB1-5 | Causative serotypes | CNS invasion, neuronal tropism |
| Neonatal vulnerability | Immature immune system + BBB | Highest risk for severe encephalitis |

## Connection to T1DM
- Neuroinflammation from persistent CVB may contribute to autonomic neuropathy
- Brain fog / cognitive symptoms in T1DM may have a persistent CVB component (overlap with ME/CFS)
- The T1DM protocol's anti-inflammatory stack (WHM, BHB, omega-3) would reduce neuroinflammation
