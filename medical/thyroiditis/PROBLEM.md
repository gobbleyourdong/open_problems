# Subacute / Autoimmune Thyroiditis — Disease 16

## Classification
**Category 1**: CVB-associated endocrine autoimmunity

## Connection to CVB
- CVB is the leading identified cause of subacute (de Quervain's) thyroiditis
- CVB detected in thyroid tissue in several case series
- Molecular mimicry: CVB peptides cross-react with thyroid antigens
- FOXP1 suppression (−67x in persistent CVB) → local Treg failure → anti-TPO / anti-thyroglobulin autoimmunity

## Clinical Presentation
- **Subacute phase** (weeks 1-4): painful thyroid, transient hyperthyroidism (follicle dumping)
- **Hypothyroid phase** (weeks 4-12): TSH rises as follicles depleted
- **Recovery** (months 3-12): 85% recover normal thyroid function
- **Permanent hypothyroidism**: 15-30% do NOT recover → lifelong levothyroxine

## The Gap
- Standard treatment: NSAIDs ± prednisone (anti-inflammatory only — same pattern as pericarditis)
- No antiviral component → TD mutants may establish → permanent follicle destruction
- 15-30% who don't recover = those where TD mutants cause chronic ongoing damage

## The Protocol Prediction
- Fluoxetine + FMD + trehalose (same as all CVB diseases)
- Thyroid-specific: κ_effective ≈ 0.37 (LAMP2 correction)
- Clearance time: ~3-4 months (similar to heart)
- Secondary prediction: anti-TPO titer declines as CVB clears

## T1DM Connection
- 25-30% of T1DM patients have autoimmune thyroid disease (polyglandular syndrome)
- Same CVB persistence → same FOXP1 mechanism → both endocrine organs affected simultaneously
- T1DM clearance protocol should simultaneously address thyroid

## Priority
LOW for independent study; HIGH as part of T1DM protocol monitoring (thyroid antibodies should be checked at baseline and at 6-month intervals for all T1DM patients on protocol).
