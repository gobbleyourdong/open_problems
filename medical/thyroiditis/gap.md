# CVB Thyroiditis — Gap Analysis (Initial)

## The Gap

### Layer 1: Establish CVB persistence in human thyroid tissue
- Evidence for CVB in thyroid: several case reports, one systematic study (Desailloud 2009)
- No GEO transcriptomics for CVB-infected human thyroid tissue (analogous to GSE184831)
- **Gap**: FOXP1, LAMP2, DMD expression in CVB-infected thyroid follicular cells not measured
- **How to close**: thyroid biopsy RNA-seq from subacute thyroiditis patients during acute phase

### Layer 2: Distinguish subacute from Hashimoto's mechanism
- Subacute: acute CVB infection → thyroid destruction → 85% recover
- Hashimoto's: chronic autoimmunity → progressive → rarely clears naturally
- **Hypothesis**: Hashimoto's = TD mutants in thyroid → FOXP1 chronically suppressed → anti-TPO persists
- **How to close**: compare thyroid CVB PCR positive vs negative Hashimoto's patients → anti-TPO trajectory

### Layer 3: Protocol prediction validation
- If protocol clears thyroid CVB: anti-TPO should decline and TSH should normalize (in patients not yet hypothyroid)
- This is a MEASURABLE secondary outcome of the T1DM patient trial
- **Cost**: ~$30 to add anti-TPO to the standard monitoring bloodwork

## What's Known (Grade B-)
- CVB causes subacute thyroiditis: Grade B (multiple case series, viral titers elevated)
- FOXP1 mechanism applies to thyroid: Grade B- (indirect inference from mechanism in other tissues)
- Protocol should address thyroid: Grade C (mechanistic inference, no trial data)

## Priority
Secondary to T1DM (same patient, same protocol). High priority for monitoring (add anti-TPO to T1DM protocol bloodwork). Low priority for independent trial.
