# Infertility — Anti-Problem

## The Question
"What do couples who conceive immediately on the first cycle look like?"

## The Answer

These couples — sometimes called "fertile myrtles" — are not lucky. They're optimized. Even when they don't realize it.

### Female phenotype (the easy conceiver)
1. **Age <30** (egg quality declines starting at 32, sharply after 35)
2. **BMI 19-25** (both extremes reduce fertility)
3. **No metabolic dysfunction** (insulin sensitive, normal HbA1c, normal lipids)
4. **TSH <2.5** (not just "normal" — actively optimal for reproduction)
5. **Vitamin D 40-60 ng/mL**
6. **Regular 26-32 day cycles** with clear ovulation
7. **No chronic inflammation** (low CRP, no autoimmune disease)
8. **Intact gut and vaginal microbiome** (Lactobacillus-dominant vaginal flora)
9. **Active Treg population** (immune system tolerates the embryo correctly)
10. **Good sleep** (7-9 hours, consistent — reproductive hormones are made during sleep)

### Male phenotype (the easy father)
1. **Sperm count >40M/mL with >50% progressive motility**
2. **Normal viscosity** (semen liquefies in <30 min)
3. **Sperm DNA fragmentation <15%**
4. **Testosterone 500-900 ng/dL** (mid-normal)
5. **No varicocele** (or treated)
6. **No chronic prostate inflammation**
7. **Sleep 7-9 hours** (testosterone is made during deep sleep)
8. **Normal BMI**
9. **No smoking, minimal alcohol**
10. **Adequate zinc, selenium, vitamin D**

### Couple-level
1. **Sex during the fertile window** (2-3 days before ovulation through ovulation day)
2. **Frequency every 2-3 days** during fertile window (not "saving up" — that worsens motility and viscosity)
3. **No sperm-toxic lubricants** (no saliva, no KY, no Astroglide — use Pre-Seed only)
4. **Both partners non-stressed** (cortisol disrupts the HPG axis in both sexes)
5. **No environmental endocrine disruptors** (BPA, phthalates, atrazine)

## Why Most People Don't Match the Profile

The "easy conceiver" phenotype is **modern industrialized civilization's anti-phenotype.** We:
- Stay in school longer → conceive later → past optimal age
- Eat processed food → metabolic dysfunction → insulin resistance → ovulatory issues
- Live with chronic stress → cortisol disruption
- Sleep less → reproductive hormone deficits
- Spend life indoors → vitamin D deficiency
- Take antibiotics often → microbiome dysbiosis
- Use plastic everything → BPA → endocrine disruption
- Drink alcohol regularly → sperm DNA damage + liver impact on hormones
- Use hot tubs / laptops on lap → testicular heat damage
- Are exposed to environmental toxins → unknown reproductive impact

**Modern infertility is largely a lifestyle disease.** Not all of it — there are real structural, genetic, and pathological causes — but a large fraction of "unexplained infertility" is just optimization debt.

## The Single Most Predictive Factor

If you had to pick ONE variable that distinguishes easy conceivers from infertile couples, it would be: **mitochondrial function**.

- Female: egg quality = mitochondrial quality (eggs have ~100K mitochondria each)
- Male: sperm motility = mitochondrial energy (sperm tail is powered by mitochondrial ATP)
- Embryo: cleavage and implantation depend on mitochondrial inheritance from the egg

CoQ10, NAD+ precursors, omega-3, exercise, sleep, fasting → all enhance mitochondrial function. Aging, oxidative stress, inflammation, processed food, alcohol → all damage mitochondria.

**Mitochondria are where fertility lives.** The protocol's mitochondrial components (CoQ10, NR/NMN, omega-3, BHB, exercise) are not optional add-ons — they ARE the protocol.
