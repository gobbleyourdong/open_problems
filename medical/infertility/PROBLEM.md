# Infertility (Male and Female Factor)

## The Problem
Couples failing to conceive after 12 months of unprotected intercourse (6 months if female age >35). Affects ~15% of couples worldwide. Roughly equal contribution from male factor (40%), female factor (40%), and combined/unexplained (20%). Standard treatment escalates from lifestyle → fertility drugs → IUI → IVF/ICSI, but the upstream optimization (gut, mitochondria, hormones, inflammation) is often skipped.

## Status: TREATABLE BUT POORLY OPTIMIZED — IVF success rates plateau at ~30-40% per cycle despite decades of research

## The Cascade (Couple-Level)

1. Conception requires: viable sperm + viable egg + open tube + receptive endometrium + correct timing
2. Sperm must: be made (spermatogenesis 74 days) → liquefy from semen → swim through cervix → reach tube
3. Egg must: develop quality (3-4 month follicular cycle) → ovulate on schedule → enter tube → meet sperm
4. Embryo must: form correctly → travel to uterus → implant in receptive endometrium
5. Pregnancy must: survive first trimester immune challenges
6. **Failure at ANY step = no baby**. Most couples have failure at multiple steps.
