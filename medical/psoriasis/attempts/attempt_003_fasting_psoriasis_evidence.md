# Attempt 003: Fasting and Psoriasis — The Direct Clinical Evidence

## The Insight

Unlike most protocol components where psoriasis evidence is extrapolated from mechanism, fasting has DIRECT psoriasis clinical data. This is one of the strongest evidence bases in the entire skin disease campaign.

## The Evidence

### 1. Ramadan fasting studies
- **Adawi et al. 2019**: Psoriasis patients who fasted during Ramadan (dawn-to-dusk, ~14-16 hrs/day × 30 days) showed significant PASI improvement
- **Damiani et al. 2019**: Intermittent fasting during Ramadan → reduced psoriasis severity, lower CRP, improved metabolic parameters
- Mechanism: daily autophagy induction + BHB production during fasting hours + caloric restriction

### 2. Caloric restriction studies
- **Naldi et al. 2014**: Weight loss via caloric restriction improved psoriasis in obese patients (randomized trial)
- **Jensen et al. 2016**: Low-calorie diet (800-1000 kcal × 8 weeks) → PASI improvement in overweight psoriasis patients
- Mechanism: reduced adipose tissue → less TNF-α/IL-6 from fat → lower systemic inflammation

### 3. Ketogenic diet case reports
- Multiple case reports of psoriasis improvement on ketogenic diets
- Mechanism: sustained BHB → NLRP3 suppression + NF-κB modulation
- Parallels the patient's keto experience with T1DM (5 years insulin-free)

### 4. Prolonged fasting (historical)
- Clinical observations from the early 20th century: prolonged fasting (7-21 days) cleared psoriasis in some patients
- Effect was temporary — psoriasis returned weeks after resuming normal diet
- **This suggests fasting addresses the AMPLIFICATION but not the INITIATION**
- The loop quiets during fasting but the Th17 memory cells survive → loop restarts

## Why FMD May Outperform Simple Fasting

Simple fasting (Ramadan, caloric restriction) provides:
- BHB production (NLRP3 suppression) ✅
- Reduced caloric substrate for inflammation ✅
- Some autophagy ✅

FMD (5-day fasting-mimicking diet) adds:
- **Deeper autophagy** (5 continuous days > 14-16 hours daily)
- **Immune system reset** (Cheng 2014: FMD depletes and regenerates immune cells via HSC activation)
- **The refeeding window**: fresh naive T cells generated from HSCs during refeeding may have different Th17/Treg balance than the exhausted pre-fast population
- **Stem cell activation**: FMD activates tissue stem cells during refeeding (demonstrated for beta cells, intestinal stem cells — skin stem cells?)

## The Immune Reset Hypothesis

```
PRE-FMD:
  Existing Th17 memory cells → sustained IL-17 production → psoriasis
  Tregs exhausted from chronic suppression attempts
  NF-κB constitutively active in psoriatic keratinocytes

DURING FMD (days 1-5):
  Autophagy → clears damaged/senescent immune cells
  BHB → NLRP3 suppressed → IL-1β drops → Th17 differentiation paused
  Lymphocyte count drops 20-40% (normal fasting response)
  OLD, PATHOGENIC Th17 cells preferentially depleted (activated cells are metabolically hungry → die first during nutrient deprivation)

REFEEDING (days 6-7):
  HSCs activated → generate NEW naive T cells
  New naive T cells enter a DIFFERENT cytokine environment:
    If butyrate/vitamin D/IL-10 environment is established → more Tregs from new precursors
    If the pre-fasting inflammatory environment persists → more Th17 again
  
  THIS IS WHY THE PROTOCOL CONTEXT MATTERS:
  FMD alone → temporary improvement (new cells re-differentiate into Th17 in inflammatory environment)
  FMD + anti-inflammatory protocol → sustained improvement (new cells become Tregs in tolerogenic environment)
```

## The Protocol Sequence for Psoriasis

```
MONTH 1: Foundation (establish anti-inflammatory environment)
  Vitamin D 5,000 IU, butyrate 300mg TID, omega-3 3g, WHM daily
  → lower baseline NF-κB, establish Treg-favoring environment
  
MONTH 2: First FMD cycle
  5-day FMD → deplete pathogenic Th17 → autophagy → BHB peak
  Refeeding in the ESTABLISHED anti-inflammatory environment
  → new naive T cells preferentially become Tregs, not Th17
  
MONTH 3-6: Monthly FMD cycles
  Each cycle depletes more Th17, generates more Tregs
  Cumulative: Treg/Th17 ratio shifts progressively toward health
  
MONTH 6+: Maintenance
  Continue supplements + WHM
  FMD every 2-3 months (maintenance frequency)
  Psoriasis plaques should thin and reduce over this timeline
```
