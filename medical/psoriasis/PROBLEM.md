# Psoriasis

## The Problem
Chronic autoimmune/autoinflammatory skin disease characterized by well-demarcated erythematous plaques with silvery scale. Affects ~2-3% of the global population. Driven by the IL-23/Th17 axis. Associated with psoriatic arthritis (~30%), cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, and depression. No cure. Managed with topicals, phototherapy, and biologics (anti-TNF, anti-IL-17, anti-IL-23).

## Status: UNSOLVED (treatable, not curable)

## Key Biology

| Component | What | Role |
|-----------|------|------|
| Th17 cells | CD4+ T helper subset | Produce IL-17A/F, IL-22 → drive keratinocyte proliferation |
| IL-23 | DC-derived cytokine | Maintains and expands Th17 population (the upstream driver) |
| IL-17A | Th17 effector cytokine | Induces keratinocyte proliferation, neutrophil recruitment, antimicrobial peptides |
| IL-22 | Th17/Th22 cytokine | Drives epidermal hyperplasia (acanthosis) |
| TNF-α | Pro-inflammatory cytokine | Amplifies inflammation, drives NF-κB in keratinocytes |
| NF-κB | Transcription factor | Central amplifier — TNF-α → NF-κB → more TNF-α + chemokines + IL-1β |
| NLRP3 inflammasome | IL-1β platform | Activated in psoriatic keratinocytes; IL-1β amplifies Th17 differentiation |
| Keratinocytes | Epidermal cells | Hyperproliferate (turnover 3-5 days vs normal 28 days) → plaque formation |
| Dendritic cells | Antigen-presenting cells | Produce IL-23 → Th17 polarization (the initiating event) |
| Neutrophils | Innate immune cells | Form Munro microabscesses in epidermis; release NETs |
| HLA-C*06:02 | MHC class I allele | Strongest genetic risk factor (~10x risk); presents autoantigens to CD8+ T cells |
| LL-37 (cathelicidin) | Antimicrobial peptide | Autoantigen in psoriasis — LL-37/DNA complexes activate pDCs → IFN-α → Th17 |
| Gut microbiome | Intestinal bacteria | Psoriasis patients have gut dysbiosis; gut-skin axis contributes |

## The Disease Cascade
1. Genetic susceptibility (HLA-C*06:02 + IL-23R + IL-12B + TNFAIP3 variants)
2. Environmental trigger (stress, infection, trauma/Koebner, medications)
3. Keratinocyte stress → LL-37 release → complexes with self-DNA
4. LL-37/DNA activates plasmacytoid DCs → IFN-α production
5. IFN-α activates myeloid DCs → IL-23 production
6. IL-23 → Th17 cell differentiation and expansion
7. IL-17A + IL-22 → keratinocyte hyperproliferation (3-5 day turnover)
8. TNF-α amplification loop → NF-κB → more inflammation
9. Neutrophil recruitment → Munro microabscesses
10. NLRP3 activation → IL-1β → further Th17 differentiation (positive feedback)
11. Chronic plaque: self-sustaining IL-23/IL-17 axis with epidermal hyperplasia
